# IMPRESS: Evaluating the Resilience of Imperceptible Perturbations Against Unauthorized Data Usage in Diffusion-Based Generative AI

## Executive Summary
This paper evaluates the effectiveness of imperceptible perturbations in protecting images from unauthorized data usage in diffusion-based generative AI models. The authors propose IMPRESS, a perturbation purification platform that leverages the inconsistency between original and diffusion-reconstructed images to remove perturbations added by existing protection methods like GLAZE and PhotoGuard. Experiments show IMPRESS significantly improves performance on style mimicking and image editing tasks compared to baselines, achieving CLIP classifier accuracies of 87.0% (style mimicking) and SSIM of 0.49 (image editing) after purifying protected images. The method is also tested on a real-world image generation service, successfully removing protective noise. While adaptive protection methods using consistency-based losses show some improvement, they remain vulnerable to IMPRESS. Overall, IMPRESS provides a comprehensive evaluation platform for testing the effectiveness of imperceptible perturbations as protective measures against unauthorized data usage in diffusion models.

## Method Summary
IMPRESS formulates image purification as an optimization problem with two losses: similarity (LPIPS) and consistency (encoder-decoder reconstruction). The method optimizes the perturbed image to minimize the difference between its reconstructed version and itself, effectively removing the perturbation while preserving image content. The optimization uses hyperparameters α=0.1, ∆L=0.1, learning rate=1e-2 for style mimicking (3000 steps), and α=1e-2, ∆L=0.1, learning rate=5e-3 for image editing (1000 steps). The approach is evaluated on style mimicking using WikiArt dataset and malicious editing using Helen dataset, with protected images generated by GLAZE and PhotoGuard methods.

## Key Results
- IMPRESS achieves 87.0% CLIP classifier accuracy on style mimicking after purifying GLAZE-protected images, compared to 3.8% without purification
- IMPRESS achieves SSIM of 0.49 on image editing tasks after purifying PhotoGuard-protected images, compared to 0.11 without purification
- IMPRESS successfully removes protective noise from real-world commercial image generation services

## Architecture Onboarding

- Component map: Image Encoder (E) -> Image Decoder (D) -> Latent Diffusion Model (LDM) -> IMPRESS Optimizer
- Critical path:
  1. Encode the perturbed image using the image encoder
  2. Decode the latent representation back into an image using the image decoder
  3. Calculate the consistency loss between the original perturbed image and its reconstructed version
  4. Calculate the similarity loss between the perturbed image and the original image
  5. Optimize the perturbed image using the combined loss to remove the perturbation
- Design tradeoffs: Balancing the similarity and consistency losses to effectively remove perturbations while preserving image content; choosing appropriate hyperparameters to ensure convergence and avoid introducing artifacts
- Failure signatures: Purified image looks noticeably different from original (similarity loss too low); purified image still gets misclassified or fails to reconstruct accurately (consistency loss too low); optimization process does not converge or takes too long (hyperparameters need tuning)
- First 3 experiments:
  1. Test IMPRESS on a simple case: apply it to an image protected by PhotoGuard or GLAZE and verify that it can successfully remove the perturbation and restore the original image
  2. Test IMPRESS on a more challenging case: apply it to an image protected by an adaptive protection method and measure its effectiveness
  3. Test IMPRESS on a real-world scenario: apply it to images protected by a commercial image generation service and evaluate its performance

## Limitations
- The evaluation relies on CLIP and diffusion classifiers which may not fully capture all aspects of style transfer or editing quality
- Adaptive protection methods show some improvement but more sophisticated defenses might better resist IMPRESS
- Effectiveness on non-diffusion-based generative models remains untested
