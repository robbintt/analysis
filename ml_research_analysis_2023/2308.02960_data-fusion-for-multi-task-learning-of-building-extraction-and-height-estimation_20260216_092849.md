# Data Fusion for Multi-Task Learning of Building Extraction and Height Estimation

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2308.02960
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.02960
- Authors: 
- Reference count: 0

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the urban reconstruction problem of building extraction and height estimation from satellite imagery using data fusion techniques. The authors implemented individual models for each task rather than a true multi-task learning approach, finding that early fusion of RGB and SAR data with ResNet-101 backbone yielded strong results for building extraction. For height estimation, early fusion with skip connections provided the best performance. The study demonstrates the effectiveness of data fusion in improving performance for both tasks in urban reconstruction.

## Method Summary
The paper implements individual models for building extraction and height estimation rather than true multi-task learning. For building extraction, Mask R-CNN with ResNet-101 backbone was used with data augmentation and early fusion of RGB and SAR data. For height estimation, various data fusion strategies were tested including early fusion with skip connections. The models were trained on satellite imagery from multiple sources including Superview-1, Gaofen-2, and Gaofeng-3 satellites, with DSM data from Gaofeng-7 and WorldView satellites covering 17 cities across 6 continents.

## Key Results
- Building extraction achieved mAP 50 score of 49.8% using Mask R-CNN with ResNet-101 backbone and early fusion of RGB and SAR data
- Height estimation achieved a score of 30.65% using early fusion with skip connections
- ResNet-101 backbone outperformed ResNet-50 for building extraction, improving mAP 50 from 49.1 to 49.3
- Early fusion strategy was superior for both tasks compared to intermediate and late fusion approaches

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: Early fusion of RGB and SAR data improves building extraction performance by combining complementary spectral and structural information at the input layer.
- Mechanism: By stacking RGB and SAR channels before feeding into the ResNet backbone, the model learns joint feature representations that capture both color/texture from optical data and backscatter/height cues from radar data.
- Core assumption: The complementary information in RGB and SAR modalities is most effectively captured when merged at the earliest stage of the network.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "early fusion of RGB and SAR data" yielded mAP 50 of 49.8%
  - [section] "To increase the modality from 3 dimensions to 4 dimensions to accommodate the concatenation of an additional SAR channel, the model was customized."
- Break condition: If the SAR data contains noise or misalignment that corrupts the RGB signal, early fusion could degrade performance rather than improve it.

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: Skip connections in height estimation models preserve spatial resolution and improve prediction accuracy by allowing gradient flow from deeper layers to earlier ones.
- Mechanism: Skip connections route low-level feature maps from encoder layers to corresponding decoder layers, enabling the model to retain fine-grained spatial details lost during downsampling.
- Core assumption: Height estimation benefits from both coarse semantic context and fine spatial detail, which skip connections can provide.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "early fusion with skip connections yielded the best results, achieving a score of 30.65%"
  - [section] "For the building height estimation, all three data fusion methods were experimented on. A few alternate decoding strategies were also studied... Attempts were made to involve transpose convolutions."
- Break condition: If the model architecture is already shallow or the input resolution is too low, skip connections may add unnecessary complexity without benefit.

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: Using a deeper ResNet backbone (ResNet-101 vs ResNet-50) increases model capacity and improves building extraction performance.
- Mechanism: ResNet-101 has more layers and parameters, allowing it to learn more complex feature hierarchies and better distinguish building structures from background.
- Core assumption: The additional computational cost of ResNet-101 is justified by the performance gain in building extraction tasks.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [section] "it was discovered that resnet101 backbone performed better than resnet50 backbone. Hence, Mask-RCNN using resnet101 improved the result of the RGB image input from mAP 50 = 49.1 baseline result to mAP 50 = 49.3"
  - [section] "After experimentation, it was discovered that resnet101 backbone performed better than resnet50 backbone."
- Break condition: If the dataset is too small or the task too simple, the increased capacity of ResNet-101 may lead to overfitting without performance gains.

## Architecture Onboarding

- Component map:
  Input: RGB satellite imagery (3 channels) + SAR imagery (1 channel) -> Backbone: ResNet-101 for feature extraction -> Detection head: Mask R-CNN for building extraction (bounding boxes + segmentation masks) -> Height estimation head: Custom decoder with skip connections for per-pixel height regression -> Fusion strategies: Early fusion (channel stacking), intermediate fusion (feature concatenation), late fusion (model averaging)

- Critical path:
  1. Data preprocessing and augmentation
  2. Early fusion of RGB and SAR channels
  3. Feature extraction using ResNet-101 backbone
  4. Mask R-CNN detection and segmentation
  5. Height estimation using custom decoder with skip connections
  6. Model evaluation and metric calculation (mAP 50 for extraction, accuracy score for height estimation)

- Design tradeoffs:
  - Early fusion vs. intermediate vs. late fusion: Early fusion is computationally efficient but may not capture modality-specific features as well as intermediate fusion.
  - ResNet-101 vs. ResNet-50: Deeper network provides better performance but increases computational cost and risk of overfitting.
  - Single-task vs. multi-task learning: Separate models allow task-specific optimization but miss potential synergies from shared feature learning.

- Failure signatures:
  - Low mAP scores: Could indicate poor data quality, insufficient training data, or suboptimal model architecture/hyperparameters.
  - High RMSE in height estimation: May suggest issues with data fusion strategy, model capacity, or label quality.
  - Mode collapse in predictions: Could indicate overfitting, insufficient regularization, or data imbalance.

- First 3 experiments:
  1. Baseline evaluation: Run Mask R-CNN with ResNet-50 on RGB data only to establish baseline mAP 50 score.
  2. Early fusion comparison: Implement early fusion of RGB and SAR data and compare performance against RGB-only baseline.
  3. Backbone comparison: Replace ResNet-50 with ResNet-101 and evaluate impact on building extraction performance.

## Limitations

- The study used a single-city dataset, limiting generalizability across diverse urban environments and building types
- Multi-task learning was not fully implemented, with tasks running separately rather than jointly, potentially missing shared feature learning benefits
- Height estimation results (30.65% score) lack context for interpretation and comparison with alternative approaches

## Confidence

- **High Confidence**: Building extraction performance metrics (mAP 50 = 49.8%) and the effectiveness of ResNet-101 backbone are well-supported by experimental results.
- **Medium Confidence**: The superiority of early fusion for both tasks is demonstrated, though comparison with intermediate and late fusion could be more comprehensive.
- **Low Confidence**: The height estimation results (30.65% score) lack context for interpretation and comparison with alternative approaches.
