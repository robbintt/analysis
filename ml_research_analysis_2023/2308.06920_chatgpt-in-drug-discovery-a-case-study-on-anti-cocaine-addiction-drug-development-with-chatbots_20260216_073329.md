# ChatGPT in Drug Discovery: A Case Study on Anti-Cocaine Addiction Drug Development with Chatbots

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2308.06920
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.06920
- Reference count: 31
- Key outcome: Novel AI-driven platform (SGNC) identifies 15 candidate compounds for anti-cocaine addiction with predicted binding affinities below -9.54 kcal/mol

## Executive Summary
This study introduces the Stochastic Generative Network Complex (SGNC), a novel AI-driven platform for anti-cocaine addiction drug discovery. By leveraging ChatGPT-4 as a virtual guide, the research team designed a multi-target optimization approach that integrates diffusion-based stochastic methods with deep learning models to generate promising drug-like molecules. The SGNC successfully identified 15 candidate compounds targeting dopamine (DAT), norepinephrine (NET), and serotonin (SERT) transporters, with predicted binding affinities below -9.54 kcal/mol for each target. Additionally, 10 ADMET properties were evaluated, and all 15 candidates met drug-likeness criteria. Molecular docking analysis confirmed hydrogen bonding interactions, further validating the effectiveness of the SGNC model. This work demonstrates the potential of integrating large language models into AI-driven drug discovery, offering a new paradigm for multi-target therapeutic development.

## Method Summary
The study developed an AI-driven platform integrating ChatGPT-4 with three personas (idea generation, methodology clarification, and coding assistance) to guide the development of a stochastic generative model for drug discovery. The core methodology involved a Sequence-to-Sequence AutoEncoder to map molecules between SMILES strings and latent space, binding affinity predictors for four targets (DAT, NET, SERT, hERG), and a stochastic-based molecular generator using Langevin equation to modify latent vectors. The process involved generating molecules in latent space, decoding to SMILES, predicting binding affinities, and screening with ADMETlab 2.0. The approach targeted multi-target optimization for cocaine addiction treatment.

## Architecture Onboarding

- Component map: Seq2Seq AE -> Binding Affinity Predictors (4 networks) -> Stochastic-based Molecular Generator (Langevin equation) -> ADMET Screening (ADMETlab 2.0) -> ChatGPT (3 personas)

- Critical path:
  1. Input reference and seed molecules into Seq2Seq AE to obtain latent vectors
  2. Modify seed vector in stochastic-based generator using Langevin equation
  3. Decode modified vectors into SMILES strings
  4. Predict binding affinities using trained predictors
  5. Filter molecules based on binding affinity and ADMET criteria
  6. Validate and refine using ADMETlab 2.0

- Design tradeoffs:
  - Balance between exploration and exploitation in latent space optimization
  - Tradeoff between model complexity (deeper networks) and computational efficiency
  - Choice of similarity metrics for selecting reference compounds

- Failure signatures:
  - Invalid SMILES strings upon decoding (latent space destabilization)
  - Poor binding affinity predictions (overfitting of predictors)
  - Inaccurate ADMET predictions (limitations of in silico tools)

- First 3 experiments:
  1. Verify the reconstruction rate of the Seq2Seq AE model using a subset of the dataset
  2. Test the binding affinity predictors on a held-out test set to assess their accuracy
  3. Generate a small set of molecules using the stochastic-based generator and evaluate their validity using RDKit
