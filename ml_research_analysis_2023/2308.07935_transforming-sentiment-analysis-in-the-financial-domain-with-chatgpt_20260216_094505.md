# Transforming Sentiment Analysis in the Financial Domain with ChatGPT

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2308.07935
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.07935
- **Reference count**: 5
- **Primary result**: ChatGPT 3.5 achieves approximately 35% enhanced sentiment classification performance and 36% higher correlation with market returns compared to FinBERT in forex market analysis

## Executive Summary
This study evaluates ChatGPT 3.5's potential for sentiment analysis in the financial domain, specifically focusing on forex market news headlines. Using a zero-shot prompting approach with a meticulously curated dataset of 2,291 forex-related news headlines, the researchers compare ChatGPT's performance against FinBERT, a well-established financial sentiment analysis model. The results demonstrate that ChatGPT exhibits significantly enhanced performance in sentiment classification and stronger correlation with actual market returns. The study emphasizes the critical role of prompt engineering in optimizing ChatGPT's performance for financial sentiment analysis tasks and makes the dataset publicly available to encourage further research in this field.

## Method Summary
The study employs a zero-shot prompting approach using ChatGPT 3.5-turbo to perform sentiment analysis on forex news headlines without task-specific fine-tuning. A dataset of 2,291 forex-related news headlines was collected from Forex Live and FXstreet platforms and manually annotated for sentiment impact on five major forex pairs. The researchers tested multiple prompt variations (GPT-P1 through GPT-P6) to optimize performance and compared results against FinBERT using standard metrics including accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, and market correlation analysis. The study also examined both categorical classification and numerical sentiment scoring approaches.

## Key Results
- ChatGPT achieved approximately 35% enhanced performance in sentiment classification compared to FinBERT
- ChatGPT showed 36% higher correlation with market returns than FinBERT
- Numerical sentiment scoring (range [-1, 1]) demonstrated better market alignment than categorical classification

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: Zero-shot prompting enables ChatGPT to perform financial sentiment analysis without task-specific fine-tuning.
- Mechanism: ChatGPT leverages its extensive pre-training on diverse textual data to understand and interpret domain-specific language patterns in forex-related news headlines. The model uses its learned linguistic representations to infer sentiment without requiring additional domain-specific training.
- Core assumption: ChatGPT's pre-training data included sufficient financial and news-related content to enable understanding of forex terminology and sentiment patterns.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "Employing a zero-shot prompting approach, we examine multiple ChatGPT prompts on a meticulously curated dataset of forex-related news headlines"
  - [section 3.3] "With zero-shot prompting, we harness ChatGPT's pre-existing training and understanding of language patterns and context to generate desired responses without any task-specific fine-tuning"
- Break condition: If ChatGPT's pre-training corpus lacked sufficient financial domain coverage, the zero-shot performance would degrade significantly.

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: Strategic prompt engineering significantly impacts ChatGPT's sentiment analysis performance in financial contexts.
- Mechanism: Different prompt formulations (role-playing as financial expert, forex trader, or sentiment analysis model) guide ChatGPT to focus on relevant aspects of the text, affecting both accuracy and market correlation. The prompts act as task instructions that leverage the model's ability to adapt to different contexts.
- Core assumption: ChatGPT's architecture allows flexible interpretation of task instructions through carefully crafted prompts.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "By underlining the significance of prompt engineering, particularly in zero-shot contexts, this study spotlights ChatGPT's potential to substantially boost sentiment analysis in financial applications"
  - [section 3.3] "We devised and conducted a series of experiments through a zero-shot prompting approach... This approach allows us to directly evaluate ChatGPT's inherent capabilities in sentiment analysis without any additional modifications"
- Break condition: If prompt variations don't substantially affect output, the observed performance differences may be due to chance rather than prompt engineering.

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: Numerical sentiment scoring (range [-1, 1]) provides better market alignment than categorical classification.
- Mechanism: Continuous sentiment scores capture nuanced variations in sentiment intensity that categorical labels cannot represent, allowing for more precise correlation with market movements. The numerical format enables better representation of subtle sentiment differences that influence trading decisions.
- Core assumption: Market responses to news are more accurately captured by continuous sentiment values rather than discrete categories.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [section 4.2] "Most models generating sentiment scores ranging from -1 to 1 instead of performing sentiment classification exhibit a more fitting alignment with the market movements"
  - [abstract] "ChatGPT exhibited approximately 35% enhanced performance in sentiment classification and a 36% higher correlation with market returns compared to FinBERT"
- Break condition: If categorical models show equivalent or superior market correlation, the benefit of numerical scoring may be context-dependent.

## Limitations
- The analysis is based on a relatively small dataset of 2,291 headlines collected over a five-month period, which may not capture full market complexity across different economic cycles
- Manual annotation introduces potential subjectivity in sentiment labeling, particularly for neutral cases where impact on forex pairs may be ambiguous
- The study focuses exclusively on forex markets, limiting generalizability to other financial domains such as stocks, commodities, or cryptocurrencies
