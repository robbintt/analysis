# Assessment of the suitability of degradation models for the planning of CCTV inspections of sewer pipes

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2307.06341
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.06341
- Reference count: 25
- Primary result: Logistic Regression provides interpretable, monotonic degradation curves suitable for inspection planning despite lower accuracy than ensemble methods

## Executive Summary
This paper evaluates statistical and machine learning models for predicting sewer pipe degradation to optimize CCTV inspection planning. Using a dataset of 4,899 German sewer pipes, the study compares Logistic Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost, SVM, ANN, and Decision Tree models on accuracy, long-term degradation simulation ability, and interpretability. While ensemble methods achieve higher accuracy, Logistic Regression produces consistent monotonic degradation curves and interpretable coefficients that domain experts can validate. The findings demonstrate that slightly less accurate models may be preferable when interpretability and reliable long-term predictions are crucial for maintenance decision-making.

## Method Summary
The study trains six machine learning models (Logistic Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost, SVM, ANN with 2 hidden layers, and Decision Tree) on a German sewer pipe dataset with 4,899 pipes and 6,279 inspections spanning 2000-2021. Features include pipe characteristics (age, length, size, depth, slope, material) and environmental factors. The models are evaluated using 70% training and 30% test splits, with performance measured through accuracy, recall, precision, AUC, and the ability to produce monotonic degradation curves. The Logistic Regression model is selected for its interpretability through coefficients and its ability to generate reliable long-term degradation predictions.

## Key Results
- Logistic Regression achieves slightly lower accuracy but produces monotonic degradation curves essential for long-term inspection planning
- Random Forest and XGBoost models show highest accuracy but fail to produce consistent monotonic long-term predictions
- Logistic Regression provides interpretable coefficients with odds ratios that domain experts can validate against domain knowledge
- The selected Logistic Regression model effectively simulates degradation curves and generates inspection plans using probability thresholds

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: Logistic Regression provides reliable long-term degradation curves while maintaining reasonable accuracy for sewer pipe inspection planning.
- Mechanism: LR's monotonic sigmoid probability output naturally captures the gradual deterioration process of sewer pipes without maintenance, while its interpretable coefficients allow domain experts to understand factor impacts.
- Core assumption: Sewer pipe deterioration is a continuous process that can be modeled as monotonic probability increase over time when no maintenance occurs.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "Logistic Regression offers a slightly less accurate model that is able to produce consistent degradation curves with a high explainability"
  - [section] "degradation curves are expected to increase monotonically with respect to time, given that no maintenance tasks are considered"

- Break condition: If pipe condition could improve without maintenance (contradicts core assumption), or if non-monotonic factors dominate deterioration.

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: Random Forest and XGBoost achieve highest accuracy but fail to produce monotonic long-term predictions.
- Mechanism: Ensemble methods capture complex non-linear relationships for accurate short-term classification, but their averaging process across decision trees doesn't guarantee monotonic probability progression over time.
- Core assumption: Higher accuracy in classification metrics directly translates to better inspection planning performance.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "ensemble models yield the highest accuracy, they are unable to infer the long-term degradation of the pipes"
  - [section] "XGB and RF... fail to show a monotonic degradation curve" and "degradation curves that result from this model do not show a continuous deterioration"
  - [corpus] Assumption: Neighbor papers mention "multi-label image classification" but don't directly address long-term prediction monotonicity
- Break condition: If monotonic degradation is not required for effective inspection planning, or if ensemble models could be constrained for monotonicity.

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: Model interpretability through coefficients is essential for domain expert trust and maintenance decision-making.
- Mechanism: Logistic Regression's coefficients provide Odds Ratios that quantify each factor's impact on failure probability, enabling experts to validate model logic against domain knowledge.
- Core assumption: Domain experts need to understand and validate model predictions rather than treating them as black boxes.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "explainability" and "Logistic Regression... offers a slightly less accurate model that is able to produce consistent degradation curves with a high explainability"
  - [section] "the LR is the only one that yields an interpretable result based on the coefficients of its linear estimator"

- Break condition: If inspection planning can be fully automated without expert validation, or if other interpretability methods suffice.
