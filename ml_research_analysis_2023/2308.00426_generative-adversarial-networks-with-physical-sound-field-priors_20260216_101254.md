# Generative adversarial networks with physical sound field priors

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2308.00426
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.00426
- Reference count: 0
- Key outcome: GAN-based sound field reconstruction with physical priors shows improved accuracy, especially at high frequencies and in extrapolation scenarios

## Executive Summary
This paper presents a novel deep learning approach for sound field reconstruction using Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) with physical priors. The method leverages a plane wave basis to learn the statistical distributions of pressure in rooms, enabling accurate reconstruction from sparse measurements. By incorporating physical constraints, the approach achieves superior performance compared to state-of-the-art methods, particularly in high-frequency ranges and when extrapolating beyond measurement regions.

## Method Summary
The proposed method combines GANs with a plane wave basis expansion to reconstruct sound fields from limited measurements. The generator network learns to map latent variables to plane wave coefficients, while the discriminator evaluates the quality of generated sound fields. The method is trained on synthetic sound fields generated using a random wave field model and evaluated on two established datasets (DTU and MeshRIR). The reconstruction is performed by optimizing the latent space to fit measured data and projecting the results onto the plane wave basis.

## Key Results
- Improved reconstruction accuracy in terms of Normalized Mean Square Error (NMSE) and Spatial Similarity (SS)
- Superior performance in high-frequency ranges compared to baseline methods
- Ability to handle varying numbers of measurement positions and configurations without performance degradation
- Successful extrapolation beyond measurement regions

- **Component map:**
  - Latent space -> Generator network -> Plane wave coefficients -> Sound field
  - Measured data -> Latent space optimization -> Plane wave coefficients -> Reconstructed sound field
  - Real sound fields -> Discriminator network -> Evaluation of generated sound fields

- **Critical path:**
  1. Train the GAN on synthetic sound fields generated using a plane wave basis
  2. Use the trained generator to map latent variables to plane wave coefficients
  3. Project the estimated plane wave coefficients onto the plane wave basis to obtain the reconstructed sound field
  4. Evaluate the reconstruction accuracy using metrics like NMSE and SS

- **Design tradeoffs:**
  - Number of plane waves vs. computational complexity

  - GAN instability during training (e.g., mode collapse)
  - Poor generalization to out-of-distribution measurement configurations

## Limitations

- Poor performance at low frequencies (below 400Hz) due to rank deficiency and highly correlated columns in the plane wave dictionary
- Dependency on synthetic training data quality and its representativeness for real-world scenarios
- Limited generalizability beyond tested datasets (DTU and MeshRIR)
