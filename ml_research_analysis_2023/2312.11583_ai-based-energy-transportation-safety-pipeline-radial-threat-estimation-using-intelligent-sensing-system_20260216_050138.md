# AI-Based Energy Transportation Safety: Pipeline Radial Threat Estimation Using Intelligent Sensing System

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2312.11583
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.11583
- Reference count: 16
- Method achieves 97.82% precision, 97.67% recall, and 0.99% false alarm rate for radial threat classification in pipeline safety monitoring

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces an AI-driven method for precise radial threat estimation in energy pipelines using distributed optical fiber sensing. The approach addresses the challenge of distinguishing external threats' radial distances from pipelines to reduce false alarms and improve safety. By employing a continuous multi-view and multi-domain feature fusion technique that extracts rich signal features across time, frequency, and space domains, the method achieves high accuracy in threat recognition and localization. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach with precision of 97.82%, recall of 97.67%, and F1-score of 97.69%, while reducing false alarm rates to 0.99%.

## Method Summary
The method combines multi-view, multi-domain feature fusion with transfer learning to estimate radial threats in pipeline safety monitoring. Raw acoustic signals from distributed optical fiber sensors are first preprocessed using variational mode decomposition (VMD) for denoising and signal reconstruction. Time-frequency maps are then constructed for each defense zone and its adjacent zones, and these are fused via image stitching to create rich feature representations. An EfficientNetV2 model with transfer learning is trained on these fused features to classify threats into three radial distance categories. The approach leverages spatial information from adjacent zones and temporal vibration patterns to enhance feature differentiation for fine-grained localization.

## Key Results
- Achieves precision of 97.82%, recall of 97.67%, and F1-score of 97.69% for radial threat classification
- Reduces false alarm rate to 0.99% compared to traditional methods
- Demonstrates effective identification of true threats with potential for generalization to broader recognition tasks

- Design tradeoffs:
  - Using adjacent defense zones increases feature richness but also computational complexity and potential noise inclusion.
  - Transfer learning speeds up training but may introduce domain mismatch if pre-trained features are not well-aligned with acoustic signal characteristics.
  - VMD improves signal quality but adds preprocessing overhead and complexity.
- Failure signatures:
  - High false alarm rate despite model accuracy may indicate issues in feature fusion or signal preprocessing.
  - Slow convergence or overfitting may suggest insufficient regularization or inappropriate transfer learning initialization.
  - Poor performance on tracking area classification may indicate feature overlap between classes or insufficient training samples.
