# Knowledge-infused Deep Learning Enables Interpretable Landslide Forecasting

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2307.08951
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.08951
- Reference count: 40
- One-line primary result: LFIT achieves superior landslide forecasting performance while providing interpretable insights through knowledge-infused transformer architecture

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces LFIT, a knowledge-infused deep learning approach that integrates geological prior knowledge and environmental variables into a transformer-based neural network for landslide forecasting. The method demonstrates improved predictive accuracy and interpretability by mapping categorical geological descriptions and environmental factors into high-dimensional embeddings, using gating mechanisms to filter relevant features, and applying self-attention to reveal temporal patterns and influential factors. The approach achieves lower error metrics compared to traditional data-driven models while providing transparent insights into the factors driving landslide behavior.

## Method Summary
LFIT employs a transformer-based neural network architecture that integrates prior geological knowledge through embeddings and gating mechanisms. The model maps categorical geological descriptions and environmental variables into high-dimensional representations, uses gate mechanisms (ELU and GLU) to filter and weight features, and applies LSTM-based encoder-decoder structures with self-attention blocks for temporal dynamics. Training uses quantile regression loss to provide prediction intervals that capture uncertainty, optimized via mini-batch gradient descent with Adam optimizer and early stopping.

## Key Results
- LFIT achieves lower error metrics (MAE, RMSE, MAPE, SMAPE) compared to traditional data-driven models
- The interpretable nature provides insights into factors driving landslide behavior and their spatial-temporal patterns
- Incorporating prior knowledge and environmental variables significantly enhances predictability, especially for complex cases like reservoir and creeping landslides on the Tibetan Plateau
