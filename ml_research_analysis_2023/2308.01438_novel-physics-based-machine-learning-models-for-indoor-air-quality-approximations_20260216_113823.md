# Novel Physics-Based Machine-Learning Models for Indoor Air Quality Approximations

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2308.01438
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.01438
- Reference count: 40
- Novel physics-based ML models combining state-space physics with GRUs and decomposition achieve superior IAQ prediction performance

## Executive Summary
This study introduces six novel physics-based machine learning models that combine state-space concepts from physics, Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs), and decomposition techniques to predict indoor air quality (IAQ), specifically carbon dioxide concentrations. Tested on data from five offices in a California commercial building, the models demonstrated superior performance compared to state-of-the-art transformer-based approaches, achieving lower Mean Squared Errors and greater computational efficiency. The best-performing model, FD-SS-GRU, achieved an MSE of 0.000988 for 3-hour-ahead predictions while maintaining strong performance across different prediction horizons.

## Method Summary
The study develops six novel physics-based ML models that integrate state-space modeling with recurrent neural networks and decomposition techniques. The models use differential equations to represent physical dynamics of IAQ systems, incorporate GRU gating mechanisms for temporal dependencies, and apply decomposition to separate trend/seasonal components from noise. The approach combines domain-specific knowledge about mass capacitance and ventilation effects directly into the neural network architecture, trained on hourly IAQ data from five offices in a California commercial building using TensorFlow 2+ on NVIDIA Volta V100 GPU.

## Key Results
- FD-SS-GRU model achieved MSE of 0.000988 for 3-hour-ahead CO2 predictions, outperforming transformer-based models
- All six proposed models demonstrated lower MSE than baseline transformer approaches across 1, 2, 3, and 6-hour prediction horizons
- Physics-based models showed greater computational efficiency while maintaining or improving prediction accuracy
- GRU-based models consistently outperformed RNN-based variants, validating the gating mechanism's effectiveness

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: Decomposition techniques reduce noise and extract meaningful temporal patterns, improving prediction accuracy for IAQ data.
- Mechanism: Decomposition separates the time series into trend, seasonal, and residual components, allowing the model to focus on the underlying physical patterns rather than high-frequency fluctuations.
- Core assumption: IAQ data contains both deterministic (trend/seasonal) and stochastic (noise) components that can be effectively separated.

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) effectively filter input data and capture temporal dependencies, enhancing the model's ability to predict IAQ.
- Mechanism: GRUs use gating mechanisms to control information flow, allowing the model to retain relevant information over long sequences while discarding noise.
- Core assumption: IAQ data exhibits temporal dependencies where past states influence future concentrations, and GRU gates can effectively learn which information to retain.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "The proposed models include an adroit combination of... Gated Recurrent Units, and Decomposition techniques."
  - [section 4.2.2] "In SS-GRU, the gate is a GRU, a type of neural network unit that uses a gate to control the flow of information into the hidden state."

- Break condition: If the gating mechanism becomes too restrictive and prevents the model from learning necessary long-term dependencies, or if the IAQ data is too noisy for the GRU to effectively filter.

  - High training MSE but low test MSE: Overfitting due to insufficient regularization.

  - Training MSE decreases but test MSE increases: Model memorizing training data; consider early stopping or data augmentation.

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out

### Open Question 1

- Basis in paper: [explicit] The study tested models on data from five offices in a commercial building in California. The paper does not mention testing on other building types.
- Why unresolved: The study's dataset is limited to a single commercial building, which may not capture the variability in IAQ patterns across different building types with varying occupancy, ventilation systems, and pollution sources.
- What evidence would resolve it: Testing the proposed models on IAQ datasets from residential, industrial, and other commercial buildings to compare performance and generalizability across building types.

## Limitations
- Study focuses exclusively on CO2 concentration prediction, limiting generalizability to other IAQ parameters
- Dataset coverage limited to single commercial building in California, raising questions about cross-building generalizability
- Paper does not address potential data quality issues or sensor calibration drifts that could affect long-term prediction reliability
