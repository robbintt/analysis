# SimPSI: A Simple Strategy to Preserve Spectral Information in Time Series Data Augmentation

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2312.05790
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.05790
- Authors: 
- Reference count: 8
- Primary result: Preserves spectral information during time series augmentation to improve classification performance across multiple domains

## Executive Summary
SimPSI addresses the fundamental problem that time series data augmentation techniques often degrade performance by destroying important spectral information. The method preserves spectral information by mixing the original and augmented input spectrum weighted by a preservation map that indicates the importance score of each frequency. Three types of preservation maps are proposed: magnitude spectrum, saliency map, and spectrum-preservative map. Experiments show that SimPSI significantly improves the performance of time series data augmentations across various benchmarks including signal demodulation, human activity recognition, sleep stage detection, and atrial fibrillation classification.

## Method Summary
SimPSI preserves spectral information during time series augmentation by converting data to frequency domain using FFT, mixing the original and augmented spectra weighted by a preservation map, then converting back using IFFT. The preservation map indicates the importance score of each frequency, allowing critical spectral regions to be retained while non-informative regions are distorted. Three preservation map types are implemented: magnitude spectrum (uses frequency magnitude), saliency map (uses gradient-based importance), and spectrum-preservative map (learned via contrastive loss). The method is applied to existing augmentation techniques like Jittering, Scaling, Frequency masking, and Permutation.

## Key Results
- On HAR dataset, SimPSI increased accuracy by up to 1.5% and AUPRC by up to 2.2% depending on augmentation technique and model architecture
- Resolved data domain dependency issues by preserving core spectral information across different time series domains
- Consistently improved performance regardless of task, unlike baseline augmentations which showed variable effectiveness

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: SimPSI preserves task-relevant spectral information during time series augmentation by mixing original and augmented spectra using a preservation map.
- Mechanism: The method applies a preservation map to weight the importance of frequency components. Frequencies deemed important retain values closer to the original spectrum, while less important frequencies are replaced by augmented values.
- Core assumption: The preservation map accurately identifies which spectral components are essential for the classification task.

  - [section] "It enables us to retain important spectral regions and distort non-informative regions during augmentation."

- Break condition: If the preservation map fails to identify task-relevant frequencies, the augmentation could still destroy important information or preserve irrelevant components.

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: The preservation map can be constructed using different strategies (magnitude spectrum, saliency map, spectrum-preservative map) to capture different notions of importance.
- Mechanism: Three preservation map types are proposed: magnitude spectrum uses frequency magnitude, saliency map uses gradient-based importance, and spectrum-preservative map uses a learned generator with contrastive loss.
- Core assumption: Different tasks benefit from different preservation map constructions, and at least one construction captures the relevant information for any given task.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [section] "Specifically, our experimental contributions are to build three distinct preservation maps: magnitude spectrum, saliency map, and spectrum-preservative map."

- Break condition: If none of the three preservation map types capture task-relevant information, SimPSI would not improve performance over standard augmentation.

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: SimPSI resolves data domain dependency issues in time series augmentation by preserving core spectral information.
- Mechanism: By preserving essential spectral information during augmentation, SimPSI ensures that augmented data remains useful across different time series domains rather than being task-specific.
- Core assumption: Spectral information is more universal across time series domains than time-domain perturbations, which are heuristic and domain-dependent.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "The method resolves the data domain dependency issue of augmentation techniques by preserving core spectral information."

- Break condition: If certain tasks rely primarily on time-domain features rather than spectral features, preserving spectral information may not resolve domain dependency issues.

## Limitations

- Computational overhead from additional FFT/IFFT operations and preservation map computation may be prohibitive for large-scale applications
- Performance improvements vary considerably across datasets and augmentation techniques, suggesting limited generalizability
- The spectrum-preservative map approach requires additional training infrastructure that may not be practical for all applications

## Confidence

- High Confidence: The core mechanism of spectral information preservation through mixing original and augmented spectra is well-established and mathematically sound. The experimental results showing consistent improvements across multiple datasets and augmentation techniques are reproducible.
- Medium Confidence: The claim that SimPSI resolves data domain dependency issues is supported by experimental evidence but may be overstated. While spectral preservation helps, the fundamental issue of domain-specific augmentation heuristics remains partially unaddressed.
- Low Confidence: The long-term generalization of the spectrum-preservative map approach and its behavior on truly unseen time series domains has not been thoroughly validated.
