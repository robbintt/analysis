# AsdKB: A Chinese Knowledge Base for the Early Screening and Diagnosis of Autism Spectrum Disorder

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID:** 2307.16773
- **Source URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.16773
- **Reference count:** 32
- **Primary result:** 81% coverage in answering ASD-related questions with 97.02% accuracy

## Executive Summary
AsdKB is a Chinese knowledge base on autism spectrum disorder created by extracting and integrating knowledge from sources including SNOMED CT, ICD-10, DSM-5, screening tools, and expert knowledge from the Web. The knowledge base contains both ontological and factual knowledge accessible as Linked Data. It supports applications in question answering, auxiliary diagnosis, and expert recommendation. Quality evaluation shows high accuracy (97.02% ± 1.21%) and effective performance in answering ASD-related questions (81% coverage).

## Method Summary
AsdKB was created through a three-step process: (1) building an ontology following Ontology Development 101 covering diagnostic standards, symptoms, and expert knowledge; (2) extracting factual knowledge using sequence labeling, template matching, table extraction, and Web crawling techniques; and (3) evaluating quality through accuracy assessment and task evaluation. The system integrates heterogeneous data sources into a unified RDF store (Neo4j) using permanent dereferenceable URIs in the w3id namespace, enabling interoperability with other medical ontologies.

## Key Results
- 97.02% accuracy (±1.21%) in triple validation from random sample of 732 triples
- 81% coverage in answering ASD-related questions
- Linked Data compliance with permanent dereferenceable URIs in w3id namespace
- Integration capability with UMLS and ADAR ontologies

## Limitations
- 97.02% accuracy represents a static snapshot from limited sample, raising questions about temporal stability
- 81% coverage claim lacks context about test question set composition and error analysis
- No validation of semantic fidelity when translating Chinese content to English standards

## Confidence
- High confidence in the Linked Data infrastructure and ontology design principles
- Medium confidence in the extraction pipeline's general approach based on established NLP techniques
- Low confidence in the real-world applicability of accuracy and coverage metrics without understanding their representativeness

## Next Checks
1. **Temporal Stability Test**: Re-run the 732-triple accuracy evaluation on samples extracted from different time periods (e.g., pre- and post-update) to assess whether accuracy degrades as new data sources are incorporated.
2. **Error Category Analysis**: Manually categorize the 19% of questions that fail in the QA coverage test to distinguish between truly unanswerable questions versus those requiring more sophisticated reasoning or additional ontology refinement.
3. **Cross-lingual Consistency Check**: Translate a subset of AsdKB content to English and compare against existing English ASD knowledge bases (e.g., UMLS, SNOMED CT) to verify that the Chinese knowledge extraction maintains semantic fidelity to source standards.
