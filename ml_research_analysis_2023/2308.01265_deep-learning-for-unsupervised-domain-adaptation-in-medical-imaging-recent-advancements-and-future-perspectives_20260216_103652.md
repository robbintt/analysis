# Deep learning for unsupervised domain adaptation in medical imaging: Recent advancements and future perspectives

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2308.01265
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.01265
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Comprehensive survey categorizing over 140 UDA papers into six technical approaches for medical imaging tasks

## Executive Summary
This paper provides a comprehensive survey of recent advancements in deep unsupervised domain adaptation (UDA) for medical imaging. The authors systematically categorize over 140 relevant papers into six technical approaches: feature alignment, image translation, combined approaches, disentangled representation learning, pseudo-labeling, and self-supervision. The survey covers various medical imaging tasks including classification, segmentation, detection, and synthesis, while analyzing datasets to assess domain divergence. The paper also identifies emerging areas like source-free adaptation and test-time adaptation as future research directions.

## Method Summary
The survey methodology involves systematically reviewing and categorizing over 140 papers on UDA in medical imaging. Papers are organized into six technical categories based on their core approach: feature alignment, image translation, combined methods, disentangled representations, pseudo-labeling, and self-supervision. The authors analyze datasets used across studies to assess domain divergence characteristics, and identify emerging trends and future research directions. The approach emphasizes technical categorization over application-specific grouping to reveal fundamental patterns in UDA methodology.
