# Adaptive Communications in Collaborative Perception with Domain Alignment for Autonomous Driving

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2310.00013
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.00013
- Reference count: 37
- Key outcome: ACC-DA achieves 55.06% overall IoU for BEV segmentation, outperforming state-of-the-art methods by 2.11%

## Executive Summary
ACC-DA addresses collaborative perception challenges in autonomous driving by considering dynamic channel variations and data heterogeneity among connected vehicles. The framework introduces three key mechanisms: transmission delay minimization using real-time channel state information, adaptive data reconstruction with rate-distortion trade-offs, and domain alignment through frequency domain transformations. These components work together to improve perception accuracy while maintaining communication efficiency. Experimental results on the OPV2V dataset demonstrate significant performance improvements, particularly in handling domain gaps between vehicles.

## Method Summary
ACC-DA is a three-part framework for collaborative perception in autonomous driving. First, it constructs communication graphs based on real-time channel state information to minimize transmission delay using Shannon capacity theorem. Second, it implements adaptive data reconstruction that dynamically adjusts compression ratios and reduces temporal redundancy through real-time model refinement. Third, it applies domain alignment by transforming images to frequency domain and aligning amplitude spectra between vehicles. The system integrates these components with a CoBEVT backbone for BEV segmentation, enabling efficient and accurate collaborative perception among connected vehicles.

## Key Results
- Achieves 55.06% overall IoU for BEV segmentation on OPV2V dataset
- Outperforms state-of-the-art methods by 2.11% in overall performance
- Domain alignment improves segmentation accuracy by 1-3 percentage points, particularly for vehicle class

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: Transmission delay minimization improves collaborative perception by dynamically adjusting the communication graph based on real-time channel state information.
- Mechanism: Uses Shannon capacity theorem to calculate channel capacity for each sub-channel, then formulates an optimization problem to minimize average transmission delay while maintaining sparsity in the communication graph.
- Core assumption: Channel conditions vary significantly enough to impact perception performance, and vehicles can accurately estimate CSI in real-time.
- Evidence anchors: [abstract] "dynamically adjust the communication graph and minimize the average transmission delay while mitigating the side effects from the data heterogeneity"; [section] "transmission delay serves as a key indicator for CA Vs, which is crucial for maintaining perception accuracy and ensuring safety"

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: Adaptive data reconstruction with rate-distortion trade-off dynamically adjusts compression based on channel conditions and reduces temporal redundancy through real-time model refinement.
- Mechanism: Adjusts compression ratio γij based on distance between vehicles and real-time channel conditions, then uses historical data to refine the encoder/decoder parameters for improved reconstruction efficiency.
- Core assumption: Temporal redundancy exists in successive frames and can be exploited for compression efficiency gains.
- Evidence anchors: [abstract] "dynamically adjust the rate-distortion trade-off to enhance perception efficiency" and "minimizes the temporal redundancy during data transmissions"; [section] "to leverage the temporal redundancy of the successive frames in vehicle-to-vehicle collaborative perception activities"

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: Domain alignment reduces performance degradation by transforming images to frequency domain and aligning amplitude spectra between vehicles with different environmental conditions.
- Mechanism: Images are decomposed into amplitude and phase components using Fourier transform, then amplitude spectra are aligned by mixing central frequency components between ego vehicle and source vehicles.
- Core assumption: Image quality differences between vehicles are primarily captured in amplitude spectra, and phase components preserve semantic information.
- Evidence anchors: [abstract] "transforms images to frequency domain and aligns amplitude spectra to mitigate domain gaps between vehicles"; [section] "transform the images to frequency domain, and then align the amplitude spectrum of the images obtained from different vehicles"

## Limitations
- Performance gains of 1-3 percentage points for domain alignment may not generalize to more challenging domain gaps
- Lack of evaluation under extreme channel conditions (high mobility, interference)
- Potential overfitting to the OPV2V dataset's specific scenarios

## Confidence
