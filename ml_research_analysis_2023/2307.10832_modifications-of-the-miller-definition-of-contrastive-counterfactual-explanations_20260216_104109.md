# Modifications of the Miller definition of contrastive (counterfactual) explanations

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2307.10832
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.10832
- **Reference count**: 28
- **Key outcome**: This paper proposes two improved variants of Miller's definition of contrastive explanations based on modified Halpern-Pearl and Borner definitions of non-contrastive explanations.

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses limitations in Miller's definition of contrastive explanations, which was originally based on the Halpern-Pearl (HP) definition of non-contrastive explanations. Since the original HP definition has known issues that produce counterintuitive results in standard examples, the Miller definition inherits and amplifies these problems. The authors propose two improved variants of the Miller definition based on the more robust modified HP and Borner definitions of non-contrastive explanations. These variants retain the spirit of the original Miller definition while addressing its limitations, and all three variants satisfy an alternative unified definition that is modular with respect to the underlying non-contrastive explanation definition.

## Method Summary
The paper compares three competing definitions of contrastive explanations: the original Miller definition based on the original HP definition, and two improved variants based on the modified HP and Borner definitions of non-contrastive explanations. The analysis involves formal proofs of equivalence between the variants and a unified modular definition, along with examples demonstrating the behavior of each definition. The authors examine how each definition handles cases involving logical omniscience and certainty conditions, showing that the proposed variants address issues present in the original Miller definition.

## Key Results
- The original Miller definition inherits problematic issues from the original HP definition, including requiring certainty of the explanandum and being too strong in its conditions
- The modified HP and Borner definitions offer more robust alternatives that produce more intuitive results in standard examples
- Two improved variants of the Miller definition are proposed based on the modified HP and Borner definitions, retaining the spirit of the original while addressing its limitations
- All three variants (original Miller, modified HP-based, and Borner-based) satisfy an alternative unified definition that is modular with respect to the underlying non-contrastive explanation definition

## Limitations
- The paper acknowledges that even the modified HP definition can produce counterintuitive results in certain scenarios involving logical omniscience, suggesting the proposed solutions may not be complete
- The analysis is based on theoretical examples and formal proofs rather than empirical evaluation on real-world datasets
- The unified definition that all variants supposedly satisfy is not fully detailed in the abstract, raising questions about the exact nature of the modular relationship

## Confidence
- **High confidence**: The identification of issues with the original HP definition and the general observation that the Miller definition inherits these problems
- **Medium confidence**: The claim that the modified HP and Borner definitions offer more robust alternatives, as this depends on the specific examples chosen
- **Low confidence**: The assertion that the proposed variants fully address all limitations of the Miller definition while retaining its essential spirit
