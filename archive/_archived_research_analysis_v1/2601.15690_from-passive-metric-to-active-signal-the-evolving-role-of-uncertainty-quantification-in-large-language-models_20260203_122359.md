> ## üìù Executive Summary
>
> The deployment of Large Language Models (LLMs) is currently constrained by reliability issues (e.g., hallucinations) and the high computational costs of complex reasoning. Historically, Uncertainty Quantification (UQ) has served as a passive, post-hoc diagnostic tool, merely indicating confidence after generation. This paper addresses the critical limitation that passive UQ cannot dynamically mitigate errors or optimize resources during the generation process.
>
> This survey introduces a **paradigm shift**, redefining UQ as an **active control signal** integrated directly into the generation loop. Grounded in Bayesian methods and Conformal Prediction, the authors establish a unified taxonomy synthesizing UQ applications across reasoning, agency, and reinforcement learning. The technical review details architectures where uncertainty drives behavioral adjustments, such as path selection, self-correction, and tool usage.
>
> Empirical analysis demonstrates that integrating active UQ significantly enhances efficiency and accuracy. Notably, the **MUR framework** achieved over a **50% reduction** in computational cost while improving accuracy. The study establishes that *Within-Question Discrimination (WQD)* is superior to global calibration for path selection. Ultimately, this research bridges the gap between theoretical rigor and practical application, setting a new standard for developing trustworthy, self-regulating AI systems.

## üîë Key Findings

*   **Paradigm Shift:** UQ in LLMs has evolved from a passive diagnostic metric to an **active control signal** that guides real-time model behavior.
*   **Application in Reasoning:** In advanced reasoning tasks, uncertainty is actively leveraged to **optimize computational resources** and trigger mechanisms for self-correction.
*   **Governance in Autonomous Agents:** Within autonomous agents, UQ functions as a governor for **metacognitive decision-making**, specifically determining when and how to utilize tools or seek information.
*   **Role in Reinforcement Learning:** UQ is utilized to mitigate **reward hacking** and enable self-improvement through the use of intrinsic rewards.
*   **Theoretical Grounding:** These advancements are unified and grounded by emerging theoretical frameworks, specifically **Bayesian methods** and **Conformal Prediction**.

## üõ†Ô∏è Technical Details

The paper proposes a paradigm shift from passive, post-hoc UQ to active, real-time UQ integrated into the generation loop.

### Architectures & Mechanisms
*   **Between Reasoning Paths:**
    *   **CISC:** Uses length-normalized probability for selection.
    *   **CER:** Uses intermediate step confidence for weighted selection.
*   **Inside Paths:**
    *   **UAG:** Retracts to checkpoints upon high uncertainty.
    *   **SPOC:** Uses proposer/verifier roles for self-correction.
    *   **Fine-tuning Methods:** Reward uncertainty on errors or teach abstention.

### Optimizing Cognitive Effort
*   **UnCert-CoT:** Activates Chain-of-Thought (CoT) selectively at critical points.
*   **ThoughtTerminator:** Implements early stopping mechanisms.
*   **MUR:** Utilizes trajectory-level dynamic thinking budgets.

### Autonomous Agents
*   **Signals:** Utilizes semantic entropy and Expected Information Gain (EIG).
*   **Control:** Controlled via threshold-based triggers, policy learning, or forward propagation.

## üìä Results

*   **Path Selection Efficacy:** *Within-Question Discrimination (WQD)* is more effective for path selection than global calibration.
*   **Computational Efficiency:** **MUR** achieved over **50% reduction** in computation while improving accuracy via trajectory-level aggregation.
*   **Selective Reasoning:** **UnCert-CoT** improved efficiency without sacrificing accuracy by selectively activating CoT only at high-uncertainty points.
*   **Utility vs. Fidelity:** A noted trade-off exists where high utility often requires sacrificing strict global fidelity.
*   **Guidance Comparison:**
    *   *Inference-time guidance:* Offers immediate correction but is limited by model capabilities.
    *   *Training-time guidance:* Provides stronger fundamental uncertainty awareness despite higher upfront costs.
