### Mathematical Formalism (MDP Framework)
The paper distinguishes Agentic MLLMs from traditional agents using a Markov Decision Process (MDP) framework.

*   **Traditional Agents:** Rely on static workflows defined as fixed sequences of functions:
    $$Agent_{MLLM} = f_T \circ \dots \circ f_1(x_1)$$
*   **Agentic MLLMs:** Operate as autonomous decision-makers characterized by:
    *   **Dynamic State Transitions:** $s_{t+1} = \delta(s_t, a_t)$
    *   **Proactive Action Execution:** Via a policy $\pi$ where $a_t \sim \pi(a|s_t)$
    *   **Generalization Objectives:** Maximizing expected rewards:
        $$\pi^* = \text{argmax}_\pi \mathbb{E}_{(x)\sim D} \left[ \sum_{t=0}^T \gamma^t r(s_t, a_t; x) \right]$$

### Architectural Categorization
*   **Dense MLLMs:** Activate all parameters (e.g., LLaVA, Qwen2.5-VL).
*   **Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) MLLMs:** Utilize sparse activation for efficiency.

### Training Pipeline
The paper outlines a six-step training pipeline:
1.  Foundational Selection
2.  Action Space Definition
3.  Continual Pre-training
4.  Supervised Fine-tuning
5.  Reinforcement Learning
6.  Evaluation
