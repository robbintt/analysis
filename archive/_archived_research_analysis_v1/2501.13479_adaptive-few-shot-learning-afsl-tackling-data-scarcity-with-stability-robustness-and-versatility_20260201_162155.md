## üìù Executive Summary

**The Problem:** Current Few-Shot Learning (FSL) methods face critical reliability bottlenecks in dynamic, real-world environments. Standard approaches like MAML and Prototypical Networks suffer from hypersensitivity to random initialization, sampling randomness, and domain shift. These fragilities result in high performance variance and vulnerability to noise, creating substantial barriers to deploying FSL in high-stakes domains like healthcare, robotics, and NLP, where data scarcity is inherent but operational robustness is mandatory.

**The Solution:** This research introduces the **Adaptive Few-Shot Learning (AFSL)** framework, a unified, modular meta-learning architecture built around four specialized components:
*   **Dynamic Stability Module:** Uses ensemble-based meta-learning to buffer against initialization sensitivity.
*   **Contextual Domain Alignment Module:** Utilizes adversarial learning to bridge source-target distribution gaps.
*   **Noise-Adaptive Resilience Module:** Leverages attention-based weighting to filter mislabeled data.
*   **Multi-Modal Fusion Module:** Integrates diverse data modalities with Explainable AI (XAI) for interpretability.

**The Impact:** Experimental validation demonstrates that AFSL significantly outperforms traditional baselines in both accuracy and reliability. Beyond metrics, the work shifts the research focus from purely optimizing accuracy to ensuring model reliability, establishing a viable pathway for deploying FSL in safety-critical applications.
