## Executive Summary

Current mitigation architectures are fragmented, typically prioritizing detection while overlooking the downstream necessities of correction and attribution. Reliance on monolithic systems creates a significant scalability bottleneck, preventing rapid adaptation to the dynamic, high-speed nature of misinformation spread across complex networks. This siloed approach fails to address the full lifecycle of false information, leaving a critical operational gap between identifying a threat and neutralizing its impact.

This paper introduces a unified Multi-Agent System (MAS) architecture that replaces rigid, monolithic designs with a distributed, modular workflow.
