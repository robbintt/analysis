### Notable Architectures
*   **RETRO:** Integrates nearest-neighbor retrieval directly into the decoder using chunk-level memory.
*   **ColBERTv2:** Utilizes lightweight late interaction by decoupling encoding and interaction processes.
*   **FiD & Atlas:** Fuses retrieved passages within the decoder and employs joint fine-tuning.
*   **InPars & BGE:** Leverages synthetic QA pairs and general-purpose embeddings.

### Mathematical Formulations

**Dense Retrieval**
Dense retrieval maps queries ($q$) and documents ($d$) via encoders. The score is typically calculated using the inner product, trained via contrastive loss:

$$L_{contrastive} = -\log \frac{\exp(score(q, d^+))}{\exp(score(q, d^+)) + \sum_{d^-} \exp(score(q, d^-))}$$

**ColBERT Scoring**
ColBERT employs token-level similarity scoring:

$$score_{ColBERT}(q, d) = \sum_i \max_j \cos(q_i, d_j)$$

**Explanatory QA**
Explanatory Question Answering is formulated as maximizing the conditional probability:

$$P(a|q, C)$$

*(Where $a$ is the answer, $q$ is the question, and $C$ is the context/retrieved content.)*
