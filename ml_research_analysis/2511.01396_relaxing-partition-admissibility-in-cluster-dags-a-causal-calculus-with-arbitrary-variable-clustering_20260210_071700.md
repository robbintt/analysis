# Relaxing partition admissibility in Cluster-DAGs: a causal calculus with arbitrary variable clustering

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2511.01396
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.01396
- Reference count: 40
- Extends Cluster-DAG framework to allow arbitrary variable clusterings and cyclic cluster graphs

## Executive Summary
This paper extends the Cluster-DAG framework to enable causal reasoning at the cluster level using arbitrary variable clustering, including cases with cyclic cluster graphs. The authors introduce a structure-based separation criterion and develop a sound, atomically complete causal calculus for this setting. They demonstrate that any cluster can be reduced to at most three nodes, making the calculus computationally efficient. The framework enables causal inference in domains where full micro-variable causal models are unknown, allowing analysis of macro-level relationships in economics, neuroscience, and other fields.

## Method Summary
The authors develop a novel causal calculus for Cluster-DAGs by relaxing the admissibility requirements for variable partitions. They introduce a structure-based separation criterion that works with arbitrary clustering of variables, including cyclic cluster graphs. The calculus is sound and atomically complete, meaning that any post-intervention distribution that can be expressed in the language can be computed from observational data. The key innovation is showing that any cluster can be reduced to at most three nodes, which dramatically simplifies computation while preserving the ability to reason about causal relationships at the cluster level.

## Key Results
- Introduces a structure-based separation criterion for arbitrary variable clustering in Cluster-DAGs
- Develops a sound and atomically complete causal calculus for cluster-level reasoning
- Proves that any cluster can be reduced to at most three nodes, ensuring computational efficiency
- Enables causal inference in domains with limited micro-level causal knowledge

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The approach works by leveraging the structure of Cluster-DAGs to perform causal reasoning at a higher level of abstraction. By allowing arbitrary clustering and relaxing admissibility requirements, the framework can handle complex causal structures that traditional DAG-based methods cannot efficiently represent. The structure-based separation criterion identifies conditional independences based on the cluster graph structure rather than requiring full specification of micro-variable relationships. This enables causal inference even when the complete micro-level causal model is unknown or computationally intractable.

## Foundational Learning
- **Cluster-DAGs**: A framework for causal modeling where variables are grouped into clusters, allowing reasoning at different levels of abstraction. Why needed: Enables causal inference when full micro-variable models are unavailable or impractical.
- **Structure-based separation criterion**: A method for identifying conditional independences based on graph structure rather than probabilistic relationships. Why needed: Allows causal reasoning without complete knowledge of micro-level relationships.
- **Atomic completeness**: A property of causal calculi ensuring that all post-intervention distributions expressible in the language can be computed. Why needed: Guarantees the calculus can answer all relevant causal queries.
- **Cyclic cluster graphs**: Cluster graphs that may contain cycles, extending beyond traditional acyclic causal models. Why needed: Captures feedback loops and complex dependencies common in real-world systems.
