# Noise-Resilient Quantum Aggregation on NISQ for Federated ADAS Learning

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2512.13196
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.13196
- Reference count: 22
- Primary result: NR-QFL achieves 86.1% accuracy and 0.84 F1-score in federated ADAS learning, outperforming classical FedAvg (79.2%, 0.76) with only 8% higher communication cost.

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces Noise-Resilient Quantum Federated Learning (NR-QFL), a hybrid quantum-classical framework designed to enhance federated learning in Advanced Driver-Assistance Systems (ADAS) using Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum (NISQ) devices. By leveraging variational quantum circuits (VQCs) for secure, low-latency model aggregation, the approach mitigates noise and improves accuracy over classical FedAvg and baseline quantum federated learning methods. Experiments using Qiskit Aer simulation on ResNet-18/CIFAR-10 workloads demonstrate that NR-QFL achieves superior performance (86.1% accuracy, 0.84 F1-score) with shallow circuits compatible with NISQ hardware.

## Method Summary
NR-QFL combines classical federated learning with quantum model aggregation on NISQ devices, using angle-encoded model parameters and adaptive gate reparameterization for noise resilience. The framework employs quantum entropy for client selection and supports multi-server coordination to enhance fairness and security. Theoretical noise-resilience bounds and CAM analysis validate the robustness of the approach under noise. The system is designed for integration into vehicular edge-cloud architectures and is shown to be feasible for NISQ hardware with shallow circuit depths (<10).

## Key Results
- NR-QFL achieves 86.1% accuracy and 0.84 F1-score, surpassing classical FedAvg (79.2%, 0.76) and baseline QFL (84.5%, 0.81).
- Only 8% higher communication cost compared to classical FedAvg.
- CAM analysis reveals superior feature attention under noise, validating theoretical noise-resilience bounds.
- Compatible with NISQ hardware using shallow circuits (<10 depth).

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
NR-QFL exploits the noise resilience of variational quantum circuits through angle encoding and adaptive gate reparameterization, which helps mitigate the impact of noise on model aggregation. Quantum entropy-based client selection ensures fairness and security in federated settings, while shallow circuits are tailored to NISQ hardware constraints. The integration of multi-server coordination enhances system robustness, and the hybrid quantum-classical design allows for secure, low-latency updates in ADAS environments.

## Limitations
- Performance validation relies on simulation, not physical NISQ hardware, potentially underestimating real-world noise and error impacts.
- ADAS scenarios involve heterogeneous clients, variable networks, and real-time constraints not fully captured in experiments.
- Theoretical noise-resilience claims and quantum entropy-based client selection require further empirical validation in practical federated environments.

## Confidence
- High: Hybrid quantum-classical architecture feasibility and simulation-based accuracy gains over classical FedAvg and baseline QFL.
- Medium: Noise-resilience claims and quantum entropy-based client selection, pending physical hardware validation.
- Low: Real-world ADAS applicability and security guarantees under practical federated conditions.
