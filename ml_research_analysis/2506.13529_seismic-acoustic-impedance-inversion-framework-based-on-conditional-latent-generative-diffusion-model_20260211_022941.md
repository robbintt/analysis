# Seismic Acoustic Impedance Inversion Framework Based on Conditional Latent Generative Diffusion Model

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2506.13529
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.13529
- Reference count: 39
- Primary result: Proposed SAII-CLDM achieves 34.76 dB PSNR and 0.9639 SSIM on Overthrust model, outperforming baselines with enhanced noise robustness and generalization

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the ill-posed seismic acoustic impedance inversion problem by proposing a conditional latent generative diffusion model framework (SAII-CLDM). The method performs inversion in a low-dimensional latent space using a VQ-GAN encoder to compress impedance and seismic data, combined with a lightweight wavelet-based module (SHWT) to project seismic data while preserving polarity information. A model-driven sampling strategy is introduced to integrate physics-based forward modeling into the diffusion process, improving accuracy and enabling faster sampling with fewer denoising steps. The framework demonstrates superior performance on synthetic and field data compared to traditional and deep learning baselines.

## Method Summary
The SAII-CLDM framework operates in latent space rather than pixel space to enable efficient large-scale seismic inversion. It uses a VQ-GAN encoder to compress impedance models into compact latent representations, with a conditional diffusion model learning and sampling entirely in this compressed space. The SHWT module embeds seismic data into latent space while preserving critical polarity information for impedance recovery. During inference, a model-driven sampling strategy integrates physics-based forward modeling into the DDIM sampling process, periodically correcting the diffusion trajectory to maintain consistency with observed seismic data. The framework is trained on augmented Marmousi II patches and validated on the Overthrust model and field data, demonstrating enhanced geological detail and consistency with well-log measurements.

## Key Results
- Achieves 34.76 dB PSNR and 0.9639 SSIM on Overthrust test set under noise-free conditions
- Outperforms baseline methods (SDL, USDL, 2D-TV) with superior noise robustness and generalization
- Field data validation confirms enhanced geological detail and consistency with well-log measurements
- Model-driven sampling reduces inference time from ~30 seconds (1000 steps) to ~4 seconds (30 steps)

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: Operating in low-dimensional latent space enables large-scale seismic inversion that pixel-based diffusion methods cannot computationally achieve.
- Mechanism: VQ-GAN encoder compresses impedance models into compact latent representations before diffusion, reducing the dimensionality of the denoising process. The conditional diffusion model learns and samples entirely in this compressed space, then decodes back to pixel space for final output.
- Core assumption: The VQ-GAN encoder-decoder preserves sufficient impedance structure information—including sharp geological boundaries—for accurate reconstruction after latent space operations.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract]: "The method performs inversion in a low-dimensional latent space using a VQ-GAN encoder to transform impedance and seismic data"
  - [Section III-A]: "we perform seismic acoustic impedance inversion in latent space instead of pixel space... we employ a Vector-Quantized Generative Adversarial Network (VQ-GAN) to encode impedance data into a low-dimensional space"
  - [corpus]: "Diffusion models for multivariate subsurface generation" (FMR=0.54) confirms diffusion models enhance multivariate subsurface modeling capabilities.

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: The SHWT wavelet-based module efficiently embeds seismic data into latent space while preserving polarity information critical for recovering relative impedance variations.
- Mechanism: Haar wavelet transform provides multi-scale feature extraction and channel expansion, learnable convolution filters suppress non-essential noise components, and tanh activation preserves seismic polarity (which encodes relative impedance variations via the reflectivity equation r_j = (z_{j+1} - z_j)/(z_{j+1} + z_j)).
- Core assumption: Seismic polarity contains the primary information needed to recover impedance variations, and noise can be filtered without losing this polarity signal.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [Section III-A, Eq. 12-13]: "preserving the polarity of seismic data is crucial... This equation illustrates that the polarity trends of seismic data contain crucial information about relative impedance variations"
  - [Section III-A]: "SHWT integrates the Haar wavelet transform (HWT), convolutional layers with batch normalization (Conv+BN), and a hyperbolic tangent (Tanh) activation function"
  - [corpus]: Weak direct evidence for SHWT specifically; related GAN-based and diffusion inversion papers do not employ wavelet-based conditioning modules.

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: Model-driven sampling integrates physics-based forward modeling into diffusion sampling, correcting for train-test distribution shifts and enabling accurate inversion with fewer denoising steps.
- Mechanism: At regular intervals during DDIM sampling, the current latent estimate is decoded to pixel space, evaluated against observed seismic data through forward modeling optimization (||d - f(D(ẑ₀))||²), re-encoded, and resampled using StochasticResample to maintain diffusion transition consistency. This physics-consistency check periodically corrects the diffusion trajectory.
- Core assumption: The forward model f(z) = WC(z) (wavelet convolution with reflectivity computed from impedance) accurately represents the relationship between true impedance and observed seismic; distribution shifts exist but are correctable through physics-based guidance.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [Section III-B, Eq. 17-21]: "we introduce a model-driven sampling strategy that complements the data-driven learning pattern, significantly enhancing inversion robustness"
  - [Section IV-B1]: "SAII-CLDM completes 30 timesteps in approximately 4 seconds, whereas SAII-DDPM requires around 30 seconds for 1000 timesteps"
  - [corpus]: "Acoustic Waveform Inversion with Image-to-Image Schrödinger Bridges" (FMR=0.48) supports physics-integrated sampling approaches for seismic inversion.

## Foundational Learning

- Concept: **Diffusion models (DDPM/DDIM)**
  - Why needed here: The entire framework is built on conditional diffusion probabilistic models. You must understand the forward noising process (Eq. 1-2), reverse denoising with learned noise prediction (Eq. 4-6), and how DDIM reduces stochasticity to enable few-step sampling (Eq. 16).
  - Quick check question: Why can DDIM achieve comparable results with 30 steps while DDPM requires 1000?

- Concept: **VQ-GAN (Vector-Quantized Generative Adversarial Network)**
  - Why needed here: The encoder-decoder pair is the gateway between pixel space and latent space. Understanding how vector quantization affects reconstruction fidelity and what information is lost during compression is essential for diagnosing resolution limits.
  - Quick check question: If the VQ-GAN codebook is too small, what artifacts would you expect in decoded impedance models?

- Concept: **Seismic forward modeling (convolution model)**
  - Why needed here: The model-driven sampling strategy relies on the forward operator f(z) = WC(z). You need to understand how reflectivity is computed from impedance (Eq. 13) and convolved with a wavelet to generate synthetic seismic data.
  - Quick check question: Given an impedance trace and a Ricker wavelet, can you manually compute the corresponding synthetic seismic trace?

## Architecture Onboarding

- Component map:
  INPUT: Seismic data (d) + Low-frequency impedance (l)
       ↓
  ENCODING:
    • SHWT module (ϕ) → dz (seismic in latent space)
    • VQ-GAN Encoder E (reused, frozen) → lz (low-freq impedance in latent space)
       ↓
  CONDITIONAL DIFFUSION TRAINING:
    • Noised impedance: zt = √ᾱₜz₀ + √(1-ᾱₜ)εₜ
    • Concatenate: [zt, lz, dz] → U-Net denoiser εθ
    • Loss: E[||εₜ - εθ(zt, lz, dz, t)||²]
       ↓
  INFERENCE (Model-Driven DDIM Sampling):
    for t = T-1, ..., 0:
      1. DDIM step: predict zt from zt+1
      2. Every N steps (interval):
         a. Decode: D(ẑ₀) → pixel-space impedance estimate
         b. Physics check: minimize ||d - f(D(ẑ₀))||²
         c. Re-encode optimized result
         d. StochasticResample back to zt manifold
       ↓
  OUTPUT: VQ-GAN Decoder D(z₀) → recovered impedance x₀

- Critical path:
  1. VQ-GAN encoder reconstruction quality determines maximum achievable resolution
  2. SHWT polarity preservation enables correct impedance trend recovery
  3. Model-driven sampling interval (hyperparameter) balances accuracy vs. speed

- Design tradeoffs:
  - Latent dimensionality vs. detail preservation: Smaller latent = faster but risks losing geological boundaries
  - Model-driven interval: Frequent correction (small interval) improves accuracy but increases compute; infrequent correction may miss distribution shifts
  - Training diversity vs. generalization: Augmented Marmousi training provides broad coverage but may require retraining for significantly different geology (Section VI notes retraining may be necessary)

- Failure signatures:
  - Shallow layer structures missing → Check model-driven correction is being applied; may indicate training/test distribution mismatch
  - Boundary blurring → VQ-GAN decoder regularization too strong or diffusion steps insufficient
  - Phase shift sensitivity (>20°) → Training data lacks phase variation; model-driven sampling cannot fully correct this distribution shift
  - Well-log misalignment at trace locations → Well-seismic depth matching issue (acknowledged in Section V-B as practical challenge)

- First 3 experiments:
  1. **VQ-GAN reconstruction baseline**: Encode-decode held-out impedance patches without diffusion; if PSNR < 35 dB, increase latent dimensionality or codebook size before proceeding.
  2. **SHWT polarity preservation test**: Pass synthetic seismic through SHWT and verify output polarity matches input; compare against naive downsampling baseline to confirm wavelet transform benefit.
  3. **Model-driven interval sweep**: On validation data with intentional wavelet frequency shift (e.g., 25 Hz training → 30 Hz test), sweep interval ∈ {5, 10, 20, 50} and plot PSNR vs. inference time to identify optimal operating point.

## Limitations
- VQ-GAN architecture details remain unspecified, creating potential latent space capacity constraints that could limit resolution of sharp geological boundaries
- The model-driven sampling strategy's correction effectiveness depends critically on accurate wavelet estimation, which may not hold for complex 3D wave propagation scenarios
- Limited test set diversity (primarily Marmousi II and Overthrust) raises questions about generalization to significantly different geological settings
- Phase sensitivity (>20° degrades performance) indicates vulnerability to real-world acquisition and processing variations

## Confidence
- High confidence: The core mechanism of operating in latent space (Mechanism 1) and the overall framework architecture are well-supported by evidence
- Medium confidence: SHWT wavelet-based conditioning module (Mechanism 2) has limited direct supporting evidence in the corpus
- Medium confidence: Model-driven sampling effectiveness (Mechanism 3) is demonstrated but depends heavily on forward model accuracy assumptions

## Next Checks
1. **VQ-GAN capacity validation**: Systematically vary latent dimensionality and codebook size on held-out Marmousi patches, measuring reconstruction PSNR and checking for loss of high-frequency geological features at smaller sizes
2. **Distribution shift robustness**: Create test sets with controlled wavelet frequency shifts (e.g., 25→30 Hz) and systematically vary model-driven sampling intervals, measuring accuracy vs. inference time tradeoffs
3. **Phase sensitivity characterization**: Generate synthetic seismic data with controlled phase rotations (0°, 10°, 20°, 30°, 45°) and measure performance degradation to quantify the practical limits of phase invariance
