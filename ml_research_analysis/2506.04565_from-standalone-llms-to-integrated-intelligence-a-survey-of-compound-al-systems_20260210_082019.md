# From Standalone LLMs to Integrated Intelligence: A Survey of Compound Al Systems

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2506.04565
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.04565
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: First unified taxonomy and architectural analysis of Compound AI Systems (CAIS) integrating LLMs with external components

## Executive Summary
This survey presents the first comprehensive framework for understanding Compound AI Systems (CAIS), which integrate large language models with external components like retrievers, agents, tools, and orchestrators to overcome standalone LLM limitations in memory, reasoning, real-time grounding, and multimodal understanding. The authors analyze over 120 sources to organize CAIS along four orthogonal axes: Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), LLM Agents, Multimodal LLMs (MLLMs), and Orchestration Frameworks. The work establishes foundational patterns for system-level artificial intelligence design while identifying key challenges around scalability, interoperability, and evaluation.

## Method Summary
The authors conducted systematic analysis of over 120 sources to develop a unified taxonomy of Compound AI Systems, organizing architectures along four orthogonal dimensions: RAG, LLM Agents, MLLMs, and Orchestration Frameworks. They distilled architectural blueprints, coordination strategies, and component interactions while highlighting recurring patterns and failure modes. The survey also proposes a comprehensive evaluation framework addressing factual accuracy, robustness, efficiency, and alignment with human goals.

## Key Results
- First unified taxonomy organizing CAIS along four orthogonal axes: RAG, Agents, MLLMs, and Orchestration
- Analysis of 120+ sources reveals architectural blueprints and coordination patterns
- Comprehensive evaluation framework covering factual accuracy, robustness, efficiency, and alignment
- Identified key challenges: scalability, interoperability, benchmarking, and coordination

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The survey succeeds by providing a systematic framework that organizes diverse CAIS architectures into coherent categories, enabling researchers to understand component interactions, design patterns, and evaluation approaches. The orthogonal organization allows for modular understanding of how different CAIS components work together while maintaining analytical clarity.

**Agent Orchestration Principles** - Methods for coordinating multiple LLM agents and external tools; needed to ensure coherent multi-component behavior; quick check: can describe planning, reflection, and tool-use coordination patterns.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Retriever -> LLM -> Agent Orchestrator -> Tools/Multimodal Components
- Information flows from retrieval through reasoning to action
- Orchestrator coordinates multiple specialized components

**Critical Path**: Retrieval → Context Integration → Reasoning → Action/Generation
- Each stage builds on previous outputs
- Performance bottlenecks typically occur at integration points

**Design Tradeoffs**: 
- Accuracy vs. latency in retrieval timing
- Model complexity vs. coordination overhead
- Specialization vs. generalization in component design
- Static vs. dynamic orchestration strategies

**Failure Signatures**: 
- Hallucinations due to poor retrieval-LLM integration
- Coordination deadlocks in multi-agent systems
- Performance degradation in multimodal fusion
- Scalability bottlenecks in orchestration
