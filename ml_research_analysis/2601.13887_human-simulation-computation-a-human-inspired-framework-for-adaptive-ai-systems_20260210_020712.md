# Human Simulation Computation: A Human-Inspired Framework for Adaptive AI Systems

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2601.13887
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.13887
- Reference count: 20

## Executive Summary
This paper presents Human Simulation Computation (HSC), a novel framework for developing adaptive AI systems inspired by human cognitive processes. HSC models intelligence as a continuous closed-loop cycle of thinking, action, learning, reflection, and activity scheduling, with a focus on long-term adaptation rather than solving isolated tasks. The framework incorporates human thinking strategies across all stages, including main-feature-oriented reasoning and on-time learning driven by environmental feedback.

The paper argues that human simulation strategies cannot be fully learned from language material alone, emphasizing the importance of human-like reasoning processes and action-grounded methods for robust adaptation. HSC aims to bridge the gap between language-based reasoning and environment-grounded interaction, enabling AI systems to effectively adapt and operate in dynamic real-world environments.

## Method Summary
The Human Simulation Computation framework is a theoretical model that conceptualizes intelligence as a continuous closed-loop process. This process involves five interconnected stages: thinking, action, learning, reflection, and activity scheduling. The framework incorporates human thinking strategies throughout these stages, such as main-feature-oriented reasoning and scope expansion through action. HSC emphasizes on-time learning driven by environmental feedback and focuses on long-term adaptation rather than solving predefined tasks.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The HSC framework works by mimicking human cognitive processes in a continuous closed-loop system. This approach allows for ongoing adaptation and learning based on environmental feedback, rather than relying solely on pre-programmed responses or language-based reasoning. By incorporating human thinking strategies throughout the process, the framework aims to create more robust and flexible AI systems that can effectively interact with and adapt to real-world environments.
