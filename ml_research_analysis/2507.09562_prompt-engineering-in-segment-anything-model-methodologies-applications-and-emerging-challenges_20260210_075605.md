## Quick Facts

## Executive Summary
This survey provides the first systematic review of prompt engineering methodologies for the Segment Anything Model (SAM), covering fundamental techniques, applications, and challenges. The paper organizes the rapidly growing body of research into a hierarchical taxonomy, analyzing how prompt engineering has evolved from simple geometric inputs to sophisticated multimodal approaches. It examines SAM's adaptation across diverse domains including medical imaging and remote sensing, while identifying key challenges and future research directions. The work fills an important gap in understanding how prompts enable SAM's success as a foundation model for segmentation tasks.

## Method Summary
The survey systematically reviews published literature on SAM prompt engineering, organizing findings into a hierarchical taxonomy of approaches based on technical characteristics. It analyzes the evolution from basic geometric prompts to advanced automated generation strategies, examining implementation details across different domains. The methodology includes case studies from medical imaging and remote sensing applications, with quantitative results cited from existing literature. The survey also identifies emerging challenges through analysis of current research limitations and proposes future directions based on observed trends in the field.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component map**: Input prompt generator -> SAM encoder -> Prompt encoder -> Mask decoder -> Output segmentation mask

**Critical path**: Prompt generation (geometric, textual, or visual) → Feature encoding → Mask prediction → Post-processing refinement

**Design tradeoffs**: Manual vs. automated prompt generation (control vs. scalability), simple vs. complex prompt representations (efficiency vs. accuracy), domain-specific vs. general prompts (specialization vs. versatility)

**Failure signatures**: Poor segmentation quality from inadequate prompt specificity, computational bottlenecks from complex prompt generation, domain adaptation failures from misaligned prompt features
