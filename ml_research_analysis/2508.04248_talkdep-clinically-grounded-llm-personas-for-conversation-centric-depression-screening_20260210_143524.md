# TalkDep: Clinically Grounded LLM Personas for Conversation-Centric Depression Screening

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2508.04248
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.04248
- **Reference count**: 31
- **Primary result**: LLM-generated depression personas achieve 86% accuracy in severity ranking and 3.92/5 clinical realism score

## Executive Summary
TalkDep introduces a clinician-in-the-loop pipeline that leverages large language models to generate clinically grounded simulated patient personas for depression screening conversations. The system conditions LLMs on structured BDI-II symptom profiles, biographical contexts, and communication styles to create coherent conversational personas that reflect diverse depression severities. Validation by both external LLMs and clinical psychologists confirms the realism and utility of these personas, which have been adopted as a benchmark for the eRisk 2025 conversational depression detection track.

## Method Summary
The TalkDep pipeline integrates structured clinical data (BDI-II scores) with contextual biographical information and communication style parameters to generate realistic patient personas. The system employs prompt engineering techniques to ensure LLMs produce coherent, symptom-accurate conversations that reflect specific depression severity levels. A clinician-in-the-loop approach maintains quality control throughout the generation process, while external LLM validation and clinical psychologist evaluations provide multi-layered assessment of persona realism and diagnostic accuracy.

## Key Results
- External LLMs achieved 86% accuracy in pairwise severity ranking of generated personas
- Clinical psychologists rated interaction realism at 3.92/5 (mean score across evaluated attributes)
- The system successfully captured diverse depression severities through structured symptom profiles
- Adopted as benchmark for eRisk 2025 conversational depression detection track

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
TalkDep succeeds by grounding LLM persona generation in clinically validated diagnostic frameworks (BDI-II) while incorporating rich contextual information that enables nuanced, realistic conversations. The clinician-in-the-loop approach ensures that generated personas maintain clinical accuracy while the structured symptom profiles provide a consistent foundation for varying depression severities. This combination of structured clinical data and contextual richness allows LLMs to produce personas that reflect the complex, individualized nature of depression presentations while maintaining diagnostic validity.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Clinical Data Input -> LLM Persona Generation -> Clinician Validation -> External Validation -> Benchmark Deployment

**Critical Path**: Structured symptom profiles → Prompt engineering → LLM generation → Clinical validation → Deployment

**Design Tradeoffs**: Prioritizes clinical accuracy over conversational naturalness; uses BDI-II framework despite known limitations; balances automated generation with human oversight

**Failure Signatures**: 
- Inconsistent symptom severity representation
- Unrealistic conversational patterns
- Misalignment between BDI-II scores and generated personas
- Clinical validation failures

## Limitations
- Dependency on BDI-II scoring introduces inherent constraints and potential biases
- Focus exclusively on depression limits generalizability to other mental health conditions
- Clinical validation sample size may not represent full diversity of clinical perspectives
- Pairwise severity ranking accuracy of 86% leaves room for critical misclassification

## Confidence

**High Confidence**:
- Technical pipeline for persona generation demonstrates robust reproducibility

**Medium Confidence**:
- Clinical realism assessments show promise but require broader clinical validation
- Benchmark utility for eRisk 2025 is well-founded but real-world performance remains unverified
