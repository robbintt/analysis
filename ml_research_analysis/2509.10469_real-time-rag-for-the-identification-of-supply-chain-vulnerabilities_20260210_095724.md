# Real-Time RAG for the Identification of Supply Chain Vulnerabilities

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2509.10469
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.10469
- Reference count: 32
- Key outcome: Novel RAG approach for real-time supply chain vulnerability identification with 78% accuracy in risk detection

## Executive Summary
This research introduces a novel approach to real-time supply chain vulnerability analysis by integrating Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) with advanced web-scraping technologies. The study evaluates the effectiveness of various RAG techniques—including fine-tuning the embedding model, adaptive iterative retrieval, and query abstraction—to improve the timeliness and accuracy of supply chain risk identification. Experiments demonstrate that fine-tuning the embedding retriever consistently yields the most significant performance gains, with adaptive iterative retrieval further enhancing results, particularly for complex queries. The system effectively identifies critical supply chain insights, often uncovering relevant information overlooked in manual reviews.

## Method Summary
The study employs a multi-faceted approach combining RAG architecture with advanced web-scraping technologies to analyze supply chain vulnerabilities in real-time. The methodology involves three main RAG configurations: fine-tuning the embedding retriever, adaptive iterative retrieval, and query abstraction techniques. The system processes large volumes of unstructured data from diverse sources including news articles, regulatory filings, and industry reports. Evaluation metrics include accuracy, recall, and precision scores, with human evaluation confirming the system's effectiveness in identifying supply chain risks. The research focuses on national security and economic policy applications, testing the system's ability to detect vulnerabilities in critical supply chains.

## Key Results
- RAG configurations achieved 78% accuracy in identifying supply chain risks through human evaluation
- Fine-tuning the embedding retriever consistently yielded the most significant performance improvements
- Adaptive iterative retrieval enhanced results particularly for complex queries, often uncovering insights missed in manual reviews
- System demonstrated potential to reduce analysis time while improving accuracy compared to traditional manual efforts

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The effectiveness stems from RAG's ability to dynamically retrieve and synthesize relevant information from large volumes of unstructured supply chain data in real-time. By fine-tuning the embedding retriever, the system better understands supply chain-specific terminology and relationships. Adaptive iterative retrieval allows the model to refine its search based on initial results, improving accuracy for complex queries. The combination of these techniques with advanced web-scraping enables comprehensive coverage of diverse data sources, capturing both obvious and subtle supply chain vulnerabilities.

## Limitations
- 78% accuracy derived from limited dataset raises generalizability concerns across diverse supply chain contexts
- Performance metrics show variation across RAG configurations with diminishing gains at scale
- Controlled experimental settings may not fully capture real-world supply chain disruption dynamics
- Emphasis on national security applications may limit immediate applicability to other supply chain domains

## Confidence

High confidence: The relative performance ranking of RAG techniques (fine-tuning > adaptive retrieval > query abstraction) is robust across multiple evaluation metrics

Medium confidence: The 78% accuracy figure for risk identification, given potential evaluation bias and limited sample size

Medium confidence: The claimed time savings compared to manual analysis, as actual implementation overhead was not fully characterized
