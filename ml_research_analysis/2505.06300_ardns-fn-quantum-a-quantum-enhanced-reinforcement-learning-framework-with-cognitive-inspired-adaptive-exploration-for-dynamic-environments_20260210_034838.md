# ARDNS-FN-Quantum: A Quantum-Enhanced Reinforcement Learning Framework with Cognitive-Inspired Adaptive Exploration for Dynamic Environments

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2505.06300
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.06300
- Reference count: 18
- Primary result: 99.5% success rate vs 81.3% (DQN) and 97.0% (PPO) in 10x10 grid-world

## Executive Summary
ARDNS-FN-Quantum is a reinforcement learning framework that integrates a 2-qubit quantum circuit with cognitive-inspired memory and adaptive exploration mechanisms. The framework was evaluated in a 10x10 grid-world environment over 20,000 episodes, demonstrating superior performance compared to traditional approaches. Key advantages include improved success rates, higher mean rewards, fewer steps to goal, and reduced reward variance, indicating enhanced stability in dynamic environments.

## Method Summary
The framework combines quantum computing elements with cognitive-inspired memory systems and adaptive exploration strategies. A 2-qubit quantum circuit is integrated into the reinforcement learning architecture to enhance decision-making capabilities. The cognitive-inspired memory component provides context-aware learning, while adaptive exploration dynamically adjusts the exploration-exploitation balance based on environmental feedback. The system was trained and evaluated in a 10x10 grid-world environment, with performance metrics tracked across 20,000 episodes.

## Key Results
- Achieved 99.5% success rate versus 81.3% for DQN and 97.0% for PPO
- Mean reward of 9.0528 versus 1.2941 for DQN and 7.6196 for PPO
- Required 46.7 steps to goal versus 135.9 for DQN and 62.5 for PPO
- Last 100 episodes: mean reward 9.1652 versus 7.0916 for DQN and 9.0310 for PPO
- Reward variance of 5.424 versus 252.262 for DQN and 76.583 for PPO
