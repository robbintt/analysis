# Adaptive Information Routing for Multimodal Time Series Forecasting

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2512.10229
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.10229
- **Reference count**: 21
- **Primary result**: AIR framework uses text-guided adaptive routing to improve multimodal time series forecasting accuracy, achieving up to 37.96% MSE improvement

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces Adaptive Information Routing (AIR), a novel framework for multimodal time series forecasting that leverages text data to dynamically guide the processing of time series information. AIR improves forecasting accuracy by decomposing standard model components into two parts connected by latent nodes, which are then weighted based on textual inputs. This allows the model to adjust information pathways according to environmental cues from text. The authors also present a text-refinement pipeline using large language models to convert raw news into structured summaries for forecasting. Experiments on real-world datasets (crude oil prices and exchange rates) show that AIR consistently outperforms unimodal baselines and other multimodal approaches.

## Method Summary
The Adaptive Information Routing framework introduces a novel approach to multimodal time series forecasting by leveraging text data to dynamically guide information processing. The core innovation lies in decomposing standard model components (like fully connected or attention layers) into two parts connected by latent nodes, which are weighted based on textual inputs. This allows the model to adaptively route information according to environmental cues from text. The framework also includes a text-refinement pipeline that uses large language models to convert raw news into structured summaries suitable for forecasting. The approach is evaluated on financial datasets including crude oil prices and exchange rates, demonstrating consistent improvements over both unimodal baselines and other multimodal approaches.

## Key Results
- AIR achieves up to 37.96% improvement in mean squared error compared to unimodal baselines
- The framework consistently outperforms other multimodal approaches on crude oil and exchange rate forecasting tasks
- Text-guided adaptive routing demonstrates effectiveness in dynamically adjusting information pathways based on environmental cues

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
AIR works by introducing adaptive information routing that allows the model to dynamically adjust how it processes time series data based on textual context. The framework decomposes standard model components into two connected parts via latent nodes, creating adaptive pathways that can be weighted according to textual inputs. This enables the model to route information differently depending on the current context described in text data. The text-refinement pipeline ensures that raw news information is converted into structured summaries that can effectively guide the routing decisions. By allowing the model to adaptively process information based on textual context, AIR can better capture the complex relationships between textual events and their impact on time series patterns.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Text Data -> Text Refinement Pipeline -> Latent Node Weighting -> Adaptive Routing Module -> Time Series Processing -> Forecast Output

**Critical Path**: Raw text → Refined text summaries → Latent node weights → Adaptive routing decisions → Enhanced time series processing → Final forecast

**Design Tradeoffs**: The framework trades increased model complexity and computational overhead for improved forecasting accuracy through adaptive routing. The text refinement step adds preprocessing requirements but enables better textual guidance. The decomposition of standard layers into adaptive components increases flexibility but may reduce parameter efficiency compared to monolithic architectures.

**Failure Signatures**: Poor text refinement quality will lead to incorrect routing weights and degraded performance. Misalignment between text timing and time series events can cause inappropriate routing decisions. Overfitting to specific text patterns may reduce generalization to new contexts. Excessive routing complexity could lead to vanishing gradients or optimization difficulties.
