# JANUS: A Dual-Constraint Generative Framework for Stealthy Node Injection Attacks

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2509.13266
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.13266
- Authors: Jiahao Zhang; Xiaobing Pei; Zhaokun Zhong; Wenqiang Hao; Zhenghao Tang
- Reference count: 13
- Primary result: Proposes JANUS, a dual-constraint generative framework for stealthy node injection attacks against GNNs, achieving superior attack success rates and stealthiness across eight benchmark datasets.

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces JANUS, a dual-constraint generative framework designed to address the challenge of stealthy node injection attacks against Graph Neural Networks (GNNs). The key problem JANUS tackles is balancing attack effectiveness with stealthiness, as existing methods often rely on indirect proxy metrics or focus only on local structures, leading to local myopia. JANUS addresses this by ensuring both local authenticity (via Optimal Transport for feature alignment) and global consistency (via structured latent variables and mutual information maximization). The framework models the attack as a sequential decision process, optimized by a reinforcement learning agent, and demonstrates significant performance improvements over existing methods in both attack success rates and stealthiness metrics.

## Method Summary
JANUS introduces a dual-constraint generative framework to overcome the limitations of existing node injection attack methods. It addresses the problem of local myopia by incorporating two key constraints: local authenticity and global consistency. Local authenticity is achieved through Optimal Transport, which aligns the feature distributions of injected nodes with the graph's feature manifold. Global consistency is ensured by using structured latent variables and maximizing mutual information to align injected structures with the graph's semantic patterns. The attack is modeled as a sequential decision process, optimized by a reinforcement learning agent. Experiments on eight benchmark datasets show that JANUS outperforms existing methods in both attack success rates and stealthiness metrics, even against advanced defense mechanisms.

## Key Results
- JANUS significantly outperforms existing methods in attack success rates across eight benchmark datasets.
- Achieves superior stealthiness metrics, including lower Closest Attribute Distance and Smoothness scores.
- Maintains high effectiveness even against advanced defense mechanisms, demonstrating holistic approach to stealthy node injection attacks.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
JANUS works by addressing the dual challenges of attack effectiveness and stealthiness through a novel dual-constraint generative framework. The local authenticity constraint, implemented via Optimal Transport, ensures that injected nodes blend seamlessly with the graph's feature manifold, avoiding detection based on feature anomalies. The global consistency constraint, achieved through structured latent variables and mutual information maximization, ensures that the injected structures conform to the graph's semantic patterns, preventing detection based on structural anomalies. By modeling the attack as a sequential decision process and optimizing it with reinforcement learning, JANUS can dynamically adapt to the graph's evolving state, further enhancing both effectiveness and stealthiness.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Graph Representation -> Local Authenticity (Optimal Transport) -> Global Consistency (Latent Variables + MI Maximization) -> Sequential Decision Process (RL Agent) -> Injected Nodes

**Critical Path**: The critical path involves the sequential decision process, where the RL agent iteratively selects actions to inject nodes that maximize attack effectiveness while maintaining stealthiness. The local authenticity constraint ensures feature alignment, and the global consistency constraint ensures structural alignment, both feeding into the RL agent's decision-making process.

**Design Tradeoffs**: JANUS trades computational complexity for improved attack effectiveness and stealthiness. The use of Optimal Transport and mutual information maximization adds overhead but ensures superior performance. The sequential decision process allows for dynamic adaptation but requires careful reward shaping to balance effectiveness and stealthiness.

**Failure Signatures**: Potential failures include poor feature alignment leading to detection based on local anomalies, or structural misalignment leading to detection based on global anomalies. Additionally, if the RL agent's reward function is not well-designed, it may prioritize effectiveness over stealthiness or vice versa, leading to suboptimal performance.

## Limitations
- The evaluation focuses primarily on homogeneous graph structures and does not address heterogeneous or temporal graphs.
- Stealthiness metrics rely on proxy measurements that may not fully capture detection in practical deployment scenarios.
- The computational overhead of the dual-constraint optimization framework, particularly the Optimal Transport component, is not thoroughly analyzed, raising questions about scalability to large-scale graphs.

## Confidence
- **High confidence** in attack effectiveness claims based on consistent performance improvements across multiple datasets and defense mechanisms.
- **Medium confidence** in stealthiness assertions due to reliance on indirect proxy metrics rather than direct detection evasion tests.
- **High confidence** in the theoretical soundness of the sequential decision process formulation, but practical implementation challenges in dynamic graph environments remain underexplored.
