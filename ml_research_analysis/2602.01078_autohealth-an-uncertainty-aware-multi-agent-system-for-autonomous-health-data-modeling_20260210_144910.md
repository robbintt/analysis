# AutoHealth: An Uncertainty-Aware Multi-Agent System for Autonomous Health Data Modeling

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2602.01078
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.01078
- Authors: Tong Xia; Weibin Li; Gang Liu; Yong Li
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Achieves 100% task success rate, outperforming baselines by 29.2% in prediction performance and 50.2% in uncertainty quantification on 17 health prediction tasks across six modalities

## Executive Summary
AutoHealth is a multi-agent system designed to autonomously model health data while addressing key limitations of existing LLM-based data science agents. The system coordinates five specialized agents to perform data exploration, task-conditioned model construction, training, and reporting, with explicit optimization for both predictive performance and uncertainty estimation. By integrating uncertainty quantification into its core decision-making process, AutoHealth generates reliable models suitable for clinical decision support.

## Method Summary
AutoHealth employs a multi-agent architecture where five specialized agents work in coordination: Data Exploration Agent, Model Construction Agent, Training Agent, Reporting Agent, and an Orchestrator. Each agent has distinct responsibilities - from understanding dataset characteristics and selecting appropriate models to training, evaluation, and generating comprehensive reports. The system explicitly optimizes for uncertainty estimation alongside prediction accuracy, addressing a critical gap in autonomous health data modeling systems.

## Key Results
- Achieves 100% task success rate on benchmark of 17 health prediction tasks
- Outperforms baselines by 29.2% in prediction performance
- Improves uncertainty quantification by 50.2% compared to existing approaches

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The system's effectiveness stems from its specialized agent architecture that addresses the heterogeneity of health data modalities and the critical need for uncertainty quantification in clinical applications. By decomposing the modeling process into focused subtasks handled by domain-specialized agents, AutoHealth can adapt its approach based on data characteristics rather than applying rigid template-based methods.

## Foundational Learning
- Multi-agent coordination: Why needed - to handle complex, heterogeneous health data; Quick check - verify agents communicate effectively without bottlenecks
- Uncertainty quantification in health modeling: Why needed - critical for clinical decision reliability; Quick check - validate uncertainty estimates match ground truth confidence intervals
- Task-conditioned model selection: Why needed - different health data modalities require different approaches; Quick check - confirm model selection matches data characteristics
- Automated data exploration: Why needed - health datasets have complex structures; Quick check - ensure all relevant features and patterns are identified
- Evaluation metric optimization: Why needed - balance between accuracy and uncertainty; Quick check - verify metrics align with clinical utility

## Architecture Onboarding

Component map: Data Exploration Agent -> Model Construction Agent -> Training Agent -> Reporting Agent, with Orchestrator coordinating all agents

Critical path: Data understanding → Model selection → Training with uncertainty optimization → Evaluation → Reporting

Design tradeoffs: Specialized agents provide focused expertise but increase coordination complexity; uncertainty optimization adds computational overhead but improves clinical reliability

Failure signatures: Poor data exploration leading to inappropriate model selection; insufficient uncertainty quantification resulting in overconfident predictions; communication failures between agents causing suboptimal coordination

First experiments:
1. Run on single-modality dataset to validate basic functionality
2. Test uncertainty estimation against known confidence intervals
3. Evaluate coordination overhead with increasing dataset complexity
