# Urban Representation Learning for Fine-grained Economic Mapping: A Semi-supervised Graph-based Approach

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2505.11645
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.11645
- Reference count: 27
- Primary result: SemiGTX achieves R² scores of 0.93, 0.96, and 0.94 for primary, secondary, and tertiary sector GDP mapping respectively

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of fine-grained economic mapping by proposing SemiGTX, an explainable semi-supervised graph learning framework for sectoral economic analysis. The framework integrates multiple geospatial data modalities into a cohesive graph structure and employs a novel semi-information loss function that combines spatial self-supervision with supervised regression. Through multi-task learning, SemiGTX concurrently maps GDP across primary, secondary, and tertiary sectors, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing methods in the Pearl River Delta region while maintaining generalizability across different urban areas.

## Method Summary
SemiGTX is an explainable semi-supervised graph learning framework that integrates geospatial data modalities into a cohesive graph structure. The framework employs a semi-information loss function that combines spatial self-supervision with locally masked supervised regression. Through multi-task learning, it concurrently maps GDP across primary, secondary, and tertiary sectors. The model was evaluated in the Pearl River Delta region and validated in Beijing and Chengdu, demonstrating strong performance with R² scores of 0.93, 0.96, and 0.94 for the three sectors respectively.

## Key Results
- Achieved R² scores of 0.93, 0.96, and 0.94 for primary, secondary, and tertiary sector GDP mapping respectively in Pearl River Delta
- Demonstrated cross-regional validity through successful validation in Beijing and Chengdu
- Showed superior performance compared to existing methods while providing explainable insights into how different data modalities influence predictions

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The effectiveness of SemiGTX stems from its innovative integration of semi-supervised learning with graph-based representation learning. By leveraging both labeled and unlabeled data through a semi-information loss function, the model can learn robust representations even in data-scarce scenarios. The multi-task learning approach enables the model to capture complex relationships between different economic sectors while maintaining computational efficiency. The graph structure effectively captures spatial dependencies and relationships between urban entities, leading to more accurate fine-grained economic mapping.

## Foundational Learning
- Graph neural networks: Essential for capturing spatial relationships between urban entities and modeling complex dependencies in geospatial data
- Semi-supervised learning: Critical for leveraging unlabeled data in data-scarce scenarios, enabling better generalization with limited labeled examples
- Multi-task learning: Enables simultaneous modeling of multiple economic sectors while sharing representations and improving overall performance
- Geospatial data integration: Required for combining diverse data modalities into a unified representation that captures the complexity of urban economic systems
