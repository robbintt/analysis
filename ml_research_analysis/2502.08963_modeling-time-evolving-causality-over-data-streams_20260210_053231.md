# Modeling Time-evolving Causality over Data Streams
## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2502.08963
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.08963
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: ModePlait is a streaming method that models time-evolving causality in multivariate data streams, achieving lower error metrics and better scalability than state-of-the-art baselines.

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces ModePlait, a streaming method for modeling time-evolving causality in multivariate co-evolving data streams and forecasting future values. The core innovation is to express exogenous variables as superpositions of modes and capture their temporal dynamics, enabling the identification of causal relationships that evolve with distinct dynamical patterns (regimes). ModePlait discovers time-evolving causality by detecting regime transitions and forecasting in a streaming fashion. Experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets (e.g., COVID-19 infection data) demonstrate that ModePlait outperforms existing methods in both discovering time-evolving causality and forecasting accuracy, while maintaining constant computational time relative to stream length.

## Method Summary
ModePlait models time-evolving causality by decomposing exogenous variables into superpositions of modes, capturing their temporal dynamics, and identifying causal relationships that shift with distinct dynamical regimes in the data stream. The method detects regime transitions and performs online forecasting, adapting to changes in the underlying causal structure as new data arrives. It leverages online learning to update models incrementally, ensuring scalability and responsiveness to evolving patterns.

## Key Results
- ModePlait achieves lower SHD/SID for causal discovery and lower RMSE/MAE for forecasting compared to baselines like TimesNet, PatchTST, DeepAR, OrbitMap, and ARIMA.
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets (e.g., COVID-19 infection data) validate the method's effectiveness.
- The method demonstrates constant computational time relative to stream length, ensuring scalability.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
ModePlait works by expressing exogenous variables as superpositions of modes, which allows it to capture complex temporal dynamics and evolving causal relationships. By detecting regime transitions in the data stream, it adapts its model to changes in the underlying causal structure, enabling accurate forecasting and causality discovery. The online learning framework ensures that the model remains responsive to new data, while the decomposition into modes provides a flexible representation for evolving patterns.

## Architecture Onboarding
- **Component map**: Data Stream -> Mode Decomposition -> Regime Detection -> Causality Discovery -> Forecasting
- **Critical path**: Incoming data is decomposed into modes, regimes are detected, causal relationships are updated, and forecasts are generated incrementally.
- **Design tradeoffs**: Balances accuracy and computational efficiency by using mode decomposition and online learning, at the cost of potential sensitivity to noise and abrupt regime changes.
- **Failure signatures**: Degraded performance during abrupt regime transitions, sensitivity to high noise levels, and potential scalability issues with extremely high-dimensional streams.
- **First experiments**: 1) Test on high-frequency, high-dimensional IoT sensor data for scalability; 2) Compare against broader set of baselines on diverse datasets; 3) Evaluate robustness under varying noise and missing data conditions.

- The method's applicability to complex, high-dimensional domains beyond the tested datasets remains uncertain.
