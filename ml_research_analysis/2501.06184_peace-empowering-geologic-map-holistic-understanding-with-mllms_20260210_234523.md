# PEACE: Empowering Geologic Map Holistic Understanding with MLLMs

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2501.06184
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.06184
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Proposed GeoMap-Agent achieves 0.811 score on GeoMap-Bench, significantly outperforming GPT-4o's 0.369 score

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of geologic map understanding using multimodal large language models (MLLMs), which struggle with high-resolution images, multiple associated components, and domain-specific knowledge. The authors introduce GeoMap-Bench, the first benchmark for evaluating MLLMs in geologic map understanding, and propose GeoMap-Agent, an AI agent specifically designed for this task. GeoMap-Agent employs a hierarchical information extraction strategy, domain knowledge injection through an expert group, and prompt-enhanced question answering to achieve superior performance.

## Method Summary
The proposed approach consists of three key components: a hierarchical information extraction strategy that breaks down complex geologic maps into manageable components, domain knowledge injection through a curated expert group mechanism, and prompt-enhanced question answering to improve response accuracy. The system was evaluated on the newly created GeoMap-Bench benchmark, which provides a standardized framework for assessing MLLM performance on geologic map understanding tasks.

## Key Results
- GeoMap-Agent achieved an overall score of 0.811 on GeoMap-Bench benchmark
- Significantly outperformed GPT-4o with a score of 0.369
- Demonstrated effectiveness in handling high-resolution images and domain-specific geological knowledge

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The hierarchical information extraction approach allows the system to process complex geologic maps by decomposing them into manageable components, reducing the computational burden on MLLMs while maintaining contextual relationships. The domain knowledge injection mechanism provides specialized geological expertise that general-purpose MLLMs lack, enabling more accurate interpretation of technical terms and relationships. The prompt enhancement strategy leverages structured templates to guide the MLLM toward more precise and relevant responses.

### Component Map

### Critical Path
Input Geologic Map → Hierarchical Information Extraction → Domain Knowledge Injection → Prompt Enhancement → Final Answer

### Design Tradeoffs
The hierarchical approach trades computational complexity for accuracy by breaking down maps into smaller components, while the expert knowledge mechanism balances comprehensiveness against maintenance overhead. The prompt enhancement strategy optimizes for precision over generality.

### Failure Signatures
Performance degradation occurs with highly interdependent map components, ambiguous semantic relationships, and incomplete domain knowledge. The system may struggle with maps containing unusual geological features not represented in the knowledge base.

## Limitations
- Evaluation constrained to synthetic benchmark dataset, limiting real-world generalizability
- Domain knowledge base curated but not extensively validated across geological contexts
- Hierarchical extraction approach may struggle with highly interdependent map components

## Confidence

High confidence: The performance gap between GeoMap-Agent and GPT-4o on GeoMap-Bench is substantial and reproducible given the reported methodology. The modular architecture is well-documented and logically structured.

Medium confidence: The generalizability of results to real-world scenarios remains uncertain due to synthetic benchmark nature. The effectiveness of expert knowledge injection depends heavily on knowledge base quality and comprehensiveness.

Low confidence: Scalability to extremely large or complex geologic maps is not demonstrated. Computational efficiency and resource requirements for practical deployment are not addressed.
