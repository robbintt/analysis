# Navigating Taxonomic Expansions of Entity Sets Driven by Knowledge Bases

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2512.16953
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.16953
- Authors: Pietro Cofone; Giovanni Amendola; Marco Manna; Aldo Ricioppo
- Reference count: 7
- Primary result: Introduces navigation primitives for taxonomic expansions using essential expansions to enable efficient local navigation without full graph construction

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces navigation primitives for taxonomic expansions of entity sets driven by knowledge bases, formalizing reasoning tasks that check whether two tuples belong to comparable, incomparable, or the same nodes in the expansion graph. The authors reformulate these tasks in terms of essential expansions, enabling efficient local navigation without requiring full graph construction. Their results show that under realistic assumptions—such as bounding input or limiting entity descriptions—all tasks are tractable. Specifically, the paper establishes that the problems are in P under the "handy" assumption, while being EXP-hard in the general case and DP-hard in the medium case. This enables practical applications by supporting incremental, local navigation of expansion graphs.

## Method Summary
The authors formalize taxonomic expansion reasoning tasks and introduce the concept of essential expansions to enable local navigation without constructing full expansion graphs. They establish complexity bounds under different assumptions: EXP-hardness in the general case, DP-hardness in the medium case, and tractability (P) under the "handy" assumption where input or entity descriptions can be effectively bounded. The approach leverages tree-structured expansions and provides a framework for efficient reasoning about entity relationships in knowledge bases.

## Key Results
- Navigation tasks can be solved efficiently using essential expansions without full graph construction
- Under the "handy" assumption, all reasoning tasks are in P
- The general case is EXP-hard, while the medium case is DP-hard
- Local navigation primitives enable practical incremental exploration of taxonomic expansions

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The approach works by reformulating taxonomic expansion reasoning in terms of essential expansions, which capture the minimal necessary information for navigation decisions. By avoiding full graph construction and focusing on local expansion properties, the method achieves computational efficiency while preserving correctness for the navigation tasks.

2. **Essential expansions** - Why needed: Enable efficient local reasoning without full graph construction. Quick check: Minimal information sufficient for navigation decisions.

3. **Complexity classes (P, EXP, DP)** - Why needed: Characterize computational tractability of different reasoning tasks. Quick check: Verify hardness results under different assumptions.

4. **Navigation primitives** - Why needed: Provide fundamental operations for exploring taxonomic structures. Quick check: Can determine comparability and equivalence relationships.

5. **Tree-structured expansions** - Why needed: Enable efficient reasoning by avoiding cycles. Quick check: Expansion graph forms a tree without cycles.

6. **Entity description bounding** - Why needed: Critical assumption for achieving tractability. Quick check: Entity descriptions have bounded complexity.

Critical path: Entity Input → Essential Expansion Generation → Local Navigation Decision → Task Resolution

Design tradeoffs: Full graph construction (complete but expensive) vs. essential expansions (incomplete but efficient)

Failure signatures: Incorrect navigation results when entity descriptions exceed assumed bounds; failure to handle cycles in expansion graphs

## Limitations
- Practical scalability depends heavily on the "handy" assumption which may not hold in real-world KBs
- Empirical validation on production-scale knowledge bases is absent
- Limited treatment of cycles, inconsistencies, and incomplete taxonomic information
- No extensive evaluation of semantic ambiguities and entity resolution errors

## Confidence
- **High**: Complexity results and formal proofs of EXP-hardness, DP-hardness, and P membership under specified assumptions
- **Medium**: The theoretical framework for essential expansions and local navigation
- **Low**: Practical scalability claims and real-world applicability without empirical validation

## Next Checks
1. Implement the essential expansion algorithm on a large-scale knowledge base (e.g., Wikidata or DBpedia) and measure performance degradation as entity description complexity increases beyond the assumed bounds
2. Evaluate the navigation primitives on KBs with known inconsistencies and cycles to assess robustness of the taxonomic reasoning
3. Conduct user studies comparing the proposed navigation approach against full graph construction for common entity exploration tasks in real applications
