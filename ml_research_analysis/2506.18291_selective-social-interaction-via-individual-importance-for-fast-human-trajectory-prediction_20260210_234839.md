# Selective Social-Interaction via Individual Importance for Fast Human Trajectory Prediction

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2506.18291
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.18291
- Reference count: 17
- Key result: Reduces FLOPs by up to 8.1% while maintaining ADE of 0.377 and FDE of 0.747

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of speeding up human trajectory prediction (HTP) by selectively focusing on the most influential neighboring people for predicting the primary person's path. The proposed method introduces an Importance Estimator that assigns each neighboring person an importance score based on their influence on the primary person's future trajectory. To train this estimator effectively, the authors employ the Gumbel-Softmax reparameterization trick, enabling differentiable sampling of discrete neighboring people selections during the forward pass. Additionally, a variance-based loss term is introduced to encourage diverse importance scores and prevent collapse. Experiments on the JRDB dataset show that the proposed method reduces computational cost by up to 8.1% in FLOPs while maintaining competitive prediction accuracy, demonstrating a favorable efficiency–performance trade-off.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method works by selectively focusing computational resources on the most influential neighboring people for predicting the primary person's trajectory. The Importance Estimator learns to assign higher importance scores to neighbors that have greater influence on the primary person's future path, while the Gumbel-Softmax reparameterization enables differentiable sampling during training. The variance-based loss term prevents the model from collapsing to uniform importance scores, ensuring that the selection remains meaningful and focused on truly influential interactions.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**
Input Data -> Importance Estimator -> Gumbel-Softmax Sampling -> Trajectory Prediction -> Output

**Critical Path**
The critical path flows from input data through the Importance Estimator, where importance scores are computed, then through the Gumbel-Softmax sampling mechanism to select influential neighbors, and finally to the trajectory prediction module that generates the output.

**Design Tradeoffs**
The method trades some prediction accuracy for computational efficiency by selectively processing only the most influential neighbors. The Gumbel-Softmax reparameterization enables end-to-end training but introduces approximation error compared to true discrete sampling. The variance-based loss prevents score collapse but may encourage artificially diverse scores in some cases.

**Failure Signatures**
The model may fail when social interactions are subtle or non-obvious, as the importance estimator might miss these weak but potentially significant influences. Dense crowd scenarios could overwhelm the selective mechanism, leading to suboptimal neighbor selection. The method may also struggle with highly dynamic environments where interaction patterns change rapidly.

## Limitations
- Scalability concerns in dense crowd scenarios where selective sampling may not capture all relevant interactions
- Potential generalization issues in environments with rapidly changing social dynamics
- Limited evaluation of variance-based loss performance in preventing importance score collapse across diverse scenarios
