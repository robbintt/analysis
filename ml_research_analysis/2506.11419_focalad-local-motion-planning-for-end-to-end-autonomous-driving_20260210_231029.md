# FocalAD: Local Motion Planning for End-to-End Autonomous Driving

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2506.11419
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.11419
- Reference count: 40
- Key outcome: 41.9% reduction in collision rate compared to DiffusionDrive on adversarial scenarios

## Executive Summary
FocalAD introduces a local motion planning approach for end-to-end autonomous driving that addresses the limitations of global motion feature aggregation. The method employs two core modules: the Ego-Local-Agents Interactor (ELAI) for explicit modeling of local interactions between the ego vehicle and nearby agents, and the Focal-Local-Agents Loss (FLA Loss) for decision-critical agent weighting during training. Extensive experiments on nuScenes, Bench2Drive, and Adv-nuScenes datasets demonstrate significant improvements in safety and planning performance compared to state-of-the-art methods.

## Method Summary
FocalAD addresses the problem of global motion feature aggregation in end-to-end autonomous driving, which often obscures critical local interactions and leads to suboptimal planning decisions. The proposed solution introduces two core modules: the Ego-Local-Agents Interactor (ELAI), which explicitly models local interactions between the ego vehicle and nearby agents through a graph-based structure, and the Focal-Local-Agents Loss (FLA Loss), which applies focal weighting to decision-critical agents during training. Extensive experiments on nuScenes, Bench2Drive, and Adv-nuScenes datasets demonstrate FocalAD's superiority over state-of-the-art methods, including a 41.9% reduction in collision rate compared to DiffusionDrive and a 15.6% reduction compared to SparseDrive on adversarial scenarios.

## Key Results
- 41.9% reduction in collision rate compared to DiffusionDrive on adversarial scenarios
- 15.6% reduction in collision rate compared to SparseDrive on adversarial scenarios
- Lowest average collision rate (0.97%) and highest driving score (45.77%) on robustness-focused evaluations

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
FocalAD improves autonomous driving safety by focusing on local agent interactions rather than global feature aggregation. The ELAI module explicitly models the relationships between the ego vehicle and nearby agents through a graph-based structure, capturing crucial spatial and temporal dependencies. The FLA Loss applies focal weighting to decision-critical agents during training, ensuring the model prioritizes learning from high-risk scenarios. This local interaction-aware approach leads to more accurate and safer motion planning decisions.

## Architecture Onboarding

### Component Map
Ego sensor inputs -> ELAI module -> Local interaction features -> Focal-Local-Agents Loss -> Driving policy

### Critical Path
Sensor data processing -> Graph construction (ELAI) -> Local interaction modeling -> Decision-making with focal weighting

### Design Tradeoffs
- Local vs. global feature aggregation: FocalAD prioritizes local interactions for improved safety
- Fixed vs. adaptive neighborhood thresholds: Current implementation uses fixed thresholds for agent selection
- Interpretability vs. performance: FocalAD aims to balance both aspects through local interaction modeling

### Failure Signatures
- Increased collision rates in highly dynamic or dense traffic scenarios
- Suboptimal performance in scenarios with non-local agent interactions
- Potential overfitting to specific traffic patterns or environments
