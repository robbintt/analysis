# Generalized Proof-Number Monte-Carlo Tree Search

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID:** 2506.13249
- **Source URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.13249
- **Reference count:** 29
- **Key outcome:** GPN-MCTS outperforms standard MCTS by 80% on 8 out of 11 games with narrow winning paths

## Executive Summary
Generalized Proof-Number Monte-Carlo Tree Search (GPN-MCTS) addresses the limitations of standard MCTS in games with narrow winning paths by integrating Proof-Number Search (PNS) principles to guide exploration. The method tracks proof numbers per player, eliminating disproof numbers and enabling multi-player games, while evaluating three bias formulas (PNRank, PNMax, PNSum) for UCT selection. By combining with Score Bounded MCTS, GPN-MCTS handles score bounds rather than just win/loss outcomes. Experiments across 11 diverse board games demonstrate significant performance improvements, with 80% win rate against standard MCTS on 8 games, while maintaining minimal computational overhead.

## Method Summary
GPN-MCTS extends MCTS by integrating proof-number search concepts to better handle games with narrow winning paths. The method tracks proof numbers per player (eliminating disproof numbers) and evaluates three different bias formulas for UCT selection: PNRank, PNMax, and PNSum. These formulas are designed to guide exploration toward promising branches while maintaining computational efficiency. The approach is further enhanced by combining with Score Bounded MCTS to handle games with score bounds rather than simple win/loss outcomes. The implementation adds minimal overhead compared to standard MCTS while significantly improving performance on games where winning paths are rare or difficult to find through random exploration alone.

## Key Results
- GPN-MCTS achieves 80% win rate against standard MCTS on 8 out of 11 tested board games
- PNMax and PNSum bias formulas provide comparable performance to PNRank while being computationally cheaper
- Knightthrough shows 63.2% win rate, with other games exceeding 60% improvement
- The method adds minimal computational overhead compared to standard MCTS implementation

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
GPN-MCTS works by addressing the fundamental limitation of MCTS in games with narrow winning paths: random exploration is unlikely to discover rare but critical moves. By incorporating proof-number search concepts, the algorithm maintains explicit estimates of how many moves are needed to prove a win, allowing it to focus computational resources on promising branches. The per-player proof number tracking eliminates the need for disproof numbers, simplifying the algorithm while maintaining effectiveness. The three bias formulas provide different strategies for balancing exploration and exploitation, with PNRank being most thorough but computationally expensive, while PNMax and PNSum offer faster alternatives with comparable results.
