# Is Architectural Complexity Overrated? Competitive and Interpretable Knowledge Graph Completion with RelatE

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2505.18971
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18971
- **Reference count**: 40
- **Primary result**: RELATE achieves competitive performance on standard benchmarks while being more efficient and interpretable than complex architectures

## Executive Summary
RELATE presents a real-valued, interpretable model for knowledge graph completion that challenges the trend toward increasingly complex architectures. By leveraging phase-modulus decomposition to capture relational patterns such as symmetry, inversion, and composition, RELATE demonstrates that architectural simplicity can yield competitive performance. The model integrates dual-role entity and relation embeddings with sinusoidal phase alignment and lightweight type constraints, achieving strong results on standard benchmarks like YAGO3-10 (MRR 0.521, Hit@10 0.680) while significantly reducing computational overhead.

## Method Summary
RELATE introduces a phase-modulus decomposition approach where entity and relation embeddings are split into real-valued components. The model uses dual-role embeddings to capture both subject and object perspectives of entities, combined with sinusoidal phase alignment to model relational patterns. Type constraints are incorporated as lightweight regularization to enhance interpretability. The scoring function leverages phase differences between entities modulated by relation-specific parameters, enabling the model to capture first-order logical inference patterns while maintaining computational efficiency through reduced parameter count and simpler operations compared to rotation-based alternatives.

## Key Results
- Achieves MRR of 0.521 and Hit@10 of 0.680 on YAGO3-10 benchmark
- Reduces training time by 24%, inference latency by 31%, and GPU memory usage by 22% compared to RotatE
- Demonstrates up to 61% less MRR degradation under structural perturbations compared to TransE

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
RELATE works by decomposing complex relational patterns into interpretable phase-modulus components. The phase alignment mechanism allows direct modeling of symmetry, inversion, and composition through simple arithmetic operations on angles, avoiding the need for complex rotational transformations. Dual-role embeddings capture the context-dependent nature of entities, while type constraints guide the learning process toward more semantically meaningful representations. This architectural simplicity translates directly to computational efficiency without sacrificing expressive power.

## Foundational Learning
- **Phase-modulus decomposition**: Why needed - separates magnitude and directional information for more interpretable embeddings; Quick check - verify phase angles follow expected patterns for symmetric relations
- **Dual-role entity embeddings**: Why needed - captures different entity behaviors as subject vs object; Quick check - measure embedding distance correlation for subject-object pairs
- **Sinusoidal phase alignment**: Why needed - enables modeling of compositional patterns through angle arithmetic; Quick check - test if inverse relations produce negative phase offsets
- **Type constraints**: Why needed - regularizes embeddings toward interpretable semantic clusters; Quick check - visualize embedding neighborhoods by entity type

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Entity Embeddings -> Phase Alignment -> Scoring Function -> Type Constraints -> Loss Optimization

**Critical Path**: Input triples → Dual-role embedding lookup → Phase alignment computation → Scoring function evaluation → Type constraint regularization → Loss backpropagation

**Design Tradeoffs**: Simplicity vs expressiveness (sacrifices some representational power for interpretability and efficiency), real-valued vs complex embeddings (easier computation but potentially less compact representation), phase-based vs rotation-based patterns (more interpretable but may miss some complex transformations)

**Failure Signatures**: Poor performance on highly asymmetric relations (phase alignment may not capture complex directional patterns), overfitting to type constraints (excessive regularization limiting generalization), phase wrapping issues (angle calculations crossing 2π boundaries)

**First Experiments**: 1) Compare phase angle distributions for symmetric vs asymmetric relations, 2) Measure embedding similarity changes when switching entity roles, 3) Test type constraint impact by training with and without regularization

## Limitations
- Benchmark comparisons limited to standard WN18RR and YAGO3-10 datasets, potentially missing diversity of real-world KGs
- Interpretability claims lack quantitative metrics beyond phase alignment visualization
- No evaluation of performance under real-world adversarial conditions or gradual schema evolution

## Confidence

**High**: Computational efficiency gains (training time, memory, latency), phase-based relational expressiveness (symmetry, inversion, composition), robustness under controlled perturbations

**Medium**: Benchmark performance parity with RotatE, generalizability across diverse KG schemas, scalability to billion-scale graphs

**Low**: Interpretability in practical deployment scenarios, performance under real-world adversarial or drift conditions, extension to multi-modal or temporal KGs

## Next Checks
1. Benchmark RELATE on a broader set of KGs including industrial-scale, highly sparse, and temporally evolving datasets (e.g., Wikidata, DBpedia update streams)
2. Conduct ablation studies isolating the impact of type constraints, phase alignment, and dual-role embeddings on both performance and interpretability metrics
3. Perform stress testing under realistic adversarial edge insertions/deletions and gradual schema evolution to measure long-term robustness
