# We Are All Creators: Generative AI, Collective Knowledge, and the Path Towards Human-AI Synergy

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2504.07936
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.07936
- Reference count: 8
- Primary result: Reframes generative AI as "alternative creativity" requiring new frameworks beyond traditional copyright

## Executive Summary
This paper presents a philosophical and policy-oriented reframing of generative AI as a form of "alternative creativity" based on mathematical pattern synthesis rather than human cognition. The authors argue that AI models, trained on vast datasets representing collective human knowledge, cannot be fairly attributed to individual creators and require fundamentally different approaches than traditional copyright frameworks. The paper advocates for "human-AI synergy" as the optimal path forward, leveraging AI's computational strengths alongside human intuition and ethical judgment to democratize creativity and accelerate innovation.

The central thesis challenges the conventional wisdom that AI-generated content should be treated through existing intellectual property frameworks. Instead, the authors propose viewing AI as a collective creative tool that emerges from shared human knowledge and therefore should be governed by principles of equitable access and collaborative benefit. This reframing has significant implications for policy makers, content creators, and technology developers seeking to navigate the rapidly evolving landscape of generative AI.

## Method Summary
The paper employs conceptual analysis and philosophical argumentation rather than empirical research methods. The authors synthesize perspectives from technology, law, ethics, and creativity studies to construct their framework of "alternative creativity" and "human-AI synergy." The approach involves examining the fundamental nature of generative AI systems, their relationship to collective knowledge, and the limitations of applying traditional copyright concepts to AI-generated content.

## Key Results
- Generative AI represents "alternative creativity" based on mathematical pattern synthesis rather than human cognition
- Traditional copyright frameworks are inadequate for addressing AI-generated content due to its collective origins
- Equitable access to AI tools is presented as an ethical imperative to prevent widening societal divides
- Human-AI synergy is proposed as the optimal framework for leveraging complementary strengths of both

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The paper's argument works by reframing the fundamental nature of generative AI from a tool that mimics human creativity to a distinct form of creativity based on mathematical pattern recognition and synthesis. This conceptual shift undermines traditional attribution models that assume individual human authorship. The mechanism relies on demonstrating how AI systems absorb and recombine vast datasets representing collective human knowledge, making individual attribution both practically impossible and philosophically inappropriate. This reframing naturally leads to the conclusion that new frameworks based on collective benefit and equitable access are needed rather than attempting to fit AI into existing copyright structures.
