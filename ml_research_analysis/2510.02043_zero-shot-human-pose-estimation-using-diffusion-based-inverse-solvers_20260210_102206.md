# Zero-shot Human Pose Estimation using Diffusion-based Inverse solvers

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2510.02043
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.02043
- Reference count: 20
- Introduces InPose, a diffusion-based inverse solver for zero-shot human pose estimation from sparse on-body sensor measurements

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of zero-shot human pose estimation using diffusion-based inverse solvers with sparse on-body sensor measurements. The key innovation is InPose, which overcomes the poor generalization across users with varying body sizes that plagues existing conditional diffusion models. The method achieves this by conditioning only on rotation measurements while using a novel inverse guidance term based on location measurements to account for body size differences. The approach leverages theoretical insights about noise propagation through non-linear measurement operators to maintain consistent performance across diverse body types and scales.

## Method Summary
InPose is a diffusion-based inverse solver that addresses zero-shot human pose estimation by decoupling rotation and location measurements in the conditioning process. The core innovation lies in using Theorem 1 to approximate how Gaussian noise propagates through non-linear measurement operators, enabling the incorporation of location measurements through an inverse guidance term. This approach allows the model to maintain consistent performance across users with different body sizes by conditioning only on rotation measurements while using location measurements to account for body size variations. The method is trained on the AMASS dataset and demonstrates robust zero-shot generalization through synthetic body size scaling experiments.

## Key Results
- Maintains consistent MPJPE and MPJRE errors regardless of body size scaling
- Demonstrates stable performance across different scaling factors compared to baseline degradation
- Achieves robust zero-shot generalization on AMASS dataset through synthetic body size scaling

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method works by leveraging the theoretical framework of Theorem 1 to understand how Gaussian noise propagates through non-linear measurement operators. By conditioning only on rotation measurements and using location measurements as an inverse guidance term, the model can account for body size variations without compromising the diffusion process. This separation of concerns allows the model to maintain consistent performance across different body types while preserving the benefits of diffusion-based inverse solving for pose estimation.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Sensor Measurements -> Measurement Operator -> Inverse Guidance Term -> Diffusion Process -> Pose Estimates

**Critical Path**: The critical path involves sensor measurements being processed through the measurement operator, then incorporated into the diffusion process via the inverse guidance term to produce final pose estimates.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The key tradeoff is between conditioning on all measurements versus separating rotation and location conditioning. The chosen approach sacrifices some potential information from location measurements in the diffusion process to gain better generalization across body sizes.

**Failure Signatures**: Performance degradation on truly diverse body types outside the AMASS dataset, sensitivity to sensor calibration errors, and breakdown under realistic noise conditions and occlusions.

**First Experiments**: 1) Test on real-world datasets with diverse human subjects across different populations, 2) Evaluate under realistic sensor noise conditions and occlusions, 3) Compare against camera-based pose estimation methods to quantify tradeoffs.

## Limitations
- Experiments limited to AMASS dataset, which may not represent real-world deployment scenarios
- Synthetic body size scaling rather than testing on truly diverse human subjects
- Does not address sensor calibration, environmental interference, or motion dynamics impacts

## Confidence

**High confidence**: Theoretical framework around inverse guidance and Theorem 1 appears sound. Core mathematical formulation for incorporating location measurements through inverse guidance term is well-established.

**Medium confidence**: Experimental results showing consistent performance across body size scaling are compelling but based on synthetic experiments. Comparison to baseline methods appears rigorous but only one baseline is explicitly mentioned.

**Low confidence**: Claims about real-world deployment scenarios not supported by experiments outside controlled AMASS dataset environment. Does not address sensor calibration, environmental interference, or motion dynamics issues.
