# Quantum Reinforcement Learning with Dynamic-Circuit Qubit Reuse and Grover-Based Trajectory Optimization

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2509.16002
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.16002
- Reference count: 0
- Proposes a fully quantum reinforcement learning framework integrating Q-MDP, dynamic circuits, and Grover's algorithm

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces a fully quantum reinforcement learning framework that eliminates classical subroutines by encoding states, actions, rewards, and transitions entirely within the quantum domain. The framework leverages dynamic circuit operations including mid-circuit measurement and reset to reuse physical qubits across multiple agent-environment interactions, achieving a 66% reduction in qubit requirements from 7*T to 7 for T time steps. Grover's algorithm is applied to amplify the probability of measuring trajectories with the highest return, enabling efficient identification of optimal policies through quantum parallelism.

## Method Summary
The framework implements a quantum Markov decision process where quantum circuits encode state-action sequences and their associated returns. Dynamic circuit operations enable qubit reuse across multiple decision steps, while quantum arithmetic computes trajectory returns. Grover's search is then applied to the superposition of evaluated trajectories to amplify optimal solutions. The approach maintains logical continuity through careful state preservation during qubit reset operations and demonstrates feasibility on IBM Heron-class quantum hardware.

## Key Results
- Achieves 66% qubit reduction by reusing 7 qubits across T time steps versus static 7*T allocation
- Preserves trajectory fidelity while maintaining logical continuity through dynamic circuit operations
- Validated on IBM Heron-class hardware, demonstrating operation within current NISQ constraints

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework exploits quantum parallelism to evaluate all possible trajectories simultaneously in superposition, while Grover's algorithm amplifies the probability amplitudes of optimal trajectories. Dynamic circuits enable efficient qubit reuse through mid-circuit measurement and reset operations, reducing hardware requirements without sacrificing computational power. Quantum arithmetic operations compute trajectory returns directly within the quantum domain, eliminating classical bottlenecks.

## Architecture Onboarding
**Component Map:** State Encoding -> Action Encoding -> Transition Computation -> Reward Calculation -> Return Computation -> Grover Amplification
**Critical Path:** State-action sequence preparation → quantum arithmetic for returns → Grover iteration → measurement of optimal trajectory
**Design Tradeoffs:** Qubit reuse reduces hardware requirements but introduces mid-circuit measurement errors; quantum parallelism provides speedup but requires careful error mitigation
**Failure Signatures:** Mid-circuit measurement errors cause trajectory corruption; insufficient coherence time prevents complete Grover iterations; gate errors accumulate in multi-step computations
**First Experiments:** 1) Test single-step state-action encoding fidelity; 2) Verify qubit reset preserves quantum state information; 3) Validate Grover amplification on small trajectory spaces

## Limitations
- Multi-step quantum state encoding fidelity under realistic noise conditions remains uncertain
- Scalability to larger MDPs with extensive state-action spaces not demonstrated
- Impact of decoherence and gate errors on long-term trajectory optimization not fully characterized
