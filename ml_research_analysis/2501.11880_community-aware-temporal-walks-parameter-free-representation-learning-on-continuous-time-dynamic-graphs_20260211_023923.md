# Community-Aware Temporal Walks: Parameter-Free Representation Learning on Continuous-Time Dynamic Graphs

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2501.11880
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.11880
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Achieves state-of-the-art temporal link prediction with AUC improvements up to 5.14% on benchmark datasets

## Executive Summary
CTWalks introduces a parameter-free framework for representation learning on continuous-time dynamic graphs by integrating community-aware temporal walks with continuous-time dynamics via ODEs. The method addresses limitations of existing approaches by incorporating meso-scale community structures, eliminating the need for handcrafted sampling parameters, and modeling temporal dynamics without discretization. CTWalks employs a community-driven sampling strategy that performs temporal walks within and across communities, an anonymization process enriched with community labels, and an encoding mechanism using ODEs to capture continuous spatiotemporal dependencies. Experiments on benchmark datasets show CTWalks achieves state-of-the-art performance in temporal link prediction tasks.

## Method Summary
CTWalks operates on continuous-time dynamic graphs by first constructing a static weighted graph from the temporal data to detect communities using Louvain algorithm. The method distinguishes "non-bridging" nodes (staying within their community) and "bridging" nodes (connecting multiple communities) to guide temporal walks. Node identities are anonymized by replacing them with structural positions and community labels. The encoding alternates between instantaneous GRU updates and continuous ODE evolution to capture temporal dynamics. The framework is trained using Adam optimizer with negative sampling for link prediction tasks.

## Key Results
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance in temporal link prediction with AUC improvements up to 5.14% compared to baselines
- Demonstrates superior generalization in inductive settings through community-aware anonymization
- Eliminates need for handcrafted sampling parameters while maintaining competitive performance
- Shows robust performance across both transductive and inductive scenarios on five benchmark datasets

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1: Community-Guided Parameter-Free Sampling
The method partitions temporal walks into strictly intra-community or inter-community trajectories, eliminating the need for handcrafted sampling hyperparameters. By using community structure to guide walks, it reduces locality bias and ensures diverse exploration of the graph space.

### Mechanism 2: Context-Rich Anonymization for Inductive Generalization
Replacing node IDs with structural positions and community labels allows the model to generalize to unseen nodes by recognizing functional roles rather than memorizing identities. This approach distinguishes nodes with identical local structures but different community roles.

### Mechanism 3: Hybrid Continuous-Discrete Temporal Encoding
Modeling time evolution as a continuous differential equation between discrete node updates captures the dynamics of the system state during intervals of inactivity. This hybrid approach combines instantaneous GRU updates with ODE integration for continuous temporal modeling.

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out
- Scalability challenges of ODE solvers for extremely large graphs require lightweight integration techniques
- Static community detection may limit ability to capture temporal evolution in community structures
- Adaptive sampling techniques are needed for networks with overlapping or poorly defined communities

## Limitations
- Static community detection assumption may fail for highly dynamic graphs where community structure evolves significantly
- Limited ablation studies on continuous-time modeling across different temporal scales and interaction densities
- Computational overhead of ODE solvers may limit scalability for massive-scale industrial graphs
