# When Large Language Models Meet UAV Projects: An Empirical Study from Developers' Perspective

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2509.12795
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.12795
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: First empirical study of LLMs in UAV systems identifies nine LLM-supported tasks across four workflows, with 59.6% of developers facing integration challenges

## Executive Summary
This paper presents the first comprehensive empirical study examining how large language models (LLMs) are being integrated into unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) systems. Through systematic analysis of 997 research papers and 1,509 GitHub projects, the study identifies nine distinct LLM-supported tasks spanning four UAV workflows. A survey of 52 developers from 15 countries reveals significant challenges in LLM integration, with 59.6% of developers facing technical maturity, performance, safety, and cost barriers despite 40.4% having attempted integration.

The research highlights a critical gap between academic research, which focuses heavily on algorithm optimization, and industry practice, which prioritizes real-time performance and stability. LLM technical maturity scores average below 3 out of 5 for most tasks, indicating substantial room for improvement. Hybrid onboard assistance emerges as the dominant integration approach, suggesting developers are seeking balanced solutions between cloud-based and onboard processing capabilities.

## Method Summary
The study employed a multi-pronged empirical approach combining literature analysis with developer surveys. Researchers systematically searched academic databases to identify 997 relevant research papers and GitHub to locate 1,509 UAV-LLM projects. They conducted comprehensive content analysis to categorize LLM applications across UAV workflows. Additionally, a survey of 52 developers from 15 countries provided insights into practical challenges, integration approaches, and technical maturity perceptions. The methodology integrated both quantitative analysis of existing work and qualitative insights from practitioner experiences.

## Key Results
- 59.6% of developers face significant challenges integrating LLMs into UAV systems due to technical maturity, performance, safety, and cost issues
- Nine LLM-supported tasks identified across four UAV workflows, with most technical maturity scores below 3/5
- Academic research focuses on algorithm optimization while industry prioritizes real-time performance and stability
- Hybrid onboard assistance is the dominant integration approach among practitioners

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The study works by bridging the gap between academic research and practical implementation through systematic empirical analysis. By examining both research literature and real-world GitHub projects, it captures the full spectrum of LLM-UAV integration efforts. The developer survey provides crucial ground-truth validation of theoretical capabilities versus practical challenges, revealing the disconnect between research focus and industry needs.

## Limitations
- Sample size of 52 developers may not fully represent the broader UAV-LLM developer community
- Keyword-based identification could miss relevant projects using different terminology
- Self-reported technical maturity scores introduce potential bias in the 5-point scale interpretation
- Focus on current practices rather than predicting future adoption patterns or long-term technical developments

- Medium confidence in developer survey results regarding challenges and integration approaches
- Medium confidence in comparison between academic and industry focus
