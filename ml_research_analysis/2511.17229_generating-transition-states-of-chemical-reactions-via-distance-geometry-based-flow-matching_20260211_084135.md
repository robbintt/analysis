# Generating transition states of chemical reactions via distance-geometry-based flow matching

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2511.17229
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.17229
- Reference count: 40
- TS-DFM achieves 30% higher structural accuracy than React-OT with mean RMSD of 0.1828 Å on Transition1X benchmark

## Executive Summary
TS-DFM introduces a flow matching framework that predicts transition states (TSs) of chemical reactions by operating in molecular distance geometry space. The method explicitly models interatomic distance changes during reactions using a novel network structure called TSDVNet to learn a velocity field that directs initial guessed TS geometries toward the true TS. The approach demonstrates superior performance compared to existing TS prediction methods, achieving 30% higher structural accuracy on the Transition1X benchmark while also providing high-quality initial structures that significantly accelerate CI-NEB optimization convergence.

## Method Summary
TS-DFM is a flow matching framework that predicts transition states of chemical reactions by operating in molecular distance geometry space, explicitly modeling interatomic distance changes during reactions. The method employs a network structure called TSDVNet to learn a velocity field that directs initial guessed TS geometries toward the true TS. This approach leverages the physical principle that distance geometry provides a complete representation of molecular structures and their evolution during chemical reactions, allowing the model to focus on the most relevant structural changes rather than working in Cartesian coordinates.

## Key Results
- 30% higher structural accuracy than React-OT on Transition1X benchmark (mean RMSD 0.1828 Å, DMAE 0.0607 Å)
- TS predictions accelerate CI-NEB optimization convergence by three times compared to IDPP initialization
- Outperforms React-OT by at least 16% on average RMSD for unseen molecular structures and reaction types in RGD1 dataset

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method leverages flow matching in distance geometry space to predict transition states by learning velocity fields that transform initial guesses into true TS structures. This approach explicitly models interatomic distance changes during reactions, which provides a more natural representation of molecular evolution compared to Cartesian coordinates.

## Foundational Learning
- Distance Geometry: Represents molecular structures through interatomic distances rather than Cartesian coordinates; needed because it captures the essential geometric relationships that change during reactions
- Flow Matching: A generative modeling technique that learns velocity fields to transform distributions; quick check: verify velocity field consistency across different reaction coordinates
- TSDVNet Architecture: A specialized neural network for learning velocity fields in distance geometry space; quick check: validate velocity field gradients match expected reaction path curvature

## Architecture Onboarding
Component Map: Initial Guess -> Distance Geometry Transformation -> TSDVNet Velocity Field Learning -> TS Prediction -> CI-NEB Validation
Critical Path: The velocity field learning through TSDVNet is the critical component, as it directly determines the quality of TS predictions
Design Tradeoffs: Distance geometry space provides better geometric representation but requires careful handling of distance constraints and triangle inequalities
Failure Signatures: Poor TS predictions occur when initial geometries are significantly distorted or when reaction coordinate is highly nonlinear

## Limitations
- Relies on high-quality input geometries, which may not always be available
- Requires both reactant and product structures as input, limiting applicability when products are unknown
- May struggle with complex molecular conformations and highly flexible systems

## Confidence
