# AI as Entertainment

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2601.08768
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.08768
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Generative AI is increasingly used for entertainment rather than productivity, requiring new evaluation frameworks

## Executive Summary
This paper argues that generative AI systems are increasingly being used for entertainment rather than productivity, a use case for which the AI field is unprepared to evaluate. While AI evaluations rigorously measure intelligence and cultural harms, they lack frameworks for assessing the benefits of cultural outputs. The authors propose "thick entertainment" as a framework for evaluating AI-generated cultural content, drawing on insights from the humanities to consider entertainment's role in meaning-making, identity formation, and social connection.

The paper contends that entertainment will become a primary business model for major AI corporations seeking returns on massive infrastructure investments, and that this will significantly influence the technology these companies produce. The authors call for more debates, discourse, and study on AI as entertainment to better prepare for its societal impacts.

## Method Summary
The paper presents a conceptual framework drawing from humanities perspectives on entertainment, particularly the concept of "thick entertainment" that considers entertainment's role in meaning-making, identity formation, and social connection. The authors analyze current trends in AI usage and business models, and critique existing AI evaluation frameworks for their focus on intelligence and harm detection rather than cultural benefit assessment.

## Key Results
- Generative AI is increasingly being used for entertainment purposes rather than productivity
- Current AI evaluation frameworks are inadequate for assessing cultural and entertainment value
- Entertainment will likely become a primary business model for major AI corporations
- The "thick entertainment" framework draws on humanities insights to evaluate AI's cultural impact

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The paper works by identifying a gap between how AI systems are actually being used (increasingly for entertainment) and how they are evaluated (primarily for intelligence and harm detection). By proposing a new framework based on humanities concepts, the authors provide a theoretical foundation for understanding and evaluating AI's cultural impact beyond traditional metrics.
