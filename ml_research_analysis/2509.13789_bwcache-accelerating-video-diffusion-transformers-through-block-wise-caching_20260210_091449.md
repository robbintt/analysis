# BWCache: Accelerating Video Diffusion Transformers through Block-Wise Caching

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2509.13789
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.13789
- Reference count: 21
- Key outcome: Training-free acceleration method achieving up to 2.24× speedup with quality preservation

## Executive Summary
BWCache introduces a training-free acceleration method for DiT-based video generation models by identifying and exploiting block-wise feature redundancy across diffusion timesteps. The approach uses a block-wise relative L1 distance indicator to detect when features can be cached and reused, with periodic recomputation to prevent latent drift. Across multiple models including Open-Sora, Open-Sora-Plan, and Latte, BWCache achieves significant speedups while maintaining or improving visual quality metrics.

## Method Summary
BWCache operates by monitoring feature similarity across diffusion timesteps in DiT blocks, using block-wise relative L1 distance as the similarity indicator. When features meet predefined similarity thresholds, they are cached and reused instead of being recomputed. To maintain generation quality, the method implements periodic recomputation of cached features to prevent latent drift and ensures the final half of denoising steps are always recomputed. This approach enables significant computational savings while preserving output quality.

## Key Results
- Achieves up to 2.24× speedup across multiple DiT-based video generation models
- Maintains comparable or improved visual quality (VBench↑0.71%, LPIPS↓53.2%, SSIM↑5.23%, PSNR↑4.66%)
- Reduces latency compared to baseline acceleration methods
- Demonstrates effectiveness across Open-Sora, Open-Sora-Plan, and Latte models

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method exploits inherent redundancy in feature representations across diffusion timesteps within DiT blocks. By identifying when block-wise features remain sufficiently similar across timesteps, BWCache can cache and reuse these computations instead of recalculating them. The relative L1 distance metric provides an effective similarity measure that balances computational efficiency with quality preservation. Periodic recomputation prevents the accumulation of errors that could arise from extended caching, while always recomputing the final denoising steps ensures output quality is maintained where it matters most.

## Foundational Learning

**DiT (Diffusion Transformer)**: A transformer-based architecture adapted for diffusion models, used for video generation tasks. Why needed: Understanding this architecture is crucial as BWCache specifically targets DiT blocks for caching optimization. Quick check: Verify familiarity with transformer attention mechanisms and diffusion model denoising processes.

**Feature Redundancy**: The phenomenon where similar or identical feature representations occur across different computational steps or timesteps. Why needed: This is the core assumption enabling BWCache's acceleration strategy. Quick check: Confirm understanding that feature similarity can be measured and exploited for computational savings.

**Relative L1 Distance**: A similarity metric measuring the normalized absolute difference between feature vectors. Why needed: This specific metric is used by BWCache to determine when features are similar enough to cache and reuse. Quick check: Ensure understanding of L1 norm calculation and normalization techniques.

**Latent Drift**: The gradual degradation or deviation of latent representations when reusing cached features over multiple timesteps. Why needed: BWCache implements periodic recomputation specifically to prevent this phenomenon. Quick check: Verify understanding of how repeated operations on cached features can accumulate errors.

## Architecture Onboarding

Component Map: Video Input -> DiT Blocks -> Feature Extraction -> Similarity Check -> Cache Management -> Output Generation

Critical Path: The similarity checking and cache management components introduce minimal overhead compared to the computational savings from caching, making them negligible in the overall performance equation.

Design Tradeoffs: The method trades minimal quality degradation (controlled through periodic recomputation) for significant computational speedup. The choice of L1 distance as similarity metric balances computational efficiency with accuracy.

Failure Signatures: Performance degradation may occur with highly dynamic video content where feature similarity assumptions break down, or when cache management overhead exceeds savings due to frequent cache misses.

## Limitations
