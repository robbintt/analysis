# TrueCity: Real and Simulated Urban Data for Cross-Domain 3D Scene Understanding

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2511.07007
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.07007
- Authors: Duc Nguyen; Yan-Ling Lai; Qilin Zhang; Prabin Gyawali; Benedikt Schwab; Olaf Wysocki; Thomas H. Kolbe
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Introduces benchmark with synchronized real and simulated point clouds for urban 3D semantic segmentation

## Executive Summary
TrueCity presents a novel benchmark dataset for cross-domain 3D urban scene understanding that combines synchronized real and simulated point cloud data from the same urban locations. The dataset aligns semantic segmentation classes with international city modeling standards and provides high-quality labeled data for training and evaluation. Experiments demonstrate that balanced mixtures of real and synthetic data (around 50-50 or 25-75 ratios) yield strong performance across multiple models, with transformer-based architectures showing particular benefit from this approach.

## Method Summary
The TrueCity dataset collects point cloud data from urban environments using both real LiDAR sensors and simulated environments, ensuring spatial and temporal synchronization between the two domains. The data undergoes careful preprocessing to align coordinate systems and sampling densities. Semantic labels follow international city modeling standards (CityGML), covering 13 urban object classes including building components (WallSurface, RoofSurface, Window, Door), transportation infrastructure (RoadSurface, RailSurface), vegetation (SolitaryVegetationObject), and water bodies (WaterSurface). The dataset includes geometric features such as intensity, range, and normal vectors, enabling comprehensive analysis of domain adaptation challenges in urban 3D understanding.

## Key Results
- Balanced real-synthetic data mixtures (50-50 or 25-75 ratios) achieve optimal performance across six representative models
- Transformer-based models benefit most from domain adaptation techniques when trained on mixed real-synthetic data
- WallSurface and RoadSurface classes transfer well from simulation, while Window and Door classes require real data due to material and occlusion complexity
- The dataset enables systematic analysis of synthetic-to-real domain shift in 3D urban scene understanding

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The effectiveness of TrueCity stems from its synchronized real-synthetic data collection approach, which ensures that both domains capture the same spatial relationships and urban structures. By maintaining consistent semantic class definitions across both domains, the dataset enables direct comparison of model performance and systematic study of domain adaptation strategies. The inclusion of geometric features and normal vectors provides rich information for distinguishing urban objects across domains, while the standardized CityGML class alignment ensures compatibility with existing urban modeling workflows.

## Foundational Learning

1. **Domain Adaptation in 3D Point Clouds**
   - Why needed: Real-world LiDAR data is expensive to collect and label, while synthetic data lacks real-world complexity
   - Quick check: Verify that model performance improves when mixing real and synthetic data compared to either domain alone

2. **CityGML Semantic Standards**
   - Why needed: Provides consistent classification scheme for urban objects across different data sources
   - Quick check: Confirm that semantic labels align with official CityGML specifications for urban modeling

3. **Point Cloud Geometric Features**
   - Why needed: Intensity, range, and normal vectors provide additional information beyond spatial coordinates
   - Quick check: Validate that geometric features improve classification accuracy compared to XYZ coordinates alone

4. **Transformer Architectures for 3D Data**
   - Why needed: Recent advances show transformers can effectively process irregular point cloud data
   - Quick check: Compare transformer-based model performance against traditional point cloud processing methods

5. **Urban Object Material Properties**
   - Why needed: Different materials (glass, concrete, vegetation) exhibit distinct reflection and absorption characteristics
   - Quick check: Analyze how material properties affect synthetic-to-real transfer for each semantic class

6. **Spatial-Temporal Synchronization**
   - Why needed: Ensures consistent capture of dynamic urban elements across real and synthetic domains
   - Quick check: Verify temporal alignment between corresponding real and synthetic point clouds

**Design Tradeoffs:**
- Real data provides authenticity but limited coverage and high cost
- Synthetic data offers unlimited samples but lacks real-world complexity
- Class balance affects model performance differently across semantic categories
- Transformer architectures require more computational resources but show better domain adaptation

**Failure Signatures:**
- Poor performance on classes with high material complexity (Window, Door)
- Degradation when training data is dominated by one domain
- Inconsistent results across different urban structures or environments
- Failure to capture fine geometric details in complex urban scenes

**Three First Experiments:**
1. Train a baseline model on real data only, then on synthetic data only, and compare performance to establish domain gap magnitude
2. Test various real-synthetic mixing ratios (100-0, 75-25, 50-50, 25-75, 0-100) to identify optimal balance
3. Apply domain adaptation techniques (e.g., adversarial training, feature normalization) to the mixed dataset and measure improvement

## Limitations

- Geographic scope limited to single urban location, constraining generalizability across diverse city architectures
- Semantic class alignment may not capture full complexity of real-world urban environments with mixed material properties
- Material and reflection characteristics in simulation may not fully represent real-world sensor interactions
