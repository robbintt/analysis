# SENDAI: A Hierarchical Sparse-measurement, EfficieNt Data AssImilation Framework

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2601.21664
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.21664
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Reconstructs full NDVI fields from 1.56% sensor coverage with 185% SSIM improvement over traditional methods

## Executive Summary
SENDAI presents a hierarchical framework for reconstructing high-resolution spatial fields from extremely sparse measurements. The system combines simulation-derived low-frequency dynamics with high-frequency corrections through sequential peeling and coordinate-based implicit neural representations. Tested across six globally diverse sites, SENDAI demonstrates significant improvements over established baselines while maintaining computational efficiency suitable for resource-constrained environments.

## Method Summary
The framework employs a hierarchical approach that first captures broad spatial patterns through simulation-based low-frequency dynamics, then applies sequential peeling to identify and correct high-frequency components. Coordinate-based implicit neural representations enable efficient encoding of spatial relationships. The system processes measurements hierarchically, first establishing baseline patterns before refining with localized corrections, enabling reconstruction from as few as 64 sensors across entire geographic regions.

## Key Results
- Achieves 185% SSIM improvement over traditional reconstruction methods
- Outperforms recent neural approaches by 36% in reconstruction quality
- Successfully preserves sharp boundaries and topological structures
- Maintains computational efficiency suitable for resource-constrained deployment

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The hierarchical frequency peeling enables interpretable decomposition of spatially heterogeneous corrections, allowing the system to separate broad spatial patterns from localized variations. By combining simulation-derived dynamics with adaptive neural corrections, SENDAI captures both predictable patterns and unexpected variations in sparse measurement scenarios.

## Architecture Onboarding
**Component Map**: Simulation Dynamics -> Sequential Peeling -> Coordinate-based INR -> Sparse Reconstruction
**Critical Path**: Low-frequency simulation output feeds into hierarchical correction pipeline, with coordinate-based representations enabling efficient high-frequency refinement
**Design Tradeoffs**: Hierarchical approach balances computational efficiency with reconstruction accuracy, but may struggle with rapid temporal changes not captured in simulation
**Failure Signatures**: Poor reconstruction at sharp boundaries when sensor density falls below critical threshold; temporal inconsistencies when simulation dynamics poorly match reality
**First Experiments**: 1) Test reconstruction accuracy across varying sensor densities (0.5% to 5%) 2) Evaluate boundary preservation in synthetic test cases with known discontinuities 3) Benchmark computational efficiency against operational data assimilation systems
