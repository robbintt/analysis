# Playing Atari Space Invaders with Sparse Cosine Optimized Policy Evolution
## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2508.08526
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.08526
- Reference count: 29
- SCOPE achieves state-of-the-art performance among derivative-free methods on Space Invaders with a peak score of 4,065

## Executive Summary
SCOPE introduces a novel approach to scaling evolutionary computation to high-dimensional visual inputs in Atari games. By applying the Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT) to compress input frames, it retains low-frequency, high-energy features while reducing dimensionality by 53%. This compressed representation is mapped to actions via a bilinear affine transformation, enabling efficient policy optimization with CMA-ES. The method demonstrates state-of-the-art performance among derivative-free approaches on Space Invaders while using only 875 parameters compared to hundreds of thousands in deep RL baselines.

## Method Summary
SCOPE addresses the challenge of scaling evolutionary computation to high-dimensional visual inputs in Atari games. The method applies Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT) to compress input frames, retaining low-frequency, high-energy features while reducing dimensionality by 53%. Sparsification then removes low-energy coefficients, further streamlining the input. This compressed representation is mapped to actions via a bilinear affine transformation, enabling efficient policy optimization with CMA-ES. The approach achieves state-of-the-art performance among derivative-free methods on Space Invaders, with a peak score of 4,065 and robust results under stochastic perturbations, while using only 875 parameters compared to hundreds of thousands in deep RL baselines.

## Key Results
- Peak score of 4,065 on Space Invaders, achieving state-of-the-art performance among derivative-free methods
- 53% dimensionality reduction through DCT compression and sparsification
- Only 875 parameters required compared to hundreds of thousands in deep RL baselines

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
SCOPE leverages DCT compression to reduce the dimensionality of visual inputs while preserving the most important features. The sparsification step removes low-energy coefficients, further streamlining the input representation. The bilinear affine transformation then efficiently maps this compressed representation to actions, making the policy optimization problem tractable for evolutionary algorithms. This combination allows SCOPE to achieve strong performance with minimal parameters, addressing the computational challenges of applying evolutionary methods to high-dimensional visual inputs.

## Architecture Onboarding
**Component Map**: Atari Frames -> DCT -> Sparsification -> Bilinear Mapping -> Actions
**Critical Path**: The sequence from DCT compression through sparsification to bilinear mapping is critical, as any bottleneck here directly impacts policy performance and parameter efficiency.
**Design Tradeoffs**: The method trades some representational capacity for parameter efficiency and computational tractability. The bilinear mapping is simpler than deep neural networks but may miss complex patterns that require deeper architectures.
**Failure Signatures**: Poor performance may indicate inadequate DCT coefficient retention, ineffective sparsification thresholds, or insufficient capacity in the bilinear mapping to capture task-relevant features.

## Limitations
- Validation was restricted to a single Atari game (Space Invaders), making generalization to other games unclear
- The 875 parameters and dimensionality reduction are impressive but lack direct comparison with deep RL baselines for parameter count or sample efficiency
- The specific nature and impact of stochastic perturbations on policy robustness could be better characterized

## Confidence
**Performance Claims (High confidence)**: The peak score of 4,065 and robust results under stochastic perturbations are well-supported by the reported experiments on Space Invaders.
**Generalization Claims (Medium confidence)**: While the method shows promise, the restriction to one game limits confidence in broader applicability across Atari or other visual RL domains.
**Efficiency Claims (Medium confidence)**: The 875 parameters and dimensionality reduction are compelling, but lack of comprehensive comparisons with deep RL architectures and ablation studies reduces confidence in the claimed advantages.
