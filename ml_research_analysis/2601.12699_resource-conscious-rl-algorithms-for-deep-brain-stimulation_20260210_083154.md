# Resource-Conscious RL Algorithms for Deep Brain Stimulation

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2601.12699
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.12699
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Novel T3P MAB algorithm reduces Parkinson's symptoms by 53% while consuming 216 μA/cm² power

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces a novel Time & Threshold-Triggered Pruned Multi-Armed Bandit (T3P MAB) algorithm for energy-efficient deep brain stimulation in Parkinson's Disease treatment. The algorithm adaptively tunes both frequency and amplitude of stimulation signals while minimizing power consumption. Tested on a computational brain model and implemented on ESP32 microcontrollers, the T3P MAB demonstrates superior performance in suppressing Parkinson's symptoms compared to existing deep-RL methods, achieving a 53% reduction in beta-band power spectral density while consuming significantly less power (216 μA/cm² vs 341 μA/cm²). The algorithm converges in under 2 minutes and requires no offline training phase, making it suitable for real-time, in-vivo deployment in resource-constrained implantable devices.

## Method Summary
The T3P MAB algorithm is designed for adaptive deep brain stimulation that optimizes both stimulation frequency and amplitude while minimizing power consumption. It employs a multi-armed bandit framework with pruning mechanisms that eliminate ineffective stimulation parameters based on time and threshold triggers. The algorithm operates without requiring an offline training phase, enabling real-time adaptation to patient-specific neural dynamics. Implementation was tested on a computational brain model simulating Parkinsonian neural activity and ported to ESP32 microcontroller units to demonstrate feasibility for implantable devices. The approach balances exploration of new parameter combinations with exploitation of effective stimulation settings through a reward function based on suppression of pathological beta-band oscillations.

## Key Results
- T3P MAB achieved 53% reduction in beta-band power spectral density biomarker compared to baseline
- Power consumption measured at 216 μA/cm², significantly lower than existing deep-RL methods (341 μA/cm²)
- Algorithm converges in under 2 minutes, demonstrating rapid adaptation capability
- No offline training phase required, enabling immediate deployment in clinical settings

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The T3P MAB algorithm succeeds by combining adaptive parameter tuning with energy-efficient decision-making. The multi-armed bandit framework allows the algorithm to explore different combinations of stimulation frequency and amplitude while quickly identifying the most effective parameters for symptom suppression. The pruning mechanism eliminates ineffective parameter combinations based on time thresholds and performance metrics, reducing computational overhead and power consumption. The time-triggered component ensures regular reassessment of stimulation parameters, while the threshold-triggered component responds to changes in patient condition. This dual-trigger approach enables both proactive optimization and reactive adaptation to fluctuating neural dynamics in Parkinson's Disease.

## Foundational Learning
- **Multi-Armed Bandit (MAB) framework**: Sequential decision-making under uncertainty, where each "arm" represents a stimulation parameter combination
  - Why needed: Enables exploration-exploitation tradeoff in parameter space without requiring prior knowledge
  - Quick check: Verify that reward function properly captures clinical efficacy metrics
- **Beta-band power spectral density**: Frequency range (13-30 Hz) that increases in Parkinson's Disease and correlates with motor symptoms
  - Why needed: Serves as objective biomarker for algorithm performance and symptom suppression
  - Quick check: Confirm frequency band definitions match clinical standards
- **Pruning mechanisms**: Elimination of ineffective parameter combinations based on performance thresholds
  - Why needed: Reduces computational complexity and power consumption in resource-constrained devices
  - Quick check: Ensure pruning criteria don't prematurely eliminate potentially effective parameters
- **Time-triggered vs threshold-triggered adaptation**: Two modes of parameter reassessment based on elapsed time or performance metrics
  - Why needed: Balances proactive optimization with reactive adaptation to patient condition changes
  - Quick check: Validate that both trigger mechanisms operate as intended under different conditions
- **ESP32 microcontroller implementation**: Demonstration of algorithm feasibility on resource-constrained hardware
  - Why needed: Proves potential for real-world implantable device deployment
  - Quick check: Verify power consumption measurements account for all operational components
