# TRELLIS-Enhanced Surface Features for Comprehensive Intracranial Aneurysm Analysis

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2509.03095
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.03095
- Authors: Clément Hervé; Paul Garnier; Jonathan Viquerat; Elie Hachem
- Reference count: 15
- Achieves 100% classification accuracy and significant improvements in segmentation and blood-flow prediction for intracranial aneurysm analysis

## Executive Summary
This study presents a novel approach for analyzing intracranial aneurysms using cross-domain feature transfer from TRELLIS, a generative model trained on large-scale non-medical 3D datasets. The method extracts geometric features from aneurysm and vessel meshes to enhance three critical tasks: classification of aneurysms versus healthy vessels, segmentation of aneurysm and vessel regions, and prediction of blood-flow fields using graph neural networks. Experiments on the Intra3D and AnXplore datasets demonstrate substantial performance improvements, including perfect classification accuracy and significant reductions in blood-flow simulation error.

## Method Summary
The approach leverages TRELLIS, a pre-trained generative model, to extract geometric features from 3D medical meshes representing intracranial aneurysms and blood vessels. These features are integrated into neural networks for three downstream tasks: aneurysm classification, segmentation of aneurysm regions, and blood-flow field prediction using graph neural networks. The cross-domain transfer allows the model to utilize rich geometric representations learned from large-scale non-medical datasets, compensating for the limited availability of annotated medical 3D data.

## Key Results
- Achieved 100% classification accuracy on Intra3D dataset, up from 86.69% baseline
- Significant improvements in segmentation metrics (IoU, DSC) for aneurysm and vessel regions
- 15% reduction in blood-flow simulation error compared to baseline approaches

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The approach works by leveraging pre-trained geometric features from TRELLIS, which has learned rich representations from large-scale non-medical 3D datasets. These general-purpose features capture essential geometric patterns that transfer effectively to medical applications, particularly for complex 3D structures like blood vessels and aneurysms. The cross-domain feature transfer addresses the fundamental challenge of limited annotated medical 3D data by providing a rich feature space that can be applied to various downstream tasks without requiring extensive medical-specific training.

  - Why needed: Vascular networks have inherent graph-like structures

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Medical 3D Mesh -> TRELLIS Feature Extractor -> Downstream Task Networks

**Critical Path**: Mesh preprocessing → TRELLIS feature extraction → Task-specific neural network processing → Output prediction

**Design Tradeoffs**: General vs. domain-specific features (cross-domain transfer provides broad coverage but may miss medical-specific nuances)

**Failure Signatures**: Poor performance on unusual aneurysm morphologies; overfitting to dataset-specific characteristics; feature extraction failures for low-quality meshes

## Limitations
- Evaluation based on relatively small medical datasets (Intra3D and AnXplore) limiting generalizability
- Perfect classification accuracy raises concerns about potential overfitting or dataset characteristics
- Does not investigate comparison with domain-specific features trained on medical data
- Blood-flow prediction still maintains non-trivial error rates that could impact clinical utility

## Confidence
- **High**: Demonstrated improvements in segmentation metrics and blood-flow simulation error reduction are robust and well-supported
- **Medium**: Classification performance claims, particularly perfect accuracy, require additional validation on larger, more diverse datasets
- **Low**: Long-term clinical impact and practical deployment feasibility have not been established

## Next Checks
1. Test the approach on larger, multi-center medical datasets with diverse patient populations and aneurysm morphologies
2. Compare TRELLIS-generated features against features from models specifically trained on medical 3D data
3. Conduct prospective clinical validation to assess real-world performance and clinical decision-making impact
