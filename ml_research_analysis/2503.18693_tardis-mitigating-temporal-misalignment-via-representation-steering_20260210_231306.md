# TARDIS: Mitigating Temporal Misalignment via Representation Steering

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2503.18693
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.18693
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Unsupervised method improves accuracy up to 19.2% without fine-tuning by steering model representations at inference time

## Executive Summary
TARDIS addresses temporal misalignment in language models by steering representations at inference time. The method extracts steering vectors from unlabeled data by computing mean differences in representations between training and target time periods. These vectors are then applied to align model outputs with the target period's distribution, enabling the model to adapt to temporal shifts without fine-tuning.

The approach demonstrates effectiveness across three classification datasets, showing improvements up to 19.2% in accuracy. TARDIS can interpolate and extrapolate steering vectors for unseen time periods and adapts dynamically even when target time periods are unknown. The method is efficient, requiring only a small subset of data for effective steering.

## Method Summary
TARDIS operates by extracting steering vectors from unlabeled data through computing the mean difference in representations between training and target time periods. These steering vectors are then applied during inference to align model outputs with the target period's distribution. The method works unsupervised, requiring no labeled data from the target period, and can handle both interpolation and extrapolation of temporal shifts through vector manipulation.

## Key Results
- Improves accuracy by up to 19.2% without fine-tuning across three classification datasets
- Enables interpolation and extrapolation of steering vectors for unseen time periods
- Maintains effectiveness even when target time periods are unknown through dynamic adaptation
- Requires only a small subset of unlabeled data for effective steering

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
TARDIS works by identifying and correcting the representation shift that occurs when language models trained on data from one time period are applied to data from another period. The method assumes that temporal misalignment manifests as a consistent shift in the model's representation space, which can be captured by computing the mean difference between representations of training and target period data. By applying these learned steering vectors at inference time, the model's outputs are effectively realigned to match the target period's distribution without requiring costly fine-tuning.

  - Why needed: Steering vectors must be applied correctly to achieve temporal alignment
  - Quick check: Confirm steering vectors effectively shift representations toward target distribution

- Temporal interpolation/extrapolation: Understanding how to extend steering vectors beyond observed time periods
  - Why needed: Real-world applications often require handling unseen time periods
  - Quick check: Validate steering vectors work for intermediate and future time periods

## Architecture Onboarding

Component Map:
Input data -> Representation extractor -> Steering vector computation -> Vector application module -> Aligned outputs

Critical Path:
The critical path involves extracting representations from both training and target period data, computing the mean difference to create steering vectors, and applying these vectors during inference to align model outputs with the target period distribution.

Design Tradeoffs:
- Memory vs. accuracy tradeoff in storing representations for steering vector computation
- Computational overhead of steering vector application at inference time
- Balance between steering vector magnitude and model stability

Failure Signatures:
- Ineffective steering when temporal shifts are non-linear or context-dependent
- Performance degradation when temporal gap between periods is too large
- Limited effectiveness when abrupt distribution changes occur

First Experiments:
1. Verify steering vector computation correctly captures mean representation differences between periods
2. Test steering vector application on a held-out subset from target period
3. Validate interpolation capabilities by creating intermediate time period representations

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out
The paper does not explicitly call out specific open questions, but the limitations section suggests areas for future investigation regarding the method's robustness to complex temporal dynamics and non-linear shifts.

## Limitations
- Assumes temporal misalignment manifests as consistent shift in representation space, which may not hold for non-linear or context-dependent changes
- Requires access to unlabeled data from both training and target time periods, which may not always be available
- Effectiveness may degrade with large temporal gaps or abrupt distribution changes

## Confidence
- Accuracy improvement up to 19.2%: Medium
- Interpolation and extrapolation of steering vectors: Medium
- Dynamic adaptation without known target periods: Low
