# AI as Entertainment

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2601.08768
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.08768
- Reference count: 40
- Key outcome: Generative AI is increasingly used for entertainment rather than productivity, requiring new evaluation frameworks that consider cultural impact and meaning-making

## Executive Summary
This paper argues that generative AI systems are increasingly being used for entertainment rather than productivity, a use case for which the AI field is unprepared to evaluate. While AI evaluations rigorously measure intelligence and cultural harms, they lack frameworks for assessing the benefits of cultural outputs. The authors propose "thick entertainment" as a framework for evaluating AI-generated cultural content, drawing on insights from the humanities to consider entertainment's role in meaning-making, identity formation, and social connection. They contend that entertainment will become a primary business model for major AI corporations seeking returns on massive infrastructure investments, and that this will significantly influence the technology these companies produce.

## Method Summary
The paper presents a conceptual framework rather than an empirical study. It synthesizes insights from the humanities, particularly cultural studies and media theory, to propose a new approach for evaluating AI-generated entertainment. The authors draw on interdisciplinary perspectives to develop the "thick entertainment" framework, which emphasizes the complex social and cultural dimensions of entertainment beyond simple enjoyment or engagement metrics. The paper primarily relies on theoretical argumentation and literature review rather than experimental validation.

## Key Results
- Current AI evaluation frameworks are inadequate for assessing entertainment outputs, as they focus on intelligence and productivity metrics
- Entertainment will likely become a primary business model for major AI corporations due to massive infrastructure costs
- The "thick entertainment" framework proposes evaluating AI-generated content based on meaning-making, identity formation, and social connection

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The paper argues that entertainment functions as a fundamental way humans create meaning, form identities, and build social connections. This "thick" conception of entertainment goes beyond simple pleasure or distraction to encompass how cultural content shapes our understanding of ourselves and our relationships with others. The mechanism works because AI systems are increasingly capable of generating content that fulfills these deeper social and psychological needs, making them viable substitutes for traditional entertainment sources. The business model works because entertainment represents a recurring revenue stream that can justify the massive infrastructure investments required for large-scale AI systems.

## Limitations
- The claim that entertainment will become the primary business model for AI corporations is speculative and depends on complex market dynamics
- The "thick entertainment" framework lacks empirical validation and specific evaluation criteria
- No concrete evidence is provided that current AI evaluation frameworks are inadequate for entertainment use cases

## Confidence
- AI evaluations are unprepared for entertainment benefits: Medium confidence
- Entertainment as primary AI business model: Low confidence
- "Thick entertainment" framework practical applicability: Low confidence
