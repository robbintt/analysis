# LACE: Exploring Turn-Taking and Parallel Interaction Modes in Human-AI Co-Creation for Iterative Image Generation

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2504.14827
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.14827
- Reference count: 22
- Key outcome: LACE enables professional artists to use generative AI through controlled prompting and iterative refinement with both turn-taking and parallel interaction modes, showing significant improvements in ownership, usability, and artistic perception compared to standard AI workflows

## Executive Summary
LACE introduces a human-AI co-creative system integrated into Adobe Photoshop that addresses key limitations in existing generative AI workflows for professional artists. The system supports two distinct interaction modes - turn-taking for structured collaboration and parallel for simultaneous refinement - enabling artists to maintain creative control while leveraging AI capabilities. Through a pilot study with 21 participants across three creative tasks, LACE demonstrated significant improvements in user satisfaction metrics compared to traditional AI workflows, particularly in ownership (p=0.002), usability (p=0.005), and artistic perception (p=0.001).

The research identifies that professional artists require different interaction modes at different stages of the creative process, with turn-taking preferred for initial ideation and parallel mode for refinement. LACE's integration into existing Photoshop workflows provides practical value while maintaining the precision and iterative coherence that artists need. The system's success highlights the importance of flexible human-AI interaction paradigms in professional creative tools.

## Method Summary
The research team developed LACE as an integrated system within Adobe Photoshop, implementing both turn-taking and parallel interaction modes for generative image creation. The system was evaluated through a pilot study involving 21 professional artists who completed three different creative tasks using LACE compared to standard AI workflows. Participants were asked to assess their experience across multiple dimensions including ownership, usability, and artistic perception. The study employed both quantitative measures (statistical analysis of satisfaction scores) and qualitative feedback to understand user preferences and workflow integration.

## Key Results
- LACE achieved significantly higher ownership scores (p=0.002, r=0.64) compared to turn-taking-only workflows
- Usability improvements were significant (p=0.005, r=0.56) with LACE's dual interaction modes
- Artistic perception scores showed strong improvement (p=0.001, r=0.71) with LACE integration
- Participants demonstrated clear preference for turn-taking during initial ideation and parallel mode during refinement phases

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
LACE succeeds by providing professional artists with controlled interaction mechanisms that preserve creative intent while leveraging AI capabilities. The dual-mode approach addresses the fundamental tension between AI's generative power and artists' need for precision and iterative control. Turn-taking mode enables structured dialogue with the AI, allowing artists to guide the creative process through deliberate prompts and refinements. Parallel mode permits simultaneous exploration and modification, supporting the rapid iteration cycles typical in professional workflows.

The system's integration into Photoshop provides familiarity and workflow continuity, reducing the cognitive load associated with adopting new tools. By maintaining compatibility with existing professional workflows while adding AI capabilities, LACE lowers the barrier to adoption and preserves the artist's sense of ownership over the creative output.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map:** Photoshop interface -> LACE interaction manager -> AI generation engine -> Image refinement pipeline -> User feedback loop

**Critical Path:** User action -> Interaction mode selection -> Prompt generation/parallel processing -> AI generation -> Image output -> User evaluation -> Next iteration

**Design Tradeoffs:** The system prioritizes workflow integration over standalone functionality, trading some AI optimization capabilities for Photoshop compatibility. This ensures practical adoption but may limit some advanced AI features.

**Failure Signatures:** 
- Loss of creative control when AI dominates the generation process
- Inconsistency in iterative refinements across multiple generations
- User confusion when switching between interaction modes
- Performance degradation during high-complexity parallel operations

**First Experiments:**
1. Test basic turn-taking interaction with simple prompt-response cycles
2. Validate parallel mode performance with simultaneous refinement operations
3. Measure workflow integration effectiveness through task completion times

## Limitations
- Small sample size of 21 participants limits generalizability to broader professional artist population
- Evaluation focuses on specific creative tasks rather than capturing full diversity of professional workflows
- Subjective measures of ownership, usability, and artistic perception may be influenced by novelty effects
- Photoshop integration may confound results by introducing platform-specific workflow factors
