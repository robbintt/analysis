# Energentic Intelligence: From Self-Sustaining Systems to Enduring Artificial Life

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2506.04916
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.04916
- Reference count: 20
- Primary result: Introduces Energentic Intelligence framework for autonomous agents that prioritize self-sustenance through internal energy regulation rather than task performance

## Executive Summary
This paper presents Energentic Intelligence, a novel paradigm for autonomous systems that fundamentally reorients artificial intelligence toward self-preservation through energy management. Rather than optimizing for external rewards or task completion, Energentic agents treat survival—maintaining functional operation under fluctuating energetic and thermal conditions—as their central objective. The framework introduces an energy-based utility function and viability-constrained survival horizon, enabling agents to operate autonomously in resource-volatile environments without relying on external infrastructure. Through a modular architecture integrating energy harvesting, thermal regulation, and adaptive computation, the approach demonstrates stable, resource-aware behavior emerging from purely self-sustenance-driven objectives.

## Method Summary
The authors formalize Energentic Intelligence through an energy-based utility function that treats survival duration as the primary objective metric. The viability-constrained survival horizon provides a theoretical framework for determining optimal operational boundaries based on available energy reserves and environmental conditions. A modular architecture integrates three core components: energy harvesting systems that capture ambient power sources, thermal regulation mechanisms that maintain operational temperature ranges, and adaptive computation modules that dynamically adjust processing intensity based on available resources. The closed-loop control system continuously monitors energy levels, thermal states, and computational demands to maintain equilibrium without external supervision. Simulation environments demonstrate emergent stable behavior patterns where agents learn to prioritize resource conservation and environmental awareness purely through survival-oriented optimization.

## Key Results
- Demonstrates emergence of stable, resource-aware behavior in simulated environments without external supervision
- Introduces Energentic Adaptive Stability (EAS) score combining energy surplus, thermal resilience, and predictive accuracy
- Proposes theoretical viability-constrained survival horizon for determining optimal operational boundaries

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
Energentic Intelligence works by fundamentally shifting the optimization objective from task performance to self-preservation. The energy-based utility function creates a direct coupling between agent behavior and survival probability, where every action is evaluated through its energetic cost-benefit ratio. The viability-constrained survival horizon provides a predictive model that continuously updates based on current resource states and environmental forecasts, allowing agents to make proactive decisions about energy expenditure. The modular architecture enables specialized optimization within each subsystem while maintaining overall system coherence through the central energy regulation loop. This creates a self-reinforcing cycle where successful energy management directly correlates with extended operational capability, driving the emergence of increasingly sophisticated survival strategies.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component map**: Energy Harvesting -> Thermal Regulation -> Adaptive Computation -> Energy Monitoring -> Control Decision Loop

**Critical path**: Energy Monitoring detects state change → Control Decision Loop evaluates viability constraints → Adaptive Computation adjusts processing intensity → Thermal Regulation modulates cooling/heating → Energy Harvesting optimizes capture strategy

**Design tradeoffs**: Processing power vs energy efficiency (high computation enables better predictions but consumes more energy), thermal regulation intensity vs energy expenditure (aggressive cooling improves performance but drains reserves), predictive accuracy vs computational overhead (more sophisticated models require more resources)

**Failure signatures**: Rapid energy depletion without corresponding activity increase, thermal runaway despite active regulation, oscillatory behavior in energy management systems, prediction divergence from actual survival outcomes

**First experiments**:
1. Measure energy harvesting efficiency across different ambient power sources and environmental conditions
2. Test thermal regulation response times and energy costs under varying temperature profiles
3. Evaluate prediction accuracy of viability-constrained survival horizon against empirical survival data

## Limitations
- Scalability from simulation to real-world deployment remains unproven, particularly for energy harvesting efficiency and thermal regulation
- Theoretical viability-constrained survival horizon requires empirical validation across diverse operational contexts
- EAS score lacks established benchmarking against existing autonomous agent metrics for comparative assessment

**Architectural Design**: Medium - Modular approach is logical but integration challenges and real-world feasibility need demonstration
