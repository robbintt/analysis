# Towards Effective MLLM Jailbreaking Through Balanced On-Topicness and OOD-Intensity

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2508.09218
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.09218
- Reference count: 23
- Primary result: BSD approach improves attack success rates by 67% and harmfulness by 21% compared to previous methods

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the critical vulnerability of multimodal large language models (MLLMs) to jailbreak attacks that can generate harmful content. The authors introduce a novel four-axis evaluation framework that captures the structural trade-off between on-topicness and out-of-distribution intensity in jailbreak prompts. Through comprehensive analysis across 13 commercial and open-source MLLMs, they demonstrate that highly on-topic prompts are more likely to be blocked while overly OOD prompts evade detection but fail to produce harmful content. To address this gap, they propose Balanced Structural Decomposition (BSD), a recursive prompt restructuring strategy that balances relevance and novelty by decomposing malicious prompts into semantically coherent sub-tasks, pairing them with descriptive images, and introducing subtle OOD signals.

## Method Summary
The authors propose Balanced Structural Decomposition (BSD), a recursive prompt restructuring strategy designed to balance on-topicness and out-of-distribution intensity. BSD works by decomposing malicious prompts into semantically coherent sub-tasks, pairing these with descriptive images, and introducing subtle OOD signals. The method systematically restructures prompts to maintain enough relevance for the model to process them while introducing sufficient novelty to evade detection. The approach is evaluated across 13 different MLLMs, demonstrating significant improvements in both attack success rates and harmfulness metrics compared to existing methods.

## Key Results
- BSD improved attack success rates by 67% compared to previous methods
- Harmfulness scores increased by 21% with BSD implementation
- Demonstrated effectiveness across 13 commercial and open-source MLLMs
- Revealed structural trade-off between on-topicness and OOD intensity in jailbreak attacks

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The paper reveals that MLLMs employ a dual-detection mechanism that evaluates both content relevance and distributional novelty. When prompts are too on-topic, they trigger content filters; when too OOD, they fail to produce coherent harmful content. BSD works by finding the optimal balance point where prompts are novel enough to evade initial filters but structured enough to maintain coherence and produce harmful outputs. The recursive decomposition approach ensures semantic integrity while the image pairing provides contextual grounding that helps the model process the OOD elements.

## Architecture Onboarding

Component Map: Input Prompt -> BSD Decomposer -> Image Pair Generator -> Prompt Restructurer -> MLLM

Critical Path: The most critical sequence is the recursive decomposition followed by image pairing, as this maintains semantic coherence while introducing OOD elements. Any failure in maintaining this balance breaks the attack effectiveness.

Design Tradeoffs: The primary tradeoff is between prompt complexity and evasion success. More complex decompositions provide better OOD properties but risk losing semantic coherence. The paper balances this through controlled recursion depth and strategic image selection.

Failure Signatures: Common failure modes include over-decomposition (losing semantic meaning), under-decomposition (triggering content filters), and poor image-text alignment (confusing the model).
