# Physio-DPO: Aligning Large Language Models with the Protein Energy Landscape to Eliminate Structural Hallucinations

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2601.00647
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.00647
- Authors: QiWei Meng
- Reference count: 13
- Primary result: Physio-DPO achieves 92.8% foldability, 1.28 Å self-consistency RMSD, and lowest average Rosetta energy (-3.05 REU) while mitigating structural hallucinations

## Executive Summary
Physio-DPO introduces a physics-informed alignment framework for protein language models that extends Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) by incorporating thermodynamic energy magnitudes. The method addresses structural hallucinations—where models generate sequences with high linguistic likelihood but low thermodynamic stability—through a magnitude-aware objective that scales optimization updates according to energy gaps between native structures and hard negatives. Experimental results demonstrate that Physio-DPO outperforms standard baselines including SFT, PPO, and standard DPO across foldability, structural consistency, and energy minimization metrics.

## Method Summary
Physio-DPO extends Direct Preference Optimization by incorporating thermodynamic energy magnitudes into the preference optimization framework. The method introduces a physics-grounded preference dataset (PhysioPref-1M) constructed from protein structures in PDB and AFDB databases, filtered based on energy-based criteria to identify hard negative samples. A magnitude-aware weighting function scales optimization updates according to continuous energy differences between native and non-native conformations. The framework preserves linguistic diversity while improving structural stability through preference learning that aligns linguistic likelihood with thermodynamic stability.

## Key Results
- Achieves 92.8% foldability rate on benchmark protein sequences
- Maintains 1.28 Å self-consistency RMSD in structural predictions
- Achieves lowest average Rosetta energy (-3.05 REU) among tested methods
- Effectively mitigates structural hallucinations by recovering biophysical interactions

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework works by aligning the linguistic likelihood space of protein language models with the thermodynamic energy landscape of protein structures. By incorporating magnitude-aware weighting based on energy differences between native and non-native conformations, Physio-DPO scales optimization updates to prioritize corrections for sequences that are both linguistically plausible but thermodynamically unstable. This approach bridges the gap between sequence generation quality and structural validity, ensuring that high-likelihood sequences also correspond to stable, foldable protein structures.

- Critical path: Sequence generation → Energy landscape evaluation → Preference ranking → Magnitude-aware optimization → Stability improvement
- Design tradeoffs: The physics-grounded preference dataset construction balances computational efficiency against conformational diversity coverage. Energy-based filtering may exclude rare but valid structural motifs.
- Failure signatures: Structural hallucinations manifest as high linguistic likelihood paired with poor thermodynamic stability. Model may struggle with novel sequence design when template structures are unavailable.

## Limitations
- PhysioPref-1M dataset construction relies on single-source protein structure databases with potentially limited conformational diversity
- Energy-based filtering criteria may inadvertently exclude valid but rare structural motifs
- Alignment between linguistic likelihood and thermodynamic stability remains heuristic rather than theoretically derived
- Evaluation methodology for "structural hallucinations" lacks standardization across the protein design field

## Confidence
- High confidence in foldability and RMSD improvements: Measured using established structural biology tools (Rosetta) with clear benchmarks
- Medium confidence in hallucination mitigation claims: Definition and quantification of structural hallucinations lacks standardization
- Medium confidence in linguistic diversity preservation: Requires more detailed analysis of sequence entropy and KL divergence metrics

## Next Checks
1. Independent replication using alternative protein structure databases (AlphaFold DB, ESM-IF1 structures) to verify PhysioPref-1M construction methodology and Physio-DPO performance consistency across data sources.
2. Ablation study removing the magnitude-aware weighting function while keeping all other components fixed, to isolate the contribution of physics-informed scaling to observed performance improvements.
3. Extended evaluation on de novo protein design tasks with no structural templates, testing whether Physio-DPO's advantages transfer to truly novel sequence generation beyond structure-based sampling scenarios.
