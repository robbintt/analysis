# Generative Adversarial Evasion and Out-of-Distribution Detection for UAV Cyber-Attacks

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2506.21142
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.21142
- Authors: Deepak Kumar Panda; Weisi Guo
- Reference count: 16
- Primary result: GAN-based framework generates stealthy adversarial attacks that evade UAV IDS, detected via CVAE-NLL with AUC ~0.99

## Executive Summary
This paper proposes a conditional GAN-based framework to generate stealthy adversarial attacks that evade intrusion detection systems in UAV cyber-physical systems. The framework perturbs attack features to produce adversarial samples that are misclassified as benign by a multi-class IDS while maintaining statistical similarity to out-of-distribution (OOD) samples. To detect such attacks, a conditional VAE is employed, using negative log-likelihood (NLL) as a metric to distinguish adversarial inputs from genuine OOD samples. Comparative evaluation shows that NLL-based detection significantly outperforms traditional Mahalanobis distance-based detectors in identifying stealthy adversarial threats, with an area under the curve (AUC) score close to 0.99.

## Method Summary
The paper presents a conditional GAN-based framework for generating stealthy adversarial attacks to evade UAV intrusion detection systems, combined with CVAE-based detection using NLL metrics. The approach involves training a multi-class IDS classifier on UAV cyber-attack data, then using a conditional GAN to generate adversarial samples that mimic benign or OOD distributions while evading detection. The generated adversarial samples are then distinguished from genuine OOD samples using a CVAE trained to model the data distribution, with NLL serving as the detection metric. The framework iteratively refines attack generation through PGD-style optimization with specific loss weighting to balance stealth and evasion objectives.

## Key Results
- Proposed NLL-based detection significantly outperforms traditional Mahalanobis distance-based detectors
- Achieved AUC score close to 0.99 for distinguishing adversarial samples from OOD samples
- Demonstrated effective evasion of multi-class IDS with adversarial samples misclassified as benign
- Maintained statistical similarity between adversarial and OOD samples while achieving high attack success

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework exploits the inherent similarity between adversarial attacks and OOD samples, using conditional GANs to generate samples that statistically resemble benign traffic while evading IDS classification. The CVAE-based detection leverages probabilistic modeling to capture the underlying data distribution, allowing NLL to effectively distinguish between adversarial samples (which have different generation mechanisms) and genuine OOD samples. The iterative refinement process with carefully balanced loss functions ensures that generated samples achieve both high attack success rates and statistical similarity to target distributions.

- **PGD-style iterative refinement**: Essential for generating high-quality adversarial samples; quick check: ensure attack success rate increases with iteration count
- **Wasserstein distance**: Used for measuring statistical similarity between distributions; quick check: verify distance decreases during optimization
- **Multi-class IDS architecture**: Baseline classifier for attack generation; quick check: confirm baseline accuracy exceeds 90% on test set
- **Negative Log-Likelihood**: Detection metric distinguishing adversarial from OOD samples; quick check: ensure adversarial NLL scores are significantly higher than OOD scores
