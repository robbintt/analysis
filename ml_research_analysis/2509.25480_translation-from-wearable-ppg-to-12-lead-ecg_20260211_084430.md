# Translation from Wearable PPG to 12-Lead ECG
## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2509.25480
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.25480
- **Reference count**: 40
- **Primary result**: P2Es achieves 16.3% MSE reduction and 87.5% specificity in MI detection using demographic-aware diffusion from PPG to 12-lead ECG

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces P2Es, a diffusion-based framework that translates wearable PPG signals into clinically valid 12-lead ECG outputs. The method uses demographic-aware affinity matrices, dual-domain noise injection, and cascaded dilated convolutions to preserve both spectral coherence and temporal morphology. Evaluated on MIMIC-III and MIMIC-IV, P2Es outperforms existing single-lead baselines and two-stage approaches in MSE, DTW, and clinical detection tasks. The approach is designed for lightweight real-time deployment on mobile devices.

## Method Summary
P2Es employs a hybrid KNN-contrastive learning module to create demographic-aware affinity matrices, capturing spatial dependencies across ECG leads. It uses frequency-temporal diffusion with dual-domain noise injection to preserve spectral coherence and temporal morphology. A temporal multi-scale generation module with cascaded dilated convolutions hierarchically recovers fine-grained features. The model is lightweight and designed for real-time mobile inference.

## Key Results
- Achieves 16.3% reduction in MSE (0.0902 vs. 0.1078) compared to single-lead PPG-to-ECG baselines
- Reduces DTW by 26.4% (0.0583 vs. 0.0792)
- Attains 87.5% specificity in myocardial infarction detection and 83.5% precision in arrhythmia classification on MIMIC-IV

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The demographic-aware affinity matrix ensures that spatial correlations among ECG leads are preserved during translation, preventing lead misalignment. Dual-domain diffusion (frequency and temporal) maintains both the spectral coherence essential for clinical interpretation and the fine-grained temporal morphology required for accurate diagnosis. Cascaded dilated convolutions in the temporal multi-scale module capture multi-resolution patterns without losing long-range dependencies, enabling accurate reconstruction of complex cardiac events.

## Foundational Learning
- **Demographic-aware affinity matrix**: Captures inter-lead spatial dependencies based on patient demographics. *Why needed*: Ensures ECG lead consistency across translations. *Quick check*: Verify demographic alignment across train/validation/test splits.
- **Dual-domain noise injection**: Adds noise separately in frequency and temporal domains. *Why needed*: Preserves both spectral coherence and temporal morphology. *Quick check*: Inspect frequency spectra and temporal waveforms post-generation.
- **Cascaded dilated convolutions**: Expands receptive fields at multiple scales. *Why needed*: Captures multi-resolution temporal features without losing long-range context. *Quick check*: Visualize receptive field coverage for different dilation rates.
- **Contrastive learning for affinity**: Learns embeddings that group similar demographics. *Why needed*: Ensures stable generation across demographic groups. *Quick check*: Measure clustering quality of learned embeddings.
- **Diffusion model architecture**: Gradually denoises latent representation to generate clean output. *Why needed*: Enables high-fidelity signal generation from noisy latent space. *Quick check*: Track SNR improvement across denoising steps.
- **Single-to-multi-lead translation**: Converts single-lead PPG to 12-lead ECG. *Why needed*: Expands diagnostic capability of wearable devices. *Quick check*: Compare generated 12-lead outputs against ground truth 12-lead ECGs.

## Architecture Onboarding
- **Component map**: PPG input -> KNN-contrastive affinity module -> Dual-domain diffusion -> Temporal multi-scale generation -> 12-lead ECG output
- **Critical path**: PPG → Affinity matrix → Diffusion → Cascaded dilated convolutions → ECG output
- **Design tradeoffs**: Lightweight diffusion model for mobile deployment vs. full-scale diffusion for maximum fidelity; demographic-aware affinity vs. static grouping
- **Failure signatures**: Degraded lead alignment, loss of high-frequency QRS components, temporal misalignment in arrhythmia detection
- **3 first experiments**: (1) Ablation study removing KNN-contrastive module to measure impact on MSE; (2) Dual-domain vs. single-domain diffusion comparison; (3) Cascaded dilated convolutions vs. standard convolutions on temporal coherence

## Limitations
- Single-center, retrospective datasets (MIMIC-III/IV) limit real-world generalizability
- Demographic groupings assumed static despite evolving age and comorbidities
- Diffusion model computational lightness not benchmarked against deployment constraints
- Label noise risk from automated label inference rather than adjudicated ground truth

## Confidence
- Technical innovation (hybrid KNN-contrastive, dual-domain diffusion): Medium
- MSE/DTW improvements over baselines: High
- Clinical utility (MI and arrhythmia detection): Medium
