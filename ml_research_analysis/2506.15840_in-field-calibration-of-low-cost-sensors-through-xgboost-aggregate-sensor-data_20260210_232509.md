# In-field Calibration of Low-Cost Sensors through XGBoost $\&$ Aggregate Sensor Data

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2506.15840
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.15840

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the critical challenge of calibrating low-cost air quality sensors, which suffer from drift and inaccuracies due to environmental sensitivity and manufacturing variability. The authors propose a spatial calibration method using XGBoost ensemble learning that consolidates data from neighboring sensor deployments. By leveraging location data, temperature, and humidity readings from multiple sensors, the model predicts calibration adjustments across the network without relying on presumed accuracy of individual sensors. Tested on the SenEURCity dataset across three European cities, the approach enables scalable, accurate, and efficient calibration of distributed low-cost sensor networks for urban air quality monitoring.

## Method Summary
The proposed spatial calibration method employs XGBoost ensemble learning to consolidate data from neighboring sensor deployments. The model uses location data, temperature, and humidity readings from multiple sensors to predict calibration adjustments across the network. This approach reduces reliance on individual sensor accuracy by leveraging the collective information from the sensor network. The model was trained on the SenEURCity dataset covering three European cities and demonstrated effectiveness in both training location (Antwerp) and new deployment areas with minimal fine-tuning requirements.

## Key Results
- Achieved RMSE of 5.248 on held-out test data in the training location (Antwerp)
- Demonstrated ability to generalize to new sensor deployments within trained area
- Required minimal fine-tuning for new locations, with post-fine-tuning RMSE values of 6.41-6.52

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The approach works by treating sensor calibration as a spatial learning problem rather than an individual sensor characterization task. By aggregating data from multiple sensors and incorporating spatial relationships, the model can identify and correct systematic biases that affect groups of sensors similarly. The XGBoost ensemble learning framework is particularly effective at capturing non-linear relationships between environmental conditions and sensor drift patterns, while the spatial component allows the model to leverage correlations between nearby sensors experiencing similar environmental conditions.
