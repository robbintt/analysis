# Aetheria: A multimodal interpretable content safety framework based on multi-agent debate and collaboration

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2512.02530
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.02530
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Aetheria achieves F1 score of 0.84 on multimodal content safety tasks vs 0.75 for best baseline

## Executive Summary
Aetheria introduces a novel multimodal interpretable content safety framework that employs five specialized agents working through a dynamic debate mechanism. The system uses Preprocessor, Supporter, Strict Debater, Loose Debater, and Arbiter agents to conduct in-depth analysis of multimodal content, addressing limitations of existing systems by providing transparent decision-making and identifying implicit risks through adversarial reasoning. The framework is grounded in RAG-based knowledge retrieval and validated on the newly proposed AIR-Bench dataset, demonstrating superior performance compared to baseline approaches.

## Method Summary
Aetheria implements a multi-agent system where five specialized agents collaborate to analyze multimodal content for safety concerns. The Preprocessor handles initial data preparation, the Supporter provides contextual information, and two debaters (Strict and Loose) represent opposing viewpoints on risk assessment. The Arbiter agent synthesizes these inputs to reach final decisions. The system employs a dynamic debate mechanism that leverages RAG-based knowledge retrieval to ground discussions in relevant information. A meta-learning loop enables continuous improvement through case library updates, allowing the framework to adapt to evolving content safety challenges.

## Key Results
- Achieves F1 score of 0.84 on multimodal content safety tasks, outperforming best baseline (0.75)
- Maintains high precision (0.83) and recall (0.85) across safety classification tasks
- Demonstrates effective identification of implicit risks through adversarial reasoning between risk-averse and context-aware agents

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework's effectiveness stems from its adversarial reasoning approach, where Strict Debater agents focus on risk aversion while Loose Debater agents emphasize contextual understanding. This creates a balanced evaluation that captures both explicit violations and subtle, implicit risks. The RAG-based knowledge retrieval grounds debates in relevant context, while the Arbiter synthesizes diverse perspectives into coherent decisions. The meta-learning loop enables continuous adaptation to emerging safety challenges.

## Architecture Onboarding

Component map: Preprocessor -> Supporter -> [Strict Debater, Loose Debater] -> Arbiter -> Output

Critical path: Content input flows through Preprocessor for initial processing, then to Supporter for context gathering. The processed content enters debate between Strict and Loose Debaters, with their arguments synthesized by the Arbiter for final safety determination.

Design tradeoffs: The five-agent architecture provides interpretability and balanced risk assessment but introduces complexity and potential latency. The adversarial debate mechanism captures nuanced risks but requires careful calibration to avoid excessive conservatism or permissiveness.

Failure signatures: Overly conservative outputs suggest Strict Debater dominance; overly permissive outputs indicate Loose Debater bias. Poor contextual understanding manifests as irrelevant debate points or missed implicit risks.
