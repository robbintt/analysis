# PRISM: A Framework for Producing Interpretable Political Bias Embeddings with Political-Aware Cross-Encoder

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2505.24646
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.24646
- Reference count: 29
- Primary result: State-of-the-art political bias classification (86.1% accuracy on NewsSpectrum) with interpretable embeddings

## Executive Summary
PRISM introduces a novel framework for producing interpretable political bias embeddings that explicitly capture ideological orientations beyond general semantic meaning. The framework operates in two stages: (1) Controversial Topic Bias Indicator Mining, which automatically extracts fine-grained political topics and their corresponding bias indicators from weakly labeled news data, and (2) Cross-Encoder Political Bias Embedding, which generates structured embeddings aligned with these interpretable bias dimensions. Through extensive experiments on NewsSpectrum and BigNews datasets, PRISM achieves state-of-the-art performance on political bias classification, demonstrating superior generalizability on out-of-domain data like BASIL.

## Method Summary
PRISM's two-stage approach begins with controversial topic bias indicator mining, where fine-grained political topics and their corresponding bias indicators are automatically extracted from weakly labeled news data. The second stage employs a cross-encoder political bias embedding mechanism that generates structured embeddings aligned with these interpretable bias dimensions. This architecture enables the framework to capture not just general semantic meaning but explicit ideological orientations, making the embeddings more interpretable and actionable for bias-aware applications.

## Key Results
- Achieves 86.1% accuracy on NewsSpectrum political bias classification, outperforming POLITICS (51.3%)
- Demonstrates superior generalizability on out-of-domain data like BASIL dataset
- Excels in politically diversified retrieval tasks, consistently outperforming baselines in balancing content relevance with ideological diversity

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
PRISM works by explicitly separating the representation of ideological content from general semantic meaning through its two-stage architecture. The first stage identifies specific political topics and their associated bias indicators, creating a structured vocabulary of ideological dimensions. The cross-encoder in the second stage then learns to map input text into a space where these dimensions are explicitly represented, allowing for both accurate classification and interpretable results. This separation enables the model to capture nuanced political orientations that general semantic embeddings miss.

## Architecture Onboarding

Component map: News data -> Topic Mining -> Bias Indicator Extraction -> Cross-Encoder -> Interpretable Embeddings

Critical path: The topic mining stage must successfully extract meaningful bias indicators, as these directly inform the cross-encoder's learning objective. Poor topic quality propagates through to degraded embedding interpretability.

Design tradeoffs: The framework trades some classification accuracy for interpretability by explicitly modeling bias dimensions rather than learning them implicitly. This makes embeddings more transparent but potentially less nuanced in capturing complex ideological positions.

Failure signatures: Poor performance typically manifests as either (1) inability to extract meaningful topics from weak labels, resulting in generic embeddings, or (2) cross-encoder overfitting to training topics, reducing generalizability.

## Limitations
- Reliance on weakly labeled news data for topic extraction introduces potential noise in mined bias indicators
- Evaluation primarily focuses on news domain applications with limited testing on other political discourse contexts
- Manual verification of topic quality by two annotators represents a relatively small sample size for comprehensive assessment
