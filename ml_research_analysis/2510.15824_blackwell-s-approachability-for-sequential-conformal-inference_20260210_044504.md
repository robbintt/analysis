# Blackwell's Approachability for Sequential Conformal Inference

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2510.15824
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.15824
- **Reference count**: 40
- **Primary result**: Formulates ACI as a repeated game using Blackwell approachability theory

## Executive Summary
This paper establishes a novel connection between sequential conformal inference and Blackwell's approachability theory, framing the problem as a two-player finite game where the learner selects miscoverage levels while the opponent chooses outcomes. The framework introduces an opportunistic approachability algorithm that can exploit restrictions on the opponent's behavior, such as distributional drift or regime switching, to achieve both validity and efficiency. Theoretical guarantees demonstrate that the algorithm can approach target coverage-efficiency pairs under mild assumptions, providing a principled way to handle conformal inference beyond exchangeability.

## Method Summary
The authors formulate adaptive conformal inference as a repeated two-player game where the learner chooses miscoverage levels and the opponent chooses outcomes. They introduce an opportunistic approachability algorithm that leverages a calibration-based forecaster to estimate the opponent's actions and adapt its strategy accordingly. The method uses Blackwell's approachability theory to characterize valid-efficiency tradeoffs, allowing the algorithm to exploit potential restrictions on the opponent's play (such as distributional drift or regime switching) while maintaining theoretical guarantees of validity.

## Key Results
- Achieves asymptotic validity and efficiency in favorable settings by exploiting restrictions on opponent behavior
- Provides theoretical guarantees for approaching target sets of coverage-efficiency pairs
- Introduces a principled framework for conformal inference beyond exchangeability assumptions

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method works by framing conformal inference as a game-theoretic problem where Blackwell's approachability theory provides the mathematical foundation for balancing validity and efficiency. The opportunistic algorithm adapts to potential restrictions in the opponent's (data-generating process's) behavior by using a calibration forecaster to estimate likely actions, allowing it to exploit favorable conditions while maintaining worst-case guarantees when needed.

## Foundational Learning

**Blackwell's Approachability Theory**: A game-theoretic framework for achieving target sets in repeated games - needed to formalize the validity-efficiency tradeoff in conformal inference; quick check: verify the approachability condition holds for the constructed game.

**Calibration Theory**: Methods for evaluating and constructing forecasters that match empirical frequencies - needed to estimate the opponent's behavior in the game; quick check: confirm calibration error bounds for the forecaster.

**Conformal Inference Fundamentals**: Distribution-free prediction sets with finite-sample coverage guarantees - needed as the base problem being addressed; quick check: ensure nominal coverage is maintained in simple exchangeable settings.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Calibration Forecaster -> Approachability Algorithm -> Coverage-Efficiency Tradeoff

**Critical Path**: Opponent action estimation (calibration) → Strategy adaptation (approachability) → Coverage validation

**Design Tradeoffs**: 
- Adaptiveness vs. computational complexity
- Exploitation of restrictions vs. maintaining worst-case guarantees
- Accuracy of calibration vs. responsiveness to changes

**Failure Signatures**: 
- Poor calibration leading to invalid coverage
- Over-adaptation causing efficiency loss in stable settings
- Computational bottlenecks limiting real-time application

## Limitations
- Reliance on specific game-theoretic structure may limit applicability to conformal problems that don't naturally fit this formulation
