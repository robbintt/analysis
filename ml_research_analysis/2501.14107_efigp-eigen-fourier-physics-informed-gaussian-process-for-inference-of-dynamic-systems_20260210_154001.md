# EFiGP: Eigen-Fourier Physics-Informed Gaussian Process for Inference of Dynamic Systems

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2501.14107
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.14107
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: EFiGP achieves up to 6× speedup over MAGI in parameter estimation and trajectory reconstruction for ODE systems, with improved accuracy at dense discretization.

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces EFiGP, a novel method for parameter estimation and trajectory reconstruction in dynamical systems governed by ODEs. By integrating Fourier transformation and eigen-decomposition into a physics-informed Gaussian Process framework, EFiGP eliminates the need for numerical integration, significantly improving computational efficiency and accuracy. The method enforces governing equations in the Fourier domain, truncates high-frequency terms for denoising, and simplifies covariance operations using eigen-decomposition. Tested on FitzHugh-Nagumo, Lotka-Volterra, and Hes1 systems, EFiGP demonstrates superior performance compared to MAGI, achieving faster computation times and more stable and accurate trajectory reconstructions, particularly at dense discretization levels.

## Method Summary
EFiGP combines Gaussian Process regression with physics-informed constraints in the Fourier domain. The method represents latent states as GPs with Matérn kernels, enforces ODE dynamics by projecting them into Fourier space, and truncates both Fourier and eigen-decompositions to reduce computational complexity. This approach avoids numerical integration of ODE constraints, instead solving a constrained optimization problem directly in the spectral domain. Key innovations include: (1) transforming ODE residuals into Fourier space to enforce physics without discretization error, (2) using eigen-decomposition to simplify GP covariance operations, and (3) truncating high-frequency terms to denoise observations and improve stability.

## Key Results
- EFiGP achieves up to 6× speedup over MAGI in computational time for parameter estimation.
- Superior trajectory reconstruction accuracy with lower RMSE compared to MAGI across all tested systems.
- Improved stability at high discretization levels where MAGI fails to converge.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
EFiGP leverages the spectral properties of dynamical systems to avoid the computational bottleneck of numerical integration. By enforcing physics constraints in Fourier space, it eliminates discretization error and reduces the problem to a constrained optimization over spectral coefficients. The eigen-decomposition further simplifies GP covariance operations, making the method scalable. Truncation of high-frequency terms acts as a natural denoising mechanism, improving robustness to noise and numerical instability.

## Architecture Onboarding

### Component Map
Data Generation -> EFiGP (Fourier Transform -> Eigen-Decomposition -> MAP Optimization) -> Parameter Inference + Trajectory Reconstruction

### Critical Path
The critical path is: Fourier Transform of observations → Truncate Fourier series → Construct GP with eigen-decomposed covariance → Optimize MAP objective → Extract parameters and trajectories.

### Design Tradeoffs
- **Truncation levels**: Balancing accuracy (more terms) vs. computational efficiency (fewer terms).
- **Kernel choice**: Matérn kernel provides smoothness control but may be sensitive to hyperparameters.
- **Discretization**: Higher discretization improves accuracy but increases computational cost for traditional methods.

### Failure Signatures
- **MAGI convergence failure**: At high discretization levels (>321 points), MAGI fails to converge.
- **Weak identifiability**: Accurate trajectory recovery with poor parameter estimates (notably for Lotka-Volterra system).
- **Initialization sensitivity**: Poor initialization may lead to local optima in MAP optimization.
