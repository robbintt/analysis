# CPR: Causal Physiological Representation Learning for Robust ECG Analysis under Distribution Shifts

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2512.24564
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.24564
- Reference count: 5
- Primary result: CPR achieves 0.632 F1 under SAP attacks, outperforming Median Smoothing (0.541) by 9.1%

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the vulnerability of deep learning models for ECG diagnosis to Smooth Adversarial Perturbations (SAP) through Causal Physiological Representation Learning (CPR). The method employs a dual-pathway autoencoder that disentangles invariant pathological features from non-causal artifacts using a physiological mask. By enforcing orthogonality between content and style latent spaces while ensuring semantic consistency, CPR demonstrates superior robustness compared to traditional methods. The approach achieves certified robustness comparable to Randomized Smoothing while maintaining single-pass inference efficiency.

## Method Summary
CPR introduces a novel dual-pathway autoencoder architecture guided by physiological masks to separate pathological features from confounding artifacts in ECG signals. The method operates by learning invariant representations of cardiac pathology while filtering out non-causal variations that could be exploited by adversarial perturbations. A key innovation is the enforcement of orthogonality between content (pathological) and style (non-pathological) latent spaces, which prevents information leakage between causal and non-causal features. The model is trained to maintain semantic consistency across different input conditions, ensuring that the learned representations remain stable under distribution shifts and adversarial attacks.

## Key Results
- CPR achieves F1 score of 0.632 under SAP attacks on PTB-XL dataset
- Outperforms Median Smoothing baseline by 9.1% absolute improvement
- Matches certified robustness of Randomized Smoothing while maintaining single-pass inference efficiency

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
CPR's effectiveness stems from its ability to disentangle pathological features from non-causal artifacts through causal representation learning. By using physiological masks that encode prior knowledge about cardiac pathology, the model can identify and preserve invariant features that are causally related to the disease while filtering out confounding variations. The orthogonality constraint between content and style latent spaces prevents adversarial perturbations from exploiting correlations between pathological and non-pathological features. This causal disentanglement ensures that the learned representations remain stable even when the input is subjected to smooth adversarial perturbations that would typically fool conventional deep learning models.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: ECG Signal -> Dual-Pathway Autoencoder -> Physiological Mask -> Content Latent Space + Style Latent Space -> Reconstructed Signal + Classification

**Critical Path**: Input ECG -> Physiological Mask Application -> Content/Style Decomposition -> Orthogonality Enforcement -> Semantic Consistency Loss -> Final Classification

**Design Tradeoffs**: 
- Uses dual-pathway architecture for explicit disentanglement vs. single-path methods
- Incorporates physiological priors vs. purely data-driven approaches
- Enforces orthogonality constraints vs. allowing feature sharing
- Maintains semantic consistency vs. focusing solely on reconstruction accuracy

**Failure Signatures**: 
- Poor performance on rare cardiac conditions not well-represented in training data
- Degradation when physiological mask knowledge is incomplete or inaccurate
- Computational overhead compared to simpler adversarial defense methods
- Potential overfitting to PTB-XL dataset characteristics

**3 First Experiments**:
1. Ablation study removing physiological mask to quantify its contribution to robustness
2. Testing on external ECG datasets with different acquisition protocols to assess generalization
3. Varying orthogonality constraint strength to find optimal balance between disentanglement and reconstruction quality

## Limitations
- Reliance on accurate physiological masks may limit generalizability to rare conditions or diverse populations
- Computational overhead of dual-pathway architecture not fully quantified against simpler baselines
- Lack of statistical significance testing and confidence intervals for reported metrics
- No evaluation on external datasets with different acquisition protocols to verify robustness generalization

## Confidence
- High: Core methodology of disentangling invariant pathological features using physiological masks is well-founded in representation learning theory
- Medium: Empirical comparison showing 9.1% F1 improvement over Median Smoothing, though statistical significance not established
- Low: Claims about superior trade-off between robustness, efficiency, and interpretability difficult to verify without detailed computational benchmarks and multi-center validation
