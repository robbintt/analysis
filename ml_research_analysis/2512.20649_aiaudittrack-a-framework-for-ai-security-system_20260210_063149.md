# AIAuditTrack: A Framework for AI Security system

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2512.20649
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.20649
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Blockchain-based framework for recording AI interaction traffic enabling governance, auditing, and risk management

## Executive Summary
AIAuditTrack (AAT) introduces a blockchain-based framework for recording and governing AI system interactions through decentralized identity (DID) and verifiable credentials (VC). The system establishes trusted identities for AI entities and records their interaction trajectories on-chain, enabling cross-system supervision and risk management. By modeling AI entities as nodes in a dynamic graph with behavioral trajectories as edges, AAT provides a verifiable solution for AI auditing and responsibility attribution in complex multi-agent environments.

## Method Summary
AIAuditTrack combines decentralized identity management with blockchain technology to create an auditable record of AI interactions. The framework uses DID and VC to establish trusted identities for AI entities, then records their interaction patterns on a blockchain ledger. AI entities are represented as nodes in a dynamic graph structure, with edges capturing time-specific behavioral trajectories. A risk diffusion algorithm analyzes this graph to trace risky behaviors and propagate early warnings across the system. The approach leverages blockchain's immutability and decentralization to provide transparent, verifiable records while maintaining scalability through optimized transaction handling.

## Key Results
- Blockchain TPS measurements demonstrate feasibility and stability under large-scale interaction recording
- Risk diffusion algorithm effectively traces risky behaviors and propagates early warnings
- Framework provides scalable and verifiable solution for AI auditing, risk management, and responsibility attribution
- Successfully models AI entities as dynamic graph nodes with behavioral trajectory edges

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework's effectiveness stems from combining three proven technologies: decentralized identity management, blockchain immutability, and graph-based behavioral analysis. DID/VC provides cryptographic trust anchors for AI entities, blockchain ensures tamper-resistant recording of interaction histories, and graph algorithms enable sophisticated pattern recognition across agent networks. The risk diffusion mechanism leverages graph theory to identify cascading risks and propagate warnings before they materialize into failures.

## Foundational Learning
- **Decentralized Identity (DID)**: Self-sovereign identity management using cryptographic proofs; needed for establishing trusted AI entity identities without centralized authorities; quick check: verify DID resolution and VC verification work across heterogeneous systems.
- **Blockchain Transaction Recording**: Immutable ledger technology for audit trails; needed to ensure interaction records cannot be altered or deleted; quick check: measure TPS under realistic load conditions.
- **Dynamic Graph Modeling**: Time-evolving network structures representing agent relationships; needed to capture behavioral trajectories and dependencies; quick check: validate graph update performance with concurrent interactions.
- **Risk Diffusion Algorithms**: Graph propagation techniques for identifying cascading risks; needed to provide early warnings of systemic threats; quick check: benchmark algorithm on networks of increasing size.
- **Verifiable Credentials (VC)**: Cryptographically verifiable identity attributes; needed to establish trust relationships between AI entities; quick check: test VC presentation and verification under different network conditions.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: AI Entities -> DID/VC Provider -> Blockchain Ledger -> Risk Diffusion Engine -> Warning System

**Critical Path**: Identity establishment → Interaction recording → Graph update → Risk analysis → Warning propagation

**Design Tradeoffs**: The system prioritizes auditability and trust over real-time performance, accepting blockchain latency for immutable records. Graph-based modeling provides rich behavioral analysis but requires significant computational resources for large agent populations.

**Failure Signatures**: Blockchain congestion leading to transaction backlogs, graph update failures during high concurrency, risk algorithm timeouts on large networks, DID resolution failures across heterogeneous systems.

**First 3 Experiments**:
1. Deploy AAT with 100 simulated AI agents performing varied interactions over 24 hours, measuring TPS and graph update latency.
2. Test risk diffusion algorithm on synthetic agent networks (1K, 10K, 100K nodes) to establish computational scaling characteristics.
3. Conduct interoperability test with three different AI platforms using heterogeneous identity management systems.

## Limitations
- Risk diffusion algorithm scalability uncertain for massive agent populations (only blockchain TPS evaluated)
- Blockchain latency and transaction costs may impact real-time auditing requirements
- Assumes standardized DID/VC implementations across heterogeneous AI systems
- Graph-based modeling may struggle with rapidly evolving behaviors and long-term trajectory accuracy

## Confidence
- **High**: Core framework design combining DID/VC with blockchain-based interaction recording is technically sound and addresses genuine AI governance needs
- **Medium**: Risk diffusion algorithm effectiveness in identifying cascading risks across complex agent networks requires empirical validation beyond TPS measurements
- **Low**: Claims about cross-system supervision assume seamless interoperability between diverse AI platforms and identity systems
