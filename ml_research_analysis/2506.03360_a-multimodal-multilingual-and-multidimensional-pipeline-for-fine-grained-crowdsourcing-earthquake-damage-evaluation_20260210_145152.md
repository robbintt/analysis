# A Multimodal, Multilingual, and Multidimensional Pipeline for Fine-grained Crowdsourcing Earthquake Damage Evaluation

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2506.03360
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.03360
- Reference count: 27
- Introduces a 3M pipeline integrating multimodal data for fine-grained earthquake damage assessment

## Executive Summary
This study presents a structured Multimodal, Multilingual, and Multidimensional (3M) pipeline designed to evaluate earthquake damage using social media data. The pipeline integrates data preparation, multimodal classification, and model evaluation, leveraging large language models to analyze multilingual text and image content. Tested across two earthquake events—Ridgecrest (2019) and Fukushima (2021)—the pipeline demonstrated strong spatial localization and correlation with ground-truth seismic intensity data. The approach offers a scalable, real-time solution for disaster assessment, highlighting the potential of multimodal models in disaster response scenarios.

## Method Summary
The pipeline integrates data preparation, multimodal classification, and model evaluation, leveraging large language models to analyze multilingual text and image content. Tested across two earthquake events—Ridgecrest (2019) and Fukushima (2021)—the pipeline demonstrated strong spatial localization and correlation with ground-truth seismic intensity data (MMI). Models like Gemini and Qwen showed Pearson correlations between 0.43 and 0.78, with Qwen achieving the highest correlation in the Ridgecrest case. Performance varied by language and epicentral distance, with better results in English and urban areas.

## Key Results
- Strong spatial localization and correlation with ground-truth seismic intensity data (MMI)
- Models like Gemini and Qwen showed Pearson correlations between 0.43 and 0.78
- Qwen achieved the highest correlation in the Ridgecrest case
- Performance varied by language and epicentral distance, with better results in English and urban areas

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The pipeline leverages the strengths of multimodal models to integrate diverse data sources, enabling fine-grained analysis of earthquake damage. By combining text and image data, the system captures both descriptive and visual evidence of damage, enhancing the accuracy of assessments. The use of large language models allows for multilingual processing, addressing the challenge of diverse linguistic contexts in disaster scenarios.

- Variability in model performance across languages and epicentral distances.
- Uncertainty in generalizability to diverse disaster contexts.
