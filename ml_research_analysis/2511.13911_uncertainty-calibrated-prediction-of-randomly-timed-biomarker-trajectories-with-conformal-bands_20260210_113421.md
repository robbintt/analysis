# Uncertainty-Calibrated Prediction of Randomly-Timed Biomarker Trajectories with Conformal Bands

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2511.13911
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.13911
- Reference count: 40

## Executive Summary
This paper presents a novel conformal prediction framework for uncertainty-calibrated prediction of biomarker trajectories with irregularly timed observations. The method addresses a critical challenge in clinical prediction: providing valid coverage guarantees when biomarker measurements are taken at variable time points. By introducing a specialized nonconformity score for trajectory prediction and developing group-conditional conformal bands, the framework ensures that prediction intervals cover unknown trajectories with user-specified probability while maintaining tighter intervals than baseline methods. The approach was validated on neuroimaging data from ADNI and BLSA studies, demonstrating consistent 90% coverage across diverse clinical subgroups.

## Method Summary
The framework employs conformal prediction to generate uncertainty-calibrated prediction bands for biomarker trajectories observed at random time points. The key innovation is a novel nonconformity score specifically designed for trajectory prediction that accounts for the temporal structure of the data. The method first constructs prediction bands using conformal inference, then develops group-conditional conformal bands to ensure coverage within clinically relevant subpopulations defined by characteristics such as sex, race, diagnosis, and genetic factors. The framework was applied to predict hippocampal and ventricular volume trajectories in Alzheimer's disease progression, using real neuroimaging data from multiple longitudinal studies.

## Key Results
- Conformalized predictors achieved consistent 90% coverage of unknown trajectories across all tested scenarios
- Group-conditional conformal bands maintained coverage within clinically relevant subpopulations while standard methods failed
- Uncertainty-calibrated risk scores identified 17.5% more high-risk MCI subjects who progressed to Alzheimer's disease compared to standard risk scores
- Conformal bands produced tighter prediction intervals than baseline methods while maintaining coverage guarantees

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method leverages the theoretical guarantees of conformal prediction to produce valid uncertainty quantification for trajectory forecasting. By constructing a nonconformity score that captures the temporal dependencies in biomarker trajectories, the framework can assess how well new trajectories fit the observed data distribution. The group-conditional extension ensures that coverage guarantees hold not just on average but within specific clinical subgroups, addressing heterogeneity in disease progression patterns. This approach is particularly valuable for clinical decision-making where both accurate predictions and reliable uncertainty quantification are essential.

## Foundational Learning
- Conformal prediction: A framework for uncertainty quantification that provides distribution-free coverage guarantees by comparing new observations to historical data
- Nonconformity scores: Measures of how well new data points fit the observed distribution, critical for assessing prediction reliability
- Group-conditional inference: Ensures statistical guarantees hold within specific subgroups, addressing heterogeneity in clinical populations
- Biomarker trajectory modeling: Captures the temporal evolution of clinical markers, essential for disease progression forecasting
- Why needed: Clinical applications require both accurate predictions and reliable uncertainty quantification, especially when data is irregularly sampled and populations are heterogeneous
- Quick check: Verify coverage rates across subgroups and compare prediction interval widths to baseline methods

## Architecture Onboarding
- Component map: Data preprocessing -> Nonconformity score computation -> Conformal prediction band construction -> Group-conditional adjustment -> Risk score calculation
- Critical path: The nonconformity score computation is central, as it determines how well new trajectories fit the observed distribution and directly impacts coverage guarantees
- Design tradeoffs: Balancing coverage probability with prediction interval width, ensuring group-conditional coverage while maintaining computational efficiency
- Failure signatures: Coverage dropping below target in specific subgroups, prediction intervals becoming excessively wide, computational bottlenecks with large numbers of subgroups
- First experiments: 1) Test coverage rates on held-out trajectories, 2) Compare prediction interval widths to baseline methods, 3) Validate group-conditional coverage across clinical subgroups

## Limitations
- Generalizability beyond specific biomarker types and clinical conditions studied remains uncertain
- Performance in real-time clinical settings with diverse patient populations and varying data quality untested
- Computational complexity of group-conditional conformal bands for large numbers of subgroups could pose practical challenges
