# MASim: Multilingual Agent-Based Simulation for Social Science
## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2512.07195
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.07195
- Reference count: 40
- Introduces MASim, a multilingual agent-based simulation framework for studying social behavior across languages and cultures

## Executive Summary
MASim is a novel framework that enables agent-based simulation of social dynamics across multiple languages and cultures. The system creates generative agents with diverse sociolinguistic profiles that can engage in multi-turn interactions, supporting both global public opinion modeling and media influence analysis through autonomous news agents. The framework is instantiated using the MAPS benchmark dataset, which combines survey questions and demographic personas from global populations.

The authors demonstrate that MASim can effectively reproduce real-world social dynamics and show sensitivity to media interventions while maintaining consistency with agent personas. Case studies on cultural assimilation and normative diffusion reveal that cross-lingual communication fosters opinion convergence and that news organizations exert stronger influence than individual users in promoting social norms.

## Method Summary
MASim implements a multilingual agent-based simulation framework where each agent is equipped with language proficiency levels, cultural backgrounds, and demographic attributes. The framework uses large language models to generate realistic agent behaviors and interactions, supporting both bilateral conversations and broadcast scenarios. Agents are instantiated from the MAPS benchmark dataset, which provides survey questions and demographic personas. The system includes autonomous news agents that can influence public opinion, and supports multi-turn interactions that capture complex social dynamics over time.

## Key Results
- MASim effectively reproduces real-world social dynamics across different cultural contexts
- Cross-lingual communication in the simulation fosters opinion convergence among agents
- News organizations exert stronger influence than individual users in promoting social norms within the simulated population

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework leverages large language models' capabilities to generate culturally and linguistically appropriate agent behaviors, combined with structured demographic data to maintain consistency. The multi-turn interaction design captures the temporal evolution of social dynamics, while the inclusion of both bilateral and broadcast communication patterns reflects real-world information flow. The autonomous news agents provide a mechanism for studying media influence at scale, and the benchmark dataset ensures agents have realistic starting profiles.

- Social simulation benchmarks: Standardized datasets for validating social models
  - Why needed: Provides ground truth for comparing simulated behaviors
  - Quick check: MAPS dataset covers diverse demographics and survey responses

- Critical path: Agent creation → Interaction generation → Behavior consistency check → Result validation
- Design tradeoffs: LLM flexibility vs. consistency, multilingual capability vs. computational cost, agent diversity vs. scalability
- Failure signatures: Inconsistent agent personas across turns, unrealistic opinion convergence speeds, language proficiency mismatches
- First experiments:
  1. Run 100-agent simulation with basic survey interactions

  3. Evaluate media influence scenario with 10 news agents and 1000 user agents

## Limitations
- Generalizability beyond specific datasets used in the MAPS benchmark remains uncertain
- LLM dependence may introduce biases and inconsistencies not fully captured in experiments
- Scalability to truly global populations with millions of agents is unproven
- Real-time dynamic social phenomena handling capabilities are untested

## Confidence
- High confidence in MASim's technical implementation as a multilingual agent-based simulation framework
- Medium confidence in effectiveness of reproducing real-world social dynamics based on benchmark validation
- Low to Medium confidence in specific findings about cultural assimilation and normative diffusion due to limited case study scope

## Next Checks
