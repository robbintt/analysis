# Physics-informed transfer learning for SHM via feature selection

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2507.19519
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.19519
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Physics-informed feature selection using MAC achieves up to 88% accuracy in transferring damage classifiers between dissimilar helicopter blades

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of transferring damage classifiers between structurally dissimilar systems in population-based structural health monitoring (PBSHM). The authors propose a physics-informed feature selection method using the Modal Assurance Criterion (MAC) to identify features with related conditional distributions across domains, enabling effective unsupervised transfer learning. The approach is validated on both numerical and experimental case studies, demonstrating significant improvements in damage classification accuracy compared to standard domain adaptation methods.

## Method Summary
The method leverages MAC between undamaged mode shapes to select vibration-based features that exhibit consistent behavior under damage across different structures. By identifying features with related conditional distributions, the approach satisfies a key assumption for unsupervised transfer learning without requiring labeled target data. The physics-informed selection is shown to correlate with supervised joint-distribution similarity measures, enabling reliable generalization when only normal-condition data are available in the target domain.

## Key Results
- Achieved up to 88% accuracy in transferring classifiers between dissimilar helicopter blades
- Improved average classification accuracy across numerical population by over 13 percentage points
- Demonstrated effective minimization of negative transfer risk in comparative studies

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method works by exploiting the physical relationship between modal characteristics and damage response. MAC similarity between mode shapes serves as a proxy for conditional distribution similarity of damage-sensitive features, ensuring that selected features behave consistently under damage across structurally dissimilar systems. This physics-informed approach circumvents the need for labeled target data while maintaining transfer effectiveness.

## Foundational Learning
- Modal Assurance Criterion (MAC): Measures modal correlation between structures - needed to quantify similarity in undamaged mode shapes; quick check: MAC values close to 1 indicate strong correlation
- Population-based SHM: Framework for sharing damage classifiers across similar structures - needed to establish context for transfer learning; quick check: requires identification of structure population characteristics
- Domain adaptation: Transfer learning techniques for different data distributions - needed to frame the problem of classifier transfer; quick check: involves aligning feature distributions between source and target domains

## Architecture Onboarding
- Component map: MAC calculation -> Feature selection -> Classifier training -> Transfer validation
- Critical path: Modal analysis of undamaged structures → MAC computation → Feature subset selection → Damage classifier training → Transfer evaluation
- Design tradeoffs: Physics-informed selection (requires modal data) vs. purely statistical methods (may lack physical interpretability)
- Failure signatures: Poor MAC correlation indicates unsuitable feature selection; low transfer accuracy suggests violation of conditional distribution assumption
- First experiments: 1) Compute MAC between mode shapes of different structures, 2) Select top-K features based on MAC values, 3) Train and evaluate damage classifier on source domain, then transfer to target

## Limitations
- Reliance on assumption that MAC similarity correlates with conditional distribution similarity may not hold for all structural systems
- Constrained to vibration-based features, potentially excluding other informative damage indicators
- Requires access to modal information from undamaged structures, which may not always be available or accurately identifiable

## Confidence
- High confidence in physics-informed feature selection methodology and theoretical basis
- Medium confidence in generalizability across diverse structural systems
- Medium confidence in minimizing negative transfer claims
