# Control Consistency Losses for Diffusion Bridges

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2512.05070
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.05070
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Self-consistency property of diffusion bridges enables iterative online learning of control drift without backpropagation, achieving competitive KL divergence (0.02–0.06) in Ornstein-Uhlenbeck bridges at lower computational cost.

## Executive Summary
This work introduces a self-consistency property for diffusion bridge solutions derived from stochastic analysis, relating control values at different times along the solution path. The authors propose an iterative online algorithm to learn the control drift without backpropagation through trajectories, leveraging this property. The method enforces terminal bridging and gradient-form properties by construction, using fixed-point-style self-consistency losses. Experiments demonstrate competitive performance with existing methods while reducing computational cost.

## Method Summary
The authors derive a self-consistency property for diffusion bridges that relates control values at different time points along the solution path. This property is leveraged to develop an iterative online learning algorithm that avoids backpropagation through trajectories. The method uses fixed-point-style self-consistency losses to enforce terminal bridging and gradient-form properties by construction. To address numerical stability issues, auxiliary drift terms are introduced as heuristics.

## Key Results
- Achieves KL divergence of approximately 0.02–0.06 in Ornstein-Uhlenbeck bridge experiments
- Competitive performance with existing methods while avoiding backpropagation through trajectories
- Demonstrates lower computational cost compared to baseline approaches
- Introduces auxiliary drift terms to mitigate numerical issues, though without rigorous analysis

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method exploits a self-consistency property of diffusion bridges that emerges from stochastic analysis, specifically relating control values at different time points along the solution path. This mathematical property enables an iterative online learning approach that circumvents the need for backpropagation through entire trajectories, which is computationally expensive. The self-consistency losses act as fixed-point constraints that naturally enforce the terminal bridging and gradient-form requirements of the diffusion process.

## Foundational Learning
- Diffusion bridges: Stochastic processes conditioned to hit specific endpoints at given times; why needed to understand the core problem of learning controlled stochastic dynamics
- Self-consistency property: Mathematical relationship between control values at different time points along a bridge; quick check: verify the property holds for the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck case
- Stochastic analysis: Mathematical framework for studying random processes; why needed to derive the self-consistency property
- Backpropagation through trajectories: Computing gradients by unrolling entire stochastic paths; quick check: compare computational complexity with and without this approach
- Fixed-point constraints: Conditions that solutions must satisfy iteratively; why needed to enforce terminal conditions without full trajectory optimization

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Diffusion bridge dynamics -> Self-consistency property derivation -> Iterative online control learning algorithm -> Auxiliary drift stabilization -> Terminal condition enforcement

**Critical Path**: The algorithm iteratively updates control estimates using the self-consistency property, applying fixed-point constraints at each step while monitoring terminal conditions. The auxiliary drift terms are introduced when numerical instability is detected.

**Design Tradeoffs**: Avoiding backpropagation reduces computational cost but may sacrifice some gradient information; using self-consistency properties simplifies learning but depends on the validity of the underlying stochastic analysis; auxiliary drift terms stabilize training but introduce heuristic elements without theoretical guarantees.

**Failure Signatures**: Poor convergence when the self-consistency property is weakly satisfied; numerical instability in high-dimensional or highly non-linear diffusion dynamics; degraded bridge fidelity when auxiliary drift terms are poorly tuned.

**First 3 Experiments**: 1) Verify self-consistency property holds for controlled Ornstein-Uhlenbeck bridges; 2) Test iterative online learning algorithm on simple one-dimensional diffusion bridges; 3) Evaluate the impact of auxiliary drift terms on numerical stability.

## Limitations
- Practical robustness across broader stochastic dynamics remains untested beyond Ornstein-Uhlenbeck bridges
- Convergence guarantees under noisy control updates are unclear
- Performance comparisons limited to specific bridge types without multi-dimensional or non-linear diffusion testing
- Auxiliary drift terms are heuristic with no rigorous analysis of their impact on bridge fidelity

## Confidence
- Theoretical derivations: High
- Experimental validation: Medium (limited scope to controlled Ornstein-Uhlenbeck bridges)
- Numerical stability analysis: Low (heuristic approaches without rigorous guarantees)
