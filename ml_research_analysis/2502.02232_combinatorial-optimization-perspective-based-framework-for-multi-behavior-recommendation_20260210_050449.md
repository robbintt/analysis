# Combinatorial Optimization Perspective based Framework for Multi-behavior Recommendation

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces COPF, a novel framework for multi-behavior recommendation that treats behavior fusion as a combinatorial optimization problem. The framework imposes different constraints at various stages of each behavior to restrict the solution space and uses DFME (Dynamic Feature-Label Mapping Enhancement) to coordinate task correlations through improved forward and backward propagation. The approach aims to mitigate negative transfer caused by differences in feature and label distributions across behaviors.

## Method Summary
COPF addresses multi-behavior recommendation by formulating it as a combinatorial optimization problem. The framework employs COGCN (Constrained Graph Convolutional Network) to impose constraints at different behavior stages, effectively narrowing the solution space. For prediction, DFME is designed to coordinate task correlations by enhancing both forward and backward propagation mechanisms, specifically targeting the mitigation of negative transfer that arises from feature and label distribution discrepancies across different behaviors.

## Key Results
- HR@10 improvements of 49.91%, 12.06%, and 24.12% compared to best baselines across three real-world datasets
- Superior performance demonstrates effectiveness of combinatorial optimization perspective
- DFME module successfully mitigates negative transfer through improved propagation mechanisms

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework works by treating multi-behavior fusion as a combinatorial optimization problem, where constraints are strategically imposed at various stages of each behavior to limit the solution space. This approach ensures that the recommendations remain within feasible regions defined by the constraints. The DFME module enhances this by coordinating task correlations through bidirectional propagation improvements, which specifically addresses the challenge of negative transfer between behaviors with different feature and label distributions.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map:** COGCN (Constrained Graph Convolutional Network) -> DFME (Dynamic Feature-Label Mapping Enhancement) -> Multi-behavior Fusion

**Critical Path:** Behavior-specific feature extraction through COGCN → Constraint application → DFME-based task coordination → Final prediction

**Design Tradeoffs:** 
- Constraint imposition provides better solution space restriction but may limit flexibility
- DFME complexity versus effectiveness in mitigating negative transfer
- Balance between behavior-specific optimization and overall system performance

**Failure Signatures:**
- Poor constraint design leading to overly restrictive solution spaces
- DFME parameters not properly tuned, causing insufficient or excessive coordination
- Inconsistent behavior representations across different stages
