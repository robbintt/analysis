# Online-PVLM: Advancing Personalized VLMs with Online Concept Learning

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2511.20056
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.20056
- Reference count: 14
- One-line primary result: Introduces Online-PVLM, a framework for online concept learning in personalized visual language models, achieving state-of-the-art performance across concept identification, question answering, and captioning tasks

## Executive Summary
Online-PVLM addresses the inefficiency of existing personalized visual language models (PVLMs) that require training separate embeddings for each new concept. The framework introduces a novel approach using hyperbolic representations and a lightweight Omni Concept Embedder to generate concept embeddings on the fly, enabling test-time adaptation without retraining. This advancement significantly improves the scalability and efficiency of personalized visual language models in handling novel concepts.

## Method Summary
The paper proposes Online-PVLM, a framework that leverages hyperbolic representations and a lightweight Omni Concept Embedder to generate concept embeddings on the fly. This approach enables test-time adaptation without retraining, addressing the scalability issues of existing methods. The model uses a hyperbolic discriminator for structured representation learning and LoRA adapters for instruction following. The authors also develop OP-Eval, a comprehensive benchmark with 1,292 concepts and over 30K instances, to evaluate online concept learning.

## Key Results
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance across concept identification, question answering, and captioning tasks
- Outperforms existing methods in both novel and cached concept settings
- Demonstrates efficient handling of personalized concepts through on-the-fly embedding generation

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
Online-PVLM works by leveraging hyperbolic representations to capture the hierarchical structure of concepts, combined with a lightweight Omni Concept Embedder that generates embeddings dynamically during inference. This eliminates the need for retraining when encountering new concepts. The hyperbolic discriminator ensures structured representation learning, while LoRA adapters maintain instruction-following capabilities without significant computational overhead.
