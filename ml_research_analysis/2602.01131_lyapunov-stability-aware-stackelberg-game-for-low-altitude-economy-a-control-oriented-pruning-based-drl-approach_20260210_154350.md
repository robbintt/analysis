# Lyapunov Stability-Aware Stackelberg Game for Low-Altitude Economy: A Control-Oriented Pruning-Based DRL Approach

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2602.01131
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.01131
- Reference count: 40
- One-line primary result: Proposed Lyapunov stability-aware Stackelberg game with pruning-based PPO maximizes utility while ensuring control loop stability in UAV-assisted LAE networks.

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of resource allocation and control stability in low-altitude economy (LAE) scenarios where UAVs must support heterogeneous user demands while maintaining physical control stability. The authors introduce a Sensing-Communication-Computing-Control (SC3) closed-loop framework that explicitly models the impact of communication latency on control stability, leveraging Lyapunov stability theory to transform abstract stability requirements into quantifiable communication constraints. A Stackelberg game is formulated where UAVs (as leaders) dynamically price resources to balance load and ensure stability, while users (as followers) optimize requests based on service urgency. To address the computational overhead of standard Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) on energy-constrained UAVs, the paper proposes a novel pruning-based Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) algorithm with dynamic structured pruning, significantly compressing neural network scale during training.

## Method Summary
The method combines Lyapunov stability theory with Stackelberg game theory and pruning-based DRL. First, Lyapunov stability conditions are used to derive minimum bandwidth requirements for each user to maintain control loop stability. Second, a Stackelberg game is formulated where UAVs set prices for bandwidth resources and users optimize their requests based on urgency and pricing. Third, a pruning-based PPO algorithm is implemented with dynamic structured pruning to reduce computational overhead. The framework is evaluated through simulations demonstrating superior performance in terms of asymptotic reward, utility maximization, and stability constraint satisfaction compared to standard PPO and other baseline algorithms.

## Key Results
- The proposed Lyapunov stability-aware Stackelberg game successfully maintains control loop stability while maximizing system utility in dynamic low-altitude environments.
- The pruning-based PPO algorithm achieves superior asymptotic reward compared to standard PPO and other baseline algorithms.
- The framework effectively balances load through dynamic pricing, preventing network congestion that would otherwise destabilize the control loop.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: Physical control stability can be mathematically transformed into minimum bandwidth requirements.
- Mechanism: The framework models the UAV control loop as a discrete-time linear system. By defining a Lyapunov function $V(\zeta)$ for the tracking error, the paper uses the Lyapunov drift condition to bound the probability of system instability. This bound is algebraically inverted to derive a minimum packet success probability, which is subsequently converted into a strict lower bound for communication bandwidth ($\kappa^{min}$).
- Core assumption: The UAV control dynamics can be approximated as a linear time-invariant system during the sampling period, and channel conditions follow a Shannon-capacity model.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "...leverage the Lyapunov stability theory to derive an intrinsic mapping between the state evolution of the control system and communication constraints..."
  - [section III-B, Proposition 1] "...minimum bandwidth $\kappa^{min}$ required for user $i$ is given by [Eq 18]..."
  - [corpus] Related work supports Lyapunov optimization in LAE, but this specific control-to-communication mapping is the paper's distinct contribution.
- Break condition: If the system dynamics become highly non-linear or noise is non-Gaussian, the Lyapunov linear approximation may fail to guarantee stability.

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: Hierarchical pricing (Stackelberg Game) prevents network congestion that would otherwise destabilize the control loop.
- Mechanism: A leader-follower game is formed where UAVs (Leaders) set unit prices $\varrho$ for bandwidth to maximize revenue, constrained by the Lyapunov stability bounds. Users (Followers) request bandwidth based on urgency and price. The backward induction ensures that the resulting equilibrium price naturally rises as stability constraints tighten (scarcity increases), suppressing non-urgent demand.
- Core assumption: Users act rationally to maximize their utility function (Log-benefit minus cost) and have access to current pricing information.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "...UAVs (as leaders) dynamically price resources to balance load and ensure stability, while users (as followers) optimize requests..."
  - [section III-B] "...backward induction method... the optimal pricing strategy... is determined by projecting [unconstrained price] onto the feasible interval."
  - [corpus] Corpus neighbors mention Stackelberg games for spectrum sharing, validating the game-theoretic approach for resource allocation in this domain.
- Break condition: If users have incomplete or delayed information about prices, or if they act irrationally (e.g., malicious flooding), the equilibrium may shift, potentially violating stability constraints.

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: Dynamic structured pruning improves asymptotic reward and reduces computational load compared to standard PPO.
- Mechanism: A binary mask is applied to the Actor network. During training, a dynamic threshold (cubic decay schedule) zeros out neurons with low contribution scores. This acts as a regularizer, reducing overfitting to noise and resulting in a compact policy that runs efficiently on edge hardware.
- Core assumption: The standard PPO network contains redundant neurons that contribute to overfitting or unnecessary computational cost.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "...pruning-based PPO algorithm... significantly compressing neural network scale during training."
  - [section V, Fig 3] "...pruning-based PPO achieves a superior asymptotic reward... [suggesting] the structured pruning mechanism acts as a regularizer."
  - [corpus] Neighbors (e.g., "Efficient Onboard Vision-Language Inference...") highlight the general necessity of efficient inference in LAE networks, supporting the motivation for pruning.
- Break condition: If the pruning rate is too aggressive or started too early/late in the training phase, the network may lose critical capacity, failing to converge to the optimal policy.

## Foundational Learning

- Concept: **Lyapunov Stability Theory**
  - Why needed here: To bridge the gap between "control system stability" (a physical property) and "bandwidth allocation" (a network resource). It provides the mathematical rigor to quantify how much delay is "too much."
  - Quick check question: How does the Lyapunov function $V(\zeta)$ relate to the probability of packet loss in this paper?

- Concept: **Stackelberg Games**
  - Why needed here: To model the hierarchical relationship between the infrastructure provider (UAV) and the service consumer (User). It explains why pricing is an effective lever for load balancing.
  - Quick check question: In a Stackelberg game, does the leader choose its strategy before or after observing the follower?

- Concept: **Dynamic Structured Pruning**
  - Why needed here: Deep Reinforcement Learning is typically too heavy for real-time, energy-constrained drones. Understanding pruning is necessary to implement the "lightweight" aspect of the solution.
  - Quick check question: Why is "structured" pruning preferred over "unstructured" pruning for deployment on standard hardware (GPUs/TPUs)?

## Architecture Onboarding

- Component map: UAV Dynamics -> Lyapunov Mapper -> Stackelberg Game Solver -> Pruning-based PPO Agent
- Critical path: The stability constraint derivation (Section III-B) is the most sensitive component. If the mapping from Lyapunov decay $\rho_n$ to bandwidth $\kappa$ is misconfigured, the system will either over-provision (waste energy) or under-provision (crash).
- Design tradeoffs:
  - **Stability vs. Utility:** Lower decay rate $\rho_n$ guarantees higher stability but increases the minimum bandwidth requirement, reducing the UAV's capacity to serve non-urgent users.
  - **Pruning Start Time ($t_0$):** Early pruning ($t_0=50$) yields higher asymptotic rewards but risks instability during early training; late pruning is safer but slower.
- Failure signatures:
  - **Oscillating Rewards:** Indicates the learning rate is too high or the pruning threshold is destabilizing the policy.
  - **Constraint Violation:** If allocated bandwidth drops below $\kappa^{min}$, the Lyapunov constraint is not being enforced strictly enough in the reward function.
- First 3 experiments:
  1. **Sanity Check:** Validate the derived relationship between latency $T_n$ and bandwidth $\kappa$ by simulating a single loop without the DRL agent to ensure Eq. 18 holds.
  2. **Pruning Ablation:** Run the PPO agent with three pruning start times ($t_0=50, 200, 300$) to reproduce the result that early intervention acts as a regularizer (Fig 4).
  3. **Scalability Test:** Increase the number of users while keeping UAVs constant to verify that the Stackelberg pricing mechanism successfully maintains system utility without violating stability constraints (Fig 6a).

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out

- **Open Question 1:** How do jointly time-varying energy and bandwidth resources impact composite end-to-end latency fluctuations in the SC3 loop?
  - Basis in paper: [explicit] The Conclusion states future work will "model and analyze composite end-to-end latency fluctuations arising from jointly time-varying energy and bandwidth resources."
  - Why unresolved: The current formulation assumes static constraints or decoupled resource variations, failing to capture the coupled degradation of communication and computing capabilities as the UAV depletes its battery.
  - What evidence would resolve it: A dynamic model where latency boundaries shrink as a function of both instantaneous bandwidth availability and remaining battery capacity.

- **Open Question 2:** Can bidirectional co-design mechanisms improve stability compared to the current control-oriented mapping?
  - Basis in paper: [explicit] The Conclusion proposes "deepen[ing] the control-communication synergy by investigating bidirectional co-design mechanisms."
  - Why unresolved: The current framework unidirectionally maps control stability (Lyapunov) to communication constraints; it does not allow communication channel states (e.g., deep fades) to actively trigger adaptive control modes (e.g., hovering).
  - What evidence would resolve it: A protocol where detected communication degradation forces a shift in the UAV control law to a more conservative, stability-preserving mode.

- **Open Question 3:** How does the framework performance degrade under realistic probabilistic LoS/NLoS channel conditions?
  - Basis in paper: [inferred] The latency model (Eq. 8) utilizes a free-space path loss model ($h_{i,n} = h_0 d^{-\alpha}_{i,n}$) without modeling shadowing or blockage.
  - Why unresolved: Low-altitude urban environments are characterized by frequent switching between Line-of-Sight (LoS) and Non-Line-of-Sight (NLoS) states, causing severe, non-monotonic latency spikes that could violate the Lyapunov stability condition.
  - What evidence would resolve it: Simulations using probabilistic LoS/NLoS channel models (e.g., standard LAP models) to verify if the derived bandwidth boundaries remain sufficient for stability.

## Limitations
- The Lyapunov-to-bandwidth mapping relies on linear approximations of UAV dynamics that may break under highly non-linear flight conditions or severe channel impairments.
- The Stackelberg game assumes perfect information and rational user behavior, which may not hold in adversarial or partially observable environments.
- The pruning-based PPO method's performance is sensitive to the cubic decay schedule and pruning start time, with no sensitivity analysis provided for the initial and target sparsity parameters (w₁, w₂) or pruning frequency (∇).

## Confidence
- **High Confidence:** The formulation of the Stackelberg game and its solution via backward induction are well-established and directly specified in the paper.
- **Medium Confidence:** The Lyapunov stability constraint derivation is mathematically sound within its assumptions, but its practical applicability to highly non-linear UAV systems is uncertain.
- **Medium Confidence:** The dynamic structured pruning mechanism is theoretically justified as a regularizer, but the specific hyperparameters (w₁, w₂, ∇) are unspecified, limiting reproducibility.
- **Low Confidence:** The simulation results showing superior performance of the pruning-based PPO are compelling, but the lack of detail on the neuron importance metric and pruning schedule raises questions about external validity.
