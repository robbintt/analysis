# Bayesian Physics-Informed Neural Networks for Inverse Problems (BPINN-IP): Application in Infrared Image Processing

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2512.02495
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.02495
- Authors: Ali Mohammad-Djafari; Ning Chu; Li Wang
- Reference count: 31
- Introduces BPINN-IP framework for inverse problems with uncertainty quantification in infrared image processing

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces a Bayesian Physics-Informed Neural Network (BPINN-IP) framework that extends classical PINNs by incorporating Bayesian inference to handle uncertainties in inverse problems. The method specifically targets infrared image processing tasks such as deconvolution and super-resolution, where physical constraints, prior knowledge, and data-driven inference must be balanced. By explicitly modeling uncertainties in both data generation and measurement through prior distributions, the framework provides not only solutions but also pixelwise uncertainty maps that identify regions of high uncertainty.

The BPINN-IP framework demonstrates improved performance over traditional approaches in terms of robustness and generalization capability, particularly in unsupervised settings where ground-truth images are unavailable. Experiments on both simulated and real industrial infrared data show that the method effectively combines physics-based forward modeling with Bayesian inference, enabling more reliable results in challenging real-world scenarios with noisy and incomplete data.

## Method Summary
The BPINN-IP framework combines physics-based forward modeling with Bayesian inference to solve inverse problems. It extends classical PINNs by explicitly incorporating training data generation uncertainties and measurement errors through prior modeling. The method uses a hierarchical Bayesian approach where the solution is estimated from the posterior distribution, allowing for uncertainty quantification. The framework integrates physical constraints through the physics-based forward model while leveraging data-driven inference through neural networks. The Bayesian formulation enables simultaneous exploitation of physical knowledge, prior information, and observed data, with the solution obtained through maximum a posteriori estimation or full posterior sampling.

## Key Results
- Demonstrates improved performance over traditional approaches in infrared image deconvolution and super-resolution tasks
- Provides pixelwise uncertainty maps that identify regions of high uncertainty, valuable for real-world applications
- Shows effectiveness in both supervised and unsupervised settings, particularly where ground-truth images are unavailable
- Maintains robustness in noisy and incomplete data scenarios common in industrial infrared imaging

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The BPINN-IP framework works by integrating physics-based constraints with Bayesian inference, allowing the model to leverage both physical knowledge and data-driven learning. The Bayesian formulation explicitly models uncertainties in the forward process and measurement noise through prior distributions, which is crucial for handling real-world scenarios where perfect data is rarely available. By treating the inverse problem within a probabilistic framework, the method can quantify uncertainty in the solutions, providing not just point estimates but distributions that capture the confidence in different regions of the reconstructed image. This dual approach of incorporating physical constraints while maintaining data-driven flexibility enables better generalization and robustness compared to purely data-driven or purely physics-based methods.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Physics-based forward model -> Bayesian prior modeling -> Neural network parameterization -> Posterior inference -> Uncertainty quantification

**Critical Path**: Forward model specification → Prior distribution definition → Neural network architecture selection → Posterior computation (MAP or sampling) → Solution extraction and uncertainty mapping

**Design Tradeoffs**: The framework balances between physics fidelity (complexity of forward model) and computational efficiency (neural network complexity). More detailed physics models increase accuracy but also computational cost. The choice between MAP estimation and full posterior sampling affects both accuracy of uncertainty quantification and computational requirements. The hierarchical Bayesian structure allows flexible modeling of different uncertainty sources but increases model complexity.

**Failure Signatures**: Poor performance may manifest as overconfident predictions in data-sparse regions, failure to respect physical constraints when prior knowledge is weak, or computational instability during posterior inference. The uncertainty maps should reveal these issues through high uncertainty values in problematic regions.

**First Experiments**: 1) Test on simple linear inverse problems with known solutions to verify uncertainty quantification accuracy 2) Apply to controlled synthetic infrared data with varying noise levels to assess robustness 3) Compare performance with classical PINNs on a standard benchmark problem to demonstrate benefits of Bayesian formulation
