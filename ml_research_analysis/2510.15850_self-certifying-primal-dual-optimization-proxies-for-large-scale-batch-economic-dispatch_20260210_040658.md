# Self-Certifying Primal-Dual Optimization Proxies for Large-Scale Batch Economic Dispatch

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2510.15850
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.15850
- Reference count: 32
- Primary result: Hybrid solver achieves 1000x speedup vs. simplex while guaranteeing <2% optimality gap

## Executive Summary
This paper presents a hybrid optimization framework that combines machine learning proxies with classical optimization solvers for large-scale economic dispatch problems. The key innovation is a self-certifying approach that uses duality theory to bound the sub-optimality of ML predictions through the duality gap, enabling efficient fallback to exact optimization only when needed. A novel joint training procedure optimizes both primal and dual proxies using a hinge-like loss function targeting user-specified optimality tolerances. The framework demonstrates over 1000x speedup compared to parallelized simplex solvers while maintaining maximum optimality gaps below 2% on systems with up to 9,241 buses.

## Method Summary
The proposed framework addresses the fundamental trade-off between speed and accuracy in large-scale economic dispatch by creating a self-certifying hybrid solver. It employs ML proxies to predict solutions rapidly, but critically incorporates duality-based certification to verify solution quality. When the duality gap indicates the ML prediction is sufficiently close to optimal (within user-specified tolerance), the prediction is accepted; otherwise, the system falls back to exact optimization. The training procedure jointly optimizes primal and dual proxies using a hinge-like loss function that directly targets the desired optimality tolerance, enabling the model to learn both good predictions and reliable quality estimates simultaneously.

## Key Results
- Achieves over 1000x speedup compared to parallelized simplex-based solvers
- Guarantees maximum optimality gaps below 2% across tested scenarios
- Scales effectively to large power systems with up to 9,241 buses
- Demonstrates reliable fallback behavior when ML predictions exceed tolerance thresholds

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The approach works by leveraging the mathematical structure of convex optimization problems, specifically the duality gap as a certificate of solution quality. By training both primal and dual proxies jointly, the system learns to predict not just good solutions but also accurate quality estimates. The hinge-like loss function during training directly targets the user-specified optimality tolerance, creating a natural stopping criterion for when to accept ML predictions versus invoking exact optimization.

## Limitations
- Performance evaluation limited to power systems applications, generalizability to other domains unverified
- Training procedure requires high-quality primal-dual solutions for hinge-like loss function, which may not be available for all problem classes
- Computational overhead of duality gap computation and potential frequent fallback to exact solvers in uncertain conditions could impact practical deployment

## Confidence
- **High Confidence**: Worst-case optimality guarantees and duality gap certification methodology are theoretically sound and well-established
- **Medium Confidence**: Empirical speedup claims are robust for tested problem sizes but scaling behavior to systems >10,000 buses remains unverified
- **Medium Confidence**: Hybrid solver's behavior under dynamic operating conditions and varying problem characteristics needs further validation
