# Theoretical Closed-loop Stability Bounds for Dynamical System Coupled with Diffusion Policies

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2511.15520
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.15520
- Authors: Gabriel Lauzier; Alexandre Girard; François Ferland
- Reference count: 17
- Primary result: Establishes theoretical stability bounds showing partial diffusion policies are equivalent to full diffusion policies when expert demonstration variance is below a threshold determined by system response and diffusion parameters

## Executive Summary
This work addresses the challenge of using diffusion policies in real-time robotics applications by investigating the stability of partially executed denoising processes. The core idea is to treat the plant dynamics and denoising diffusion process as coupled dynamical systems rather than decoupled ones, allowing for faster inference by taking actions before full denoising completion. The primary result establishes theoretical stability bounds showing that partial diffusion policies are equivalent in stability to full diffusion policies when the variance of expert demonstrations is below a threshold determined by system response and diffusion parameters. This yields a practical metric (σ < g√(α/A)) for ensuring stability and provides a framework for trading denoising depth against latency, making diffusion policies more viable for real-time applications.

## Method Summary
The authors establish theoretical stability bounds by analyzing the coupled dynamics between the plant system and the denoising diffusion process. They treat both systems as interacting dynamical systems and derive conditions under which partial execution of the denoising process maintains closed-loop stability. The analysis focuses on Gaussian noise in expert demonstrations and leverages the specific structure of the diffusion process defined by parameters α and A. The key contribution is showing that when the variance of expert demonstrations is below a critical threshold (σ < g√(α/A)), partial diffusion policies achieve the same stability properties as full diffusion policies, enabling faster inference without sacrificing stability guarantees.

## Key Results
- Establishes theoretical equivalence between partial and full diffusion policies under a specific variance threshold condition
- Provides a practical stability metric: σ < g√(α/A) where σ is expert demonstration variance, g is system response, and α/A are diffusion parameters
- Demonstrates that treating plant dynamics and denoising diffusion as coupled systems enables faster inference while maintaining stability
- Shows that partial denoising can be safely implemented in real-time robotics applications when demonstration noise is sufficiently small

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The stability guarantee emerges from treating the plant dynamics and denoising process as coupled systems. When expert demonstrations have low variance (below the threshold), the partial denoising output remains sufficiently close to the optimal action, preventing destabilizing feedback. The diffusion parameters α and A control the convergence rate of the denoising process, and their ratio determines how much noise can be tolerated while maintaining stability. The system response gain g captures how quickly the plant reacts to control inputs, scaling the stability threshold appropriately.

## Architecture Onboarding
- **Component map**: Expert Demonstrations -> Diffusion Process (with parameters α, A) -> Partial Denoising Output -> Plant Dynamics -> System Response (g) -> Stability Check (σ threshold)
- **Critical path**: The path from expert demonstrations through partial denoising to plant dynamics and stability verification represents the core computational and analytical flow
- **Design tradeoffs**: The framework trades denoising depth (and thus inference time) against stability margins. More aggressive partial denoising reduces latency but requires lower demonstration noise variance
- **Failure signatures**: Instability occurs when σ ≥ g√(α/A), manifesting as oscillatory or divergent system behavior. The threshold provides a clear diagnostic for when partial denoising becomes unsafe
- **Three first experiments**: 1) Verify the stability bound on a simple linear system with varying demonstration noise levels, 2) Test the latency-performance trade-off on a simulated robot manipulator, 3) Validate the threshold condition on a real robot with different diffusion parameter settings

## Limitations
- The analysis assumes Gaussian noise in expert demonstrations and may not generalize to other noise distributions
- The stability condition σ < g√(α/A) provides a sufficient but potentially conservative bound that may limit practical applicability
- The framework focuses on single-step stability and does not fully characterize multi-step or transient stability behavior during partial denoising execution

## Confidence
- **High confidence**: The core mathematical derivation establishing the relationship between demonstration noise variance, system response, and diffusion parameters appears sound. The equivalence result between partial and full diffusion policies under the stated condition is well-supported.
- **Medium confidence**: The practical applicability of the stability bound in real-world robotics scenarios, given that actual systems rarely meet the idealized assumptions. The choice of g and its relationship to specific robot dynamics needs empirical validation.
- **Low confidence**: The performance trade-offs between denoising depth and latency in practical implementations remain largely theoretical, with limited experimental validation across different robot platforms and task complexities.
