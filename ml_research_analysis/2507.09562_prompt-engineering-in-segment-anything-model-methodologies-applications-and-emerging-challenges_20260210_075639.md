# Prompt Engineering in Segment Anything Model: Methodologies, Applications, and Emerging Challenges

## Quick Facts

## Executive Summary
This paper provides the first systematic examination of prompt engineering methodologies for the Segment Anything Model (SAM), addressing a significant gap in the literature. The survey organizes and analyzes the rapidly growing body of research in this emerging field, covering fundamental methodologies, practical applications, and key challenges. The work reveals how prompt engineering has evolved from simple geometric inputs to sophisticated multimodal approaches, enabling SAM's adaptation across diverse domains including medical imaging and remote sensing.

The paper introduces a hierarchical taxonomy of prompt engineering approaches and analyzes advanced automated generation strategies. It fills an important knowledge gap by providing a structured framework for understanding and advancing prompt engineering in foundation models for segmentation. The survey is particularly valuable for researchers and practitioners seeking to understand the critical role of prompts in SAM's success and the challenges that remain in this evolving field.

## Method Summary
The survey systematically reviewed and organized published research on prompt engineering for SAM and its variants. The methodology involved analyzing academic publications to construct a hierarchical taxonomy of prompt engineering approaches, examining the evolution from geometric to multimodal prompts, and identifying emerging challenges and future research directions. The work provides a comprehensive framework for understanding how different prompt engineering techniques contribute to SAM's effectiveness across various applications.

## Key Results
- Established the first hierarchical taxonomy of prompt engineering approaches for SAM
- Demonstrated the evolution from simple geometric prompts to sophisticated multimodal approaches
- Identified promising future research directions in automated prompt generation and domain-specific adaptations

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The survey works by systematically organizing the rapidly expanding field of SAM prompt engineering research into a coherent framework. By categorizing approaches based on their input types, generation methods, and application domains, the paper reveals patterns and relationships that were previously scattered across individual publications. This structured approach enables researchers to understand the progression of techniques and identify gaps in the current state of the art.
