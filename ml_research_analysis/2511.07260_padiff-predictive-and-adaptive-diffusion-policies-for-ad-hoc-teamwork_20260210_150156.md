# PADiff: Predictive and Adaptive Diffusion Policies for Ad Hoc Teamwork

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2511.07260
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.07260
- Authors: Hohei Chan; Xinzhi Zhang; Antao Xiang; Weinan Zhang; Mengchen Zhao
- Reference count: 32
- Primary result: Achieves 35.25% average performance improvement over existing AHT methods across three cooperative environments

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces PADiff, a diffusion-based policy framework designed to address the ad hoc teamwork (AHT) challenge where agents must cooperate effectively with unseen teammates. Unlike traditional RL methods that collapse to single behaviors, PADiff captures multimodal cooperation patterns essential for effective collaboration with novel teammates. The core innovation lies in integrating a Predictive Guidance Block (PGB) that anticipates teammate intentions and an Adaptive Feature Modulation Net (AFM-Net) that dynamically adjusts to teammate behavior changes.

## Method Summary
PADiff employs a diffusion-based policy framework that generates multimodal action distributions rather than deterministic policies. The system integrates two key components: a Predictive Guidance Block that anticipates teammate intentions through behavioral modeling, and an Adaptive Feature Modulation Net that dynamically adjusts policy parameters based on observed teammate behaviors. This architecture enables the agent to maintain flexible cooperation strategies rather than collapsing to single behavior patterns when encountering new teammates.

## Key Results
- Achieves 35.25% average performance improvement compared to existing AHT methods
- Successfully captures multimodal cooperation patterns across three cooperative environments
- Demonstrates superior adaptability and robustness in dynamic team settings

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The effectiveness stems from addressing a fundamental limitation of traditional RL approaches in AHT scenarios: the tendency to collapse to single behavioral modes. By using diffusion policies that naturally produce multimodal distributions, PADiff can represent the diverse ways teammates might behave. The Predictive Guidance Block enables proactive adaptation by anticipating teammate actions before they occur, while the Adaptive Feature Modulation Net provides reactive adjustment capabilities. This dual mechanism allows the system to both predict and adapt to teammate behaviors in real-time, maintaining effective cooperation across varying team compositions.

## Architecture Onboarding
Component map: Input -> PGB -> AFM-Net -> Diffusion Policy -> Output
Critical path: Observation → Predictive Guidance Block → Adaptive Feature Modulation Net → Action Sampling
Design tradeoffs: Diffusion policies offer natural multimodality but require more complex training; predictive guidance adds computational overhead but enables proactive cooperation
Failure signatures: Performance collapse to single behavior patterns; poor adaptation to teammate behavioral shifts; high prediction error from PGB

## Limitations
- Empirical validation limited to three cooperative environments without specified complexity or diversity
- Missing ablation studies to isolate contributions of PGB and AFM-Net components
- No analysis confirming actual multimodal behavior capture versus weighted averages

## Confidence
Medium - Results are promising but incompletely characterized. The 35.25% improvement claim lacks validation across diverse scenarios and does not establish whether gains come from core framework or added complexity.
