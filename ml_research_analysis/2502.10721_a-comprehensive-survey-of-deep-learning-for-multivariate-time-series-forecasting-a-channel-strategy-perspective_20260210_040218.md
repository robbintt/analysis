# A Comprehensive Survey of Deep Learning for Multivariate Time Series Forecasting: A Channel Strategy Perspective

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2502.10721
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.10721
- Reference count: 13
- This paper provides the first comprehensive survey of deep learning methods for multivariate time series forecasting (MTSF) from a channel strategy perspective

## Executive Summary
This survey paper presents a novel taxonomy for deep learning approaches to multivariate time series forecasting (MTSF) organized around three hierarchical levels: strategy (Channel Independence, Channel Dependence, Channel Partiality), mechanism (e.g., transformer-based, CNN-based, GNN-based), and characteristic (e.g., asymmetry, lagginess, group-wise). The authors systematically review state-of-the-art methods, analyze their strengths and limitations, and provide insights for future research. A key contribution is the identification that Channel Partiality strategies often achieve the best performance by balancing inter-channel dependencies and independence. The survey includes an up-to-date GitHub repository (CS4TS) with all surveyed papers.

## Method Summary
The survey organizes deep learning methods for MTSF through a hierarchical taxonomy that captures how different approaches handle channel relationships in multivariate time series data. The taxonomy spans three levels: strategies that define how channels interact (independence, dependence, or partiality), mechanisms that implement the forecasting (including transformers, CNNs, and graph neural networks), and characteristics that describe specific modeling choices (asymmetry, lagginess, group-wise). The authors systematically reviewed and classified recent papers according to this framework, analyzing performance patterns and identifying research gaps. They also created and maintain a GitHub repository (CS4TS) that catalogs all surveyed papers and their classifications.

## Key Results
- Channel Partiality strategies often achieve the best performance by balancing inter-channel dependencies and independence
- The hierarchical taxonomy provides a structured framework for understanding and comparing MTSF approaches
- The survey reveals that transformer-based mechanisms and asymmetry characteristics are increasingly prevalent in recent methods
- The CS4TS GitHub repository provides a valuable resource for researchers entering the MTSF field

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The survey's hierarchical taxonomy works because it captures the fundamental trade-offs in MTSF: whether to model channels independently, jointly, or with selective dependencies. Channel strategies directly address the core challenge of multivariate time series - how to leverage relationships between different series while avoiding overfitting or information loss. The mechanism layer maps these strategic choices to concrete architectural implementations, while characteristics capture the nuanced modeling decisions that affect performance. This multi-level organization enables systematic comparison and reveals patterns across seemingly disparate approaches.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Strategy Selection -> Mechanism Architecture -> Characteristic Implementation -> Forecasting Output

**Critical Path**: Channel Strategy Definition → Mechanism Selection → Characteristic Configuration → Model Training → Performance Evaluation

**Design Tradeoffs**: Channel Independence offers simplicity but may miss important dependencies; Channel Dependence captures relationships but risks overfitting; Channel Partiality balances these concerns but requires careful design of dependency structures.

**Failure Signatures**: Poor performance with Channel Independence when strong inter-channel dependencies exist; overfitting with Channel Dependence on noisy data; suboptimal performance with Channel Partiality when dependency structures are incorrectly specified.

**First Experiments**: 1) Compare all three channel strategies on a simple MTSF benchmark using the same mechanism; 2) Implement a Channel Partiality approach with varying dependency structures; 3) Test the impact of asymmetry and lagginess characteristics on forecasting accuracy.

## Limitations

- The taxonomy's hierarchical structure, while innovative, may benefit from further refinement and consensus on classification criteria
- The survey focuses primarily on deep learning approaches, potentially overlooking valuable insights from classical statistical methods or hybrid approaches
- Performance analysis comparing different channel strategies is based on reported results across various studies, which may be influenced by dataset-specific factors and evaluation protocols

## Confidence

- Taxonomy framework: High confidence in the systematic organization and categorization approach
- Channel Partiality performance claims: Medium confidence due to variability in reported results across different studies
- Mechanism-specific analyses: Medium confidence, as some mechanisms may be underrepresented
