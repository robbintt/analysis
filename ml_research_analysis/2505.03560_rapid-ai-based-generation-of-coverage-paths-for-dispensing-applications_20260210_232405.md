# Rapid AI-based generation of coverage paths for dispensing applications

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2505.03560
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.03560
- Reference count: 10
- Key outcome: AI-based method predicts dispense paths in under one second versus one-week computation time, achieving 93.7-97.4% area coverage

## Executive Summary
This paper presents an AI-based approach for generating efficient dispense paths for Thermal Interface Materials (TIM) in power electronics manufacturing. The method addresses the challenge of designing coverage paths that minimize voids while maximizing material usage efficiency. By training a second artificial neural network (ANN) to predict dispense paths directly from target cooling areas, the approach eliminates the need for computationally expensive optimization while requiring no labeled training data.

The proposed method leverages a pretrained quality model composed of multiple ANNs for 2D discretization, flow behavior, and void detection. This quality model generates objective function values during training, enabling the path prediction ANN to learn optimal dispensing strategies. The approach achieves significant improvements in computational efficiency while maintaining high coverage rates of 93.7-97.4% for real product geometries, making it suitable for automated manufacturing applications.

## Method Summary
The authors propose an AI-based method that uses a pretrained quality model to train a second ANN for direct dispense path prediction. The quality model, composed of multiple ANNs for 2D discretization, flow behavior, and void detection, generates objective function values during training without requiring labeled data. The path prediction ANN learns to map target cooling areas to optimal dispense paths. The approach is validated on both synthetic living room shapes and actual product geometries, demonstrating area coverage of 93.7-97.4% for real products. The AI model generates feasible paths without voids in under one second, representing a significant improvement over the one-week computation time of previous TIMtrace optimization approaches.

## Key Results
- AI model generates feasible dispense paths without voids in under one second
- Area coverage rates of 93.7-97.4% achieved for real product geometries
- Computational efficiency improved from one-week optimization to sub-second generation
- Predicted paths are directly transferable to automated manufacturing equipment

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method works by leveraging a pretrained quality model that simulates the physical behavior of dispensed materials, including flow dynamics and void formation. This quality model serves as a surrogate objective function during training, allowing the path prediction ANN to learn optimal dispensing strategies without requiring actual labeled data from real manufacturing processes. The approach exploits the structure of the dispensing problem, where the relationship between path geometry and coverage quality can be learned through simulation rather than expensive physical experimentation.
