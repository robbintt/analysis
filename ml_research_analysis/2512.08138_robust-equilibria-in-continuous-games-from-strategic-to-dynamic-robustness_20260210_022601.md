# Robust equilibria in continuous games: From strategic to dynamic robustness

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2512.08138
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.08138
- Reference count: 40
- Key outcome: Introduces strategic and dynamic robustness concepts for Nash equilibria in continuous games, establishing a correspondence between them

## Executive Summary
This paper explores the robustness of Nash equilibria in continuous games under both strategic and dynamic uncertainty. The authors introduce strategic robustness, where equilibria remain stable under small perturbations to the game's payoff structure, and characterize such equilibria geometrically as extreme points with sharp gradient conditions. The study then examines dynamic robustness through follow-the-regularized-leader (FTRL) dynamics under uncertainty, establishing that strategic robustness implies dynamic robustness. The work also demonstrates geometric convergence rates for entropically regularized learning in affinely constrained games.

## Method Summary
The authors employ a combination of geometric analysis and dynamical systems theory to characterize robust equilibria. They use convex analysis techniques to define strategic robustness through extreme points and gradient conditions, then apply dynamical systems theory to analyze FTRL dynamics under uncertainty. The convergence analysis for regularized learning leverages Lyapunov stability methods to establish geometric rates under specific constraint structures.

## Key Results
- Strategic robustness defined as equilibria invariant under small payoff perturbations
- Geometric characterization of robust equilibria as extreme points with sharp gradient conditions
- Structural correspondence established: strategic robustness implies dynamic robustness
- Geometric convergence rates proven for entropically regularized learning in affinely constrained games

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework works by connecting equilibrium stability under payoff uncertainty (strategic robustness) with algorithmic stability under learning dynamics (dynamic robustness). The geometric characterization provides a tractable way to identify robust equilibria through extreme point analysis and gradient conditions. The convergence results leverage the inherent regularization properties of entropy-based learning algorithms.
