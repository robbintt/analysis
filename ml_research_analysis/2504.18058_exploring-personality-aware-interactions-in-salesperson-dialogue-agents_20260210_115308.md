# Exploring Personality-Aware Interactions in Salesperson Dialogue Agents

## Executive Summary
This study examines how personality traits, defined by MBTI, influence user interactions with sales-oriented dialogue agents. Using a pre-trained SALES AGENT, we conducted experiments with MBTI-based user simulators to assess the agent's effectiveness and adaptability across diverse personality types. Our analysis revealed significant patterns in task completion rates, dialogue naturalness, and user engagement, highlighting the importance of tailoring dialogue strategies to individual personality traits. For example, Extraverted (E) users achieved the highest task success rate at 82.7%, while Feeling (F) users sustained longer conversations. To support future research, we also released a set of persona-defined user simulators, offering a valuable resource for developing personalized conversational systems.

## Method Summary
The study utilized a pre-trained sales agent and MBTI-based user simulators to evaluate personality-aware interactions. Experiments were conducted to measure task completion rates, dialogue naturalness, and user engagement across different personality types. The methodology involved creating persona-defined user simulators to simulate various MBTI personality traits, allowing systematic assessment of the agent's adaptability and effectiveness.

## Key Results
- Extraverted (E) users achieved the highest task success rate at 82.7%
- Feeling (F) users sustained longer conversations
- Different personality types showed significant variations in task completion rates and engagement patterns

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The effectiveness stems from tailoring dialogue strategies to individual personality traits, as personality influences communication preferences and interaction patterns. The MBTI framework provides a structured way to categorize user types and predict their behavioral tendencies in sales conversations.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: SALES AGENT -> MBTI Simulators -> Performance Metrics

**Critical Path**: The core evaluation pipeline involves the sales agent processing user inputs from MBTI-based simulators, with success measured through task completion rates and engagement metrics.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The study prioritizes systematic personality-based evaluation over real-user testing, sacrificing ecological validity for controlled experimentation. This enables reproducible results but may miss nuanced human behaviors.

**Failure Signatures**: Potential issues include over-reliance on MBTI's validity, limited generalizability from simulated to real interactions, and possible oversimplification of complex personality traits into discrete categories.

## Limitations
- The use of MBTI as a personality framework remains controversial in scientific literature due to questions about its reliability and validity
- Experimental setup relies entirely on user simulators rather than real human interactions
- Reported task success rates and conversation metrics cannot be independently verified without access to underlying experimental data

## Confidence
- **High confidence**: The observation that different personality types show varying task completion rates and engagement patterns is well-supported by the experimental results.
- **Medium confidence**: The claim about tailoring dialogue strategies to individual personality traits is supported by the data but requires further validation with real users.
- **Low confidence**: The broader implications for personalized conversational systems development may overstate the practical applicability given the simulated nature of the experiments.

## Next Checks
