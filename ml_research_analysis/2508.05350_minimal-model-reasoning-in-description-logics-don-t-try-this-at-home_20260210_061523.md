# Minimal Model Reasoning in Description Logics: Don't Try This at Home!

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2508.05350
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.05350
- Reference count: 40
- Key outcome: Concept satisfiability in minimal models is undecidable for EL, but regains decidability with strong/weak acyclicity conditions, achieving NExpNP-completeness for weakly acyclic ELIO⊥

## Executive Summary
This paper investigates the complexity of reasoning with minimal models in Description Logics (DLs). The authors establish that concept satisfiability in minimal models is undecidable for the lightweight DL EL, and this undecidability extends to very restricted fragments of tuple-generating dependencies. To address this challenge, they introduce strong and weak acyclicity conditions on TBoxes, which allow them to regain decidability while providing tight complexity bounds. The key technique involves using flooding to simulate universal quantification required for minimization.

## Method Summary
The authors employ a combination of theoretical analysis and complexity-theoretic reductions to establish their results. They first prove undecidability of concept satisfiability in minimal models for EL through complex reductions. They then introduce strong and weak acyclicity conditions as restrictions on TBoxes that enable decidability. The flooding technique is used to simulate universal quantification, which is crucial for model minimization. Complexity bounds are established through careful analysis of the computational requirements of the acyclicity conditions and the minimal model construction process.

## Key Results
- Concept satisfiability in minimal models is undecidable for EL
- Undecidability extends to very restricted fragments of tuple-generating dependencies
- Weak acyclicity condition yields NExpNP-completeness for weakly acyclic ELIO⊥
- ΣP2-completeness in data complexity for weakly acyclic ELIO⊥
- ExpSpace-hardness for minimal model reasoning in DL-Lite horn

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The flooding technique works by systematically expanding models to ensure all necessary universal quantifications are captured while maintaining minimality. This allows the authors to simulate the complex universal quantification patterns required for minimal model semantics without explicitly computing them. The acyclicity conditions (strong and weak) prevent infinite expansion during flooding by restricting the structure of the TBox, thus ensuring termination and decidability.

## Architecture Onboarding

Component Map: TBox -> Acyclicity Check -> Flooding Simulation -> Minimal Model Construction

Critical Path: The critical path involves checking acyclicity conditions on the TBox, followed by the flooding simulation to construct minimal models, and finally verifying concept satisfiability.

Design Tradeoffs: The main tradeoff is between expressiveness and decidability. More expressive TBoxes lead to undecidability, while acyclicity conditions restrict expressiveness but enable decidability.

Failure Signatures: Failure can occur due to infinite expansion during flooding (without acyclicity conditions) or undecidability in highly expressive TBoxes.
