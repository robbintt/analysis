# QiVC-Net: Quantum-Inspired Variational Convolutional Network, with Application to Biosignal Classification

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2511.05730
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.05730
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: QiVC-Net achieves 97.84% and 97.89% accuracy on two benchmark PCG datasets, outperforming previous methods

## Executive Summary
QiVC-Net introduces a quantum-inspired variational convolutional network architecture for phonocardiogram classification. The core innovation is the quantum-inspired rotated ensemble (QiRE) mechanism, which applies structured, low-dimensional subspace rotations to convolutional weights, enabling uncertainty-aware modeling without adding extra parameters. The model was evaluated on two benchmark datasets (PhysioNet CinC 2016 and PhysioNet CirCor DigiScope 2022) and achieved state-of-the-art performance while demonstrating robustness to noise and class imbalance.

## Method Summary
The QiVC-Net architecture integrates quantum-inspired rotations into a variational convolutional framework. The QiRE mechanism performs structured rotations within low-dimensional subspaces of the weight space, creating an ensemble effect without additional parameters. This approach enables uncertainty quantification while maintaining computational efficiency. The network processes one-dimensional phonocardiogram signals through multiple convolutional blocks, each incorporating the QiRE mechanism to capture both local temporal patterns and global uncertainty characteristics.

## Key Results
- Achieved 97.84% accuracy on PhysioNet CinC 2016 dataset
- Achieved 97.89% accuracy on PhysioNet CirCor DigiScope 2022 dataset
- Outperformed previous state-of-the-art methods on both benchmarks
- Demonstrated robustness to noise and class imbalance

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The QiRE mechanism works by applying quantum-inspired rotations to convolutional weights within structured low-dimensional subspaces. These rotations create diverse weight configurations that collectively capture uncertainty in the model's predictions. Unlike traditional ensemble methods that require multiple model instances, QiRE achieves similar diversity through weight space rotations alone, maintaining computational efficiency. The quantum-inspired aspect leverages the mathematical properties of rotations to ensure the weight variations remain meaningful and structured rather than random perturbations.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map:**
Input -> Preprocessing -> Conv Block 1 (QiRE) -> Conv Block 2 (QiRE) -> ... -> Conv Block N (QiRE) -> Pooling -> Dense Layers -> Output

**Critical Path:**
The critical path involves the sequential application of convolutional blocks with QiRE, where each block transforms the feature representation while maintaining uncertainty information. The QiRE mechanism in each block is the key differentiator from standard CNNs.

**Design Tradeoffs:**
- QiRE adds computational overhead during training but minimal overhead during inference
- The quantum-inspired approach trades theoretical simplicity for potentially better uncertainty calibration
- Parameter efficiency vs. performance: QiRE achieves ensemble-like performance without multiple model instances
- The structured rotation approach may limit exploration of weight space compared to random initialization

**Failure Signatures:**
- Training instability if rotation parameters are not properly initialized or constrained
- Loss of uncertainty quantification if QiRE is disabled or improperly implemented
- Performance degradation on datasets with significantly different characteristics from the training distribution
- Potential overfitting if the rotation mechanism creates too much diversity in the weight space

## Limitations
- Theoretical grounding for why low-dimensional subspace rotations specifically enhance uncertainty quantification remains limited
- Evaluation restricted to two specific phonocardiogram datasets, limiting generalizability to other biosignal modalities
- Computational overhead characterization is incomplete, particularly for training time and inference latency
- Cross-domain generalization to different physiological signals (ECG, EEG, EMG) has not been tested

## Confidence
- **High confidence**: Reported accuracy metrics (97.84% and 97.89%) on benchmark datasets are reproducible
- **Medium confidence**: Noise robustness claims supported but need broader testing across noise types and SNR ranges
- **Medium confidence**: Class imbalance handling demonstrated but requires validation on datasets with more severe imbalance
