# GRIT-LP: Graph Transformer with Long-Range Skip Connection and Partitioned Spatial Graphs for Accurate Ice Layer Thickness Prediction

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2511.18716
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.18716
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: 24.92% RMSE improvement over state-of-the-art methods

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces GRIT-LP, a graph transformer model designed to predict ice layer thickness in polar regions by leveraging geographical and thickness information from upper ice layers. The model addresses the challenge of accurately modeling spatiotemporal patterns in ice sheet data through two key innovations: partitioned spatial graph construction and long-range skip connections. By forming overlapping, fully connected local neighborhoods, GRIT-LP preserves spatial coherence while suppressing noise, and the long-range skip connections improve information flow and mitigate oversmoothing in deeper attention layers. Evaluated on Greenland snow radar echogram data, GRIT-LP demonstrates significant improvements in prediction accuracy compared to existing methods.

## Method Summary
GRIT-LP is a graph transformer model that predicts ice layer thickness by constructing partitioned spatial graphs from radar echogram data. The model builds overlapping local neighborhoods to maintain spatial relationships while reducing noise, then applies a graph transformer architecture with long-range skip connections to capture both local and global dependencies. The partitioned spatial graph construction strategy divides the spatial domain into overlapping regions, creating fully connected subgraphs that preserve neighborhood information. The long-range skip connections allow information to bypass intermediate layers, improving gradient flow and preventing the oversmoothing problem common in deep graph attention networks.

## Key Results
- Achieved 24.92% improvement in root mean squared error compared to state-of-the-art methods
- Successfully leverages upper ice layer information to predict deeper layer thickness
- Demonstrated effectiveness of partitioned spatial graphs in preserving spatial coherence

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The partitioned spatial graph construction strategy works by creating overlapping local neighborhoods that maintain spatial relationships while reducing noise through localized aggregation. The long-range skip connections address the oversmoothing problem in deep graph attention networks by allowing information to bypass intermediate layers, preserving both local details and global dependencies. This combination enables the model to capture complex spatiotemporal patterns in ice layer data by balancing localized structural features with long-range dependencies across internal ice layers.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map:** Input Radar Data -> Partitioned Spatial Graph Construction -> Graph Transformer with Long-Range Skip Connections -> Ice Layer Thickness Prediction

**Critical Path:** The most important processing path is from partitioned spatial graph construction through the graph transformer layers with skip connections to the final prediction layer, as this captures both spatial relationships and long-range dependencies.

**Design Tradeoffs:** The partitioned spatial graph approach trades computational efficiency for improved spatial coherence, while the long-range skip connections add computational overhead but prevent oversmoothing and improve gradient flow.

**Failure Signatures:** Model may fail when local neighborhood size is too small (losing spatial context) or too large (introducing noise), or when skip connections are absent (leading to oversmoothing and poor gradient flow).
