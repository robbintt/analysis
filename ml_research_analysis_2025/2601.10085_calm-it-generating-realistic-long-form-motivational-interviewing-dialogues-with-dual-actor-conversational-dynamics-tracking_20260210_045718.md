# CALM-IT: Generating Realistic Long-Form Motivational Interviewing Dialogues with Dual-Actor Conversational Dynamics Tracking

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2601.10085
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.10085

## Executive Summary
CALM-IT introduces a dual-actor conversational dynamics framework for generating realistic long-form Motivational Interviewing (MI) dialogues. Unlike existing models that optimize locally for the next turn, CALM-IT explicitly models both therapist and client internal states—such as rapport, emotional state, stage of change, and conversational goals—and uses these evolving states to guide strategy selection and utterance generation. Across large-scale evaluations, CALM-IT consistently outperforms baselines (KMI, CAMI+STAR, and an ablated variant) on key MI metrics including Effectiveness (4.45 vs. 2.86–4.27), Goal Alignment (4.73 vs. 2.13–4.60), and stability across conversation lengths. It also achieves the highest client acceptance rate for therapist-initiated redirections (64.3%), demonstrating more precise and therapeutically aligned intervention timing. CALM-IT's explicit state modeling enables both more effective redirections and more stable performance in extended interactions, providing evidence that modeling evolving conversational dynamics is essential for generating high-quality long-form synthetic therapeutic dialogues.

## Method Summary
CALM-IT employs a dual-actor conversational dynamics framework that explicitly models the evolving internal states of both therapist and client throughout Motivational Interviewing dialogues. The model tracks multiple dimensions including rapport, emotional state, stage of change, and conversational goals, using these states to guide both strategy selection and utterance generation. Unlike traditional approaches that optimize locally for the next turn, CALM-IT's architecture maintains and updates comprehensive state representations that evolve across the conversation. The framework integrates these state dynamics into a generation pipeline that produces therapeutically appropriate responses while maintaining coherence over extended interactions. Through systematic evaluation against multiple baselines, CALM-IT demonstrates superior performance on key MI metrics while maintaining stability across varying conversation lengths.

## Key Results
- CALM-IT achieves superior Effectiveness scores (4.45 vs. 2.86–4.27) compared to baselines
- Demonstrates highest Goal Alignment (4.73 vs. 2.13–4.60) among all evaluated approaches
- Maintains stable performance across conversation lengths with 64.3% client acceptance rate for redirections

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
CALM-IT's effectiveness stems from its explicit modeling of dual-actor conversational dynamics, where both therapist and client internal states are tracked and evolved throughout the conversation. By maintaining representations of rapport, emotional state, stage of change, and conversational goals, the model can make more contextually appropriate decisions about when and how to intervene therapeutically. This state-aware approach enables more precise timing of redirections and ensures that generated responses align with the therapeutic goals of Motivational Interviewing. The explicit state modeling also contributes to improved conversational stability across extended interactions, as the model can better maintain coherence and therapeutic focus over longer dialogue sequences.

- **MI-specific conversational metrics**: Uses specialized evaluation criteria like Effectiveness and Goal Alignment to assess therapeutic quality, critical for validating MI dialogue generation
- **State evolution mechanisms**: Employs continuous updating of internal states throughout conversations, enabling adaptation to changing therapeutic contexts
- **Redirection timing optimization**: Incorporates precise timing of therapeutic interventions based on conversational state, essential for effective MI practice
- **Long-form stability**: Maintains conversational coherence across extended interactions, addressing the challenge of dialogue consistency over time
