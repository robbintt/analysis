# Unified Multimodal Vessel Trajectory Prediction with Explainable Navigation Intention

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2511.14265
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.14265
- Authors: Rui Zhang; Chao Li; Kezhong Liu; Chen Wang; Bolong Zheng; Hongbo Jiang
- Reference count: 40
- Key outcome: DI-MTP framework achieves 18.28% ADE and 8.51% FDE improvements on Zhoushan, and 27.48% ADE and 27.40% FDE improvements on Hainan maritime datasets

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces DI-MTP, a unified multimodal trajectory prediction framework that explicitly models both sustained (stable, long-term) and transient (dynamic, short-term) navigation intentions. The method constructs sustained intention trees from historical data and uses a CVAE-based model with global non-local attention to capture interactions between intentions, enabling diverse, explainable predictions. Evaluated on real AIS datasets from Zhoushan and Hainan maritime regions, DI-MTP outperforms state-of-the-art baselines while providing clear explanation of navigational intentions for each predicted trajectory.

## Method Summary
DI-MTP is a unified multimodal trajectory prediction framework that explicitly models both sustained (stable, long-term) and transient (dynamic, short-term) navigation intentions. The method constructs sustained intention trees from historical trajectory data and uses a CVAE-based model with global non-local attention to capture interactions between intentions. This enables diverse, explainable predictions that outperform state-of-the-art baselines on real AIS datasets from Zhoushan and Hainan maritime regions.

## Key Results
- Achieves 18.28% ADE and 8.51% FDE improvements on Zhoushan maritime dataset
- Achieves 27.48% ADE and 27.40% FDE improvements on Hainan maritime dataset
- Provides clear explanation of navigational intentions for each predicted trajectory

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework's effectiveness stems from its explicit modeling of two distinct types of navigation intentions - sustained intentions captured through intention trees constructed from historical data, and transient intentions captured through CVAE-based modeling with global non-local attention. This dual-intention approach allows the model to better represent the complex decision-making processes of vessels, accounting for both long-term navigational plans and short-term reactive behaviors. The global non-local attention mechanism enables effective interaction modeling between different intention types, leading to more accurate and diverse trajectory predictions.

## Architecture Onboarding

Component Map: Historical Data -> Sustained Intention Tree -> CVAE Encoder -> Global Non-local Attention -> Latent Space -> CVAE Decoder -> Trajectory Prediction

Critical Path: Input trajectory and context → Sustained intention tree construction → CVAE encoding with attention → Latent variable sampling → Trajectory decoding → Multiple diverse predictions with explained intentions

Design Tradeoffs: The framework balances between modeling accuracy (through complex attention mechanisms) and interpretability (through explicit intention representation), while managing computational complexity for real-time applications.

Failure Signatures: Performance degradation may occur with insufficient historical data for intention tree construction, highly atypical vessel behaviors not captured in training data, or in scenarios with rapidly changing environmental conditions that weren't adequately represented in the training set.
