# A Multi-Modal Knowledge-Enhanced Framework for Vessel Trajectory Prediction

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2503.21834
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.21834
- Reference count: 29
- Primary result: 12.08%-17.86% improvement in vessel trajectory prediction accuracy

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces MAKER, a novel framework for vessel trajectory prediction using AIS data that addresses the challenges of irregular sampling intervals and complex trajectory patterns. The framework combines two key modules: an LLM-guided Knowledge Transfer (LKT) module that leverages pre-trained language models with contextual prompts, and a Knowledge-based Self-paced Learning (KSL) module that progressively integrates complex patterns during training. Experiments on two real vessel trajectory datasets demonstrate significant performance improvements over seven state-of-the-art methods.

## Method Summary
MAKER employs a dual-module approach to vessel trajectory prediction. The LLM-guided Knowledge Transfer module handles irregular sampling intervals by using pre-trained language models with contextual prompts to process and interpret maritime trajectory data. The Knowledge-based Self-paced Learning module progressively integrates complex trajectory patterns during training, starting with simpler patterns and gradually incorporating more challenging ones. This combination allows the framework to effectively manage both the temporal irregularities and spatial complexity inherent in vessel movement data.

## Key Results
- MAKER outperforms seven state-of-the-art methods by 12.08%-17.86% in prediction accuracy
- The framework demonstrates effectiveness on two real vessel trajectory datasets
- Both the LKT and KSL modules contribute to the overall performance improvement

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework works by addressing two fundamental challenges in vessel trajectory prediction: irregular sampling intervals and complex trajectory patterns. The LKT module uses language models' ability to understand context and relationships to fill temporal gaps in the data, while the KSL module ensures robust learning by progressively exposing the model to increasingly complex patterns. This combination allows MAKER to capture both short-term dynamics and long-term trajectory characteristics effectively.
