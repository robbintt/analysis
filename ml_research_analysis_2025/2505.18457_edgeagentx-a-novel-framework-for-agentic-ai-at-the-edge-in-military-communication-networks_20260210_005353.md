# EdgeAgentX: A Novel Framework for Agentic AI at the Edge in Military Communication Networks

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2505.18457
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18457
- **Reference count**: 8
- **Primary result**: Achieves 25 ms average latency and 20% higher throughput compared to baselines in military edge AI networks

## Executive Summary
EdgeAgentX presents a comprehensive framework for deploying autonomous AI agents at the tactical edge of military communication networks. The system integrates federated learning, multi-agent reinforcement learning, and adversarial defenses to enable distributed decision-making without centralized data or constant connectivity. By allowing edge devices to collaboratively learn coordinated strategies while maintaining local data privacy, EdgeAgentX addresses critical military challenges including denied connectivity, adversarial interference, and the need for real-time autonomous operation in contested environments.

## Method Summary
EdgeAgentX combines federated learning for distributed model training, multi-agent reinforcement learning for coordinated decision-making, and adversarial defenses for robust operation in contested environments. The framework enables edge devices to learn collaboratively without sharing raw data, achieving faster convergence and better performance than independent learning approaches. The system incorporates real-time adaptation mechanisms and threat detection to maintain performance under adversarial conditions, with a focus on achieving low-latency responses suitable for military tactical scenarios.

## Key Results
- Achieves 25 ms average latency and 20% higher throughput compared to baseline approaches
- Converges 2x faster to effective policies than independent learning methods
- Maintains stable performance under adversarial attacks with only 5% degradation versus 20% without defenses

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework's effectiveness stems from its multi-layered approach to distributed intelligence. Federated learning enables collaborative knowledge sharing without compromising data privacy or requiring continuous connectivity. Multi-agent reinforcement learning allows devices to learn coordinated strategies that outperform isolated decision-making. The integration of adversarial defenses provides resilience against attacks that could otherwise degrade system performance. This combination addresses the fundamental tension between distributed operation (needed for military resilience) and the benefits of coordinated learning (needed for optimal performance).
