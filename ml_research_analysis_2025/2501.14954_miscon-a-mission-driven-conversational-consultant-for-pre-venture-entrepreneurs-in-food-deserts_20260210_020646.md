# MISCON: A Mission-Driven Conversational Consultant for Pre-Venture Entrepreneurs in Food Deserts

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2501.14954
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.14954
- Reference count: 3
- Primary result: MISCON provides accessible, context-aware business guidance to underserved food desert entrepreneurs using state-space models and adaptive intent management

## Executive Summary
MISCON is a conversational AI system designed to support aspiring food entrepreneurs in underserved food desert communities by providing accessible business guidance through natural dialogue. The system addresses the critical gap in business consulting resources for pre-venture entrepreneurs who often lack access to traditional business support services. By leveraging a heterogeneous knowledge graph, large language models, and adaptive intent management, MISCON guides users through essential business milestones while dynamically adjusting to their education level and conversational context. The system aims to balance factual accuracy, user-level interpretability, responsiveness, and cost-effectiveness in complex multi-session dialogues.

## Method Summary
MISCON employs a state-space model architecture that integrates a heterogeneous knowledge graph with large language models (LLMs) to provide context-aware business guidance. The system uses adaptive intent management to track user progress through business milestones and maintain conversational context across multiple sessions. A key innovation is the dynamic adjustment of responses based on user education level, ensuring accessibility for diverse populations. The knowledge graph captures domain-specific business concepts and relationships, while the LLM component generates responses that are both informative and interpretable. The system processes user inputs through intent recognition, contextual understanding, and response generation modules that work in concert to provide relevant guidance.

## Key Results
- F1 scores exceeding 0.9 for conversational intent recognition demonstrate high accuracy in understanding user queries
- Dynamic response adjustment based on user education level shows system adaptability to diverse user populations
- Integration of heterogeneous knowledge graph with LLMs enables context-aware business guidance across multiple conversational sessions

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The system's effectiveness stems from its multi-layered approach to conversational AI that combines structured knowledge representation with flexible language generation. The heterogeneous knowledge graph provides a solid foundation of business concepts and relationships specific to food entrepreneurship, ensuring factual accuracy and domain relevance. LLMs add the flexibility to generate natural, context-appropriate responses that can adapt to user education levels and conversational nuances. Adaptive intent management maintains coherence across sessions by tracking user progress through business milestones and preserving conversational context. This combination allows MISCON to balance the competing demands of accuracy, interpretability, responsiveness, and cost-effectiveness that are critical for mission-driven applications in underserved communities.

## Architecture Onboarding

Component Map: User Input -> Intent Recognition -> Context Management -> Knowledge Graph Query -> LLM Response Generation -> Output Adjustment -> User Output

Critical Path: The system processes user inputs through intent recognition, which triggers context management to maintain conversational state. Context management queries the heterogeneous knowledge graph for relevant business concepts, then passes this information to the LLM for response generation. The output adjustment module ensures responses are appropriately tailored to the user's education level before delivery.

Design Tradeoffs: The architecture balances structured knowledge (for accuracy) against flexible generation (for naturalness), with the knowledge graph providing factual grounding while LLMs handle conversational fluidity. The adaptive intent management trades computational complexity for improved context awareness and user experience. Response adjustment based on education level prioritizes accessibility over maximum technical detail.

Failure Signatures: Poor intent recognition leads to irrelevant responses; knowledge graph gaps result in missing or incorrect information; LLM hallucinations introduce factual errors; inadequate context management causes repetitive or contradictory responses; improper education-level adjustment makes content too complex or overly simplified.
