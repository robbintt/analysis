# Contraction and entropy production in continuous-time Sinkhorn dynamics

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2510.12639
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.12639
- Reference count: 40
- This paper establishes a formal thermodynamic structure for the Sinkhorn flow by identifying it as the gradient flow of entropy in a nonlocal Wasserstein space.

## Executive Summary
This paper establishes a formal thermodynamic structure for the Sinkhorn flow by interpreting the continuous-time limit of the Sinkhorn algorithm as a mirror descent in the space of probability measures. The dynamics of the free marginal are governed by a nonlocal diffusion operator, leading to exponential contraction in the Fisher-Rao metric under certain coercivity conditions. The work proves a Poincaré inequality for the associated Dirichlet form and establishes that entropy decay is exponential if and only if a logarithmic Sobolev inequality holds, with the entropy decay rate exactly equal to the negative of the Fisher information.

## Method Summary
The method interprets continuous-time Sinkhorn dynamics as mirror descent on the set of couplings with fixed marginals. Starting from the entropic optimal transport problem, the authors derive the primal-dual dynamics where the joint distribution evolves according to a Gibbs kernel modulated by the dual potential. The free marginal satisfies a nonlocal diffusion equation involving conditional expectation operators. The approach leverages tools from diffusion geometry and information theory to establish contraction properties and entropy production rates, connecting optimal transport with classical results on diffusion processes.

## Key Results
- Sinkhorn flow is exponentially contracting in the Fisher-Rao metric under coercivity conditions
- Entropy decay rate equals the negative of the Fisher information (de Bruijn identity)
- Poincaré inequality holds with constant related to spectral gap of nonlocal operator
- Logarithmic Sobolev inequality is equivalent to exponential entropy decay

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The mechanism works because the Sinkhorn algorithm, when viewed as mirror descent in probability space, naturally produces dynamics that preserve the constraint of fixed marginals while minimizing entropy. The continuous-time limit creates a gradient flow structure where the entropy functional decreases along trajectories. The nonlocal diffusion operator captures the essential coupling between marginals, and its spectral properties determine contraction rates. The thermodynamic structure emerges because the dynamics can be written as a gradient flow of entropy with respect to a Wasserstein-like metric that incorporates the entropic regularization.

Marginals (μ,ν) -> Cost function c(x,y) -> Entropic OT problem -> Sinkhorn kernel -> Dual potentials -> Continuous flow dynamics -> Entropy decay/contraction

**Critical path:**
The essential computation path is: marginals → cost → entropic coupling → dual potentials → primal evolution → entropy monitoring. The entropy decay identity serves as the primary diagnostic for correctness.

**Design tradeoffs:**
The entropic regularization parameter ε controls the tradeoff between computational tractability and fidelity to the original optimal transport problem. Larger ε yields smoother dynamics but weaker convergence to the unregularized solution.

**Failure signatures:**
- If the Poincaré constant approaches 1, the dynamics lack exponential contraction
- Numerical instability occurs when ε → 0 due to singular kernels
- Poor discretization resolution leads to violation of positivity assumptions

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out

**Open Question 1**
- Question: What are the microscopic details (particle-level stochastic processes) of a physical system that implements the entropic gradient flow defined by the nonlocal Onsager operator?
- Basis in paper: Page 25 states, "The microscopic details [43] of a physical system which implements the entropic gradient flow (92) remain open."
- Why unresolved: The paper establishes macroscopic thermodynamic structure but not the specific underlying microscopic dynamics
- What evidence would resolve it: Derivation of a many-particle stochastic process whose large-scale limit recovers the nonlocal diffusion equation

**Open Question 2**
- Question: How can the Poincaré constant be quantitatively related to the entropic regularization parameter ε and the specific geometry of the imposed marginals?
- Basis in paper: Page 25 lists need to "quantitatively relate the Poincaré constant (107) and the entropic regularization parameter ε and imposed marginals"
- Why unresolved: While existence of positive Poincaré constant is proven, explicit scaling relationships are not provided
- What evidence would resolve it: Explicit inequalities showing functional dependence of convergence rate λ on ε and marginal properties

**Open Question 3**
- Question: How can the integral curvature condition Γ₂(u) ≥ λΓ(u) be interpreted in terms of the properties of the imposed marginal densities e⁻ᵁ and e⁻ⱽ?
- Basis in paper: Page 25 lists need to "interpret the curvature-like condition (127) in terms of the imposed marginals e⁻ᵁ, e⁻ⱽ"
- Why unresolved: The integral condition differs from standard differential curvature criteria and its geometric meaning is unclear
- What evidence would resolve it: Theorem connecting standard geometric properties of marginals to satisfaction of nonlocal Γ-Γ₂ condition
