# A Theoretical Framework for Discovering Groups and Unitary Representations via Tensor Factorization

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2511.23152
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.23152
- Reference count: 9

## Executive Summary
This paper develops a theoretical framework for the HyperCube model, which discovers group structures and their unitary representations through tensor factorization. The key insight is decomposing the optimization objective into base term B (regulating factor scales) and misalignment term R (enforcing directional alignment), creating a tractable analysis space. The framework proves that the collinear manifold R=0 admits feasible solutions exclusively for group isotopes and that these achieve strictly minimal objective values among all quasigroups of fixed order.

## Method Summary
The method analyzes HyperCube's operator-valued tensor factorization by decomposing its objective H into base term B and misalignment term R. This decomposition isolates the collinear manifold R=0 where theoretical analysis becomes tractable. The framework proves that any unitary collinear factorization is unitarily equivalent to the group's regular representation, and that group isotopes achieve the minimal objective value H_min = 3|δ|. The approach provides a differentiable proxy for group structure discovery through the H_min metric.

## Key Results
- Any unitary collinear factorization is unitarily equivalent to the group's regular representation
- Group isotopes achieve strictly minimal objective value H_min = 3|δ| among quasigroups of fixed order n
- Empirical scaling shows misalignment R dominates potential savings in B, with H(δ) ≈ 3+0.36·nv(δ)

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework works by decomposing the complex optimization landscape into tractable components. By isolating the collinear manifold R=0, the analysis becomes manageable while preserving the essential properties needed for group recovery. The base term B creates variational pressure that drives factors toward unitarity, while the misalignment term R enforces directional consistency. This decomposition allows proving that group structures emerge naturally as optimal solutions.

## Limitations
- The theoretical framework focuses on the restrictive collinear manifold R=0, which may not represent typical optimization convergence
- The variational pressure driving toward unitarity needs empirical validation in practical scenarios
- The framework's generalization to non-discrete group structures and semigroups remains unverified

## Confidence

- Group isotopes achieve minimal H_min: Medium
- Empirical scaling laws showing R dominates B: Medium
