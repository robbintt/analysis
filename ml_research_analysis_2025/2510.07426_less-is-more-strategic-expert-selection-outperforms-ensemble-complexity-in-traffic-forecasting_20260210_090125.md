# Less is More: Strategic Expert Selection Outperforms Ensemble Complexity in Traffic Forecasting

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2510.07426
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.07426
- Authors: Walid Guettala; Yufan Zhao; László Gulyás
- Reference count: 26
- Key outcome: Strategic expert selection outperforms ensemble complexity in traffic forecasting

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of traffic forecasting in intelligent transportation systems, where existing mixture-of-experts approaches like TESTAM lack explicit incorporation of physical road network topology, limiting spatial modeling capabilities. The authors propose TESTAM+, an enhanced spatio-temporal forecasting framework that introduces a novel SpatioSemantic Expert integrating physical road topology with data-driven feature similarity through hybrid graph construction. Their comprehensive ablation studies reveal that strategic expert selection fundamentally outperforms naive ensemble aggregation, with individual experts demonstrating remarkable effectiveness.

The findings establish that fewer, strategically designed experts outperform complex multi-expert ensembles, providing superior computational efficiency for real-time deployment. The Identity + Adaptive configuration achieves an 11.5% MAE reduction compared to state-of-the-art MegaCRN on METR-LA while reducing inference latency by 53.1% compared to the full four-expert TESTAM+. This challenges the conventional wisdom that more experts always yield better performance in mixture-of-experts architectures.

## Method Summary
The authors enhance the TESTAM framework by introducing a SpatioSemantic Expert that explicitly incorporates physical road network topology alongside data-driven feature similarity. The proposed TESTAM+ framework constructs hybrid graphs that combine traditional data-driven similarity metrics with physical road connectivity information. The framework employs an expert selection mechanism that dynamically routes inputs to the most appropriate expert based on both spatial and temporal characteristics. The ablation studies systematically evaluate various expert configurations to identify optimal combinations that balance performance and computational efficiency.

## Key Results
- Identity Expert achieves 1.63 MAE on PEMS-BAY, matching Adaptive Expert performance
- Optimal Identity + Adaptive configuration achieves 11.5% MAE reduction vs. MegaCRN on METR-LA (2.99 vs. 3.38)
- 53.1% inference latency reduction achieved with optimal configuration vs. full four-expert TESTAM+
- Individual experts demonstrate remarkable effectiveness, outperforming naive ensemble aggregation

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The success of strategic expert selection stems from the principle that specialized, well-designed experts can outperform generalized ensembles when properly matched to specific input characteristics. The SpatioSemantic Expert's integration of physical road topology addresses a fundamental limitation in previous approaches that relied solely on data-driven similarity, enabling more accurate spatial modeling of traffic patterns. The adaptive routing mechanism ensures that inputs are processed by the most suitable expert, avoiding the computational overhead of processing through all experts while maintaining or improving accuracy.

## Foundational Learning

- Why needed: Enables dynamic routing of inputs to specialized sub-networks for improved efficiency
- Quick check: Verify that gating mechanism properly balances load across experts

**Spatio-temporal Graph Neural Networks**
- Why needed: Captures both spatial dependencies (road network topology) and temporal patterns in traffic data
- Quick check: Ensure graph convolutions properly aggregate information from neighboring nodes

**Hybrid Graph Construction**
- Why needed: Combines physical infrastructure constraints with learned data patterns for robust modeling
- Quick check: Validate that hybrid graph maintains connectivity while incorporating semantic similarities

**Adaptive Expert Selection**
- Why needed: Routes inputs to most appropriate expert based on input characteristics
- Quick check: Monitor routing decisions to ensure balanced expert utilization

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**
TESTAM+ -> SpatioSemantic Expert + Identity Expert + Adaptive Expert + Routing Mechanism

**Critical Path**
Input Data -> Routing Mechanism -> Selected Expert(s) -> Output Prediction

**Design Tradeoffs**
The framework trades ensemble diversity for computational efficiency, accepting potential blind spots in exchange for faster inference and lower resource consumption. The strategic selection of only two experts (Identity + Adaptive) provides near-optimal performance while significantly reducing complexity.

**Failure Signatures**
Performance degradation occurs when traffic patterns fall outside the specialization domains of the selected experts, particularly for unusual congestion scenarios not well-represented in training data. The framework may also struggle with rapidly changing road network topologies.

## Limitations
- Results may not generalize beyond urban traffic networks with specific characteristics
- No detailed energy consumption or memory usage analysis for edge computing deployment
- Computational trade-off analysis lacks comprehensive real-world deployment validation

## Confidence
**High confidence**: Core claim supported by comprehensive ablation studies and statistically significant performance improvements (11.5% MAE reduction, 53.1% latency reduction)
**Medium confidence**: Generalizability beyond tested datasets uncertain due to focus on specific urban traffic networks
**Low confidence**: Computational trade-off analysis incomplete without energy consumption and memory usage measurements

## Next Checks
1. Cross-domain validation: Test framework on non-transportation spatio-temporal datasets (weather forecasting, power grid monitoring)
2. Real-time deployment stress testing: Implement on embedded systems with varying computational constraints
3. Long-term stability analysis: Conduct multi-month continuous evaluation to assess performance degradation over time
