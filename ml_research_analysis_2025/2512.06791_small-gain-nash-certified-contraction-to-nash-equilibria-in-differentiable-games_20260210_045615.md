# Small-Gain Nash: Certified Contraction to Nash Equilibria in Differentiable Games

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2512.06791
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.06791
- Reference count: 40
- Key outcome: Introduces SGN, a structural condition certifying convergence to Nash equilibria in differentiable games via block-weighted metrics and explicit step-size bounds.

## Executive Summary
This paper presents Small-Gain Nash (SGN), a novel structural condition that certifies convergence to Nash equilibria in differentiable games. SGN transforms player-wise curvature and cross-player coupling bounds into a matrix inequality that guarantees contraction in a tailored geometry. By designing a block-weighted metric, the pseudo-gradient becomes strongly monotone, enabling explicit step-size bounds for both projected Euler and RK4 discretizations. The method identifies a finite "timescale band" where a single-step-size dynamics is provably contractive, contrasting with asymptotic two-timescale update rules.

## Method Summary
The SGN framework certifies convergence by constructing a block-weighted metric in which the pseudo-gradient is strongly monotone. Player-wise curvature and coupling bounds are assembled into a matrix inequality, whose feasibility yields explicit step-size bounds for both projected Euler and RK4 discretizations. The approach identifies a finite timescale band for safe step sizes, contrasting with asymptotic two-timescale approaches. Empirical validation shows SGN successfully certifies convergence in non-monotone quadratic games where Euclidean analysis fails, and extends to mirror/Fisher geometries for entropy-regularized policy gradient in Markov games.

## Key Results
- SGN certifies convergence to Nash equilibria in non-monotone quadratic games where Euclidean analysis fails.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
SGN works by designing a block-weighted metric where the pseudo-gradient becomes strongly monotone. This transformation converts player-wise curvature and cross-player coupling bounds into a tractable matrix inequality that guarantees contraction. The method leverages the geometry of the game dynamics to identify safe step sizes and convergence rates that are not apparent in standard Euclidean analysis.

## Limitations
- SGN relies on accurate estimation of curvature, coupling, and Lipschitz parameters within a compact region; if dynamics leave this region, the certificate may fail.
- The "timescale band" is derived from conservative bounds and may not reflect actual convergence speed.
- The mirror geometry extension is only illustrated for entropy-regularized policy gradients; broader applicability remains untested.
- Numerical verification of the block-weight optimization step is not provided.

## Confidence
- High: The theoretical derivation of SGN as a sufficient condition for contraction in the block-weighted metric.
- Medium: The empirical validation on non-monotone quadratic games and the RK4 discretization step-size guarantees.
- Low: The generalizability of the mirror/Fisher geometry results beyond the entropy case and the robustness of the offline certification pipeline to inaccurate parameter estimates.
