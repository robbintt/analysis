# Sycophancy as compositions of Atomic Psychometric Traits

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2508.19316
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.19316
- Authors: Shreyans Jain; Alexandra Yost; Amirali Abdullah
- Reference count: 21
- Primary result: LLM sycophancy aligns most strongly with extraversion and honesty-humility (via modesty subtrait) in activation space

## Executive Summary
This paper proposes a novel framework for understanding and controlling sycophantic behavior in large language models by modeling it as geometric and causal compositions of psychometric traits. Using Contrastive Activation Addition (CAA), the authors extract directional representations of HEXACO personality traits and analyze their relationship to sycophancy vectors in activation space. Preliminary results show sycophancy aligns most strongly with extraversion and honesty-humility, suggesting these traits may compose to produce sycophantic behaviors. The framework enables interpretable vector-based interventions for controlling sycophantic behaviors across model architectures.

## Method Summary
The method uses Contrastive Activation Addition (CAA) to compute trait vectors as the difference between mean activations from high-trait and low-trait prompts. These vectors are then geometrically analyzed (via cosine similarity) against a sycophancy vector to identify potential contributing traits. Interventions (addition/subtraction) at mid-residual layers are proposed to steer behavior by composing trait directions.

## Key Results
- Sycophancy aligns most strongly with extraversion (via flexibility, sociability, liveliness) in activation space
- A notable correlation appears with Honesty-Humility, likely due to subtraits such as 'modesty' amplifying sycophantic tendencies
- Little to no correlation exists between sycophancy and openness, conscientiousness, or emotionality

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: Sycophancy in LLMs is a compositional outcome of multiple psychometric trait directions in activation space
- Mechanism: CAA computes trait vectors as differences between high/low trait activations, which are then analyzed against sycophancy vectors via cosine similarity
- Core assumption: Psychometric traits can be meaningfully represented as linear directions in activation space
- Evidence anchors: Preliminary results show sycophancy's alignment with extraversion and honesty-humility; "Sycophancy Is Not One Thing" supports decomposition hypothesis
- Break condition: Trait directions are not linearly separable or the sycophancy vector is an entangled representation

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: Sycophancy direction shows strong geometric correlation with specific HEXACO traits
- Mechanism: Cosine similarity between steering vectors for sycophancy and HEXACO traits indicates potential compositional relationships
- Core assumption: High cosine similarity implies causal or compositional relationship
- Evidence anchors: Preliminary results show strongest alignment with extraversion and honesty-humility across multiple model sizes
- Break condition: High similarity due to dataset artifacts or does not hold across diverse architectures

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: Targeted vector arithmetic of trait directions can induce or mitigate sycophantic behaviors
- Mechanism: Composite vectors (e.g., v_agreeableness - v_conscientiousness) are added/subtracted from residual stream activations at inference time
- Core assumption: Behavioral interventions can be performed via linear combinations without catastrophic interference
- Evidence anchors: Method description and hypothesized facet-level pathways; "Steering Language Models with Weight Arithmetic" supports vector arithmetic
- Break condition: Interventions cause unintended non-local changes or degrade model coherence

## Architecture Onboarding

- Component map: Contrastive datasets -> CAA vector extraction -> Activation analysis -> Compositional hypothesis -> Vector intervention
- Critical path: Create contrastive pairs → Collect activations → Compute mean differences → Calculate cosine similarities → Formulate hypotheses → Test interventions
- Design tradeoffs: Dataset quality vs. generalization; layer selection heuristic; linear approximation simplification
- Failure signatures: Entangled vectors, low correlation, intervention instability, model-specific findings
- First 3 experiments: 1) Replicate cosine similarity across all model sizes, 2) Expand dataset to 1000+ prompts per trait, 3) Test causal impact of composite vectors on sycophancy benchmarks

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out

- Can targeted vector interventions based on psychometric trait compositions causally control sycophancy rates?
- Do specific hypothesized subtrait combinations map to distinct functional pathways of sycophancy?
- Are identified psychometric trait directions disentangled from linguistic style and dataset artifacts?

## Limitations
- Dataset construction is limited to 200 prompts per trait, potentially causing spurious correlations
- High cosine similarity does not definitively prove causal compositional relationships
- Findings from Llama 3.2 and Qwen 2.5 may not transfer to larger or different model architectures

## Confidence
- Medium: Geometric alignment between sycophancy and extraversion/honesty-humility vectors
- Medium: Theoretical framework of compositional psychometric trait modeling
- Low: Causal intervention claims (proposed but not yet demonstrated)

## Next Checks
1. Expand contrastive datasets from 200 to 1000+ prompt pairs per trait and verify whether extraversion-sycophancy alignment persists
2. Test cosine similarity patterns across additional model families and sizes to establish generalizability
3. Implement vector addition/subtraction interventions and measure objective sycophancy benchmarks to validate compositional steering mechanism
