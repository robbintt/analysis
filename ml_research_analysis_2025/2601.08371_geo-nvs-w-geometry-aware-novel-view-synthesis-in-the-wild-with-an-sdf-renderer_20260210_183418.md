# Geo-NVS-w: Geometry-Aware Novel View Synthesis In-the-Wild with an SDF Renderer

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2601.08371
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.08371
- Reference count: 21
- Primary result: Achieves PSNR of 25.40 dB on Brandenburg Gate scene with 4-5× lower energy consumption than baselines

## Executive Summary
Geo-NVS-w introduces a geometry-aware framework for novel view synthesis from in-the-wild image collections, addressing the challenge of geometric inconsistencies and artifacts in existing volumetric methods. The core idea is to use a Signed Distance Function (SDF) as a geometric backbone to guide rendering, paired with an octree-based feature volume for efficiency. A novel Geometry-Preservation Loss ensures that fine structural details are not eroded by transient mask networks. The method achieves competitive rendering quality, with PSNR values reaching 25.40 dB on the Brandenburg Gate scene, while demonstrating a 4-5× reduction in energy consumption compared to similar methods, completing training in 2.05 kWh versus 7.71–9.7 kWh for baselines. The approach produces photorealistic results with sharp, geometrically coherent details.

## Method Summary
Geo-NVS-w combines a Signed Distance Function (SDF) renderer with an octree-based feature volume to achieve geometry-aware novel view synthesis from in-the-wild images. The framework uses a NeuS formulation where density is derived from SDF values, concentrating color integration around surfaces for sharper geometry. A Geometry-Preservation Loss (GPL) penalizes transient masks from removing high-curvature static structures, while dual octrees (SDF foreground + NeRF background) enable efficient sparse computation. The system includes per-image appearance embeddings for handling illumination variations and a CGNet-based transient mask network. Training employs a composite loss with eikonal, curvature, and Lipschitz regularization, achieving 300K iterations on a single NVIDIA A10G GPU with mixed precision.

## Key Results
- Achieves PSNR of 25.40 dB on Brandenburg Gate scene
- Reduces energy consumption by 4-5× compared to NeRF-W (2.05 kWh vs 7.71-9.7 kWh)
- Produces sharp, geometrically coherent details in novel views
- Demonstrates competitive quality on Sacré-Cœur (23.23 dB), Trevi Fountain (22.49 dB), and Taj Mahal (22.54 dB)

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1: SDF-Constrained Surface Rendering
The framework replaces volumetric density with a Signed Distance Function (SDF) to reduce geometric ambiguity and minimize semi-transparent artifacts. The SDF value directly determines alpha through a sigmoid function, forcing color accumulation within a narrow band around the surface. This hard-codes a "surface prior" into the rendering integral, ensuring geometrically consistent views. This works well for scenes with clear, well-defined surfaces like Brandenburg Gate spires or Trevi Fountain basin.

### Mechanism 2: Geometry-Preservation Loss (GPL)
The GPL explicitly penalizes the transient mask network for activating on high-curvature geometry, preventing the "erosion" of fine structural details. It calculates a penalty proportional to the product of the transient mask and an edge indicator derived from SDF gradients and curvature. This forces the mask to remain low when rays traverse geometric edges, preserving permanent scene structures. The mechanism assumes geometric edges correspond to static features rather than transient objects.

### Mechanism 3: Octree-Based Sparse Acceleration
The dual octree structure (SDF foreground + NeRF background) reduces energy consumption and training time by restricting computation to occupied space. Rays traverse the octree, skipping empty voxels entirely, with features interpolated only within occupied leaf nodes. This reduces FLOPs per ray, directly translating to lower GPU energy draw. The approach assumes scenes are sparsely populated, allowing significant gains over dense representations.

## Architecture Onboarding

- **Component map**: Ray origin/direction + Image Index (appearance code) -> Dual Sparse Octrees (Foreground SDF + Background NeRF) -> Small MLPs for SDF s(x), Color c(x,d), Deviation Network for ζ -> CGNet transient mask + Geometry-Preservation Logic -> RGB Color + Depth

- **Critical path**: 1. Ray Sampling: March rays through dual octrees, 2. Feature Lookup: Interpolate features from octree nodes (Trilinear), 3. Geometry Decode: MLP predicts SDF value s, 4. Alpha Conversion: Convert SDF s to Alpha α using Eq. 1 (requires finite differences for normals), 5. Compositing: Accumulate color C(r) using weights w_i, 6. Regularization: Apply LGPL (Mask * Curvature) and Eikonal Loss (||∇s|| - 1)

- **Design tradeoffs**: SDF vs. Density trades flexibility (handling fog/transparency) for geometric sharpness; Octree vs. Hash Grid offers structured sparsity vs. lookup speed in dense regions; MLP downsizing relies on octree features doing heavy lifting

- **Failure signatures**: "Floater" artifacts (clouds of geometry in empty space if Eikonal loss too weak), Mask Erosion (sharp corners disappear if LGPL inactive), Geometry Collapse (SDF degenerates into blob if sphere initialization skipped)

- **First 3 experiments**: 1. Ablation of GPL: Train on Brandenburg Gate with LGPL=0, compare sharpness of gate spires/railings vs. full model, 2. Energy Profile: Log GPU power draw (kWh) for 50k iterations on NeRF-W baseline vs. Geo-NVS-w to verify 4-5x efficiency claim, 3. Transient Mask Visualization: Render mask M(r) and Geometry Map Φ(r) side-by-side to verify GPL behavior

## Limitations
- Sensitivity to occluder coverage: If transient objects persist in many views, SDF formation becomes unstable
- Potential artifacts in translucent regions: SDF hard-constrains surface, limiting handling of fog or transparent materials
- Reliance on adequate training data density: Stable octree feature learning requires sufficient image coverage of scene geometry

## Confidence
- Geometric constraint via SDF: **High confidence** for scenes with clear, well-defined surfaces
- GPL mechanism: **Medium confidence** - novel but ablation shows clear degradation when disabled
- Octree efficiency claim: **Medium confidence** - 4-5× energy reduction is plausible but depends on implementation details
