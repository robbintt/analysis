# From Imperfect Signals to Trustworthy Structure: Confidence-Aware Inference from Heterogeneous and Reliability-Varying Utility Data

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2508.05791
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.05791
- Reference count: 23
- Primary result: >95% topology reconstruction accuracy validated on 3 feeders with over 8000 meters

## Executive Summary
This paper presents a scalable framework for distribution grid topology identification that integrates heterogeneous data sources while handling data quality issues. The core innovation is a confidence-aware inference mechanism that preserves imperfect but structurally informative inputs and quantifies reliability of each inferred connection. Physical feasibility is enforced through embedded operational constraints like transformer capacity limits and radial topology requirements. The method combines geographical data with electrical measurements, uses outlier detection to identify and reconnect misassigned meters, and applies clustering and mutual information analysis for reconnection.

## Method Summary
The framework combines geographical data with electrical measurements to identify distribution grid topology while handling data quality issues. It employs a confidence-aware inference mechanism that preserves imperfect but structurally informative inputs and quantifies reliability of each inferred connection. The method enforces physical feasibility through operational constraints including transformer capacity limits and radial topology requirements. Outlier detection identifies misassigned meters, which are then reconnected using clustering and mutual information analysis.

## Key Results
- Achieved over 95% topology reconstruction accuracy on validation feeders with more than 8000 meters
- Improved confidence calibration while maintaining high accuracy
- Demonstrated 70% computational efficiency gains compared to baseline methods

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework succeeds by integrating heterogeneous data sources while explicitly handling data quality variations through confidence-aware inference. Rather than discarding imperfect data, it quantifies and preserves the reliability of each input, allowing the system to leverage all available information while understanding its limitations. The combination of geographical data with electrical measurements provides complementary information that improves overall accuracy. Physical constraints are embedded directly into the inference process, ensuring that results are operationally feasible.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Geographical data -> Outlier detection -> Electrical measurements -> Confidence weighting -> Clustering & mutual information -> Physical constraint enforcement -> Topology reconstruction

**Critical Path**: Data input → Outlier detection → Confidence-aware inference → Physical constraint enforcement → Topology output

**Design Tradeoffs**: The framework prioritizes accuracy and reliability over computational speed, though it still achieves 70% efficiency gains. It accepts the complexity of handling multiple data types to leverage all available information rather than simplifying inputs at the cost of accuracy.

**Failure Signatures**: Poor performance on highly noisy datasets, failure to reconnect meters when outlier detection thresholds are too strict, and inability to handle non-radial topologies due to constraint enforcement.

**First Experiments**:
1. Validate outlier detection accuracy on synthetic datasets with known misassignments

3. Evaluate physical constraint enforcement on grids with known operational limits

## Limitations
- Framework performance in real-world deployment scenarios with active reconfiguration events remains uncertain
- Scalability claims require validation across diverse grid topologies and sizes beyond the tested feeders
- Confidence calibration methodology's effectiveness in capturing all uncertainty sources needs further investigation

## Confidence

**High Confidence**: Topology reconstruction accuracy (>95%) on validation feeders, computational efficiency improvements (70%), and physical feasibility enforcement through operational constraints

**Medium Confidence**: Confidence-aware inference mechanism performance across diverse operational conditions, generalization to different grid architectures

**Low Confidence**: Long-term stability of the framework under continuous operational stress and adaptation to emerging grid technologies

## Next Checks
