# Generative Discovery of Partial Differential Equations by Learning from Math Handbooks

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2505.05869
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.05869
- Reference count: 40
- Proposed knowledge-guided generative framework, EqGPT, for discovering PDEs from data by integrating prior knowledge from mathematical handbooks

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces EqGPT, a knowledge-guided generative framework for discovering partial differential equations from data by leveraging prior knowledge from mathematical handbooks. The method encodes existing PDEs into structured sequences to guide the generation of free-form PDEs through an iterative loop of generation, evaluation, and optimization. Experiments show high accuracy and efficiency in discovering both canonical and complex PDEs, even from sparse and noisy data in irregular domains and high dimensions. Notably, the framework successfully uncovered a new PDE governing nonlinear surface gravity waves from real-world experimental data, demonstrating its potential for genuine scientific discovery.

## Method Summary
EqGPT employs a generative framework that integrates existing PDEs from mathematical handbooks as prior knowledge to guide the discovery of new PDEs from data. The approach uses structured sequence encoding of known equations and iteratively generates candidate PDEs through a loop of generation, evaluation, and optimization. The method is designed to work effectively with sparse and noisy data, and can handle complex scenarios including irregular domains and high-dimensional problems. The framework balances search space coverage with optimization efficiency, outperforming both traditional numerical methods and large language model approaches in PDE discovery tasks.

## Key Results
- High accuracy and efficiency in discovering canonical and complex PDEs from sparse and noisy data
- Successful identification of a new PDE governing nonlinear surface gravity waves from real-world experimental data
- Superior performance compared to traditional and LLM-based methods in both synthetic and real-world discovery scenarios

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method works by constraining the search space through encoded prior knowledge from mathematical handbooks, which guides the generative process toward physically meaningful and mathematically consistent PDE structures. The iterative loop of generation, evaluation, and optimization allows the framework to refine candidates based on data fit while maintaining adherence to known mathematical patterns. This approach effectively balances exploration of the solution space with exploitation of established mathematical relationships, making it particularly effective for discovering PDEs that share structural similarities with known equations while remaining open to novel formulations.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Data Input -> Handbook Knowledge Encoding -> Generative Model -> Evaluation Module -> Optimization Loop -> PDE Output

**Critical Path**: The core workflow follows a continuous loop: Generative Model produces PDE candidates → Evaluation Module assesses fit to data and physical plausibility → Optimization Loop refines parameters and structure → PDE Output delivers final equation

**Design Tradeoffs**: The framework balances between strict adherence to handbook knowledge (which improves efficiency but may limit novelty) and open-ended generation (which allows discovery but increases search space complexity). The iterative evaluation process trades computational cost for accuracy and physical consistency.

**Failure Signatures**: 
- Overfitting to handbook patterns when encountering data from entirely new physical regimes
- Convergence to local optima when evaluation criteria are too restrictive
- Poor performance with extremely sparse or noisy data that obscures underlying patterns

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out
The paper highlights the need for independent verification of its claim regarding the discovery of a new PDE governing nonlinear surface gravity waves, as this represents a significant scientific finding. It also raises questions about the framework's ability to discover truly unprecedented PDEs that operate under entirely different principles, given its dependence on existing handbook equations as prior knowledge. The potential bias toward rediscovering variations of known equations rather than identifying fundamentally new mathematical structures is noted as an area requiring further investigation.

## Limitations
- Dependence on existing handbook equations may bias discoveries toward variations of known PDEs rather than fundamentally new structures
- The significant claim of discovering a new PDE requires independent verification to confirm the finding
- Performance on truly unprecedented physical phenomena remains uncertain due to the knowledge-guided approach
