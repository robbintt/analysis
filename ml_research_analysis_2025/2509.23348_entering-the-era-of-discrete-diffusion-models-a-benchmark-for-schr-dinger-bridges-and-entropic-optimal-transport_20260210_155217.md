# Entering the Era of Discrete Diffusion Models: A Benchmark for Schrödinger Bridges and Entropic Optimal Transport

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID:** 2509.23348
- **Source URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.23348
- **Reference count:** 34
- **Primary result:** Introduces first benchmark for evaluating discrete Schrödinger bridge methods using rank-1 CP decomposition, demonstrating DLightSB's superior performance on high-dimensional Gaussian mixture benchmarks

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of evaluating discrete Schrödinger bridge (SB) methods by introducing the first comprehensive benchmark with analytically known solutions. The authors construct probability distribution pairs using rank-1 Canonical Polyadic decomposition, enabling efficient computation and sampling in high-dimensional discrete spaces. Building on this framework, they develop two novel discrete SB solvers - DLightSB and DLightSB-M - and an extension of the Categorical SB Matching method. Experimental results demonstrate that DLightSB consistently outperforms existing methods on synthetic benchmarks, achieving conditional Shape Scores and Trend Scores above 0.97 across various dimensions.

## Method Summary
The authors introduce a novel benchmark construction methodology using rank-1 Canonical Polyadic (CP) decomposition to parameterize the scalar-valued function v* in discrete Schrödinger bridge problems. This approach enables the creation of analytically tractable solution pairs of probability distributions. Building on this framework, they develop DLightSB, which directly learns the CP parameters through a neural network architecture, and DLightSB-M, which extends to dynamic setups. The benchmark also includes α-CSBM, an extension of the Categorical SB Matching method. The evaluation is conducted on high-dimensional Gaussian mixture benchmarks, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing methods.

## Key Results
- DLightSB consistently achieves conditional Shape Scores and Trend Scores above 0.97 across various dimensions and reference processes
- The rank-1 CP decomposition enables efficient computation and sampling in high-dimensional discrete spaces
- DLightSB outperforms existing discrete SB methods on the proposed benchmark by a significant margin

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The paper's approach works by providing a mathematically tractable framework for constructing and evaluating discrete Schrödinger bridge methods. The rank-1 CP decomposition creates analytically known solution pairs that serve as ground truth for benchmarking. The DLightSB architecture directly learns these CP parameters, bypassing the need for complex optimization procedures typically required in discrete SB methods. This direct learning approach, combined with the efficient parameterization, enables superior performance on the benchmark tasks.

## Foundational Learning

**Schrödinger Bridge Problem**
*Why needed:* Core optimization framework for distribution matching under entropic regularization
*Quick check:* Understand that SB finds a coupling between two distributions minimizing KL divergence plus entropy

**Entropic Optimal Transport**
*Why needed:* Provides the theoretical foundation for regularized optimal transport problems
*Quick check:* Recognize that entropy regularization makes the problem computationally tractable

**Canonical Polyadic Decomposition**
*Why needed:* Enables efficient parameterization of high-dimensional functions
*Quick check:* Understand that rank-1 decomposition approximates v* with minimal parameters

**Categorical Distribution Matching**
*Why needed:* Provides baseline methods for discrete distribution alignment
*Quick check:* Know that CSBM matches categorical distributions through iterative refinement

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**
DLightSB (neural network) -> CP parameters (rank-1 decomposition) -> Schrödinger Bridge solution

**Critical Path**
1. Initialize CP parameters
2. Neural network predicts CP factors
3. Construct v* from CP decomposition
4. Compute SB solution using v*
5. Evaluate against ground truth

**Design Tradeoffs**
- Rank-1 CP decomposition: computationally efficient but may limit expressiveness
- Direct learning approach: faster training but potentially less stable
- Synthetic benchmarks: enable rigorous evaluation but may not reflect real-world complexity

**Failure Signatures**
- Poor convergence during training indicates learning rate or architecture issues
- Suboptimal Shape/Trend Scores suggest insufficient model capacity
- High variance across runs indicates training instability

**3 First Experiments**
1. Train DLightSB on 2D Gaussian mixture benchmark
2. Compare convergence rates with existing discrete SB methods
3. Evaluate sensitivity to CP rank and initialization

## Limitations
- Rank-1 CP decomposition assumption may not generalize to complex target distributions beyond Gaussian mixtures
- Benchmark relies on analytically tractable solutions, limiting applicability to real-world scenarios
- Superior performance on synthetic benchmarks doesn't guarantee practical utility for real-world applications
