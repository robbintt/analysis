# Advanced Crash Causation Analysis for Freeway Safety: A Large Language Model Approach to Identifying Key Contributing Factors

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2505.09949
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.09949
- Reference count: 0
- Primary result: LLM fine-tuned with traffic safety studies effectively identifies freeway crash causes with 88.89% researcher agreement

## Executive Summary
This study addresses the challenge of understanding traffic crash causation by leveraging large language models (LLMs) to analyze freeway crash data and identify key contributing factors. A comprehensive training dataset of 226 traffic safety studies was compiled, and the Llama3 8B model was fine-tuned using QLoRA to enhance its domain-specific understanding of freeway crashes. The fine-tuned model was then employed to perform zero-shot classification of crash causation without pre-labeled data, providing detailed explanations for each identified cause. Results demonstrate that the LLM effectively identifies primary crash causes such as alcohol-impaired driving, speeding, aggressive driving, and driver inattention. The model's practical applicability was validated by high agreement among traffic safety researchers, with 88.89% endorsing the crash causation analysis.

## Method Summary
The research team compiled a comprehensive training dataset of 226 traffic safety studies and fine-tuned the Llama3 8B model using QLoRA to enhance domain-specific understanding of freeway crashes. The fine-tuned model performed zero-shot classification of crash causation without requiring pre-labeled data, generating detailed explanations for each identified cause. Expert validation was conducted with traffic safety researchers to assess the model's practical applicability and accuracy.

## Key Results
- LLM effectively identifies primary crash causes including alcohol-impaired driving, speeding, aggressive driving, and driver inattention

- Zero-shot classification capability demonstrated without pre-labeled data requirements

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The approach leverages LLMs' natural language understanding capabilities to analyze complex crash narratives and identify causation patterns. Fine-tuning with domain-specific traffic safety literature enables the model to develop specialized knowledge of freeway crash factors and their relationships.
