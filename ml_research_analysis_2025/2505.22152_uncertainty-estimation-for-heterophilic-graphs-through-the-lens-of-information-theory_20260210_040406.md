# Uncertainty Estimation for Heterophilic Graphs Through the Lens of Information Theory

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2505.22152
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.22152
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: JLDE achieves SOTA epistemic uncertainty estimation on heterophilic graphs

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses uncertainty estimation for graph neural networks in heterophilic settings, where nodes preferentially connect to nodes of different classes. The authors analyze GNNs through an information-theoretic lens, introducing a novel analog to the data processing inequality that captures how information about node labels is processed through GNN layers. They show that in heterophilic graphs, each layer's representation contains distinct information about the target variable, unlike in homophilic graphs where deeper layers amplify similar information. This leads to the key insight that effective uncertainty estimation in heterophilic graphs requires jointly considering all latent representations.

## Method Summary
The authors propose a simple post-hoc density estimator on the joint embedding space (JLDE) that achieves state-of-the-art epistemic uncertainty estimation on heterophilic datasets while maintaining competitive performance on homophilic data without relying on homophily assumptions. The method leverages the observation that in heterophilic graphs, each GNN layer captures unique information about node labels, making the joint embedding space rich for uncertainty quantification. JLDE operates by estimating the density of node representations in this joint space, where low density regions indicate high epistemic uncertainty.

## Key Results
- JLDE achieves state-of-the-art epistemic uncertainty estimation on heterophilic datasets
- Method maintains competitive performance on homophilic data without homophily assumptions
- Simple post-hoc density estimation approach outperforms complex uncertainty quantification methods

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The core mechanism relies on information-theoretic analysis showing that GNNs process label information differently in heterophilic versus homophilic graphs. In homophilic graphs, deeper layers amplify similar information about node labels, while in heterophilic graphs, each layer captures distinct information. This creates a rich joint embedding space where density estimation can effectively capture epistemic uncertainty.

## Architecture Onboarding

Component Map:
Input Graph -> GNN Layers -> Joint Embedding Space -> Density Estimator -> Uncertainty Scores

Critical Path:
The critical path flows from the input graph through GNN layers to generate multi-layer representations, which are then combined into a joint embedding space for density estimation. The density estimator is the key component that transforms the joint representations into uncertainty scores.

Design Tradeoffs:
- Simplicity vs Performance: JLDE trades complex uncertainty quantification methods for a simple density estimation approach
- Homophily-agnostic design vs specialized methods that assume specific graph structures
- Post-hoc application vs integrated uncertainty estimation during training

Failure Signatures:
- Poor uncertainty calibration on graphs with mixed homophily levels
- Sensitivity to bandwidth selection in density estimation
- Performance degradation on extremely sparse or dense graphs
