# Tackling Incomplete Data in Air Quality Prediction: A Bayesian Deep Learning Framework for Uncertainty Quantification

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2511.02175
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.02175
- Authors: Yuzhuang Pian; Taiyu Wang; Shiqi Zhang; Rui Xu; Yonghong Liu
- Reference count: 35
- Primary result: CGLU-BNF achieves state-of-the-art performance with RMSE of 7.26 μg/m³ and MAE of 4.04 μg/m³ on London dataset

## Executive Summary
This study introduces CGLU-BNF, an end-to-end Bayesian deep learning framework designed to address incomplete observations in air quality prediction. The framework integrates graph attention networks with Fourier feature embeddings to capture both spatial and temporal dependencies in air quality data. A key innovation is the channel gated learning unit with learnable activations that filters and enhances informative features, while the Bayesian output layer generates calibrated predictive distributions and confidence intervals.

Evaluated on real-world air quality datasets from London and Hong Kong under four typical missing data patterns, CGLU-BNF demonstrates superior performance compared to baseline methods. The framework achieves RMSE of 7.26 μg/m³ and MAE of 4.04 μg/m³ on the London dataset, and 3.22 μg/m³ and 2.27 μg/m³ on the Hong Kong dataset, respectively. These results represent improvements of 4.35%-7.95% in accuracy over existing methods, while also producing sharper prediction intervals that effectively quantify uncertainty.

## Method Summary
CGLU-BNF combines graph attention networks with Fourier feature embeddings to capture spatial and temporal dependencies in air quality data. The framework employs a channel gated learning unit with learnable activations to filter and enhance informative features, while the Bayesian output layer generates calibrated predictive distributions and confidence intervals. The model is evaluated on two real-world air quality datasets under four typical missing data patterns, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance with significant improvements over baseline methods.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The integration of graph attention networks with Fourier feature embeddings allows CGLU-BNF to effectively capture complex spatial and temporal dependencies in air quality data. The channel gated learning unit with learnable activations selectively filters and enhances informative features, improving the model's ability to handle incomplete observations. The Bayesian output layer provides calibrated uncertainty estimates through predictive distributions and confidence intervals, which is crucial for reliable air quality forecasting under data uncertainty.

## Foundational Learning
The framework builds upon established concepts in graph neural networks, attention mechanisms, and Bayesian deep learning. The use of graph attention networks for spatial modeling draws from recent advances in graph-based learning for spatiotemporal data. Fourier feature embeddings for temporal modeling are inspired by techniques from signal processing and kernel methods. The Bayesian approach for uncertainty quantification follows established practices in probabilistic deep learning, enabling the model to provide meaningful confidence intervals alongside predictions.

## Architecture Onboarding
CGLU-BNF's architecture consists of multiple components working in sequence: graph attention networks process spatial relationships between monitoring stations, Fourier feature embeddings handle temporal dependencies, and the channel gated learning unit refines feature representations by selectively activating informative channels. The Bayesian output layer then transforms these processed features into predictive distributions with uncertainty estimates. This modular design allows for interpretability and potential adaptation to other spatiotemporal prediction tasks with incomplete data.
