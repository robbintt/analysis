# Tracing Footsteps of Similar Cities: Modeling Urban Economic Vitality with Dynamic Inter-City Graph Embeddings

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2511.22325
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.22325
- Reference count: 32
- Primary result: ECO-GROW achieves 20.46% RMSE reduction and 5.96% R² increase in urban economic vitality prediction

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces ECO-GROW, a multi-graph framework that models urban economic vitality by integrating dynamic inter-city networks over 15 years in China. The framework combines Dynamic Top-K Graph Convolutional Networks to adaptively select influential inter-city connections and an adaptive Graph Scorer mechanism to dynamically weight cross-regional impacts. By incorporating industrial linkages, POI similarities, migration patterns, and temporal network evolution, ECO-GROW generates urban embeddings that effectively model economic vitality indicators such as entrepreneurial activities and employment trends.

## Method Summary
ECO-GROW operates through a multi-graph framework that captures urban economic vitality by modeling dynamic inter-city relationships. The system employs Dynamic Top-K GCN to identify and maintain the most influential city connections while filtering noise, combined with an adaptive Graph Scorer that assigns dynamic weights to cross-regional impacts based on temporal and structural changes. The framework integrates four key input signals: industrial linkages between cities, point-of-interest similarities, migration patterns, and the temporal evolution of these networks. This approach generates urban embeddings that can predict economic vitality indicators with superior accuracy compared to conventional models.

## Key Results
- ECO-GROW achieves 20.46% reduction in RMSE compared to baseline models
- The framework demonstrates 5.96% increase in R² for economic vitality predictions
- Superior accuracy in predicting entrepreneurial activities and employment trends

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework's effectiveness stems from its ability to dynamically capture evolving inter-city relationships while filtering irrelevant connections. By combining adaptive selection of influential links (Dynamic Top-K GCN) with context-aware weighting of cross-regional impacts (Graph Scorer), ECO-GROW maintains computational efficiency while preserving critical network structures. The integration of multiple data signals (industrial, POI, migration) creates a comprehensive representation of urban economic ecosystems that evolves with temporal changes.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map:**
Data Sources -> Dynamic Top-K GCN -> Adaptive Graph Scorer -> Urban Embeddings -> Economic Vitality Prediction

**Critical Path:**
Industrial Linkages + POI Similarities + Migration Patterns -> Dynamic Top-K GCN (connection filtering) -> Adaptive Graph Scorer (weight assignment) -> Urban Embeddings (feature generation) -> Economic Vitality Prediction

**Design Tradeoffs:**
- Fixed vs. adaptive connection selection: Adaptive approach improves accuracy but increases complexity
- Static vs. dynamic weighting: Dynamic weighting captures temporal changes but requires more computational resources
- Single vs. multi-signal integration: Multi-signal approach improves robustness but requires careful feature engineering

**Failure Signatures:**
- Overfitting to training period if temporal dynamics not properly captured
- Poor generalization to cities with different economic structures
- Performance degradation when key data signals are missing or noisy
