# One Fits All: General Mobility Trajectory Modeling via Masked Conditional Diffusion
## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2501.13347
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.13347
- Reference count: 40
- Key result: GenMove achieves >13% improvement in trajectory generation and >18% in scarcity-constrained prediction over state-of-the-art baselines

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of building a unified model capable of handling multiple trajectory-related tasks (generation, recovery, prediction) in mobility modeling. The authors propose GenMove, a general trajectory modeling framework based on masked conditional diffusion models. Their core method idea involves using task-specific and pattern-general masking strategies to unify diverse task formats, combined with contextual trajectory embeddings from historical data to handle complex task conditions. The model employs a classifier-free guidance approach to flexibly adjust outputs based on different conditions.

## Method Summary
GenMove introduces a unified trajectory modeling framework that leverages masked conditional diffusion to handle multiple trajectory tasks simultaneously. The approach uses task-specific and pattern-general masking strategies to standardize diverse task formats, while contextual trajectory embeddings from historical data capture complex task conditions. The framework employs classifier-free guidance to flexibly adjust outputs based on different conditions, enabling the model to perform generation, recovery, and prediction tasks within a single architecture.

## Key Results
- GenMove significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines across six trajectory-related tasks on two real-world datasets
- Achieves performance improvements exceeding 13% in trajectory generation tasks
- Demonstrates strong zero-shot performance with improvements of over 18% in scarcity-constrained prediction and nearly 20% in long-term prediction

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework's success stems from its ability to leverage shared mobility patterns across different tasks while maintaining task-specific flexibility. By using masked conditional diffusion, GenMove can handle diverse trajectory formats through standardized masking strategies. The classifier-free guidance approach allows for adaptive output generation based on task conditions, while contextual embeddings capture the complex dependencies in mobility data. This unified approach enables the model to benefit from multi-task training, where different tasks reinforce each other through shared underlying patterns.

## Limitations
