# SANDRO: a Robust Solver with a Splitting Strategy for Point Cloud Registration

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2503.07743
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.07743
- Reference count: 39
- 20% improvement over ADAPT (62% vs 43% success rate) on Redwood dataset

## Executive Summary
SANDRO addresses the challenge of point cloud registration in scenarios with high outlier rates and symmetric distributions, which are common in real-world applications. The method combines Iteratively Reweighted Least Squares (IRLS) with Graduated Non-convexity (GNC) using the Geman-McClure robust loss function. A novel splitting strategy divides point clouds into smaller subsets to handle skewed outlier distributions and improve convergence. When tested on the Redwood real dataset, SANDRO achieved a 62% success rate compared to 43% for ADAPT, representing a 20% improvement. The method demonstrates particular robustness in scenarios with symmetric distributions and multiple similar objects.

## Method Summary
SANDRO is a novel solver for point cloud registration that integrates Iteratively Reweighted Least Squares (IRLS) with Graduated Non-convexity (GNC) using the Geman-McClure robust loss function. The key innovation is a splitting strategy that divides point clouds into smaller subsets to handle skewed outlier distributions and improve convergence. This approach addresses the limitations of traditional methods when dealing with symmetric distributions and multiple similar objects in a scene. The method is computationally efficient, running in hundreds of milliseconds, making it suitable for online applications like SLAM. SANDRO's performance was validated on both the Redwood real dataset and synthetic data with up to 95% outlier rates.

## Key Results
- 62% success rate on Redwood real dataset compared to 43% for ADAPT (20% improvement)
- Over 60% success rate at 95% outlier rates on synthetic data
- FAST achieved 25% and ADAPT achieved 10% success rate at 95% outlier rates
- Computational efficiency of hundreds of milliseconds suitable for online applications

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
SANDRO's effectiveness stems from its ability to handle high outlier rates and symmetric distributions through a combination of robust optimization techniques and strategic problem decomposition. The integration of IRLS with GNC using the Geman-McClure loss function provides robustness to outliers by downweighting their contribution to the optimization. The splitting strategy is particularly crucial as it addresses the limitation of traditional methods when outlier distributions are skewed or when multiple similar objects create symmetric ambiguity. By breaking down the problem into smaller subsets, SANDRO can better localize the registration problem and avoid getting trapped in local minima that arise from symmetric configurations. This dual approach of robust loss functions and problem decomposition allows SANDRO to maintain high success rates even in extremely challenging scenarios with 95% outlier rates.

## Foundational Learning
**Iteratively Reweighted Least Squares (IRLS)**: An optimization technique that iteratively solves weighted least squares problems, where weights are updated based on residuals. Needed to handle outliers by reducing their influence on the solution. Quick check: Verify weight update formula and convergence criteria.

**Graduated Non-convexity (GNC)**: A continuation method that gradually transitions from convex to non-convex optimization to avoid local minima. Needed to handle the non-convex nature of point cloud registration. Quick check: Confirm the schedule for decreasing the convexity parameter.

**Geman-McClure Loss Function**: A robust loss function that provides smooth transition between inliers and outliers. Needed to provide differentiability while maintaining robustness to outliers. Quick check: Validate the derivative of the loss function.

**Symmetric Distribution Handling**: The ability to distinguish between similar objects in symmetric configurations. Needed because traditional methods often fail when multiple similar objects create ambiguous correspondences. Quick check: Test on datasets with multiple identical objects in different poses.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Point Clouds -> Splitter -> Multiple Local Registrations -> Merger -> Global Registration -> SANDRO Output

**Critical Path**: The core computation follows: point cloud preprocessing → subset splitting → local registration using IRLS+GNC → consensus building → final transformation estimation. The splitting strategy is critical as it determines the quality of local registrations.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The splitting strategy trades computational overhead (processing multiple subsets) for improved robustness to outliers and symmetric distributions. The method prioritizes robustness over raw speed, though it still achieves hundreds of milliseconds runtime.

**Failure Signatures**: Performance degradation occurs when: (1) subsets become too small to contain sufficient inliers, (2) symmetric ambiguity exists within subsets, or (3) the splitting strategy fails to separate distinct objects.
