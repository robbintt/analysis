# Characterizing the Accuracy-Communication-Privacy Trade-off in Distributed Stochastic Convex Optimization

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID:** 2501.03222
- **Source URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.03222
- **Reference count:** 40
- **Primary result:** Establishes fundamental lower bounds and optimal algorithm for accuracy-communication-privacy trade-off in distributed stochastic convex optimization under differential privacy

## Executive Summary
This paper provides a comprehensive characterization of the three-way trade-off among accuracy, communication, and privacy in distributed stochastic convex optimization (DP-SCO) under differential privacy constraints. The authors establish matching lower and upper bounds showing that achieving optimal accuracy requires Ω(d²) communication cost when ε_D P ≥ √(d/N). They propose Charter, a novel algorithm based on Vaidya's plane cutting method that achieves this fundamental trade-off with O(d²) communication cost and (ε_D P, δ_D P)-differential privacy guarantees.

## Method Summary
The Charter algorithm operates in two stages: a learning stage using Vaidya's plane cutting method with private gradient estimates, and a verification stage to select the final solution. The key innovation is demonstrating that plane cutting methods can achieve optimal communication complexity in distributed settings while maintaining privacy. Charter requires only O(N) gradient computations per client, improving upon state-of-the-art methods. The gradient estimation scheme ensures both privacy and generalization, enabling the algorithm to achieve the optimal accuracy bound of O(1/√(MN) + √(d/(MNε_D P))).

## Key Results
- Establishes Ω(d²) communication lower bound for achieving optimal accuracy when ε_D P ≥ √(d/N)
- Proposes Charter algorithm achieving optimal accuracy with O(d²) communication cost
- Demonstrates plane cutting methods can achieve optimal communication complexity in distributed DP-SCO
- Achieves (ε_D P, δ_D P)-differential privacy while requiring only O(N) gradient computations per client

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
Charter leverages Vaidya's plane cutting method to efficiently explore the parameter space while maintaining privacy through carefully designed gradient estimation. The two-stage approach allows for both learning and verification while controlling communication overhead. The gradient estimation scheme balances privacy noise with generalization requirements.
