# DELST: Dual Entailment Learning for Hyperbolic Image-Gene Pretraining in Spatial Transcriptomics

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2503.00804
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.00804
- Reference count: 25
- Key outcome: DELST achieves F1 score improvements up to 0.034 over baselines on human breast tissue benchmarks in spatial transcriptomics image-gene pretraining

## Executive Summary
DELST introduces a novel framework for image-gene pretraining in spatial transcriptomics that leverages hyperbolic geometry and dual entailment learning. The method addresses the challenge of incorporating gene-specific hierarchical information into ST data analysis by establishing order relationships between genes and images through cross-modal entailment, while simultaneously encoding gene expression patterns as hierarchical relationships through intra-modal entailment. This approach enables improved predictive performance on spatial transcriptomics benchmarks, particularly for human breast tissue analysis.

## Method Summary
DELST employs hyperbolic embeddings to capture hierarchical relationships inherent in gene expression data while implementing dual entailment learning at two levels. The cross-modal entailment learning establishes order relationships between genes and images to enhance image representation generalization, while the intra-modal entailment learning encodes gene expression patterns as hierarchical relationships within single-modal representations. This dual approach integrates biological insights into the pretraining process, creating representations that better reflect the underlying biological structure of spatial transcriptomics data.

## Key Results
- DELST achieves F1 score improvements up to 0.034 over existing baselines on human breast tissue benchmarks
- The method demonstrates superior predictive performance compared to conventional image-gene pretraining approaches
- Code and models are publicly available for reproducibility and further research

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The dual entailment learning framework works by simultaneously capturing hierarchical relationships at both cross-modal and intra-modal levels. Cross-modal entailment learning creates structured relationships between genes and their corresponding spatial images, allowing the model to learn meaningful correspondences that enhance image representation. Intra-modal entailment learning encodes the inherent hierarchical structure of gene expression patterns, ensuring that biological relationships are preserved in the learned representations. The hyperbolic geometry provides the mathematical foundation for naturally representing these hierarchical structures without distortion.
