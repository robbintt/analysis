# StructuredDNA: A Bio-Physical Framework for Energy-Aware Transformer Routing

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2512.08968
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.08968
- Reference count: 14
- Primary result: 97.7% EUD reduction on BioASQ with SSI=0.998

## Executive Summary
StructuredDNA introduces a bio-physical, energy-aware routing framework for Transformers that replaces dense MoE gating with semantic energy minimization. Inspired by biological molecular organization, inputs are dynamically grouped into semantic codons, and a single expert is selected by minimizing a global energy functional that combines cohesion, uncertainty, and computational cost. On BioASQ (K=50), the method achieves a 97.7% reduction in Energy Utilization Density (EUD) and a Semantic Stability Index (SSI) of 0.998. Extending to WikiText-103 (K=2048), StructuredDNA demonstrates >99% energy efficiency while maintaining high semantic stability, supporting a "Semantic Scaling Law" where stability improves with expert granularity in open domains. The work provides empirical evidence linking bio-physical principles to sparse, modular AI architectures, enabling interpretable, low-energy routing suitable for resource-constrained environments. Limitations include limited dataset size and baseline comparisons, with future work targeting differentiable energy objectives and dynamic expert management. The StructuredDNA implementation is publicly available for further research.

## Method Summary
StructuredDNA replaces dense MoE gating with bio-physical, energy-aware routing that groups tokens into semantic codons and routes to a single expert via energy minimization. The framework embeds tokens using Bio_ClinicalBERT (BioASQ) or bert-base-uncased (WikiText-103), computes pairwise cosine similarity to merge adjacent tokens into codons when similarity exceeds threshold τ=0.75, and creates K codon centers via K-Means (K=50 for BioASQ, K=100–2048 for WikiText-103). Routing is performed by minimizing a global energy functional Etotal = Σ_k(1-Fbinding(Ck)) + β·Ha + γ·Lc, where Fbinding represents intra-codon cohesion, Ha is entropy, and Lc is latency. The method achieves interpretable, low-energy routing by selecting a single expert per codon based on semantic energy minimization.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
StructuredDNA works by replacing dense MoE gating with bio-physical, energy-aware routing that groups tokens into semantic codons and routes to a single expert via energy minimization. The framework uses a global energy functional that combines semantic cohesion, uncertainty, and computational cost to dynamically select the most appropriate expert for each semantic unit. By organizing inputs into codons based on semantic similarity and minimizing energy through a single expert selection, StructuredDNA achieves interpretable, low-energy routing while maintaining high semantic stability.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Token Embeddings -> Codon Assembly -> K-Means Clustering -> Energy Functional Computation -> Expert Routing -> Output Generation

**Critical Path**: The critical path is Token Embeddings → Codon Assembly → K-Means Clustering → Energy Functional Computation → Expert Routing. Each step must complete before the next can begin, with energy minimization being the bottleneck for routing decisions.

**Design Tradeoffs**: Fixed K experts provide stability but limit adaptability; dynamic expert creation would increase flexibility but add complexity. The bio-physical energy functional trades computational overhead for interpretability and efficiency.

**Failure Signatures**: 
- High EUD variance indicates unstable routing decisions or incorrect energy computation
- Low SSI suggests poor semantic coherence in codon formation or energy minimization
- Excessive codon merging indicates τ threshold too low, reducing routing granularity
- Repeated expert selection suggests binding force computation issues or limited expert diversity

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out
- Can energy cost and SSI be integrated as differentiable components in a loss function for end-to-end learning?
- What mechanisms enable real-time dynamic creation, pruning, or merging of experts?
- Does StructuredDNA maintain efficiency advantages against stronger routing baselines on accuracy benchmarks?
- What architectural modifications close the stability gap on open-domain text without increasing latency?

## Limitations
- Unspecified β and γ hyperparameters in energy functional prevent exact reproduction
- Expert activation mechanism unclear without trained expert modules
- Exact latency computation method unspecified for energy quantification
- Limited baseline comparisons focused on energy rather than task performance

## Confidence
**High Confidence**: Bio-physical conceptual framework and SSI metric definition are clearly specified and reproducible. BioASQ experiment setup (K=50, Bio_ClinicalBERT, 200 docs) is sufficiently detailed.

**Medium Confidence**: Codon assembly algorithm (cosine similarity threshold, K-Means) is reproducible but outcomes depend on preprocessing and seed settings.

**Low Confidence**: Energy functional implementation, expert routing mechanism, and WikiText-103 scaling experiments lack sufficient specification for verification.
