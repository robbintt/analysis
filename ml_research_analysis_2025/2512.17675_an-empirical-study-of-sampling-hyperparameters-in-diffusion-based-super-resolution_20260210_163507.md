# An Empirical Study of Sampling Hyperparameters in Diffusion-Based Super-Resolution

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2512.17675
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.17675
- Authors: Yudhistira Arief Wibowo
- Reference count: 13
- Key outcome: DPS conditioning strategy consistently outperforms MCG across all metrics; conditioning step size has larger impact than diffusion step count, with optimal range [2.0, 3.0]

## Executive Summary
This paper investigates how sampling hyperparameters affect diffusion-based single-image super-resolution performance. The study compares two conditioning strategies - MCG and DPS - for incorporating measurement information into pretrained diffusion models. Through systematic ablation studies on the FFHQ dataset, the research demonstrates that DPS consistently outperforms MCG across all evaluation metrics. The key finding reveals that conditioning step size has a significantly larger impact on performance than the number of diffusion steps, with the optimal range being [2.0, 3.0] for balancing reconstruction accuracy and perceptual quality.

## Method Summary
The authors evaluate two conditioning strategies for incorporating measurement information into diffusion models: MCG (Measurement Conditioning Generation) and DPS (Direct Parameter Shift). Both methods use a pretrained diffusion model (S2SMLD) to perform super-resolution by progressively refining a low-resolution image through iterative sampling steps. The study systematically varies the conditioning step size (how much measurement information is injected) and the number of diffusion steps to understand their relative impacts on reconstruction quality. The experiments were conducted on the FFHQ dataset using automated evaluation metrics including PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS, and NIQE to assess both distortion and perceptual quality.

## Key Results
- DPS conditioning strategy consistently outperforms MCG across all tested metrics (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS, NIQE)
- Conditioning step size has significantly larger impact on performance than the number of diffusion steps
- Step sizes in the range [2.0, 3.0] yield the best overall results, balancing reconstruction accuracy and perceptual quality
- Overly large step sizes introduce noise artifacts, while increasing step count alone improves distortion metrics but degrades perceptual quality

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The conditioning step size directly controls how much measurement information is incorporated at each sampling iteration. Larger step sizes allow the model to better align with the target high-resolution distribution by providing more frequent guidance from the measurement information. The DPS strategy appears to be more effective because it directly shifts the model parameters rather than relying on generation-based conditioning, which may introduce additional uncertainty. The optimal range of [2.0, 3.0] represents a sweet spot where sufficient measurement information is provided without overwhelming the diffusion process or introducing noise artifacts.

## Limitations
- Evaluation limited to FFHQ dataset (portrait images), may not generalize to other image domains
- Tested only with S2SMLD pretrained diffusion model, findings may not apply to other architectures
- Ablation studies focused on specific ranges, potentially missing optimal configurations outside tested bounds
- Perceptual quality assessments relied on automated metrics rather than human evaluations

## Confidence
