# QiNN-QJ: A Quantum-inspired Neural Network with Quantum Jump for Multimodal Sentiment Analysis

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2510.27091
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.27091
- Authors: Yiwei Chen; Kehuan Yan; Yu Pan; Daoyi Dong
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Achieves state-of-the-art Acc-7: 54.52, Corr: 0.88 on CMU-MOSI

## Executive Summary
QiNN-QJ introduces a quantum-inspired neural network that models multimodal sentiment analysis through dissipative quantum dynamics. Unlike prior methods relying solely on unitary evolution, QiNN-QJ jointly learns Hamiltonian and Lindblad operators to generate controllable cross-modal entanglement. The model encodes each modality as a quantum pure state, transforms separable product states into entangled representations via the Quantum Jump module, and projects these for sentiment prediction. It achieves state-of-the-art performance on CMU-MOSI (Acc-7: 54.52, Corr: 0.88), CMU-MOSEI (Acc-7: 55.63, Corr: 0.811), and CH-SIMS (Acc-5: 46.92, Corr: 0.689), outperforming classical, quantum-inspired, and large language model baselines.

## Method Summary
The model processes multimodal inputs through frozen BERT/ViT/Wav2Vec encoders, reduces features to low-dimensional complex pure states via trainable adapters, and creates joint quantum states via tensor products. The Quantum Jump (QJ) module then iteratively applies unitary evolution (Hamiltonian) and stochastic jumps (Lindblad operators) to generate controllable entanglement. Finally, trainable measurement vectors project the entangled state onto sentiment classes using quantum fidelity. The architecture includes a contrastive loss for multimodal alignment and uses AdamW optimization with early stopping.

## Key Results
- Achieves state-of-the-art Acc-7: 54.52, Corr: 0.88 on CMU-MOSI
- Outperforms classical, quantum-inspired, and LLM baselines on CMU-MOSI, CMU-MOSEI, and CH-SIMS
- Demonstrates superior interpretability through von Neumann entanglement entropy quantification
- Shows stability benefits from dissipative dynamics compared to purely unitary approaches

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1: Dissipative Entanglement Regularization
The QJ module simulates an open quantum system with learnable Lindblad operators that introduce structured stochasticity and steady-state attractor properties. This controls cross-modal entanglement strength better than unitary evolution alone, preventing overfitting while maintaining expressive power. The dissipative nature enables precise modulation of entanglement complexity.

### Mechanism 2: Semantic Superposition via Complex States
Representing modalities as unit-norm complex quantum pure states allows single vectors to encode superpositions of semantic interpretations. This complex Hilbert space geometry preserves richer semantic ambiguity than classical real-valued vectors, enabling non-classical correlations between modalities.

### Mechanism 3: Fidelity-Based Measurement
The model projects entangled states onto trainable measurement vectors using quantum fidelity (inner product square). This converts quantum amplitudes back into classical probability distributions for classification, where semantic alignment corresponds to geometric proximity in the learned Hilbert space.

## Architecture Onboarding

- **Component map**: Frozen Encoders (BERT/ViT/Wav2Vec) -> Dimension Adapters (Linear layers to complex states) -> Tensor Product (Create bimodal pairs) -> QJ Evolution (Entanglement generation) -> Inner Product (Measurement) -> Row-max pooling -> Linear classifier
- **Critical path**: Input → Adapter (Normalize to Unit Norm) → Tensor Product (Create Pairs) → QJ Evolution (Entanglement Generation) → Inner Product (Measurement) → Loss
- **Design tradeoffs**:
  - Time Steps: Too few = under-entangled; too many = over-entangled (Fig 2 shows inflection point)
  - State Dimension: Small dimensions (6-14) risk compressing semantic nuance
  - Hamiltonian vs. Lindblad: Controls trade-off between expressive power and stability
- **Failure signatures**:
  - Runaway Entropy: If entropy rises continuously without accuracy improvement (overfitting to noise)
  - Mode Collapse: If removing modalities doesn't significantly impact performance (failed entanglement)

  1. Compare "Pure Unitary" vs. "QJ" on validation accuracy to verify dissipative benefits

  3. Remove each modality systematically to test robustness of entanglement mechanism

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out

### Open Question 1
Can classical simulation of QJ dynamics be mapped to parameterized quantum circuits on NISQ devices?
- Basis: Conclusion states future research will extend to variational quantum circuit implementations
- Why unresolved: Current classical simulation doesn't address noise constraints and circuit depth challenges of translating non-unitary Lindblad operators to quantum hardware
- Evidence needed: Successful quantum hardware implementation maintaining trainability and comparable performance

### Open Question 2
Can optimal QJ time steps be determined adaptively rather than tuned as fixed hyperparameter?
- Basis: Section V-B notes necessity for adaptive determination of optimal termination time for random evolution
- Why unresolved: Current architecture uses fixed time steps selected via grid search, risking over-entanglement or under-fitting
- Evidence needed: Dynamic stopping criterion based on entanglement entropy convergence

### Open Question 3
Do learned Hamiltonian and Lindblad operators generalize to cross-domain multimodal tasks beyond sentiment analysis?
- Basis: Conclusion identifies cross-domain multimodal transfer as future research focus
- Why unresolved: Experimental validation restricted to sentiment analysis benchmarks; unclear if operators capture universal cross-modal interactions
- Evidence needed: Zero-shot or fine-tuning performance on distinct multimodal tasks using pre-trained operators

## Limitations
- Number and parameterization of Lindblad operators remain unspecified despite being central to regularization claims
- Measurement vector dimensions and initialization schemes are not fully detailed
- Contrastive cross-attention architecture is broadly described without specific implementation details
