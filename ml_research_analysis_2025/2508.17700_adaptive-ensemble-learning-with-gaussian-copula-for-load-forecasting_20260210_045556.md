# Adaptive Ensemble Learning with Gaussian Copula for Load Forecasting

## Quick Facts

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces Adaptive Ensemble Learning with Gaussian Copula (GC-AEL), a hybrid approach for load forecasting that addresses data sparsity challenges in power consumption prediction. The method integrates three key components: Gaussian Copula for data complementation, five individual machine learning models (CNN, LSTM, TCN, TRMF, XGBoost) for predictions, and an adaptive ensemble strategy for final weighted results. Experiments using monthly electric load data demonstrate that GC-AEL outperforms individual models with a mean MAPE of 2.13% compared to the best single model at 56 wins versus 4 losses.

## Method Summary
The GC-AEL framework combines three key components to address load forecasting challenges. First, Gaussian Copula handles data sparsity by generating synthetic samples that maintain statistical relationships between variables. Second, five diverse machine learning models (CNN, LSTM, TCN, TRMF, XGBoost) provide individual predictions from different perspectives. Third, an adaptive ensemble mechanism dynamically weights these predictions based on their recent performance, effectively combining the strengths of multiple models while filtering out less accurate predictions. The system processes monthly electric load data through this pipeline to generate final forecasts.

## Key Results
- GC-AEL achieves mean MAPE of 2.13% versus 4.87% for best individual model
- Ensemble model demonstrates 56 wins versus 4 losses across all evaluation metrics
- Gaussian Copula successfully handles sparse data while maintaining forecast accuracy
- Adaptive ensemble mechanism effectively combines multiple model strengths

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The GC-AEL approach succeeds by addressing three critical challenges in load forecasting: data sparsity, model diversity, and dynamic performance adaptation. Gaussian Copula generates realistic synthetic data that preserves statistical relationships, enabling robust predictions even with limited historical data. The ensemble of five diverse models captures different temporal and spatial patterns in power consumption, while the adaptive weighting mechanism ensures that more accurate models contribute more heavily to final predictions. This multi-pronged approach creates resilience against individual model failures and adapts to changing conditions.

## Limitations
- Single dataset evaluation limits generalizability across different power consumption scenarios and geographic regions
- Claims of "superior forecasting accuracy" based on comparisons with only five individual models
- Adaptive ensemble mechanism robustness under varying data quality conditions remains unclear
- 2.13% MAPE achievement lacks statistical significance testing to confirm it's not due to random variation
