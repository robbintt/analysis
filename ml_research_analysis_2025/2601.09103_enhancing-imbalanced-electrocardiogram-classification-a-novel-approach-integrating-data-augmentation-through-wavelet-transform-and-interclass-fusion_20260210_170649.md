# Enhancing Imbalanced Electrocardiogram Classification: A Novel Approach Integrating Data Augmentation through Wavelet Transform and Interclass Fusion

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2601.09103
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.09103
- **Reference count**: 36
- **Primary result**: Proposed wavelet transform-based interclass fusion approach achieves up to 99% accuracy for normal class and 92-98% average accuracy across nine ECG categories on CPSC 2018 dataset

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the class imbalance problem in electrocardiogram classification through a novel data augmentation approach using wavelet transform-based interclass fusion. The method generates synthetic training and test feature libraries by fusing features from different ECG classes, creating more balanced datasets for training deep learning models. Evaluated on the CPSC 2018 dataset using VGG16, LeNet5, Inception, and LSTM models, the approach demonstrates significant improvements in classification accuracy while maintaining noise resilience, outperforming existing algorithms in ECG classification tasks.

## Method Summary
The proposed method integrates wavelet transform-based data augmentation with interclass fusion to address class imbalance in ECG classification. The approach first extracts wavelet coefficients from ECG signals to capture multi-scale temporal features. These features are then fused across different ECG classes to generate synthetic samples, creating augmented training and test feature libraries. The fused features are combined with original data to create balanced datasets that are used to train deep learning models including VGG16, LeNet5, Inception, and LSTM architectures. This fusion-based augmentation strategy effectively increases minority class representation while preserving the discriminative characteristics of each ECG category.

## Key Results
- Achieved up to 99% recognition accuracy for Normal class ECG signals
- Obtained average recognition accuracy ranging from 92% to 98% across nine ECG categories
- Demonstrated superior performance compared to existing algorithms in handling class imbalance while maintaining noise resilience

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The wavelet transform-based interclass fusion approach works by decomposing ECG signals into multiple frequency subbands, capturing both time and frequency information simultaneously. By fusing features across different ECG classes in the wavelet domain, the method creates synthetic samples that preserve the temporal patterns of various arrhythmias while maintaining class-specific characteristics. This process effectively increases the representation of minority classes in the training data, allowing deep learning models to learn more balanced decision boundaries. The multi-scale nature of wavelet decomposition ensures that both high-frequency transient features and low-frequency baseline variations are preserved during the fusion process, resulting in synthetic samples that are clinically meaningful and discriminative.

## Foundational Learning

**Wavelet Transform**: Signal processing technique that decomposes signals into time-frequency representations across multiple scales. Why needed: Captures both temporal and spectral characteristics of ECG signals essential for arrhythmia detection. Quick check: Verify that wavelet coefficients preserve key ECG features like P-waves, QRS complexes, and T-waves.

**Interclass Fusion**: Method of combining feature representations from different classes to generate synthetic samples. Why needed: Addresses class imbalance by increasing minority class representation while maintaining discriminative features. Quick check: Ensure fused features remain separable by classification models and don't create ambiguous regions.

**Data Augmentation**: Techniques to artificially expand training datasets through synthetic sample generation. Why needed: Mitigates overfitting and improves model generalization, particularly important for imbalanced datasets. Quick check: Validate that augmented samples maintain clinical validity and don't introduce artifacts that could mislead classification.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: ECG signal -> Wavelet Transform -> Feature Extraction -> Interclass Fusion -> Augmented Dataset -> Deep Learning Models (VGG16, LeNet5, Inception, LSTM) -> Classification Output

**Critical Path**: Wavelet decomposition and feature extraction form the foundation, followed by interclass fusion to generate augmented data, which then feeds into deep learning models for final classification.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The method balances between synthetic sample diversity and clinical validity, choosing wavelet transform for its ability to preserve both temporal and frequency characteristics while enabling effective feature fusion across classes.

**Failure Signatures**: Potential issues include over-fusion leading to loss of class-specific characteristics, wavelet artifacts affecting signal quality, and synthetic samples that may not represent real clinical scenarios accurately.
