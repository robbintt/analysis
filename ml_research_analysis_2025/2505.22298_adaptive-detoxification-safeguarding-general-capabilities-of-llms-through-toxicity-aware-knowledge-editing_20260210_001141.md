# Adaptive Detoxification: Safeguarding General Capabilities of LLMs through Toxicity-Aware Knowledge Editing

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2505.22298
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.22298
- Authors: Yifan Lu; Jing li; Yigeng Zhou; Yihui Zhang; Wenya Wang; Xiucheng Li; Meishan Zhang; Fangming Liu; Jun Yu; Min Zhang
- Reference count: 17
- TOXEDIT achieves 97.78% detoxification success rate on LLaMA3-8B-Instruct

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces TOXEDIT, a novel toxicity-aware knowledge editing approach for large language models that dynamically detects toxic activation patterns during forward propagation and routes computations through adaptive inter-layer pathways to mitigate toxicity while preserving general capabilities. The method addresses the over-editing problem common in existing LLM detoxification methods by selectively intervening only when toxic patterns are detected. TOXEDIT demonstrates superior performance compared to state-of-the-art baselines, achieving 97.78% detoxification success rate while maintaining 95.36% defense locality on LLaMA3-8B-Instruct.

## Method Summary
TOXEDIT implements a dynamic knowledge editing framework that operates during inference rather than requiring retraining. The system monitors activation patterns in real-time, identifying toxic content through learned toxic activation detection mechanisms. When toxicity is detected, the model routes computations through adaptive inter-layer pathways that modify the toxic knowledge while preserving general capabilities. This selective intervention approach prevents the over-editing problem where general capabilities are degraded through blanket detoxification methods. The framework integrates seamlessly with existing transformer architectures without requiring fundamental architectural changes.

## Key Results
- TOXEDIT achieves 97.78% detoxification success rate on LLaMA3-8B-Instruct compared to 82.89% for best baseline
- Defense locality of 95.36% demonstrates strong preservation of general capabilities during detoxification
- Strong generalization across various malicious prompts and model architectures
- Effectively addresses over-editing problem common in existing LLM detoxification methods

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
TOXEDIT works by combining dynamic toxic activation detection with adaptive routing mechanisms that selectively intervene only when necessary. The key innovation is the real-time monitoring of activation patterns that allows the system to distinguish between toxic and non-toxic content, applying knowledge editing only to the former. This selective approach prevents the degradation of general capabilities that occurs with blanket detoxification methods. The adaptive inter-layer pathways provide alternative computational routes that can bypass or modify toxic knowledge while maintaining the model's ability to generate appropriate responses for benign prompts.

Design Tradeoffs: Selective intervention provides better capability preservation but requires more complex detection mechanisms; blanket methods are simpler but degrade general performance
Failure Signatures: False negatives in toxic detection lead to missed detoxification; false positives cause unnecessary routing and potential capability degradation
