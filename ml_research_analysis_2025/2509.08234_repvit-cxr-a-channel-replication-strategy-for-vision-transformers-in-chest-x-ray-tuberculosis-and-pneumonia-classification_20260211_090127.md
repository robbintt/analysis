# RepViT-CXR: A Channel Replication Strategy for Vision Transformers in Chest X-ray Tuberculosis and Pneumonia Classification

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID:** 2509.08234
- **Source URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.08234
- **Reference count:** 36
- **Key result:** Channel replication strategy enables ViTs to achieve state-of-the-art performance on TB and pneumonia classification tasks

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of applying Vision Transformers (ViTs) to single-channel grayscale chest X-ray images, as ViTs are pretrained on three-channel RGB images and require adaptation for grayscale medical imaging. The authors propose RepViT-CXR, a channel replication strategy that converts single-channel CXR images into three-channel inputs without additional information loss, enabling the use of pretrained ViTs. The method is evaluated on three benchmark datasets: TB-CXR, Pediatric Pneumonia, and Shenzhen TB. Results show that RepViT-CXR achieves state-of-the-art performance, with 99.9% accuracy and 99.9% AUC on TB-CXR, 99.0% accuracy on Pediatric Pneumonia, and 91.1% accuracy on Shenzhen TB, outperforming previous CNN and topology-based methods.

## Method Summary
RepViT-CXR introduces a channel replication strategy that converts single-channel grayscale CXR images into three-channel inputs by replicating the grayscale channel across all three color channels. This approach allows pretrained Vision Transformers, which are designed for three-channel RGB images, to process grayscale medical images without requiring retraining on grayscale data. The replicated channels maintain the same information, avoiding information loss while preserving the computational efficiency of pretrained models. The method is tested on three benchmark datasets for tuberculosis and pneumonia classification, demonstrating superior performance compared to traditional CNN approaches and existing topology-based methods.

## Key Results
- Achieves 99.9% accuracy and 99.9% AUC on TB-CXR dataset
- Achieves 99.0% accuracy on Pediatric Pneumonia dataset
- Achieves 91.1% accuracy on Shenzhen TB dataset
- Outperforms previous CNN and topology-based methods on all three datasets

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The channel replication strategy works by leveraging the pretrained knowledge of Vision Transformers on three-channel RGB images while adapting them to grayscale medical imaging. By replicating the single grayscale channel across all three channels, the model maintains the same spatial information and intensity values while conforming to the expected three-channel input format. This approach avoids the need for retraining on grayscale data, which would be computationally expensive and potentially less effective than leveraging pretrained models. The strategy preserves the original information while enabling the use of powerful pretrained ViT architectures for medical imaging tasks.

## Foundational Learning

**Vision Transformers (ViTs):** Deep learning models that use self-attention mechanisms to process images, originally designed for RGB inputs. Needed to understand the computational architecture being adapted for medical imaging.

**Channel replication:** The process of duplicating a single-channel image across multiple channels to create multi-channel input. Required to understand how grayscale images are adapted for three-channel models.

**Pretrained models:** Neural networks trained on large datasets that can be fine-tuned for specific tasks. Essential for understanding why leveraging existing models is advantageous.

**Binary classification:** A machine learning task where the model predicts one of two possible outcomes. Important for understanding the evaluation metrics used.

**AUC (Area Under Curve):** A performance metric for classification models that measures the area under the ROC curve. Needed to interpret the model's discriminative ability.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component map:** CXR image → Channel replication → Pretrained ViT backbone → Classification head → Output prediction

**Critical path:** The most critical components are the channel replication layer and the pretrained ViT backbone, as these determine the model's ability to process grayscale images effectively.

**Design tradeoffs:** The approach trades computational efficiency for potentially slight information loss, though the authors claim preservation of all original information.

**Failure signatures:** Potential failures could include loss of subtle intensity variations due to channel replication, overfitting to specific CXR acquisition protocols, or inability to generalize to multi-class scenarios.

## Limitations
- Evaluation limited to binary classification tasks, limiting generalizability to more complex medical imaging scenarios
- Computational efficiency claims lack comprehensive benchmarking against CNN alternatives
- Model robustness to variations in image quality, artifacts, and different CXR acquisition protocols remains untested
- Exclusive focus on TB and pneumonia detection limits generalizability to other chest pathologies

## Confidence

**High confidence:** Performance metrics on the three benchmark datasets presented
**Medium confidence:** Claims about state-of-the-art performance relative to previous methods
**Medium confidence:** Channel replication strategy effectiveness without information loss
**Low confidence:** Generalization to clinical deployment and real-world scenarios
