# medDreamer: Model-Based Reinforcement Learning with Latent Imagination on Complex EHRs for Clinical Decision Support

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2505.19785
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.19785
- Reference count: 40
- medDreamer achieves estimated mortality reductions of 11.19% and 5.83% compared to clinician performance in sepsis treatment and mechanical ventilation management

## Executive Summary
medDreamer introduces a model-based reinforcement learning framework that leverages latent imagination to learn effective treatment policies from irregular and incomplete Electronic Health Records (EHRs). The framework addresses key challenges in clinical decision support by handling irregular sampling and missingness through an Adaptive Feature Integration (AFI) module, while enabling safe counterfactual reasoning through a two-phase policy training strategy. Extensive evaluation on sepsis treatment and mechanical ventilation management tasks using MIMIC-IV and eICU datasets demonstrates significant improvements over existing model-free and model-based baselines, with estimated mortality reductions of 11.19% and 5.83% compared to clinician performance.

## Method Summary
medDreamer employs a model-based reinforcement learning approach that constructs robust patient representations through an Adaptive Feature Integration module to handle irregular sampling and missingness in clinical data. The framework uses a two-phase policy training strategy: first grounding the policy on hybrid trajectories of real and imagined steps, then refining it using fully imagined trajectories. This approach enables the model to learn personalized treatment strategies that generalize beyond historical clinical practices while maintaining safety and clinical alignment. The world model reliably reconstructs patient physiology under varying missing rates, and the learned value functions show strong correlation with patient outcomes.

## Key Results
- Estimated mortality reductions of 11.19% and 5.83% compared to clinician performance in sepsis and mechanical ventilation tasks
- Superior offline policy evaluation metrics including WIS, WPDIS, and CWPDIS compared to baselines
- Reliable patient physiology reconstruction under varying missing rates, with value functions strongly correlating with patient outcomes

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework's effectiveness stems from its ability to handle irregular sampling and missingness in clinical data through the Adaptive Feature Integration module, which captures both intra-feature temporal dynamics and inter-feature interactions. The two-phase policy training strategy enables safe counterfactual reasoning by first grounding the policy on real data before allowing fully imagined trajectories, ensuring clinical alignment while exploring novel treatment strategies. The latent imagination component allows the model to simulate potential future states and outcomes, enabling more robust policy learning than approaches limited to observed trajectories alone.

## Foundational Learning
- **Irregular time-series processing**: Essential for handling clinical data where measurements are taken at inconsistent intervals and some features may be missing. Quick check: Verify AFI module maintains performance across varying sampling frequencies.
- **Latent variable modeling**: Enables compact representation of patient states while preserving clinically relevant information. Quick check: Assess reconstruction accuracy of patient physiology from latent representations.
- **Counterfactual reasoning in RL**: Critical for evaluating potential outcomes of alternative treatment decisions. Quick check: Validate that imagined trajectories produce clinically plausible patient trajectories.
- **Off-policy evaluation metrics**: Necessary for assessing policy quality without deploying in real clinical settings. Quick check: Compare WIS, WPDIS, and CWPDIS scores across different policy architectures.

## Architecture Onboarding

Component map: EHR data -> AFI module -> Latent space representation -> World model -> Policy network -> Value network

Critical path: EHR data → AFI module → Latent space representation → World model → Policy network → Treatment action → Value network → Outcome prediction

Design tradeoffs: The framework balances between model complexity and interpretability, using latent representations to capture complex patient states while maintaining some connection to observable clinical features. The two-phase training strategy trades off between exploration of novel treatments and safety constraints from real clinical data.

Failure signatures: Poor reconstruction of patient physiology indicates world model inadequacy; high variance in imagined trajectories suggests unstable latent representations; low correlation between predicted and actual outcomes indicates policy misalignment.

First experiments: 1) Validate AFI module performance on benchmark irregular time-series datasets; 2) Test world model reconstruction accuracy under controlled missingness patterns; 3) Compare policy performance on simplified clinical scenarios before scaling to full MIMIC-IV/eICU datasets.
