# GCN-Based Throughput-Oriented Handover Management in Dense 5G Vehicular Networks

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID:** 2505.04894
- **Source URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.04894
- **Reference count:** 30
- **Primary result:** Graph neural network approach for handover management in dense 5G vehicular networks that reduces handovers by up to 78% and improves signal quality by 10% while maintaining high throughput

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces TH-GCN, a graph neural network approach for handover management in dense 5G vehicular networks. The method models vehicles and base stations as nodes in a dynamic graph with edge weights based on throughput, signal quality, and distance, enabling real-time handover decisions that jointly optimize throughput and signal quality. TH-GCN employs edge-weighted aggregation and triplet loss optimization to reduce unnecessary handovers and ping-pong effects. Simulation results demonstrate significant improvements over existing methods in handover reduction, signal quality, and throughput maintenance in dense urban scenarios.

## Method Summary
TH-GCN represents vehicles and base stations as nodes in a dynamic graph where edge weights are determined by throughput, signal quality, and distance metrics. The graph neural network processes this topology to predict optimal handover decisions through edge-weighted aggregation that prioritizes high-quality connections. Triplet loss optimization is used during training to minimize unnecessary handovers while maintaining connection quality. The model operates in real-time, continuously updating as vehicles move through the network, making decisions that balance the competing objectives of minimizing handover frequency and maximizing both throughput and signal quality.

## Key Results
- Reduces handovers by up to 78% compared to existing methods
- Improves signal quality by 10% while maintaining high throughput
- Achieves low packet loss rates in dense urban scenarios

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The approach works by leveraging graph neural networks to capture the complex spatial relationships between moving vehicles and static base stations. By modeling the vehicular network as a dynamic graph with edge weights that encode quality metrics, TH-GCN can make informed handover decisions that consider the entire network topology rather than just local metrics. The edge-weighted aggregation allows the model to emphasize high-quality connections during message passing, while triplet loss optimization specifically penalizes unnecessary handovers and ping-pong effects that plague traditional methods.
