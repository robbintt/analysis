# Federated Self-Supervised Learning for One-Shot Cross-Modal and Cross-Imaging Technique Segmentation

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2503.23507
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.23507
- Reference count: 40
- Introduces the first federated self-supervised one-shot segmentation framework for medical imaging

## Executive Summary
This paper presents FedCoWPro, a novel framework for federated self-supervised one-shot segmentation in medical imaging that preserves privacy while enabling decentralized learning across multiple institutions. The method adapts the CoWPro self-supervised few-shot segmentation framework to a federated learning setting, using pseudo-segmentation masks generated from local datasets for self-supervised pre-training followed by federated aggregation using FedAvg. The framework is enhanced with a fused dice loss combining spatial and edge-aware components, and is evaluated on a novel public multi-organ gynecological MR brachytherapy dataset (MOGaMB) along with several abdominal datasets across different imaging modalities (MR and CT).

## Method Summary
The proposed FedCoWPro framework operates in two main stages: self-supervised pre-training and federated aggregation. During pre-training, each client generates pseudo-segmentation masks from their local datasets using a combination of spatial and edge-aware dice losses. These pseudo-labels are then used to train local models in a self-supervised manner. The federated aggregation stage employs the FedAvg algorithm to combine model updates from all clients while preserving data privacy. The key innovation is the fused dice loss function that integrates both spatial and edge-aware components to improve segmentation quality. The framework is specifically designed to handle cross-modal and cross-imaging technique scenarios, making it applicable to diverse medical imaging modalities including MR and CT.

## Key Results
- FedCoWPro achieves performance comparable to or better than the FedAvg version of CoWPro on held-out validation datasets
- The fused dice loss combining spatial and edge-aware components improves segmentation performance
- Experiments conducted on a novel public multi-organ gynecological MR brachytherapy dataset (MOGaMB) and several abdominal datasets
- Framework successfully handles cross-modal scenarios with data distributed across clients using different imaging modalities (MR and CT)

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The effectiveness of FedCoWPro stems from its ability to leverage pseudo-segmentation masks for self-supervised learning in a federated setting. By generating these masks locally on each client's data, the framework avoids the need for manual annotation while preserving privacy. The federated aggregation through FedAvg allows models to learn from diverse data distributions across institutions without sharing raw data. The fused dice loss plays a crucial role by combining spatial information (capturing overall segmentation accuracy) with edge-aware components (preserving boundary details), which is particularly important for medical image segmentation where precise organ boundaries are critical. The cross-modal capability enables knowledge transfer between different imaging modalities, enhancing generalization across diverse clinical scenarios.

## Foundational Learning
- **Federated Learning (FedAvg)**: A distributed machine learning approach where multiple clients train models locally and share only model updates with a central server. Needed to enable privacy-preserving collaborative learning across medical institutions. Quick check: Verify that model updates are properly aggregated without exposing individual client data.

- **Self-supervised Learning**: A learning paradigm where models generate their own supervisory signals from unlabeled data. Required here to create pseudo-segmentation masks without manual annotation. Quick check: Ensure pseudo-label quality by validating against a small set of ground truth labels.

- **Few-shot Segmentation**: A learning paradigm where models learn to segment objects from very few examples. Essential for medical imaging where obtaining large annotated datasets is challenging. Quick check: Test performance on varying shot numbers (1-5) to establish the framework's few-shot capabilities.

- **Dice Loss**: A metric-based loss function commonly used in segmentation tasks that measures overlap between predicted and ground truth masks. Forms the basis for the fused loss component. Quick check: Monitor dice coefficient during training to ensure convergence.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Client Data -> Pseudo-label Generation -> Self-supervised Pre-training -> Local Model Updates -> FedAvg Aggregation -> Global Model -> Validation

**Critical Path**: The critical path flows from local data through pseudo-label generation, self-supervised training, and FedAvg aggregation to produce the final global model. Any bottleneck in pseudo-label generation or communication during FedAvg will directly impact overall performance.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The framework trades computational overhead on individual clients (for pseudo-label generation and local training) against improved privacy and generalization. The fused dice loss adds complexity but provides better boundary preservation compared to standard dice loss.

**Failure Signatures**: Performance degradation may occur due to poor quality pseudo-labels, excessive client heterogeneity causing model divergence, or communication bottlenecks during FedAvg. Monitoring pseudo-label quality and client model divergence can help identify these issues.

**First Experiments**:
1. Baseline evaluation with standard dice loss (without edge-aware component) to quantify the benefit of the fused loss
2. Test with varying numbers of clients (2, 4, 8) to assess scalability and performance stability
3. Cross-modal validation by training on MR data and testing on CT data (and vice versa) to verify cross-modal generalization

## Limitations
- Experiments rely on a relatively small dataset with only 4 clients in primary evaluation
- No comprehensive ablation studies on the impact of varying client counts, data heterogeneity levels, or communication rounds
- Proposed fused dice loss lacks thorough theoretical justification for its specific benefits in the federated self-supervised setting
- Evaluation primarily focuses on dice coefficient metrics without exploring other clinically relevant segmentation quality measures or their statistical distributions across runs

## Confidence
- High confidence: The basic feasibility of implementing federated self-supervised one-shot segmentation using the CoWPro framework
- Medium confidence: The specific improvements achieved through the fused dice loss formulation
- Medium confidence: The claim of comparable or better performance relative to FedAvg-CoWPro baseline
