# CoCre-Sam (Kokkuri-san): Modeling Ouija Board as Collective Langevin Dynamics Sampling from Fused Language Models

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2507.11906
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.11906
- Reference count: 11
- Primary result: Presents CoCre-Sam, a framework modeling the Ouija board phenomenon as collective Langevin dynamics sampling from fused language models

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces CoCre-Sam (Collective-Creature Sampling), a novel framework that models the Ouija board phenomenon as collective Langevin dynamics sampling from fused language models. Each participant is represented as an agent with an internal language model defining an energy landscape, and their stochastic actions based on local gradients are shown to correspond to sampling from the sum of individual energy landscapes. The framework provides a computational mechanism linking individual implicit knowledge, embodied collective action, and emergent linguistic phenomena.

Theoretical analysis proves the collective dynamics are equivalent to Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA) sampling from a fused distribution, approximating a Product-of-Experts form. Simulations demonstrate that CoCre-Sam effectively fuses different language models to generate meaningful character sequences (e.g., flower names like "petunia", "jacobinia"), while ablation studies confirm the essential roles of collective interaction and stochasticity.

## Method Summary
CoCre-Sam models each Ouija board participant as an autonomous agent equipped with an individual language model that defines its local energy landscape. Agents interact through a shared physical medium (the board) and update their positions based on stochastic gradient descent in their respective energy landscapes, incorporating noise to enable exploration. The collective dynamics of all agents are theoretically shown to be equivalent to Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA) sampling from a fused distribution that approximates the Product-of-Experts (PoE) form. This fusion allows the system to combine implicit knowledge from multiple agents without requiring explicit model sharing or centralized coordination. The framework captures how embodied collective action can lead to emergent agency and shared meaning-making through distributed sampling processes.

## Key Results
- CoCre-Sam theoretically establishes equivalence between collective agent dynamics and ULA sampling from a fused distribution approximating Product-of-Experts
- Simulations demonstrate successful generation of meaningful character sequences (flower names) through model fusion
- Ablation studies confirm collective interaction and stochasticity are essential for effective knowledge fusion

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
CoCre-Sam works by representing each participant as an agent with an internal language model that defines an energy landscape over possible outputs. Through stochastic gradient-based movement influenced by local gradients and noise, agents collectively explore the joint solution space. The key insight is that the sum of individual energy landscapes (from all agents) creates a fused landscape that can be sampled using collective Langevin dynamics. This approach naturally implements a Product-of-Experts approximation without requiring explicit model combination or centralized coordination, allowing implicit knowledge from multiple sources to be effectively fused through embodied interaction.

## Foundational Learning

**Langevin Dynamics**: Stochastic optimization method combining gradient descent with noise injection, needed for enabling exploration and preventing local minima trapping; quick check: verify noise level affects exploration vs exploitation balance.

**Product-of-Experts (PoE)**: Probabilistic framework for combining multiple expert models by multiplying their distributions, needed for understanding how individual agent knowledge combines; quick check: confirm PoE approximation holds under different agent configurations.

**Energy Landscape**: Representation of a model's "preference" over possible outputs as a potential function, needed to formalize how each agent's internal model influences collective behavior; quick check: validate energy landscape shapes affect convergence quality.

**Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)**: Markov Chain Monte Carlo method using Langevin dynamics for sampling, needed to connect collective agent dynamics to formal sampling theory; quick check: ensure mixing time is reasonable for practical applications.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Individual Agent Models -> Energy Landscapes -> Collective Dynamics -> Fused Output Distribution

**Critical Path**: Agent initialization → Energy landscape computation → Stochastic gradient update → Collective state update → Output generation

**Design Tradeoffs**: 
- More agents improve knowledge diversity but increase computational cost
- Higher noise enables better exploration but may reduce output coherence
- Simpler energy landscapes are faster but may limit fusion quality

**Failure Signatures**:
- Poor output quality indicates insufficient agent diversity or inadequate noise levels
- Slow convergence suggests suboptimal energy landscape design or excessive noise
- Mode collapse indicates dominant agents overwhelming collective dynamics
