# Perfect reconstruction of sparse signals using nonconvexity control and one-step RSB message passing

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2512.17426
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.17426
- Reference count: 30
- Primary result: 1RSB-AMP with nonconvexity control achieves modestly improved reconstruction limits over RS-AMP but remains slightly inferior to Bayes-optimal thresholds

## Executive Summary
This paper develops a one-step replica-symmetry-breaking (1RSB) extension of approximate message passing (AMP) for compressed sensing with the smoothly clipped absolute deviation (SCAD) penalty. The key innovation is applying the 1RSB framework to navigate nonconvex optimization landscapes more effectively than the replica-symmetric approach, particularly in regions where RS-AMP diverges. The authors derive explicit update rules for 1RSB-AMP and corresponding state evolution equations, and propose a new criterion for optimizing the Parisi parameter by minimizing the size of the diverging region rather than using the conventional zero-complexity condition. When combined with the nonconvexity-control protocol, the 1RSB-AMP achieves a modestly improved algorithmic limit for perfect reconstruction compared to RS-AMP, though it remains slightly inferior to the Bayes-optimal threshold.

## Method Summary
The authors develop a one-step RSB extension of AMP for compressed sensing with SCAD regularization. The method propagates distributions over belief-propagation messages (ensembles of pure states) weighted by the Parisi parameter ℓ, rather than tracking single messages. They derive explicit update rules and state evolution equations for this 1RSB-AMP, and introduce a nonconvexity-control (NCC) protocol that gradually decreases the regularization parameter λ while monitoring convergence to navigate between success and failure phases. The Parisi parameter is optimized by minimizing the size of the diverging region rather than using the conventional zero-complexity condition.

## Key Results
- 1RSB-AMP and 1RSB-SE agree remarkably well at macroscopic level even in regions where RS-AMP diverges
- Proposed Parisi parameter optimization via divergence-region minimization achieves improved algorithmic limits over RS-AMP
- NCC protocol successfully navigates algorithm trajectory from failure phase to success phase by gradually decreasing λ
- Reconstruction performance remains modestly better than RS-AMP but slightly inferior to Bayes-optimal thresholds

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1: Ensemble Averaging Over BP Fixed Points
The 1RSB extension maintains macroscopic stability by propagating distributions over messages corresponding to exponentially many metastable states. The Parisi parameter ℓ controls weighting of these states in ensemble averages, with estimators computed by averaging across the state ensemble rather than selecting single states. This approach remains stable even when RS-AMP diverges, though the 1RSB description may not be thermodynamically exact in these regions.

### Mechanism 2: Parisi Parameter Optimization via Divergence-Region Minimization
The paper optimizes ℓ to minimize the size of the diverging phase boundary, which depends on ℓ due to the 1RSB ansatz. For each λ, sweeping ℓ ∈ [0, 20] identifies ℓ* that maximizes the boundary sparsity threshold, thereby extending the achievable reconstruction region. This differs from conventional zero-complexity conditions, which were avoided due to numerical difficulties with computed complexity exhibiting non-convex or negative behavior.

### Mechanism 3: Nonconvexity-Control (NCC) Protocol as Phase Navigation
The NCC protocol navigates from failure to success phase by initializing λ at a large value (success phase) and gradually decreasing it while monitoring convergence via susceptibility χ(t) and divergence measure D(t). This annealing-like procedure allows the AMP trajectory to avoid the diverging phase that would block direct optimization at small λ, with Δλ = 0.1 recommended as sufficiently safe from the SE perspective.

## Limitations
- Thermodynamic accuracy: 1RSB description is not expected to be exact in divergent regions, requiring full RSB for thermodynamic exactness
- Numerical precision issues: Complexity computation exhibited non-convex behavior and negative values, violating thermodynamic consistency
- Limited parameter validation: Analysis focuses on specific parameter choices without systematic exploration of broader parameter spaces

## Confidence

- **High Confidence**: Mathematical derivation of 1RSB-AMP update equations and state evolution is rigorous; 1RSB framework for nonconvex landscapes is well-established
- **Medium Confidence**: Numerical results showing improved reconstruction limits over RS-AMP are convincing but comparison with Bayes-optimal thresholds is limited
- **Low Confidence**: Thermodynamic exactness in divergent regions and optimality of divergence-region minimization criterion are not rigorously established
