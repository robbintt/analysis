# Temporal Alignment Guidance: On-Manifold Sampling in Diffusion Models

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2510.11057
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.11057
- **Reference count**: 40
- **Key outcome**: TAG consistently improves sample quality with significant gains in FID and accuracy metrics under strong guidance conditions

## Executive Summary
Temporal Alignment Guidance (TAG) addresses the off-manifold phenomenon in diffusion models caused by arbitrary external guidance. The method introduces a corrective term based on a time predictor that estimates temporal deviations and steers samples back toward the correct manifold at each timestep. By formulating this as a Time-Linked Score (TLS) that linearly combines scores from other timesteps, TAG theoretically reduces score approximation error and improves convergence guarantees while demonstrating practical improvements across image, molecule, and audio generation tasks.

## Method Summary
TAG operates by leveraging a time predictor to estimate temporal deviations during the diffusion sampling process. The key innovation is the Time-Linked Score (TLS), which is expressed as a linear combination of scores from other timesteps, allowing samples to be attracted toward the target manifold and repelled from competing manifolds. This corrective term is applied at each timestep during sampling, effectively aligning the generation process with the true data manifold despite the presence of external guidance that might otherwise push samples off-manifold.

## Key Results
- TAG consistently improves sample quality across multiple domains (images, molecules, audio)
- Significant gains in FID and accuracy metrics, particularly under strong guidance conditions
- Theoretical analysis shows reduced expected score approximation error and improved convergence guarantees

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
TAG works by correcting the temporal misalignment that occurs when external guidance is applied during diffusion sampling. The time predictor identifies when and where the sampling trajectory deviates from the correct manifold, and the TLS term provides a mathematically principled way to steer the sample back on track. This is particularly effective under strong guidance, where the risk of off-manifold sampling is highest. The linear combination of scores from different timesteps creates an attractor-repeller dynamic that maintains manifold consistency throughout the generation process.

## Foundational Learning
- **Diffusion models**: Sequential denoising processes that generate data by reversing a noising process; needed to understand the baseline generation framework
- **Score matching**: Learning to estimate the gradient of log probability density; critical for understanding how diffusion models navigate data manifolds
- **Manifold hypothesis**: Real data concentrates near low-dimensional manifolds; essential for grasping why off-manifold sampling is problematic
- **Temporal alignment**: Synchronizing sampling trajectories with the correct manifold across timesteps; the core challenge TAG addresses
- **Time predictor**: A model that estimates temporal deviations during sampling; the mechanism by which TAG identifies misalignment
- **Linear combination of scores**: The mathematical formulation of TLS; key to understanding how TAG steers samples between manifolds

## Architecture Onboarding
- **Component map**: Time predictor -> TLS computation -> Corrective term application -> Diffusion sampling loop
- **Critical path**: During each sampling timestep, the time predictor estimates deviation, TLS computes corrective score combination, and this is applied before the next denoising step
- **Design tradeoffs**: TAG trades additional computational overhead (time predictor and TLS computation) for improved sample quality and manifold consistency, particularly beneficial under strong guidance
- **Failure signatures**: Poor time predictor accuracy leads to incorrect corrective terms, potentially pushing samples further off-manifold; linear combination assumption breakdown for complex manifolds
- **First experiments**: 1) Test TAG on a simple synthetic dataset with known manifold structure, 2) Apply TAG to a standard image diffusion model with classifier-free guidance, 3) Evaluate TAG's sensitivity to time predictor noise levels

## Limitations
- Effectiveness depends on time predictor quality and validity of linear combination approximation
- Limited ablation studies on hyperparameter sensitivity and impact of time predictor noise
- No investigation of failure modes when underlying diffusion model is poorly conditioned

## Confidence
- **High**: Theoretical framework and application to standard diffusion models
- **Medium**: Generalizability to all guidance scenarios and data types due to limited robustness analysis
