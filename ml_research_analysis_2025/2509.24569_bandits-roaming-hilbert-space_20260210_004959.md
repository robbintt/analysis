# Bandits roaming Hilbert space
## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2509.24569

## Executive Summary
This thesis explores exploration-exploitation tradeoffs in online learning of quantum states using multi-armed bandit frameworks. The work establishes information-theoretic lower bounds and develops optimal strategies with matching upper bounds for quantum state tomography. A key contribution is demonstrating that for pure states with continuous actions, polylogarithmic regret can be achieved through a sample-optimal algorithm based on weighted online least squares estimation.

The research reframes quantum state tomography to efficiently learn states while minimizing measurement disturbance, with applications extending to quantum recommender systems and thermodynamic work extraction where exponential advantages over naive protocols are shown. The algorithm controls design matrix eigenvalues through geometric arguments and employs the optimistic principle for effective learning in quantum bandit settings.

## Method Summary
The thesis employs information-theoretic analysis to establish lower bounds for quantum state learning in bandit settings. For the upper bound construction, a weighted online least squares estimator is used, which controls the eigenvalues of the design matrix through careful geometrical arguments. The algorithm leverages the optimistic principle to balance exploration and exploitation effectively. For pure states with continuous actions, the approach achieves polylogarithmic regret by optimizing the measurement strategy and leveraging the structure of quantum state space.

## Key Results
- Information-theoretic lower bounds established for quantum state learning regret
- Optimal strategies developed with regret scaling as square root of rounds
- Polylogarithmic regret achieved for pure states with continuous actions using weighted online least squares estimator
- Applications to quantum recommender systems and thermodynamic work extraction show exponential advantages

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The algorithm's effectiveness stems from its ability to control the eigenvalues of the design matrix through geometric arguments specific to quantum state space. By leveraging the optimistic principle, the method balances exploration of unknown quantum states with exploitation of acquired knowledge. The weighted online least squares estimator adapts to the quantum measurement outcomes, ensuring sample-optimal learning rates. The framework exploits the mathematical structure of Hilbert space to achieve tighter regret bounds than classical bandit approaches.

## Architecture Onboarding
Component map: Measurement operators -> Design matrix -> Weighted least squares estimator -> State estimate -> Regret calculation

Critical path: The algorithm proceeds by selecting measurements based on optimistic estimates, updating the design matrix with new measurement outcomes, computing the weighted least squares estimate of the quantum state, and evaluating the resulting regret. The key bottleneck is maintaining control over the eigenvalues of the design matrix to ensure stable estimation.

Design tradeoffs: The approach trades off between measurement disturbance and learning efficiency, requiring careful selection of measurement bases. Continuous action spaces offer finer control but increase computational complexity. The geometric control of eigenvalues provides stability but may limit adaptability to rapidly changing quantum states.

Failure signatures: Poor eigenvalue control in the design matrix leads to unstable estimates and increased regret. Inappropriate measurement selection can cause excessive disturbance to the quantum state being learned. Numerical instability may arise when dealing with near-degenerate quantum states or insufficient measurement statistics.
