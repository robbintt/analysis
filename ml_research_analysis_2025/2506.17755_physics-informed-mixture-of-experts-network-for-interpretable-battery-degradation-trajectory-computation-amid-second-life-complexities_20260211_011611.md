# Physics-informed mixture of experts network for interpretable battery degradation trajectory computation amid second-life complexities

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2506.17755
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.17755
- Reference count: 40
- Major achievement: PIMOE achieves 0.88% MAPE and 0.43 ms inference time for battery degradation prediction using only partial field data

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces a Physics-Informed Mixture of Experts (PIMOE) network that predicts battery degradation trajectories using minimal field-accessible signals, addressing the challenge of second-life battery deployment where historical data is unavailable. PIMOE combines voltage relaxation and partial charging data at random states of charge to classify degradation modes and predict long-term degradation trends without requiring full charge/discharge cycles. The approach achieves state-of-the-art accuracy (0.88% MAPE) with 50% better performance than existing methods, while operating with minimal data (5MB) and providing interpretable degradation mode classification.

## Method Summary
PIMOE operates by extracting partial, field-accessible signals such as voltage relaxation and partial charging data collected at random states of charge. These signals are used to classify degradation modes through expert weight synthesis, where multiple experts contribute based on their relevance to specific degradation patterns. The model then predicts long-term degradation trends using a use-dependent recurrent network that captures the temporal dynamics of battery aging. The physics-informed component ensures that predictions adhere to known battery degradation mechanisms, while the mixture of experts architecture allows for flexible adaptation to different degradation modes. This approach eliminates the need for full charge/discharge cycles and historical data, making it suitable for second-life battery applications.

## Key Results
- Achieves 0.88% average MAPE on 207 batteries across 77 use conditions and 67,902 cycles
- Delivers 0.43 ms inference time, outperforming state-of-the-art methods by 50% in both accuracy and speed
- Supports 150-cycle forecasts with 1.50% average MAPE using only 5MB of data

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
PIMOE works by leveraging the physics-informed approach to constrain predictions within known degradation mechanisms, ensuring physical plausibility. The mixture of experts architecture allows the model to dynamically assign weights to different degradation modes based on partial field data, enabling accurate classification even with incomplete information. The use-dependent recurrent network captures the temporal dependencies of battery aging, allowing for precise long-term predictions. By synthesizing partial signals (voltage relaxation, partial charging) at random states of charge, PIMOE bypasses the need for full charge/discharge cycles, making it practical for real-world deployment. The model's interpretability stems from the explicit degradation mode classification, which provides insights into the underlying aging processes.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component map**: Field data (voltage relaxation, partial charging) -> Expert weight synthesis -> Degradation mode classification -> Use-dependent recurrent network -> Long-term degradation prediction

**Critical path**: Partial field signals are processed through expert weight synthesis to classify degradation modes, which are then fed into the recurrent network for long-term prediction. The physics-informed constraints ensure physical plausibility throughout the pipeline.

**Design tradeoffs**: The model trades off computational complexity for interpretability and minimal data requirements. The use of partial signals reduces data collection overhead but may limit accuracy in highly variable conditions. The mixture of experts architecture enhances flexibility but increases model complexity.

**Failure signatures**: Model performance may degrade when applied to batteries with degradation patterns significantly different from the training data. Over-reliance on partial signals could lead to misclassification in cases of noisy or incomplete data. The interpretability of expert weight synthesis may diminish as the number of degradation modes increases.

## Limitations
- Generalizability beyond the specific battery chemistry and use conditions tested (207 batteries, 77 conditions).
- Reliance on partial field-accessible signals may face challenges with different degradation patterns or operating environments.
- Performance metrics based on controlled test conditions; real-world variability could impact accuracy.
- 5MB data requirement may pose constraints in ultra-low-resource edge devices.
- Interpretability of expert weight synthesis may become complex as the number of degradation modes increases.

## Confidence

**High**: Accuracy (0.88% MAPE) and inference speed (0.43 ms) based on rigorous testing on a large dataset (67,902 cycles).

**Medium**: Scalability and real-world applicability, as performance in uncontrolled environments remains to be validated.

**Medium**: Interpretability of degradation mode classification, as complexity of expert weight synthesis could increase with more modes.
