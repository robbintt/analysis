# ProFi-Net: Prototype-based Feature Attention with Curriculum Augmentation for WiFi-based Gesture Recognition

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2504.20193
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.20193
- Reference count: 24
- ProFi-Net achieves 66.5% accuracy in 5-way 1-shot and 84.1% in 5-way 5-shot settings

## Executive Summary
ProFi-Net introduces a novel few-shot learning framework specifically designed for WiFi-based gesture recognition, addressing the challenges of limited training data and sparse feature representations. The framework combines prototype-based metric learning with a feature-level attention mechanism that dynamically refines distance calculations by emphasizing discriminative feature dimensions. A unique curriculum-inspired data augmentation strategy is applied exclusively to the query set, progressively introducing Gaussian noise of increasing magnitude to enhance generalization and robustness to overfitting.

The approach demonstrates significant improvements over conventional prototype networks and other state-of-the-art few-shot learning methods, achieving 66.5% accuracy in 5-way 1-shot and 84.1% in 5-way 5-shot settings. These results represent up to 7.1% improvement over baseline methods, suggesting strong potential for real-world deployment in environments where labeled data is scarce.

## Method Summary
ProFi-Net employs a prototype-based metric learning architecture enhanced with a feature-level attention mechanism for WiFi-based gesture recognition. The framework uses a curriculum-inspired data augmentation strategy that exclusively augments the query set with progressively increasing Gaussian noise to improve generalization. The attention mechanism dynamically refines Euclidean distance calculations by emphasizing the most discriminative feature dimensions, while the curriculum augmentation approach helps prevent overfitting to limited training data.

## Key Results
- Achieves 66.5% accuracy in 5-way 1-shot gesture recognition
- Achieves 84.1% accuracy in 5-way 5-shot gesture recognition
- Demonstrates up to 7.1% improvement over baseline methods

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework's effectiveness stems from combining prototype-based metric learning with attention mechanisms that can identify and emphasize the most discriminative features in sparse WiFi signal representations. The curriculum augmentation strategy on the query set allows the model to learn robustness progressively, starting with less challenging perturbations and gradually increasing complexity. This approach addresses the fundamental challenge of limited training data in few-shot learning scenarios by enhancing the model's ability to generalize from limited examples.
