# DriveGen: Towards Infinite Diverse Traffic Scenarios with Large Models

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2503.05808
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.05808
- Authors: Shenyu Zhang; Jiaguo Tian; Zhengbang Zhu; Shan Huang; Jucheng Yang; Weinan Zhang
- Reference count: 40
- Generates infinite diverse traffic scenarios using large language models for autonomous driving training

## Executive Summary
DriveGen introduces a novel traffic simulation framework that leverages large language models (LLMs) and visual language models (VLMs) to generate diverse and realistic microscopic traffic scenarios for autonomous driving training and testing. The framework addresses the limitations of existing data-driven approaches that rely on single real-world datasets, which restricts their diversity and hinders downstream algorithm optimization. DriveGen operates in two stages: scenario initialization using LLMs to generate semantic maps and vehicle assets, followed by scenario rollout where VLMs analyze simulated images to select realistic waypoint goals while a diffusion-based trajectory planner generates smooth vehicle trajectories.

The system demonstrates significant improvements in scenario diversity (50%+ better than prediction baselines and 20%+ than generative baselines) while maintaining superior realism. Additionally, DriveGen-CS enables efficient corner case generation by automatically generating challenging scenarios based on failures of target driving algorithms, achieving 80% reduction in GPU hours compared to baseline methods. The framework's ability to generate infinite diverse scenarios while maintaining high realism makes it particularly valuable for training robust autonomous driving systems that can handle rare and challenging traffic situations.

## Method Summary
DriveGen operates through a two-stage generation process: scenario initialization and scenario rollout. During initialization, LLMs generate semantic maps and vehicle assets based on text descriptions, creating the foundational structure for traffic scenarios. In the rollout stage, VLMs analyze simulated images to select realistic waypoint goals, while a diffusion-based trajectory planner generates smooth vehicle trajectories. This two-staged process enables DriveGen to produce traffic scenarios with greater diversity while maintaining high realism compared to state-of-the-art baselines.

For efficient corner case generation, DriveGen-CS automatically generates challenging scenarios by analyzing failures of target driving algorithms. The system uses failure features as knowledge prompts for VLMs without requiring retraining or fine-tuning, making it highly efficient for iterative algorithm optimization. This approach allows the framework to identify and generate scenarios that specifically target algorithm weaknesses, improving the robustness of autonomous driving systems through targeted training.

## Key Results
- Achieves 50%+ better diversity than prediction baselines and 20%+ than generative baselines
- Maintains superior realism compared to existing methods
- DriveGen-CS generates challenging corner cases with 80% reduction in GPU hours compared to baseline methods
- Training driving algorithms on DriveGen scenarios leads to better performance across multiple metrics

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
DriveGen leverages the generative capabilities of large language models to create diverse traffic scenarios while using visual language models to ensure realism through image analysis. The two-stage process allows for creative scenario generation in the initialization phase, followed by rigorous validation in the rollout phase. The diffusion-based trajectory planner ensures smooth vehicle movements that mimic real-world driving behavior. DriveGen-CS's ability to automatically generate corner cases based on algorithm failures enables targeted training that addresses specific weaknesses, making the autonomous driving system more robust to rare but critical situations.

## Foundational Learning
- Large Language Models (LLMs): Generative models that can create diverse text-based scenarios from prompts - needed for creative scenario initialization, quick check: can generate varied semantic maps and vehicle descriptions
- Visual Language Models (VLMs): Models that can analyze images and extract meaningful information - needed for realism validation during rollout, quick check: can identify realistic waypoint goals from simulated images
- Diffusion-based Trajectory Planning: A generative approach for creating smooth trajectories - needed for realistic vehicle movement, quick check: generates collision-free, smooth paths
- Semantic Maps: High-level representations of traffic scenarios - needed for scenario structure definition, quick check: captures essential traffic elements like lanes, intersections, and obstacles
- Corner Case Generation: Identifying and creating rare but critical scenarios - needed for robust algorithm training, quick check: can generate scenarios that expose algorithm weaknesses
- Failure Feature Analysis: Using algorithm failures to guide scenario generation - needed for targeted training, quick check: can extract meaningful features from failure cases

## Architecture Onboarding

Component Map: LLM -> Semantic Map Generation -> VLM -> Waypoint Selection -> Diffusion Planner -> Trajectory Generation -> Simulation Environment

Critical Path: Text Description → LLM → Semantic Map → VLM → Waypoint Selection → Diffusion Planner → Vehicle Trajectories → Drive Simulation

Design Tradeoffs:
- Diversity vs. Realism: The two-stage process balances creative generation with validation, potentially adding computational overhead
- Open-ended Generation vs. Controlled Generation: Text-based initialization allows for infinite scenarios but may introduce bias based on prompt quality
- Automated Corner Case Generation vs. Manual Engineering: DriveGen-CS reduces human effort but depends on the quality of failure analysis

Failure Signatures:
- Unrealistic Vehicle Interactions: May occur if VLM waypoint selection fails to capture complex traffic dynamics
- Geometric Inconsistencies: Can arise from LLM-generated semantic maps that don't align with physical constraints
- Repetitive Patterns: May emerge if the generation process converges to similar scenarios despite diversity goals

## Limitations
- Text description quality and linguistic diversity can introduce bias in scenario initialization
- VLM effectiveness depends heavily on training data distribution and may struggle with novel situations
- Two-stage generation process may introduce cumulative errors that compound through the simulation pipeline
- 80% GPU hours reduction claim lacks direct comparison methodology details
