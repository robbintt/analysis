## Executive Summary
This survey provides a comprehensive overview of diffusion models applied to molecular generative tasks. The authors address the challenge of navigating the fragmented landscape of molecular diffusion models, which arises from diverse formulations, data modalities, and task types. By proposing a novel taxonomy based on method formulations (DDPM, SMLD, SDE, and variants), data modalities (2D topological space, 3D geometric space, and 2D&3D joint space), and task types (de novo generation, molecular optimization, conformer generation, molecular docking, and transition state generation), the survey aims to provide a structured framework for understanding and further developing this area.

## Method Summary
The survey systematically reviews existing methods in molecular diffusion modeling, categorizing them based on their formulation (DDPM, SMLD, SDE, and variants), data modalities (2D topological, 3D geometric, and joint spaces), and task types (de novo generation, molecular optimization, conformer generation, molecular docking, and transition state generation). The authors summarize existing methods and highlight opportunities for future research, emphasizing the need for more sophisticated diffusion models, challenging generative tasks, and expressive network architectures.

## Foundational Learning

**DDPM (Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models)**: A diffusion model formulation that uses a fixed noise schedule and learns to denoise data step-by-step. Why needed: Provides a stable and effective approach for molecular generation. Quick check: Verify the model can successfully denoise simple molecular structures.

**SMLD (Score Matching with Langevin Dynamics)**: A diffusion model that learns the score function of the data distribution and uses Langevin dynamics for sampling. Why needed: Offers an alternative approach to molecular generation with potentially different strengths. Quick check: Compare performance on molecular tasks against DDPM.

**SDE (Stochastic Differential Equations)**: A formulation of diffusion models using continuous-time stochastic processes. Why needed: Provides a continuous-time perspective on molecular generation, potentially offering more flexibility. Quick check: Evaluate the model's ability to handle continuous molecular representations.

**2D Topological Space**: Represents molecular structures using graph-based representations focusing on connectivity. Why needed: Captures essential molecular information while being computationally efficient. Quick check: Verify the model can accurately represent molecular connectivity.

**3D Geometric Space**: Represents molecules using 3D coordinates and conformations. Why needed: Captures spatial information crucial for molecular properties and interactions. Quick check: Evaluate the model's ability to generate valid molecular conformations.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Data Representation (2D/3D) -> Diffusion Model (DDPM/SMLD/SDE) -> Task-specific Network -> Output (Generated Molecules/Conformers)

**Critical Path**: Data Representation -> Diffusion Model -> Task-specific Network
The diffusion model is the core component, with data representation and task-specific networks adapting it to molecular tasks.

**Design Tradeoffs**: 
- 2D vs 3D representations: 2D is more efficient but 3D captures spatial information
- DDPM vs SMLD vs SDE: Different formulations offer trade-offs in stability, efficiency, and flexibility
- Task-specific adaptations: Balancing generality and specialization for different molecular tasks

**Failure Signatures**:
- Invalid molecular structures in 2D generation
- Unrealistic conformations in 3D generation
- Poor performance on specific molecular tasks despite good general generation
