# High-Throughput Computational Screening and Interpretable Machine Learning of Metal-organic Frameworks for Iodine Capture

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2502.15764
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.15764
- Reference count: 40
- Key outcome: R²=0.941, MAE=18.276 for iodine uptake prediction in 1816 MOFs under humid conditions

## Executive Summary
This study combines high-throughput computational screening with interpretable machine learning to identify optimal metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) for iodine capture under humid conditions. The research analyzes structural characteristics to determine optimal pore parameters, then trains Random Forest and CatBoost models using structural, molecular, and chemical descriptors to predict adsorption performance. Feature importance analysis reveals that Henry's coefficient and heat of adsorption are the most crucial chemical factors, while six-membered ring structures and nitrogen atoms serve as key structural features enhancing iodine adsorption.

## Method Summary
The methodology involves computational screening of 1816 MOF structures from the CoRE MOF 2014 database, filtered by pore limiting diameter greater than 3.34 Å. Grand Canonical Monte Carlo (GCMC) simulations using RASPA generate adsorption data under specific conditions (423K, 1 bar, 100% RH). Six structural descriptors, 25 molecular descriptors, 8 chemical descriptors (Henry's coefficients and heats of adsorption), and 4 molecular fingerprint types are extracted. Random Forest and CatBoost models are trained progressively with increasing feature sets, and SHAP values provide interpretability for feature importance.

## Key Results
- Optimal pore confinement for iodine capture occurs at Largest Cavity Diameter (LCD) between 4-7.8 Å, with 4-5.5 Å showing strongest performance
- Progressive incorporation of structural, molecular, and chemical descriptors improves CatBoost prediction accuracy to R²=0.941 and MAE=18.276
- Henry's coefficient and heat of adsorption emerge as the two most crucial chemical factors for iodine uptake prediction
- Six-membered ring structures and nitrogen atoms are identified as key structural features enhancing iodine adsorption

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- **Claim:** Optimal iodine capture in humid environments is strongly dependent on specific pore confinement limits (LCD 4-7.8 Å) rather than maximum surface area.
- **Mechanism:** When LCD < 4 Å, steric hindrance blocks iodine; when > 5.5 Å, interaction energy weakens causing desorption. A narrow "sweet spot" maximizes host-guest interactions while excluding excess water accumulation.
- **Core assumption:** Iodine molecules behave as rigid spheres with kinetic diameter 3.34 Å within rigid framework structures.
- **Evidence anchors:** Relationship between structural characteristics identifying optimal parameters; adsorption interaction becomes dominant when 4 Å < LCD < 5.5 Å.
- **Break condition:** If MOF frameworks exhibit significant flexibility (breathing effects) not modeled in rigid GCMC, these pore size limits may shift.

### Mechanism 2
- **Claim:** Prediction accuracy for humid iodine capture relies heavily on competitive adsorption thermodynamics (Henry's coefficient and Heat of Adsorption) rather than geometric descriptors alone.
- **Mechanism:** In humid conditions, water is a competitive adsorbate. High heat of adsorption for water is strongly negatively correlated with iodine uptake. Effective materials must possess high affinity for iodine while minimizing affinity for water.
- **Core assumption:** Thermodynamic parameters calculated at infinite dilution correlate linearly with performance at higher pressures.
- **Evidence anchors:** Henry's coefficient and heat of adsorption found most crucial chemical factors; H₂O heat strongly negatively correlated with iodine uptake.
- **Break condition:** If synergistic co-adsorption effects occur, simple competitive thermodynamic correlations will fail.

### Mechanism 3
- **Claim:** Specific electronic environments, defined by six-membered rings and nitrogen atoms, serve as the primary active sites for iodine binding.
- **Mechanism:** Molecular fingerprinting identifies that electron-rich regions—specifically aromatic rings (pi-systems) and nitrogen lone pairs—act as preferential adsorption sites. Nitrogen presence also correlates with suppressed pore volume and lower water affinity.
- **Core assumption:** Binary presence/absence of structural keys is sufficient to represent complex quantum mechanical interactions.
- **Evidence anchors:** Six-membered ring structures and nitrogen atoms identified as key structural factors; six-membered aromatic rings provide electron-rich adsorption sites.
- **Break condition:** If metal nodes or defects are the primary binding sites, ligand-based fingerprints will lose predictive power.

## Foundational Learning

- **Concept: Grand Canonical Monte Carlo (GCMC)**
  - **Why needed here:** This is the "ground truth" generator. GCMC simulates adsorption isotherms by statistically inserting/deleting molecules into a fixed framework based on chemical potentials.
  - **Quick check question:** Does the simulation allow the MOF framework atoms to move during the GCMC cycles? (Answer: No, the text specifies "fixed rigid structures".)

- **Concept: MACCS Molecular Fingerprints**
  - **Why needed here:** This translates chemical structure into machine-readable bits (166 keys). The paper uses this to move beyond simple atom counts to structural patterns.
  - **Quick check question:** Why might MACCS fingerprints lower prediction accuracy compared to continuous molecular descriptors? (Answer: They are binary keys and lose "quantity" or "proportion" information.)

- **Concept: SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)**
  - **Why needed here:** The paper uses "Interpretable Machine Learning." SHAP values decompose a prediction to show how much each feature contributed to a specific outcome.
  - **Quick check question:** According to SHAP analysis, is H₂O heat positively or negatively correlated with iodine uptake? (Answer: Negatively.)

## Architecture Onboarding

- **Component map:** CoRE MOF 2014 database -> GCMC simulations (RASPA) -> Structural descriptors (Zeo++) -> Molecular descriptors (lammps_interface) -> Fingerprints (OpenBabel + PaDEL-Descriptor) -> ML models (Random Forest, CatBoost) -> SHAP interpretation

- **Critical path:** The workflow is sequential and bottlenecked by simulation. You cannot train the ML model until GCMC generates the adsorption data for the 1816 MOFs.

- **Design tradeoffs:**
  - Rigidity vs. Reality: Assuming rigid frameworks speeds up GCMC but ignores breathing or gate-opening phenomena in flexible MOFs.
  - Accuracy vs. Interpretability: Using MACCS fingerprints slightly lowers R² compared to continuous descriptors but provides clearer chemical design rules.
  - Generic vs. Specific: The model is tuned for 423K, 1 bar, 100% RH. Performance will degrade if applied to ambient temperature or dry conditions without retraining.

- **Failure signatures:**
  - Water-Loving Metals: If the model suggests MOFs with Lanthanide metals (Bit_6), expect failure in humid conditions due to high water affinity.
  - Over-porous Structures: If LCD > 10 Å is allowed, the model will likely predict low iodine selectivity due to weak confinement forces.
  - Data Leakage: Ensure Henry's coefficients are calculated independently of the GCMC uptake labels to prevent circular validation.

- **First 3 experiments:**
  1. Reproduce Feature Correlation: Calculate Pearson correlation between I₂ Heat and H₂O Heat for top 100 performing MOFs to verify competitive adsorption hypothesis.
  2. Fingerprint Ablation: Train CatBoost model using only structural features, then add MACCS fingerprints. Quantify MAE drop to measure value of chemical context.
  3. Domain Validation: Select top 3 predicted MOFs (e.g., BARZUR, CUVGOQ) and verify stability in 100% relative humidity using short NVT molecular dynamics.

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out

### Open Question 1
- **Question:** How does framework flexibility influence the iodine adsorption performance of the identified top-performing MOFs?
- **Basis in paper:** The methodology section states that MOFs were treated as "fixed rigid structures" during GCMC simulations.
- **Why unresolved:** The rigid model excludes dynamic structural changes, such as gate-opening or breathing effects, which are known to occur in flexible MOFs.
- **What evidence would resolve it:** Molecular dynamics simulations or experimental adsorption isotherms showing step-wise uptake profiles under humid conditions.

### Open Question 2
- **Question:** Do the identified optimal structural features (six-membered rings, nitrogen atoms) effectively predict capture performance for organic iodides like methyl iodide (CH3I)?
- **Basis in paper:** The introduction highlights the danger of various radioactive iodine species and mentions organic iodide capture in the literature review, but the simulations exclusively model I2 molecules.
- **Why unresolved:** The machine learning models were trained and validated only on I2/H2O mixtures, leaving the adsorption behavior of other critical nuclear waste components unexplored.
- **What evidence would resolve it:** Extension of the computational screening to CH3I adsorption or experimental validation using methyl iodide.

### Open Question 3
- **Question:** Do the top-ranked computational candidates (e.g., BARZUR, CUVGOQ) retain their predicted high iodine capacities when synthesized and tested experimentally?
- **Basis in paper:** The study relies entirely on high-throughput computational screening and machine learning predictions without providing experimental synthesis or validation data.
- **Why unresolved:** Real-world factors such as synthesis defects, framework stability, and kinetic limitations are not fully captured by the thermodynamic simulations (GCMC).
- **What evidence would resolve it:** Synthesis of the top-ranked materials and measurement of their iodine uptake and selectivity under 100% relative humidity.

## Limitations
- The rigid framework assumption in GCMC simulations may not capture breathing effects or structural flexibility in certain MOFs
- Model applicability is constrained to specific conditions (423K, 1 bar, 100% RH) and may not generalize to other temperatures, pressures, or humidity levels
- Binary nature of MACCS fingerprints may oversimplify complex chemical interactions, potentially missing nuanced electronic effects

## Confidence
- **High Confidence:** Relationship between LCD and iodine capture performance is well-supported by computational data and established physical principles of molecular confinement
- **Medium Confidence:** Predictive power of thermodynamic parameters for humid conditions is supported by correlation analysis, but linear assumption between Henry's regime and 1 bar performance requires further validation
- **Medium Confidence:** Identification of six-membered rings and nitrogen atoms as key structural features is based on fingerprint analysis, but causal relationship needs experimental verification
