# Multi-class Seismic Building Damage Assessment from InSAR Imagery using Quadratic Variational Causal Bayesian Inference

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2502.18546
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.18546
- Authors: Xuechun Li; Susu Xu
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Multi-class seismic building damage assessment framework using InSAR imagery with QVCBI

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces a novel framework for multi-class seismic building damage assessment using InSAR imagery, addressing challenges of overlapping damage signatures, computational complexity, and rapid regional-scale processing. The approach employs quadratic variational causal Bayesian inference (QVCBI) to integrate InSAR observations with USGS ground failure models and building fragility functions. The framework achieves improved damage classification accuracy (AUC: 0.94-0.96) across five major earthquakes, demonstrating up to 35.7% improvement over existing approaches while reducing computational overhead by over 40% through strategic pruning.

## Method Summary
The framework combines InSAR displacement measurements with ground failure probability models and building fragility functions through a quadratic variational causal Bayesian inference approach. This method captures non-linear damage patterns and causal relationships between seismic events, ground failures, and building damage states. The QVCBI framework incorporates spatial dependencies and uncertainty quantification while maintaining computational efficiency through pruning strategies that reduce model complexity by over 40%.

## Key Results
- Achieved AUC values of 0.94-0.96 for damage classification across five major earthquakes
- Demonstrated 35.7% improvement over existing damage assessment approaches
- Successfully distinguished building damage from secondary hazards like landslides and liquefaction
- Reduced computational overhead by over 40% through pruning strategies

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework's effectiveness stems from integrating multiple data sources through causal Bayesian inference, capturing the complex relationships between seismic events, ground failures, and building damage. QVCBI handles the non-linear and overlapping nature of damage signatures by modeling the underlying causal mechanisms rather than relying on purely discriminative approaches. The quadratic variational formulation allows for efficient approximation of complex posterior distributions while maintaining accuracy.

## Limitations

- Performance metrics primarily validated on five earthquakes, limiting generalizability across different seismic regions and building typologies
- Computational efficiency gains stated without baseline comparisons or processing conditions
- Framework's ability to distinguish damage from secondary hazards relies on USGS ground failure models, whose accuracy in complex terrain remains uncertain
- Building fragility functions assume uniform building stock characteristics, potentially misrepresenting heterogeneous urban environments
