# LACE: Exploring Turn-Taking and Parallel Interaction Modes in Human-AI Co-Creation for Iterative Image Generation

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID:** 2504.14827
- **Source URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.14827
- **Reference count:** 22
- **Primary result:** Professional artists showed significant improvements in satisfaction and ownership when using LACE's dual-mode AI co-creation system compared to standard AI workflows

## Executive Summary
LACE is a human-AI co-creative system integrated into Adobe Photoshop that enables professional artists to use generative AI through controlled prompting and iterative refinement. The system supports both turn-taking and parallel interaction modes, addressing key limitations in existing AI workflows related to precision, iterative coherence, and workflow compatibility. Through a pilot study with 21 professional artists across three creative tasks, LACE demonstrated significant improvements in user satisfaction, ownership, usability, and artistic perception compared to standard AI workflows.

The research highlights the importance of flexible interaction modes in professional creative workflows, with participants preferring turn-taking for initial ideation and parallel mode for refinement. The system's design addresses critical gaps in current AI tools by providing better control over output while maintaining the creative flow that artists need. Statistical significance was achieved across multiple metrics including ownership (p=0.002, r=0.64), usability (p=0.005, r=0.56), and art perception (p=0.001, r=0.71).

## Method Summary
The study employed a within-subjects experimental design where 21 professional artists completed three creative tasks using both LACE and standard AI workflows. Participants were exposed to both turn-taking and parallel interaction modes within LACE, with task order counterbalanced to control for learning effects. The evaluation measured user satisfaction, ownership, usability, and artistic perception through standardized questionnaires and qualitative feedback. The experimental setup focused on real-world creative tasks that artists would encounter in professional settings, ensuring ecological validity of the findings.

## Key Results
- LACE achieved statistically significant improvements in ownership (p=0.002, r=0.64) compared to turn-taking-only workflows
- Usability scores showed significant improvement (p=0.005, r=0.56) with LACE implementation
- Art perception ratings demonstrated the strongest improvement (p=0.001, r=0.71) in LACE conditions
- Participants consistently preferred turn-taking mode for initial ideation and parallel mode for refinement tasks

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The dual-mode approach addresses the fundamental tension between precision control and creative flow in AI-assisted image generation. Turn-taking mode provides the structured, deliberate interaction needed for precise control and initial ideation, while parallel mode enables the fluid, iterative refinement process that professional artists require. This combination allows artists to leverage AI's generative capabilities while maintaining creative agency and workflow coherence.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map:** User Interface -> Interaction Mode Selector -> AI Engine -> Output Generator -> Feedback Loop -> Canvas Integration

**Critical Path:** User selects interaction mode → AI processes prompt/control inputs → System generates output → Artist reviews/refines → Output integrates with Photoshop canvas

**Design Tradeoffs:** The system prioritizes workflow compatibility over maximum AI autonomy, sacrificing some generative power for better integration with professional tools and processes. This means artists get less "wild" AI creativity but maintain better control and coherence.

**Failure Signatures:** Common failure modes include mode-switching confusion, output degradation during parallel refinement, and integration lag with Photoshop. These typically manifest as decreased artist satisfaction and increased time to task completion.

## Limitations
- Small participant pool of 21 professional artists may not capture full diversity of creative workflows
- Limited to three specific creative tasks, potentially missing other professional use cases
- Focus on Adobe Photoshop integration may limit generalizability to other creative software ecosystems

## Confidence
