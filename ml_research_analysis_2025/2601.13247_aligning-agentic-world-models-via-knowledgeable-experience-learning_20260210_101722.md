# Aligning Agentic World Models via Knowledgeable Experience Learning

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID:** 2601.13247
- **Source URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.13247
- **Reference count:** 40
- **Primary result:** WorldMind framework achieves state-of-the-art performance on EB-ALFRED and EB-Habitat benchmarks by constructing a symbolic World Knowledge Repository from environmental feedback

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the critical challenge of "physical hallucinations" in agentic world models, where large language models generate logically sound but physically unexecutable plans. The authors propose WorldMind, a framework that autonomously constructs a symbolic World Knowledge Repository by synthesizing environmental feedback into two key components: Process Experience (derived from prediction errors to enforce physical feasibility) and Goal Experience (distilled from successful trajectories to guide task optimality). Experiments demonstrate significant performance improvements on EB-ALFRED and EB-Habitat benchmarks, with notable gains in Success Rate and Goal-Conditioned Success metrics. The framework shows remarkable cross-model and cross-environment transferability, suggesting the constructed knowledge captures universal physical laws independent of the specific backbone model.

## Method Summary
WorldMind addresses the alignment problem by autonomously constructing a symbolic World Knowledge Repository that unifies Process Experience and Goal Experience. Process Experience is derived from prediction errors in agent-environment interactions, enforcing physical feasibility constraints. Goal Experience is distilled from successful trajectories to guide task optimality. The framework synthesizes environmental feedback to build this knowledge repository, which then informs and constrains the planning process of agentic world models. This approach moves beyond traditional post-hoc alignment methods by integrating physical knowledge directly into the planning process through continuous learning from environmental interactions.

## Key Results
- WorldMind achieves state-of-the-art performance on EB-ALFRED and EB-Habitat benchmarks
- Significant improvements in Success Rate (SR) and Goal-Conditioned Success (GC) metrics compared to baselines
- Demonstrates cross-model and cross-environment transferability, suggesting universal physical knowledge capture
- Effective mitigation of "physical hallucinations" where logically sound plans fail physical execution

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
WorldMind works by creating a continuous feedback loop between the agent and its environment. When the agent attempts actions, environmental feedback reveals prediction errors that highlight physical impossibilities or constraints. These errors are systematically incorporated into the World Knowledge Repository as Process Experience, which enforces physical feasibility in future planning. Simultaneously, successful trajectories provide Goal Experience that captures optimal strategies for task completion. This dual approach ensures that the agent's world model aligns with both physical reality (through Process Experience) and task objectives (through Goal Experience), creating a more robust and executable planning framework.

## Architecture Onboarding
**Component Map:** Environmental Feedback -> World Knowledge Repository -> Agent Planning
**Critical Path:** Agent action attempt → Environmental response → Prediction error analysis → Knowledge repository update → Refined planning
**Design Tradeoffs:** Prioritizes knowledge quality and transferability over raw computational efficiency; balances between comprehensive knowledge capture and repository maintenance overhead
**Failure Signatures:** Performance degradation when environmental feedback is ambiguous or contradictory; knowledge repository becoming outdated in dynamic environments

## Limitations
- Evaluation limited to EB-ALFRED and EB-Habitat benchmarks, raising generalizability concerns
- Insufficient detail on error handling and knowledge quality control mechanisms
- Claims of capturing "universal physical laws" may overstate findings without testing on substantially different domains
- No discussion of computational overhead or long-term stability of the knowledge repository

## Confidence
- **Performance Claims (SR/GC improvements):** Medium - statistically significant on tested benchmarks but limited scope
- **Transferability Claims:** Medium - demonstrated within constrained task domains, not fully general
- **Universal Physical Laws Claims:** Low - extrapolation beyond demonstrated evidence
- **Framework Robustness Claims:** Low - insufficient detail on edge cases and error handling

## Next Checks
1. Test WorldMind on diverse, real-world robotics tasks outside EB-ALFRED and EB-Habitat domains to assess practical generalizability
2. Conduct ablation studies removing either Process Experience or Goal Experience components to quantify their individual contributions
3. Measure and report computational overhead, including repository maintenance costs and inference time impacts, across different model sizes
