# PIBNet: a Physics-Inspired Boundary Network for Multiple Scattering Simulations

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2512.02049
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.02049
- Reference count: 21
- PIBNet achieves error amplitude of 0.091 on Helmholtz Dirichlet problem with three obstacles, outperforming second-best method (0.148)

## Executive Summary
PIBNet introduces a novel learning-based approach for simulating multiple scattering problems using the boundary element method (BEM). The method focuses on approximating the solution trace on domain boundaries, addressing the main computational bottleneck in BEM. By leveraging a physics-inspired graph-based strategy and a multiscale graph neural network architecture, PIBNet efficiently models obstacles and their long-range interactions. The approach demonstrates superior performance on benchmark datasets for 3D exterior Laplace and Helmholtz problems with multiple disjoint obstacles, particularly showing strong generalization to settings with increased numbers of obstacles.

## Method Summary
PIBNet is a learning-based approach for multiple scattering simulations that focuses on approximating the solution trace on domain boundaries. The method uses a physics-inspired graph-based strategy to model obstacles and their long-range interactions efficiently. A novel multiscale graph neural network architecture is introduced to simulate multiple scattering. The approach leverages the boundary element method (BEM) framework, where the computational bottleneck is the boundary solution approximation. By focusing on boundary traces and using graph neural networks to capture long-range interactions, PIBNet aims to improve efficiency and accuracy in multiple scattering simulations.

## Key Results
- PIBNet achieves an error amplitude of 0.091 on the Helmholtz Dirichlet problem with three obstacles
- The method surpasses existing state-of-the-art learning-based methods on considered tasks
- PIBNet exhibits superior generalization to settings with increased numbers of obstacles compared to competing approaches

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
PIBNet works by focusing on the boundary solution approximation, which is the main computational bottleneck in traditional BEM. The physics-inspired graph-based strategy efficiently models obstacles and their long-range interactions by representing the scattering environment as a graph. The multiscale graph neural network architecture processes this graph representation, capturing both local and global scattering effects. This approach allows PIBNet to leverage the strengths of deep learning while incorporating physical insights about wave propagation and scattering, resulting in improved accuracy and generalization compared to existing methods.

## Architecture Onboarding
- **Component Map**: Physical domain -> Boundary discretization -> Graph construction -> Multiscale GNN -> Boundary solution approximation -> Scattered field reconstruction
- **Critical Path**: Graph construction and multiscale GNN processing form the core of PIBNet's approach, with boundary discretization serving as the interface between the physical problem and the learning model.
- **Design Tradeoffs**: The physics-inspired graph strategy balances between capturing essential long-range interactions and maintaining computational efficiency. The multiscale architecture allows for modeling both local and global scattering effects.
- **Failure Signatures**: Performance degradation is expected for highly complex geometries not represented in the training data, and for extremely high-frequency regimes where the wavelength approaches the mesh resolution.
