# Calibration of Multiple Asynchronous Microphone Arrays using Hybrid TDOA

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2502.06195
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.06195
- Reference count: 37
- Proposed method achieves improved accuracy for low to moderate TDOA noise levels in microphone array calibration

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the problem of calibrating multiple asynchronous microphone arrays for accurate sound source localization and tracking. The authors propose a two-stage calibration approach that incorporates hybrid TDOA (time difference of arrival) information, including both inter-array measurements (TDOA-M) and measurements between adjacent sound events (TDOA-S), along with direction of arrival (DOA) measurements and odometer data. The method demonstrates improved performance under low to moderate TDOA noise conditions compared to existing approaches.

## Method Summary
The proposed two-stage calibration method first estimates initial values through weighted nonlinear least squares, using hybrid TDOA, DOA, and odometer data to estimate microphone array positions, orientations, time offsets, clock drift rates, and sound source locations. The second stage performs joint optimization to refine these estimates through simultaneous optimization of all parameters. The method leverages both inter-array and inter-event TDOA measurements, combined with DOA and odometer data, to achieve more accurate calibration than existing approaches.

## Key Results
- Simulation results show improved accuracy in microphone array location estimation (average RMSE of 1.21-45.27 cm versus 0.91-39.94 cm for baseline)
- Orientation estimation accuracy improved (1.06-15.11° versus 1.23-21.92° for baseline)
- Time offset estimation achieved 10^-6s level accuracy
- Real-world experiments showed slightly better final calibration performance compared to baseline method

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method's effectiveness stems from its hybrid TDOA approach that combines inter-array measurements (TDOA-M) with measurements between adjacent sound events (TDOA-S). This dual measurement strategy provides more constraints for the optimization problem, allowing for better estimation of both array parameters and sound source locations. The two-stage approach first obtains reasonable initial estimates before performing joint optimization, which helps avoid local minima and improves convergence.

**Design Tradeoffs**: 
- Hybrid TDOA approach trades computational complexity for improved accuracy
- Two-stage optimization trades initial computation for better convergence
- Combined inter-array and inter-event measurements trade measurement diversity for calibration robustness

**Failure Signatures**:
- Performance degradation when TDOA noise exceeds 0.5ms
- Convergence issues if initial estimates are poor
- Suboptimal results if weighting parameters are not properly tuned
