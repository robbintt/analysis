# Neural network embeddings recover value dimensions from psychometric survey items on par with human data

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2509.24906
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.24906
- Authors: Max Pellert; Clemens M. Lechner; Indira Sen; Markus Strohmaier
- Reference count: 28
- Primary result: Semantic embeddings processed with SQuID recover psychometric value structures with 55% variance explanation compared to human data

## Executive Summary
This study demonstrates that semantic embeddings processed with Survey and Questionnaire Item Embeddings Differentials (SQuID) can recover the structure of human values from psychometric survey items with comparable accuracy to human rater judgments. The approach achieves a 55% variance explanation in dimension-dimension similarities compared to human data and produces MDS configurations aligned with pooled human data from 49 countries. SQuID successfully addresses the challenge of obtaining negative correlations between dimensions without requiring domain-specific fine-tuning.

## Method Summary
The method uses pre-trained semantic embedding models to encode psychometric items, then applies SQuID transformation (subtracting the questionnaire mean vector from each item embedding) to enable negative correlations. Items are aggregated to dimensions, correlation matrices are computed, and Multidimensional Scaling (MDS) produces interpretable value structures. The approach is validated against human data from Schwartz and Cieciuch (2022) and tested on multiple personality inventories.

## Key Results
- SQuID achieves 55% variance explanation in dimension-dimension similarities compared to human data
- Cronbach's alpha per dimension exceeds 0.7 for most dimensions, with some surpassing human data
- MDS configurations produce circular circumplex structures aligned with pooled human data
- Method generalizes successfully to IPIP, BFI-2, and HEXACO personality inventories

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
Embedding differentials (SQuID) enable recovery of negative correlations between psychometric dimensions by subtracting the questionnaire mean vector from individual item embeddings, removing shared linguistic features that prevent negative correlations.

### Mechanism 2
Semantic similarity in high-quality embedding models approximates psychological distance because models optimized for Semantic Textual Similarity map linguistic opposition to semantic opposition, which correlates with psychological opposition.

### Mechanism 3
Aggregating item-level embeddings to dimension level yields reliable latent structures comparable to aggregated human raters, as averaging embeddings of items belonging to the same dimension creates a stable centroid for that trait.

## Foundational Learning

- **Concept: Multidimensional Scaling (MDS)**
  - Why needed: Primary validation technique used in paper (Section 5.2)
  - Quick check: If two dimensions are negatively correlated in input matrix, where will MDS place them relative to each other?

- **Concept: Cronbach's Alpha**
  - Why needed: Used to prove synthetic dimensions are internally consistent (Section 5.1.1)
  - Quick check: Does a Cronbach's Alpha of 0.9 mean the dimension is "real" or just that items are textually similar?

- **Concept: Procrustes Transformation**
  - Why needed: To quantitatively compare AI-generated structure to human structure (Section 5.3)
  - Quick check: Why can't we just correlate raw X,Y coordinates of two MDS plots directly without rotating them first?

## Architecture Onboarding

- **Component map:** Input -> Encoder -> Preprocessing (SQuID) -> Aggregator -> Analysis (Correlation -> MDS -> Visualization)

- **Critical path:** The SQuID subtraction step is the single point of failure. Without it, correlation range compresses and circumplex structure collapses.

- **Design tradeoffs:**
  - Static Embeddings vs. LLM Generation: Uses static embeddings (deterministic, cheap, fast) rather than stochastic LLM generation
  - STS Models vs. General Models: Uses models specifically trained for Semantic Textual Similarity rather than generic generation models

- **Failure signatures:**
  - The "All Positive" Matrix: If correlation matrix has no values < 0, you forgot SQuID subtraction
  - Random Clustering: If Cronbach's Alpha is near 0 or negative, items may be semantically incoherent
  - PCA Failure: PCA instead of MDS may not reveal circular structure clearly

- **First 3 experiments:**
  1. Baseline Replication: Apply SQuID to PVQ-RR items, verify negative correlations appear
  2. Structural Validation: Run MDS on SQuID-adjusted matrix, check for circumplex structure
  3. Generalization Test: Apply same pipeline to HEXACO or IPIP, verify correlation range expansion

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out

- **Question:** To what extent is the 45% unexplained variance attributable to psychometric noise versus systematic biases in LLM training data?
  - Basis: "Future research should decompose this unexplained variance to better understand the complementary information each data source provides."

- **Question:** Can SQuID and MDS configurations be used to systematically quantify the cultural alignment of LLMs by comparing their embeddings against country-specific human value structures?
  - Basis: "We see an exciting opportunity to use our approach and MDS configurations on the level of those individual countries to systematically assess closeness or distance of an LLM to certain cultural value orientations."

- **Question:** Does computing separate scale embeddings for regular and reverse-keyed items improve recovery of negative correlations in complex inventories?
  - Basis: "One possible approach could involve computing separate scale embeddings for regular and reverse-keyed items, then subtracting separately from the two types of items."

- **Question:** Can SQuID effectively recover latent structures from questionnaires with hierarchical, formative, or network-based structures?
  - Basis: "Testing on hierarchical models, formative constructs or network-based psychometric structures would clarify boundary conditions."

## Limitations

- Model dependency: Strong performance relies heavily on specific embedding model (Linq-Embed-Mistral), creating uncertainty about generalizability
- Corpus representativeness: Assumes general language corpora contain sufficient information about psychological value structures, but may contain cultural biases
- Validation methodology: Relies on Procrustes transformations and congruence coefficients that may not fully capture meaningful differences in psychological structure

## Confidence

- **High Confidence:** SQuID methodology is technically sound and demonstrably solves the negative correlation problem
- **Medium Confidence:** Claim of 55% variance explanation is supported but generalizability across models remains uncertain
- **Low Confidence:** Interpretation that MDS configurations represent meaningful "psychological distance" involves substantial assumptions

## Next Checks

1. **Model Generalization Test:** Apply exact SQuID pipeline to three additional embedding models and compare resulting correlation matrices and MDS configurations

2. **Cross-Cultural Validation:** Apply method to value surveys from non-Western cultural contexts and compare recovered structures to established models

3. **Ablation Study on SQuID Components:** Systematically test which components are essential by comparing raw embeddings, per-dimension means, and alternative normalization techniques
