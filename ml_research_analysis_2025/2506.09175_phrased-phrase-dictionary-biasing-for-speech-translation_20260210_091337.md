# PHRASED: Phrase Dictionary Biasing for Speech Translation

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2506.09175
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.09175
- **Reference count**: 8
- **Primary result**: PHRASED improves rare phrase recall by 21% for streaming models and 85% for multimodal LLMs

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces PHRASED, a method to improve speech translation of rare phrases using phrase dictionary biasing. The approach addresses the challenge of accurately translating infrequent or specialized phrases by leveraging explicit source-to-target phrase mappings. Two variants are proposed: phrase selection, which selects target phrases based on source phrase matches in intermediate representations, and joint biasing, which explicitly uses both source and target phrases. The method is evaluated on both streaming speech translation models and multimodal large language models, demonstrating significant improvements in phrase recall.

## Method Summary
PHRASED (Phrase Dictionary Biasing for Speech Translation) addresses the challenge of translating rare phrases in speech translation by leveraging explicit phrase mappings between source and target languages. The method operates by biasing the translation model toward specific target phrases when corresponding source phrases are detected in the input speech. Two variants are introduced: phrase selection, which selects target phrases based on source phrase matches in intermediate representations, and joint biasing, which explicitly uses both source and target phrases to guide translation. The approach is designed to be applicable to both streaming speech translation models, which process audio incrementally, and multimodal large language models, which can incorporate both audio and text inputs.

## Key Results
- PHRASED improves phrase recall by 21% relatively for streaming speech translation models
- PHRASED achieves 85% relative improvement in phrase recall for multimodal LLMs
- The method demonstrates effectiveness across both streaming and non-streaming architectures

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method works by providing explicit phrase-level guidance to translation models. When rare phrases are encountered in source speech, PHRASED biases the model to generate corresponding target phrases by leveraging pre-defined phrase mappings. This approach is particularly effective because traditional speech translation models often struggle with rare or domain-specific terminology. By explicitly providing these mappings, PHRASED reduces the reliance on the model's internal knowledge and improves translation accuracy for critical phrases.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component map**: Audio input -> Feature extraction -> Encoder -> PHRASED biasing module -> Decoder -> Text output

**Critical path**: The critical path involves detecting source phrases in intermediate representations, retrieving corresponding target phrases from the dictionary, and biasing the decoder to favor these target phrases during generation.

**Design tradeoffs**: The method trades increased model complexity and potential latency for improved translation accuracy of rare phrases. The phrase dictionary adds memory overhead but provides explicit control over translation output. Streaming vs. non-streaming architectures require different implementation strategies for phrase biasing.

**Failure signatures**: The approach may fail when source phrases don't match dictionary entries, when multiple valid translations exist for a phrase, or when the biasing interferes with overall translation fluency. Performance degradation may occur with very large phrase dictionaries due to increased computational overhead.
