# The Decoupled Risk Landscape in Performative Prediction

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2506.09044
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.09044
- Reference count: 40
- Introduces decoupled risk landscape visualization for Performative Prediction, showing how to separate model parameters (θM) from data distribution parameters (θD)

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces a novel visualization framework for Performative Prediction (PP) that decouples model parameters from those influencing the data distribution. The key insight is to visualize the risk landscape as a function of both parameter sets (θM, θD), revealing how existing PP algorithms traverse this landscape. Building on this visualization, the authors propose Extended Performative Prediction (EPP), which allows θM ≠ θD, reflecting practical scenarios where users lack access to the deployed model. Experiments show that optimizing for the decoupled optimum yields better performance in terms of revenue and accuracy compared to standard PP methods.

## Method Summary
The authors propose a decoupled risk visualization method that separates model parameters (θM) from data distribution parameters (θD). They define the decoupled performative risk DR(θM, θD) = Ez∼D(θD)[ℓ(z; θM)] and establish new properties for stable and optimal points. The framework enables visualization of how existing PP algorithms (RGD, RRM, PerfGD) traverse the landscape. For Extended PP, they introduce DPerfGD, which optimizes for the decoupled optimal point (θ*M, θ*D) = arg min(θM,θD) DR(θM, θD), computing separate gradients for θM and θD.

## Key Results
- Introduces decoupled risk landscape visualization for PP, revealing stable and optimal point properties
- Proposes Extended Performative Prediction (EPP) framework where θM ≠ θD for practical scenarios
- Shows DPerfGD achieves better performance (revenue, accuracy) than standard PP methods on pricing and credit datasets
- Releases library implementing visualizations and algorithms for further research

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The decoupled visualization works by separating the parameter space into two components: θM (model parameters used for predictions) and θD (parameters influencing the data distribution). This separation allows researchers to visualize how different PP algorithms traverse the risk landscape and understand their convergence behavior. The Extended PP framework works by optimizing for a decoupled optimal point that achieves risk no greater than the standard performative optimum, which is particularly relevant when users lack access to the deployed model.

**Stable and Optimal Points**: Theoretical properties where stable points satisfy ∇M DR(θST, θST) = 0 and optimal points satisfy ∇M DR(θOP, θOP) + ∇D DR(θOP, θOP) = 0. Needed to understand convergence guarantees.
