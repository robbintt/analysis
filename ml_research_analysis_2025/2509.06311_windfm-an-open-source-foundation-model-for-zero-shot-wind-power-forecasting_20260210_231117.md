# WindFM: An Open-Source Foundation Model for Zero-Shot Wind Power Forecasting

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2509.06311
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.06311
- **Reference count**: 40
- **Primary result**: 8.1M parameter foundation model achieves state-of-the-art zero-shot wind power forecasting accuracy

## Executive Summary
WindFM is a lightweight generative foundation model designed for zero-shot wind power forecasting. The model discretizes multivariate wind time series into hierarchical tokens and learns temporal dynamics via a decoder-only Transformer architecture. Pre-trained on 150 billion steps from over 126,000 sites, WindFM delivers superior performance on both deterministic and probabilistic forecasting tasks compared to specialized models and larger foundation models, while maintaining generalizability to unseen locations and cross-continental data.

## Method Summary
WindFM employs a decoder-only Transformer architecture that processes discretized multivariate wind time series through hierarchical token representations. The model was pre-trained on 150 billion steps from over 126,000 sites, learning temporal dynamics without fine-tuning requirements. The approach combines sequential modeling with hierarchical discretization to capture complex wind patterns, enabling zero-shot forecasting capabilities across different geographical locations and conditions.

## Key Results
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy for zero-shot wind power forecasting without fine-tuning
- Superior performance on both deterministic and probabilistic forecasting tasks
- Compact 8.1M parameter architecture enables efficient deployment while outperforming larger models

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The model's success stems from its hierarchical token discretization approach that effectively captures the multivariate nature of wind time series data. By pre-training on massive datasets spanning 126,000+ sites and 150 billion steps, WindFM develops robust temporal representations that generalize across geographical boundaries. The decoder-only Transformer architecture efficiently processes sequential patterns while maintaining computational efficiency through its compact parameter size.

## Foundational Learning
1. **Hierarchical Token Discretization**: Converting continuous wind time series into discrete tokens at multiple levels - needed for efficient processing and capturing multi-scale patterns; quick check: verify token vocabulary size matches data complexity
2. **Decoder-Only Transformer Architecture**: Specialized for sequence generation and forecasting - needed for temporal modeling without future context leakage; quick check: confirm attention mask prevents peeking at future tokens
3. **Large-Scale Pre-Training**: Training on 150B steps across 126K+ sites - needed for developing generalizable temporal representations; quick check: validate training data covers diverse meteorological conditions

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Input Data -> Hierarchical Tokenizer -> Decoder-Only Transformer -> Forecasting Output

**Critical Path**: The transformer's self-attention mechanism processes hierarchical tokens to generate forecasts, with token discretization being the primary bottleneck for data preprocessing speed.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The 8.1M parameter size prioritizes deployment efficiency over maximum theoretical accuracy, trading parameter count for faster inference and lower resource requirements while maintaining competitive performance.

**Failure Signatures**: Performance degradation occurs when input data significantly deviates from training distribution, particularly in regions with unique meteorological patterns not well-represented in the pre-training corpus.
