# Bi-cephalic self-attended model to classify Parkinson's disease patients with freezing of gait

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2507.20862
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.20862
- Authors: Shomoita Jahid Mitin; Rodrigue Rizk; Maximilian Scherer; Thomas Koeglsperger; Daniel Lench; KC Santosh; Arun Singh
- Reference count: 17
- Primary result: Multi-modal BiSAM model achieves 88% accuracy in classifying PD patients with freezing of gait using EEG and clinical variables

## Executive Summary
This study introduces BiSAM, a bi-cephalic self-attended deep learning model designed to classify Parkinson's disease patients with freezing of gait (PDFOG+) from those without FOG and healthy controls. The model integrates resting-state EEG signals with objective clinical variables including age, education, and disease duration from 124 participants. The research demonstrates that multi-modal approaches significantly outperform unimodal models, with the BiSAM-8 and BiSAM-4 variants achieving 88% accuracy and F1-scores of 88% and 83% respectively. The findings highlight the potential of combining minimal EEG channels with clinical data for robust, scalable FOG detection, offering practical applications for clinical monitoring and early diagnosis of PD-related gait dysfunction.

## Method Summary
The study employed a multi-modal deep learning framework using resting-state EEG combined with objective clinical variables. EEG signals and descriptive features (age, education, disease duration) were extracted from 124 participants across three diagnostic groups: PDFOG+, PDFOG-, and healthy controls. Three BiSAM model configurations were trained: signal-only, descriptive-only, and multi-modal. The models were evaluated using accuracy and F1-scores, with statistical analysis confirming the superiority of multi-modal approaches over unimodal ones.

## Key Results
- Multi-modal BiSAM models achieved 88% accuracy and F1-scores of 88% and 83% for BiSAM-8 and BiSAM-4 variants respectively
- EEG-only and descriptive-only models showed significantly lower performance compared to multi-modal approaches
- Statistical analysis confirmed multi-modal superiority with p < 0.001 across all metrics
- Integrating minimal EEG channels with clinical variables enables robust FOG detection

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The BiSAM model's effectiveness stems from its ability to capture both temporal patterns in EEG signals and the clinical context provided by objective variables. The self-attention mechanism allows the model to identify relevant features across different EEG channels while accounting for spatial relationships. By combining these neurophysiological signals with disease-specific clinical information, the model creates a more comprehensive representation of FOG manifestations than either modality alone could provide.

## Limitations
- Small sample size of 124 participants may limit generalizability
- EEG data collection and preprocessing details are not fully specified
- Limited clinical variables (age, education, disease duration) may not capture full FOG complexity
- Model performance on external validation datasets remains unknown
- Does not address medication status or motor fluctuations as potential confounders

## Confidence

**High confidence**: Multi-modal integration of EEG with clinical variables improves FOG classification performance compared to unimodal approaches

**Medium confidence**: Specific accuracy (88%) and F1-score (88-83%) values due to small sample size and lack of external validation

**Low confidence**: Clinical applicability without further testing across diverse populations and real-world conditions
