# Multi-Stage Patient Role-Playing Framework for Realistic Clinical Interactions

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2601.10951
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.10951
- Authors: Shijie Jiang; Zefan Zhang; Kehua Zhu; Tian Bai; Ruihong Zhao
- Reference count: 8
- Key outcome: Introduces Ch-PatientSim dataset and MSPRP framework to improve personalized clinical dialogue simulation

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses critical limitations in patient simulation datasets by creating Ch-PatientSim, the first Chinese patient simulation dataset constructed from real clinical interactions in gastroenterology. The dataset comprises 591 patients and 5,935 dialogue samples, overcoming the authenticity and diversity issues prevalent in existing LLM-generated datasets. To enhance model performance, the authors propose a Multi-Stage Patient Role-Playing (MSPRP) framework that decomposes clinical interactions into three stages, ensuring both personalization and realism in model responses.

The research identifies that current state-of-the-art LLMs produce overly formal responses lacking individual personality, even when trained on the new dataset. The MSPRP framework demonstrates significant improvements across multiple dimensions of patient simulation, including persona consistency, factual accuracy, naturalness, and contextual relevance. This work bridges the gap between artificial patient simulations and realistic clinical interactions, potentially advancing medical training and clinical dialogue systems.

## Method Summary
The authors construct Ch-PatientSim by collecting real clinical interactions in gastroenterology and structuring patient personas across five dimensions. They augment the dataset using few-shot generation techniques followed by manual verification to ensure quality and authenticity. To address the limitation of LLMs producing overly formal responses, they develop the MSPRP framework, which decomposes interactions into three distinct stages. This training-free approach leverages persona information systematically to generate more personalized and realistic patient responses without requiring additional model training.

## Key Results
- MSPRP significantly improves model performance across multiple dimensions of patient simulation
- Notable enhancements observed in persona consistency, factual accuracy, naturalness, and contextual relevance
- Ch-PatientSim dataset provides 591 patients and 5,935 dialogue samples for Chinese clinical dialogue research

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The MSPRP framework works by decomposing complex clinical interactions into manageable stages, allowing models to focus on specific aspects of persona expression and contextual understanding. By structuring the generation process across three stages, the framework ensures that personality traits are consistently maintained while preserving factual accuracy and natural conversational flow. This staged approach prevents the model from defaulting to overly formal responses and enables more nuanced, patient-specific interactions that better reflect real clinical scenarios.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Input → Stage 1 (Context Setup) → Stage 2 (Persona Integration) → Stage 3 (Response Generation) → Output

**Critical Path**: The framework processes clinical dialogue through three sequential stages, each building upon the previous one to ensure comprehensive persona expression and contextual relevance.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The training-free approach sacrifices potential performance gains from fine-tuning in exchange for flexibility and reduced computational costs. Manual verification ensures quality but limits scalability.

**Failure Signatures**: Models may fail to maintain consistent personas across stages, produce factually incorrect medical information, or generate responses that sound artificial rather than natural. Stage transitions may introduce inconsistencies if not properly managed.

## Limitations
- Evaluation relies on subjective human assessments, introducing potential rater bias
- Focus exclusively on gastroenterology limits generalizability to other medical domains
- Manual verification process is labor-intensive and may not scale to larger datasets
- Lacks explicit error analysis across different persona types and clinical scenarios

## Confidence

**High Confidence**: Dataset construction methodology and basic evaluation framework are well-documented and reproducible; five-dimensional persona structure is clearly defined.

**Medium Confidence**: Claims about MSPRP outperforming baseline models are supported by experimental results, though evaluation metrics could benefit from more diverse and objective measures beyond human ratings.

**Low Confidence**: The assertion that this is the "first Chinese patient simulation dataset" would require verification against all existing Chinese medical dialogue corpora.
