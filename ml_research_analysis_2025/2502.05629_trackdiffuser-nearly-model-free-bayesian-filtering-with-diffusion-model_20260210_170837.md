# TrackDiffuser: Nearly Model-Free Bayesian Filtering with Diffusion Model

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2502.05629
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.05629
- Authors: Yangguang He; Wenhao Li; Minzhe Li; Juan Zhang; Xiangfeng Wang; Bo Jin
- Reference count: 6
- Primary result: MSE improvements up to 20% over classical methods in non-linear measurement scenarios

## Executive Summary
TrackDiffuser presents a novel approach to state estimation by reformulating Bayesian filtering as a conditional diffusion model. The method addresses two key limitations of classical approaches: inaccurate state space models and the need for explicit noise priors. By implicitly learning system dynamics from data while maintaining model interpretability, TrackDiffuser establishes a direct relationship between measurements and states without requiring traditional approximations.

The core innovation lies in modeling the predict step using prior state estimates during denoising and implementing the update step through recursive measurement-conditional guidance. This approach naturally handles non-linear dynamics and non-Gaussian uncertainties while preserving the interpretability advantages of traditional filtering methods.

## Method Summary
TrackDiffuser reformulates Bayesian filtering by casting it as a conditional diffusion process. The predict step is modeled through the diffusion process using prior state estimates, while the update step is implemented via recursive measurement-conditional guidance. This approach learns system dynamics implicitly from data rather than requiring explicit state space models, eliminating the need for noise prior specifications. The diffusion model directly learns the relationship between measurements and states, enabling handling of non-linear dynamics and non-Gaussian uncertainties while maintaining the interpretability advantages of traditional filtering methods.

## Key Results
- Achieves MSE improvements of up to 20% in non-linear measurement settings compared to classical methods
- Shows superior robustness under system dynamics mismatch and training-testing mismatch conditions
- Real-world testing on Michigan NCLT dataset achieves MSE of 13.94 dB vs 16.01 dB for KalmanNet and 19.02 dB for EKF

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
TrackDiffuser works by transforming the Bayesian filtering problem into a conditional diffusion process. The key insight is that diffusion models can learn complex state-transition relationships implicitly from data, eliminating the need for explicit mathematical models. By using the diffusion process to model prediction and recursive measurement guidance for updates, the method can handle non-linear dynamics and non-Gaussian uncertainties naturally. The implicit learning of system dynamics from data allows the model to adapt to model inaccuracies while maintaining interpretability through the diffusion process structure.

## Architecture Onboarding
**Component map:** Measurement input -> Diffusion model encoder -> State space denoiser -> Output state estimate

**Critical path:** The forward diffusion process models prediction using prior states, while the reverse diffusion process implements updates conditioned on measurements through recursive guidance.

**Design tradeoffs:** TrackDiffuser trades explicit model specification for implicit data-driven learning, gaining flexibility at the cost of increased computational complexity and potential interpretability challenges in extreme scenarios.

**Failure signatures:** Poor performance may manifest when training data is insufficient or unrepresentative, when system dynamics change dramatically between training and deployment, or when the state space becomes too high-dimensional for practical diffusion model application.

**First experiments:** 1) Validate performance on a simple linear system with known ground truth. 2) Test robustness to increasing levels of model mismatch. 3) Evaluate scalability by testing on progressively larger state spaces.

## Limitations
- Scalability challenges to high-dimensional state spaces due to computational complexity of diffusion models
- Reduced interpretability compared to traditional model-based approaches when system dynamics are learned implicitly
- Performance dependence on training data quality and quantity, particularly problematic in data-scarce environments

## Confidence
**High confidence:** The core methodology of reformulating Bayesian filtering using conditional diffusion models is technically sound and well-supported by experimental results. Improvements over classical methods in non-linear measurement scenarios and under system dynamics mismatch are well-demonstrated.

**Medium confidence:** The claim of maintaining model interpretability while implicitly learning dynamics requires further validation across diverse applications. Real-world performance on Michigan NCLT dataset shows promise, but generalizability to other scenarios needs more extensive testing.

**Low confidence:** The assertion that TrackDiffuser naturally handles all forms of non-Gaussian uncertainties is based on limited experimental evidence. Further testing with various non-Gaussian noise distributions is needed to substantiate this claim fully.
