# Semantic Bridges Between First Order c-Representations and Cost-Based Semantics: An Initial Perspective

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2510.00817
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.00817
- Authors: Nicholas Leisegang; Giovanni Casini; Thomas Meyer
- Reference count: 32
- Primary result: Establishes bidirectional translations between cost-based semantics and c-representations for ALCO description logics under specific compatibility conditions

## Executive Summary
This paper bridges two formalisms for handling inconsistency in description logics: cost-based semantics (Bienvenu et al.) and c-representations (Kern-Isberner). Both assign numerical penalties to interpretations based on violated rules, but from different theoretical traditions. The authors show that under certain conditions, a weighted knowledge base and a c-representation can generate the same ordering of interpretations (equivalent relative cost). They provide translations between the formalisms: from c-representations to weighted KBs (always possible), and from weighted KBs to c-representations (when certain compatibility conditions hold). They also demonstrate correspondences between entailment relations in both frameworks.

## Method Summary
The paper establishes formal translations between cost-based semantics and c-representations for ALCO description logics. It defines weighted knowledge bases with TBox (GCIs), ABox (assertions), and DBox (defeasible concept inclusions C ⊏∼ D) and cost functions ω mapping axioms to penalties. The method includes: (1) implementing ALCO parsers and Herbrand interpretation generators, (2) computing violation counts for axioms and ABox assertions, (3) defining cost functions for WKBs and ranking functions for c-representations, and (4) providing bidirectional translation functions with compatibility conditions. The approach uses finite Herbrand interpretations over universe U = N_I and establishes equivalence through formal proofs.

## Key Results
- Translations from c-representations to weighted KBs are always possible
- Translations from weighted KBs to c-representations require c-compatibility and strong c-compatibility conditions
- Equivalence of relative cost ordering is proven under specified conditions
- Correspondence between entailment relations (⊨opt_c, ⊨opt_p) with κ-entailment and skeptical/credulous c-inference is established

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The paper works by exploiting the common structure of both formalisms: they both assign numerical penalties to interpretations based on violated axioms. The key insight is that both frameworks can be reduced to ranking interpretations by violation counts, which enables translation between them. The mechanism relies on careful mapping of violation functions and normalization constants, with compatibility conditions ensuring the preservation of entailment relations.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map:**
ALCO KB -> Parser -> Violation Counter -> Cost Function -> Translation -> c-representation or WKB

**Critical Path:**
1. Parse ALCO knowledge base with weights
2. Generate Herbrand interpretations over finite universe
3. Compute violation counts for all axioms and assertions
4. Apply translation functions with compatibility checking
5. Verify equivalence of relative cost ordering

**Design Tradeoffs:**
- Finite vs infinite interpretations: Paper restricts to Herbrand (finite) for computability
- Strict vs weak ABox translation: Different handling based on weight values
- Compatibility checking: Computationally expensive but necessary for accurate translation

**Failure Signatures:**
- Incorrect domain size in interpretations
- Miscomputation of violation counts (especially for defeasible inclusions)
- Ignoring compatibility conditions when translating WKBs to c-representations
- Incorrect handling of ABox axioms with different weight values

**First Experiments:**
1. Verify violation counting on simple penguin-triangle variant with Logician/Scientist/Experiments
2. Test WKB cost computation on Example 1 knowledge base
3. Implement c-compatibility checking algorithm on synthetic KBs of varying sizes
