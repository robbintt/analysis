# Pose-Robust Calibration Strategy for Point-of-Gaze Estimation on Mobile Phones

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2508.10268
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.10268
- Reference count: 40
- Key outcome: Dynamic phone movement during calibration significantly improves point-of-gaze estimation robustness compared to static calibration

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the critical challenge of improving person-specific point-of-gaze (PoG) estimation on mobile phones when users' head poses vary during use. The authors identify that traditional static calibration methods struggle with head pose variation, leading to poor estimation accuracy. To tackle this, they construct MobilePoG, a dataset capturing facial images of individuals focusing on designated points under both fixed and continuously changing head poses. Through systematic analysis, they demonstrate that introducing diverse head poses during calibration substantially enhances the estimator's robustness to pose variation, while simply increasing calibration point quantity offers limited benefit. Based on these findings, they propose a dynamic calibration strategy where users move their phones while fixating on calibration points, naturally incorporating head pose variation into the calibration process.

## Method Summary
The authors develop a comprehensive approach to address head pose variation challenges in mobile gaze estimation. They first construct the MobilePoG dataset containing facial images captured during both fixed and continuously changing head pose conditions while users focus on designated points. Through controlled experiments, they systematically analyze how different calibration strategies affect estimation accuracy under various head pose scenarios. Their analysis reveals that head pose diversity during calibration is more critical than the number of calibration points for achieving robust performance. Based on these insights, they implement a dynamic calibration strategy where users naturally introduce head pose variation by moving their phones while maintaining gaze on calibration targets. This approach creates a more representative calibration dataset that better captures the real-world conditions the estimator will encounter during actual use.

## Key Results
- Dynamic calibration strategy significantly outperforms static calibration in handling head pose variations
- Head pose diversity during calibration proves more critical than increasing the number of calibration points
- The proposed method reduces sensitivity to head pose changes compared to conventional static calibration approaches

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The proposed dynamic calibration strategy works by directly addressing the fundamental limitation of static calibration methods: they fail to capture the relationship between head pose variation and gaze estimation during the calibration phase. When users move their phones while fixating on calibration points, the resulting dataset naturally includes the pose-gaze relationship variations that occur during real-world usage. This enriched calibration data enables the PoG estimator to learn more robust mappings between facial features and gaze directions across different head poses, rather than learning a single pose-specific mapping. The mechanism effectively bridges the domain gap between controlled calibration conditions and the diverse head pose scenarios encountered during actual mobile phone usage.

- Dynamic vs static calibration: Understanding the tradeoffs between these approaches helps in designing more effective calibration strategies

## Limitations
- Dataset diversity and sample size remain unspecified, potentially limiting generalizability
- The study does not address interaction effects between calibration point quantity and head pose variation
- Controlled experimental conditions may not fully represent real-world mobile phone usage complexity
