# A New Approach for Multicriteria Assessment in the Ranking of Alternatives Using Cardinal and Ordinal Data

- **arXiv ID**: 2507.08875

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of multi-criteria assessment when decision-making units (DMUs) are evaluated using both cardinal (quantitative) and ordinal (qualitative Likert scale) data. Existing methods like DEA, SFA, and MCDM face limitations due to assumptions, subjective judgments, and difficulties handling ordinal data. The authors propose a novel MCA approach that integrates two Virtual Gap Analysis (VGA) modelsâ€”one for identifying top-tier DMUs and another for selecting the best DMU among them. The method uses linear programming-based models without additional assumptions and verifies duality properties through strong complementary slackness conditions. It effectively handles heterogeneity among DMUs and incorporates both quantitative and qualitative criteria. The approach was validated using two numerical examples, demonstrating accurate and transparent evaluations.

## Method Summary
The paper proposes a two-stage Virtual Gap Analysis (VGA) approach for multi-criteria assessment. Stage I (obPT) identifies top-tier DMUs by minimizing total virtual gap using a linear programming model that handles both cardinal and ordinal data through adjustment ratios and intensity variables. Stage II (osPT) selects the best DMU from the top-tier group by minimizing super-virtual gap. The method incorporates strong complementary slackness conditions to verify duality properties and ensure reliable solutions. A unified goal price ensures comparability across metrics, and the approach handles heterogeneity among DMUs while maintaining transparency through 2D visualization.

## Key Results
- Novel two-stage VGA approach successfully ranks DMUs using mixed cardinal and ordinal data
- Method achieves accurate and transparent evaluations without additional assumptions
- Verified duality properties through strong complementary slackness conditions
- Demonstrated reliability through 2D visualization and two numerical examples
- Unified goal price ensures metric comparability across different evaluation criteria

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method works by decomposing the complex MCA problem into two manageable LP stages. Stage I identifies units with zero virtual gap (top-tier), while Stage II ranks these units through super-virtual gap minimization. The strong complementary slackness verification ensures solution reliability, and the unified goal price normalizes disparate metrics for meaningful comparison.

## Foundational Learning
- **Virtual Gap Analysis**: Measures the distance between observed performance and optimal reference set performance - needed to quantify inefficiency in MCA
- **Strong Complementary Slackness**: Verifies primal-dual optimality conditions - needed to ensure LP solutions are reliable and meaningful
- **Likert Scale Integration**: Maps qualitative ordinal data to LP constraints - needed to handle mixed cardinal-ordinal assessment systematically
- **Two-Stage Decomposition**: Separates identification from ranking tasks - needed to reduce computational complexity and improve interpretability
- **Goal Price Unification**: Normalizes different metrics to comparable scales - needed to enable fair cross-metric evaluation
