# Beyond Fixed Anchors: Precisely Erasing Concepts with Sibling Exclusive Counterparts

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2510.16342
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.16342
- **Authors**: Tong Zhang; Ru Zhang; Jianyi Liu; Zhen Yang; Gongshen Liu
- **Reference count**: 40
- **Primary result**: SELECT framework achieves superior concept erasure with 4-second average anchor mining time across objects, celebrities, artistic styles, and NSFW content

## Executive Summary
The paper addresses the fundamental challenge of concept erasure in text-to-image diffusion models, where existing fixed-anchor approaches suffer from concept re-emergence and collateral erosion of related content. SELECT introduces a dynamic anchor selection framework that automatically discovers optimal Sibling Exclusive Concepts (SECs) for precise erasure. By employing a two-stage evaluation mechanism based on contextual activation and semantic coherence, SELECT identifies the most effective anchors while protecting related concepts through an Anchor-Guided Retain mechanism. Extensive experiments demonstrate significant improvements over existing baselines across multiple erasure tasks.

## Method Summary
SELECT tackles concept erasure by moving beyond fixed anchor concepts to dynamically discover optimal Sibling Exclusive Concepts (SECs) that enable precise removal without collateral damage. The framework operates through two key stages: first, it employs contextual activation analysis to identify candidate anchors that maximally activate the target concept while minimizing activation of related concepts; second, it uses semantic coherence evaluation to ensure selected anchors maintain contextual relevance and don't introduce artifacts. The Anchor-Guided Retain mechanism protects semantically related concepts during erasure, addressing the erosion problem common in traditional approaches. The entire process achieves efficient anchor mining with an average time of 4 seconds per concept, making it practical for real-world deployment.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
SELECT works by addressing the fundamental limitation of fixed anchor approaches where pre-selected concepts cannot adapt to the complex semantic relationships in diffusion models. The dynamic SEC discovery process identifies anchors that are semantically related enough to effectively trigger the target concept during training, but exclusive enough to avoid collateral damage to similar concepts. The two-stage evaluation ensures both effectiveness (high contextual activation) and precision (high semantic coherence), creating a balanced approach that maintains content quality while achieving complete erasure. The Anchor-Guided Retain mechanism further refines this by explicitly protecting related concepts during the erasure process, preventing the model from over-generalizing the removal to semantically similar content.

## Limitations

- Claims of "precise" erasure lack quantitative validation against ground truth annotations of complete removal versus content preservation
- Anchor mining relies heavily on image-text pair alignment quality, which may vary significantly across datasets and concepts
- Long-term stability of erased concepts and potential for re-emergence over extended use remains untested

## Confidence

**High Confidence**: Core observation about fixed anchor limitations and re-emergence/erosion problems is well-established and empirically validated; two-stage evaluation mechanism is logically sound.

**Medium Confidence**: Claims about "superior erasure efficiency" are supported but could benefit from more granular metrics beyond mining time; precision claims rely on metrics that may not capture all perceptual aspects.

**Low Confidence**: Claims about SEC discovery being universally optimal across all concept types are not fully substantiated; long-term stability and generalization to underrepresented concepts remain unverified.
