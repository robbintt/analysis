# Efficient and Programmable Exploration of Synthesizable Chemical Space

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2512.00384
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.00384
- Authors: Shitong Luo; Connor W. Coley
- Reference count: 13
- PrexSyn achieves 94% reconstruction rate on Enamine REAL space, 60× speedup over baselines

## Executive Summary
PrexSyn introduces a generative model for synthesizable molecular design that addresses the critical challenge of sampling molecules that are both synthetically accessible and possess desired properties. The model uses a decoder-only transformer architecture trained on a billion-scale dataset of synthesizable pathways paired with molecular properties, enabled by a high-throughput C++ data generation engine. By leveraging postfix notation of synthesis, PrexSyn generates molecules conditioned on property prompts while ensuring synthesizability according to predefined reaction rules. This approach enables efficient exploration of synthesizable chemical space with unprecedented reconstruction rates and inference speed.

## Method Summary
PrexSyn is a decoder-only transformer architecture that generates molecules conditioned on property prompts using postfix notation of synthesis. The model is trained on a billion-scale dataset created by a high-throughput C++ data generation engine that pairs synthesizable pathways with molecular properties. The use of postfix notation ensures that generated molecules are inherently synthesizable according to predefined reaction rules. PrexSyn supports composite property queries using logical operators (AND, NOT, OR), enabling users to program complex generation objectives. The model achieves state-of-the-art performance with a 94% reconstruction rate on the Enamine REAL space and demonstrates over 60 times speedup in inference time compared to existing baselines.

## Key Results
- Achieves 94% reconstruction rate on Enamine REAL space, significantly outperforming previous methods
- Demonstrates over 60× speedup in inference time compared to highest-accuracy baseline
- Supports composite property queries with logical operators for complex molecular generation objectives
- Achieves higher sampling efficiency than synthesis-agnostic baselines for black-box oracle optimization

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The effectiveness of PrexSyn stems from its integration of synthesizability constraints directly into the generative process through postfix notation. By training on a billion-scale dataset of synthesizable pathways, the model learns to generate molecules that are inherently synthesizable according to predefined reaction rules. The decoder-only transformer architecture allows for efficient generation while maintaining high fidelity to the training distribution. The support for composite property queries enables precise control over the generated molecular properties, allowing users to "program" complex molecular design objectives that combine multiple constraints.

## Architecture Onboarding

Component Map:
PrexSyn consists of a decoder-only transformer that takes property prompts as input and generates molecules in postfix notation. The architecture includes an embedding layer for property tokens, multiple transformer layers for sequence processing, and a final output layer that produces the molecular structure. A billion-scale dataset of synthesizable pathways serves as training data, generated by a high-throughput C++ engine.

Critical Path:
Property prompt -> Token embedding -> Transformer layers -> Output layer -> Postfix notation molecule

Design Tradeoffs:
The choice of postfix notation ensures synthesizability but may limit the model's ability to generate certain molecular structures that require non-standard synthetic routes. The billion-scale training dataset provides comprehensive coverage but requires significant computational resources. The support for logical operators in property queries enables complex objectives but may increase generation time for compound constraints.

Failure Signatures:
Generation failures may occur when property queries are too restrictive or when the desired molecular properties fall outside the distribution of the training data. The model may also struggle with extreme property combinations that are rare in the synthesizable chemical space. Performance degradation can be observed when attempting to generate molecules with properties that require synthetic routes not covered by the predefined reaction rules.

First Experiments:
1. Generate molecules for a single property constraint (e.g., specific molecular weight range) and verify synthesizability
2. Test composite property queries using logical operators (AND, NOT, OR) with moderate constraints
3. Evaluate reconstruction rate on a subset of the Enamine REAL space to validate performance claims

## Limitations

- Performance on clinically relevant molecular targets not validated beyond synthetic benchmarks
