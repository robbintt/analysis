# StrokeNUWA: Tokenizing Strokes for Vector Graphic Synthesis

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID:** 2401.17093
- **Source URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.17093
- **Reference count:** 9
- **Primary result:** Introduces stroke tokens for vector graphic synthesis, achieving 94× speedup and 6.9× compression over prior methods

## Executive Summary
StrokeNUWA introduces a novel approach to vector graphic synthesis by tokenizing graphics into stroke tokens rather than traditional grid-based tokens. This innovation addresses the semantic disruption inherent in raster image conversion, providing a more natural and compressed representation compatible with large language models. The method significantly outperforms existing LLM-based and optimization-based approaches across multiple metrics, offering both computational efficiency and semantic fidelity in vector graphic generation.

## Method Summary
StrokeNUWA employs a VQ-Stroke module to tokenize vector graphics into stroke tokens, which inherently preserve visual semantics and are highly compressed. This approach is designed to be naturally compatible with large language models, allowing for efficient synthesis of vector graphics. The method is evaluated against traditional LLM-based and optimization-based techniques, demonstrating substantial improvements in speed and compression while maintaining high-quality output.

## Key Results
- Achieves up to 94× speedup in inference over prior methods
- Provides a compression ratio of 6.9× compared to traditional approaches
- Significantly outperforms traditional LLM-based and optimization-based methods across various metrics

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
StrokeNUWA leverages stroke tokens to maintain the inherent visual semantics of vector graphics, avoiding the disruption caused by grid-based tokenization of raster images. By using a VQ-Stroke module, the method ensures that the semantic information is preserved and naturally compatible with LLMs, leading to more efficient and accurate synthesis of vector graphics.
