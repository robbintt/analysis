# Weakly-supervised causal discovery based on fuzzy knowledge and complex data complementarity

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2405.08699
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.08699
- Reference count: 40
- Introduces KEEL, a weakly-supervised causal discovery method using fuzzy knowledge and data complementarity

## Executive Summary
KEEL is a novel weakly-supervised causal discovery method that addresses limitations in existing approaches by leveraging fuzzy causal knowledge and data complementarity. The method introduces a fuzzy causal knowledge schema that encapsulates diverse types of fuzzy knowledge and forms weakened constraints, allowing various types of limited and error-prone fuzzy knowledge to guide causal discovery. This approach enhances generalization and robustness, particularly in high-dimensional and small-sample scenarios. KEEL integrates the extended linear causal model (ELCM) to handle multi-distribution and incomplete data, demonstrating superior performance over state-of-the-art methods in accuracy, robustness, and computational efficiency.

## Method Summary
KEEL combines fuzzy knowledge with data-driven approaches through a unique framework that incorporates a fuzzy causal knowledge schema. This schema allows for the integration of various types of imprecise or incomplete causal knowledge into the discovery process. The method uses weakened constraints derived from fuzzy knowledge to guide causal discovery, making it more robust to noise and inconsistencies in the prior knowledge. KEEL also integrates the extended linear causal model (ELCM) to handle complex data scenarios, including multi-distribution data and missing values. The approach is designed to excel in high-dimensional and small-sample settings, where traditional methods often struggle.

## Key Results
- Superior performance in high-dimensional and small-sample scenarios compared to state-of-the-art methods
- Effective handling of incomplete data and multi-distribution datasets through integration of ELCM
- Outperforms benchmark methods in real protein signal transduction processes with limited data
- Demonstrates improvements in accuracy, robustness, and computational efficiency

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
KEEL's effectiveness stems from its ability to incorporate fuzzy causal knowledge into the discovery process, which allows it to leverage limited and error-prone prior knowledge. The fuzzy causal knowledge schema creates weakened constraints that guide the search for causal relationships without being overly rigid, making the method more robust to inconsistencies in the prior knowledge. The integration of ELCM enables KEEL to handle complex data scenarios, including incomplete and multi-distribution datasets, which are common in real-world applications. This combination of fuzzy knowledge and data complementarity allows KEEL to excel in scenarios where traditional methods struggle, particularly in high-dimensional and small-sample settings.

Critical Path: The integration of fuzzy knowledge through the schema to create weakened constraints, which then guide the causal discovery algorithm, is the critical path. This is where KEEL's unique approach differentiates it from other methods.
Design Tradeoffs: KEEL trades off some precision in causal structure for robustness and applicability in real-world scenarios with limited or noisy prior knowledge. This tradeoff allows for better performance in high-dimensional and small-sample settings but may result in slightly less accurate causal structures in ideal conditions.
Failure Signatures: KEEL may struggle when the fuzzy knowledge is entirely incorrect or when the data is extremely noisy and inconsistent with any prior knowledge. In such cases, the method might produce unreliable causal structures.
