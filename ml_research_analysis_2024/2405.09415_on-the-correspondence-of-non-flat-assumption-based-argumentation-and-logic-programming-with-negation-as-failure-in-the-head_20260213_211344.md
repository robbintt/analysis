# On the Correspondence of Non-flat Assumption-based Argumentation and Logic Programming with Negation as Failure in the Head

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2405.09415
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.09415
- Reference count: 35
- Key outcome: This paper establishes a correspondence between non-flat assumption-based argumentation (ABA) and logic programs (LPs) with negation as failure in the head under stable and set-stable semantics. The authors show that each LP can be translated into a non-flat ABA framework, and identify a fragment of ABA (LP-ABA) that corresponds 1-1 with LPs. They extend this result to set-stable semantics by defining set-stable model semantics for LPs and proving the correspondence. The translation is semantics-preserving and can be computed efficiently. This work bridges non-flat ABA and a more general LP fragment beyond normal programs, providing theoretical insights and practical benefits for both fields.

## Executive Summary
This paper establishes a correspondence between non-flat assumption-based argumentation (ABA) and logic programs (LPs) with negation as failure in the head under stable and set-stable semantics. The authors show that each LP can be translated into a non-flat ABA framework, and identify a fragment of ABA (LP-ABA) that corresponds 1-1 with LPs. They extend this result to set-stable semantics by defining set-stable model semantics for LPs and proving the correspondence. The translation is semantics-preserving and can be computed efficiently. This work bridges non-flat ABA and a more general LP fragment beyond normal programs, providing theoretical insights and practical benefits for both fields.

## Method Summary
The authors define a translation from LPs to ABAFs and identify a fragment of ABA (LP-ABA) that corresponds 1-1 with LPs. They extend this result to set-stable semantics by defining set-stable model semantics for LPs and proving the correspondence. The translation is semantics-preserving and can be computed efficiently. The key insight is that each LP can be translated into a non-flat ABA framework, and vice versa, preserving the semantics under stable and set-stable semantics.

## Key Results
- Each LP can be translated into a non-flat ABA framework
- A fragment of ABA (LP-ABA) corresponds 1-1 with LPs
- The correspondence extends to set-stable semantics
- The translation is semantics-preserving and can be computed efficiently

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The paper leverages the structural similarities between non-flat ABA and LPs with negation as failure in the head. By defining appropriate translations and identifying the LP-ABA fragment, the authors establish a 1-1 correspondence between the two formalisms under stable and set-stable semantics.
