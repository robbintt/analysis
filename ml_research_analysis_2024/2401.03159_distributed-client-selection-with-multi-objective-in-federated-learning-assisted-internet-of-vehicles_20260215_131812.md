# Distributed client selection with multi-objective in federated learning assisted Internet of Vehicles

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID:** 2401.03159
- **Source URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.03159
- **Reference count:** 40
- **Primary result:** Distributed client selection framework reduces communication overhead by >50% while maintaining accuracy comparable to centralized methods in federated IoV

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the communication overhead challenge in federated learning for Internet of Vehicles by proposing a distributed client selection framework. The approach eliminates the need for the server to collect and maintain active states of all participating vehicles by shifting the evaluation process to individual vehicles. Each vehicle can assess itself and its neighbors using a fuzzy logic-based evaluator that considers multiple factors including sample quantity, throughput availability, computational capability, and dataset quality. The distributed approach aims to achieve accuracy comparable to centralized selection methods while significantly reducing communication overhead, particularly in large-scale vehicular networks.

## Method Summary
The proposed framework introduces a distributed client selection mechanism where evaluation responsibility shifts from a centralized server to individual vehicles. Each vehicle employs a fuzzy logic-based evaluator to assess itself and neighboring vehicles based on four key metrics: sample quantity, throughput availability, computational capability, and local dataset quality. This self-assessment and neighbor evaluation process allows vehicles to make informed participation decisions without requiring the server to gather and process information from all participants. The distributed approach aims to reduce the communication overhead associated with centralized client selection methods while maintaining comparable learning accuracy.

## Key Results
- Distributed client selection achieves accuracy comparable to centralized methods
- Communication overhead reduced by more than 50% compared to centralized client selection when participant numbers are large
- The framework demonstrates scalability benefits as the number of participants increases
- Fuzzy logic-based evaluation effectively captures multi-objective criteria for client selection

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The distributed approach works by decentralizing the client evaluation process, eliminating the need for vehicles to continuously report their status to a central server. By moving the fuzzy logic-based assessment to each individual vehicle, the framework reduces the communication burden while maintaining effective client selection through localized decision-making. The multi-objective evaluation considers critical factors that directly impact federated learning performance, ensuring that selected clients contribute meaningfully to the global model updates.

## Foundational Learning

### Fuzzy Logic-based Evaluation
**Why needed:** To handle imprecise and uncertain information about vehicle capabilities and network conditions
**Quick check:** Evaluate performance with different membership functions and rule sets

### Multi-objective Client Assessment
**Why needed:** To balance multiple competing factors (computational resources, data quality, connectivity) for optimal client selection
**Quick check:** Test sensitivity to weighting of different evaluation criteria

### Distributed Decision-making
**Why needed:** To reduce communication overhead and improve scalability in large vehicular networks
**Quick check:** Measure communication savings at different network scales

## Architecture Onboarding

### Component Map
Vehicle -> Fuzzy Logic Evaluator -> Self-Assessment & Neighbor Evaluation -> Participation Decision

### Critical Path
Vehicle collects local metrics → Fuzzy logic evaluation of self and neighbors → Decision to participate or not → Transmission of local model updates (if selected)

### Design Tradeoffs
- **Communication vs. Accuracy:** Distributed evaluation reduces communication but may introduce variance in client selection quality
- **Computational Overhead:** Fuzzy logic evaluation adds processing requirements on resource-constrained vehicles
- **Security vs. Decentralization:** Distributed decision-making reduces central attack surface but may enable localized manipulation

### Failure Signatures
- Inconsistent client selection leading to poor model convergence
- High variance in selected client quality across iterations
- Increased dropout rates from selected clients during training

### First 3 Experiments
1. Compare convergence speed and final accuracy between distributed and centralized selection methods
2. Measure communication overhead reduction at different scales (10, 50, 100 vehicles)
3. Evaluate the impact of different fuzzy logic rule sets on selection quality

None

## Limitations
- Performance in highly dynamic vehicular environments with rapid topology changes is unclear
- Computational overhead of fuzzy logic evaluator on resource-constrained vehicles not thoroughly quantified
- Security implications of distributed selection and potential manipulation of self-evaluation metrics require further investigation

## Confidence

### High Confidence
- Communication overhead reduction (>50% improvement) is well-supported by simulation results

### Medium Confidence
- Accuracy parity with centralized methods, as real-world vehicular conditions may introduce additional complexity
- Scalability benefits of the distributed approach, pending validation in larger, more heterogeneous vehicular networks

## Next Checks
1. Conduct field tests in realistic vehicular environments with varying mobility patterns to assess performance under dynamic topology changes
2. Perform resource consumption analysis of the fuzzy logic evaluator on actual vehicular hardware with different computational capabilities
3. Implement and evaluate security mechanisms to prevent manipulation of self-reported metrics in the distributed selection process
