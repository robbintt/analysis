# A Conceptual Framework for Conversational Search and Recommendation: Conceptualizing Agent-Human Interactions During the Conversational Search Process

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2404.08630
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.08630
- Reference count: 18
- This paper proposes a conceptual framework for conversational search that categorizes user and agent actions and intents, and identifies key decision points during the search process.

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces a conceptual framework for conversational search and recommendation systems, focusing on the interactions between agents and humans during the search process. The framework categorizes user actions such as revealing information needs, inquiring about options, navigating results, and interrupting the agent, alongside corresponding agent actions like eliciting criteria, revealing options, summarizing results, and making suggestions. It highlights the trade-offs agents face, such as balancing efficiency with exploration and deciding when to elicit versus reveal information. While the paper does not provide empirical results or metrics, it serves as a foundation for formalizing research, development, and evaluation of conversational search agents.

## Method Summary
The paper presents a conceptual framework through a qualitative analysis of conversational search and recommendation processes. It categorizes user and agent actions, identifies key decision points, and explores the trade-offs agents face during interactions. The framework is designed to guide the development and evaluation of conversational search agents, though it lacks empirical validation or quantitative metrics.

## Key Results
- Proposes a conceptual framework categorizing user actions (e.g., revealing information needs, inquiring about options) and agent actions (e.g., eliciting criteria, revealing options).
- Identifies key decision points during the search process, such as balancing efficiency with exploration.
- Highlights trade-offs agents face, including when to elicit versus reveal information.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework works by systematically categorizing the interactions between users and agents during conversational search. It provides a structured approach to understanding the dynamics of information exchange, decision-making, and trade-offs in search processes. By identifying key actions and decision points, it offers a foundation for designing and evaluating conversational search agents.

## Limitations
- The framework lacks empirical validation, as it is conceptual in nature without providing data or metrics to support the identified actions and trade-offs.
- The applicability of the framework across different domains and user populations remains untested.
- The trade-offs identified, such as balancing efficiency with exploration, lack quantitative measures to assess their impact on search outcomes.

- **Low**: The trade-offs agents face, such as balancing efficiency with exploration, are speculative without quantitative data to validate their significance or practical implications.
