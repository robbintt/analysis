# Bilingual BSARD: Extending Statutory Article Retrieval to Dutch

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2412.07462
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.07462
- **Reference count**: 28
- **Primary result**: Extends BSARD dataset to Dutch, enabling evaluation of statutory article retrieval models in both French and Dutch

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces bBSARD, a bilingual dataset that extends the Belgian Statutory Article Retrieval Dataset (BSARD) to include Dutch language content. The dataset provides parallel Belgian statutory articles in both French and Dutch, along with translated legal questions from the original BSARD. The authors benchmark various retrieval models across lexical, zero-shot dense, and fine-tuned settings, finding that while BM25 remains competitive, fine-tuning small language-specific models like RobBERT and FlauBERT can match or surpass proprietary models. The work addresses the critical need for Dutch legal retrieval evaluation data, as most existing benchmarks focus on English or other major languages.

## Method Summary
The authors constructed bBSARD by translating the original French legal questions from BSARD into Dutch and collecting parallel statutory articles in both languages. They conducted comprehensive experiments comparing BM25, zero-shot dense retrieval models of varying sizes, and fine-tuned models across both language pairs. The evaluation used standard retrieval metrics including top-1 and top-5 accuracy. They tested both open and proprietary models in zero-shot settings and fine-tuned language-specific models (RobBERT for Dutch, FlauBERT for French) on the bBSARD training set. The study also analyzed performance differences between French and Dutch models, attributing variations to data availability differences.

## Key Results
- BM25 remains competitive with more complex retrieval approaches across both languages
- Zero-shot dense models show improved performance with increased model size
- French models generally outperform Dutch models, likely due to data availability differences
- Fine-tuning small language-specific models can match or surpass proprietary models in fine-tuned settings

## Limitations
- Dataset construction relied on volunteer translators rather than professional translators, potentially introducing subtle errors
- Only parliamentary questions were used as query sources, limiting generalizability to other legal question types
- Evaluation focuses primarily on top-1 and top-5 accuracy metrics, which may not fully capture nuanced differences in retrieval quality
- The study does not address cross-lingual retrieval scenarios where queries and articles are in different languages
- Performance comparisons are limited to the specific Belgian statutory context and may not generalize to other jurisdictions or legal systems
