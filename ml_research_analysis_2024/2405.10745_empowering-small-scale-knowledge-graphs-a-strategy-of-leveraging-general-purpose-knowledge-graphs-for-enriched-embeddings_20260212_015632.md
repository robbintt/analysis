# Empowering Small-Scale Knowledge Graphs: A Strategy of Leveraging General-Purpose Knowledge Graphs for Enriched Embeddings

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2405.10745
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.10745

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces a framework that enhances small, domain-specific knowledge graphs (KGs) by leveraging embeddings from well-established general-purpose KGs. The method aligns entities between the two KGs using similarity scores from textual embeddings and creates artificial links between them. The combined KG is then used to train a translation-based model for KG completion. Experiments in both synthetic and real-world scenarios show that this approach significantly improves performance, with up to a 44% increase in the Hits@10 metric. The framework demonstrates the potential for improving small-scale KGs by enriching them with general-purpose knowledge, offering a promising direction for enhancing their utility in real-world applications.

## Method Summary
The proposed framework addresses the challenge of limited data in small-scale knowledge graphs by leveraging information from general-purpose KGs. The approach consists of three main steps: entity alignment using similarity scores from textual embeddings, creation of artificial links between aligned entities, and training a translation-based model on the enriched knowledge graph. By combining the structural information from the domain-specific KG with the rich semantic information from the general-purpose KG, the method aims to improve the quality of embeddings and subsequently enhance KG completion performance. The framework was evaluated through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating significant improvements in KG completion metrics.

## Key Results
- The framework achieved up to a 44% increase in the Hits@10 metric for KG completion tasks
- Performance improvements were observed in both synthetic and real-world scenarios
- The method successfully enriched small-scale knowledge graphs by leveraging general-purpose KG embeddings

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework works by bridging the information gap between small, domain-specific knowledge graphs and rich, general-purpose knowledge graphs. By aligning entities and creating artificial links, it effectively transfers semantic knowledge from the general-purpose KG to the domain-specific one. This enrichment process provides additional context and relationships that help improve the quality of embeddings in the smaller KG. The translation-based model then benefits from this enhanced representation, leading to better performance in KG completion tasks.

## Foundational Learning
- **Knowledge Graph Embeddings**: Dense vector representations of entities and relations in a KG; needed to capture semantic relationships in a continuous space; quick check: can be used for link prediction and similarity search.
- **Entity Alignment**: Process of matching entities between different KGs; needed to identify corresponding entities across knowledge graphs; quick check: successful alignment enables knowledge transfer between KGs.
- **Translation-based Models**: KG embedding models based on translational distance (e.g., TransE); needed for capturing relational patterns in KGs; quick check: can model compositional relationships between entities.
- **Textual Embeddings**: Dense vector representations of text; needed for measuring entity similarity in entity alignment; quick check: cosine similarity between embeddings indicates semantic relatedness.
- **KG Completion**: Task of predicting missing links in a knowledge graph; needed to evaluate the quality of KG embeddings; quick check: improved embeddings should lead to better link prediction performance.

## Architecture Onboarding

### Component Map
General-Purpose KG -> Entity Alignment -> Artificial Link Creation -> Combined KG -> Translation-based Model -> Enhanced Embeddings -> KG Completion

### Critical Path
The critical path for improving KG completion performance involves: 1) Entity alignment between general-purpose and domain-specific KGs, 2) Creation of artificial links based on alignment scores, 3) Training a translation-based model on the enriched KG, and 4) Using the resulting embeddings for KG completion tasks.

### Design Tradeoffs
- **Alignment Granularity**: Balancing precision and recall in entity alignment affects the quality of artificial links created.
- **Link Creation Threshold**: Setting appropriate thresholds for artificial link creation impacts the trade-off between enrichment and noise introduction.
- **Model Complexity**: Choosing between simpler or more complex translation-based models affects performance and computational requirements.

### Failure Signatures
- Poor entity alignment leading to incorrect artificial links and degraded performance
- Overly aggressive link creation introducing noise and confusing the translation model
- Insufficient domain-specific information in the combined KG, limiting the benefits of enrichment
