# Augmenting Ground-Level PM2.5 Prediction via Kriging-Based Pseudo-Label Generation

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2401.08061
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.08061
- **Reference count**: 32
- **Primary result**: Kriging-based pseudo-label generation improves PM2.5 prediction accuracy with 10.1% RMSE reduction and 11.4% MAE decrease

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of fusing abundant satellite data with sparse ground measurements for PM2.5 prediction. The authors propose a data augmentation strategy that generates pseudo-labels for unlabeled satellite images using ordinary kriging to interpolate ground measurements. These pseudo-labeled images are paired with satellite data and added to the training set. The augmented dataset is used to train a CNN-RF model, achieving significant improvements in prediction accuracy compared to baseline models without pseudo-labels.

## Method Summary
The approach combines satellite RGB imagery with ground PM2.5 measurements and meteorological attributes through a two-stage process. First, ordinary kriging with an exponential semivariogram interpolates ground measurements to generate pseudo-labels for unlabeled satellite images. These pseudo-labeled images are filtered based on spatial criteria (minimum 4 nearby stations within 0.2 km radius) and paired with satellite data. Second, a CNN-RF model is trained on the augmented dataset, where the CNN learns residuals between RF predictions and actual values. The method leverages the density of satellite data to compensate for sparse ground measurements while maintaining spatial correlation through kriging interpolation.

## Key Results
- 10.1% reduction in RMSE (from 37.64 to 32.56)
- 11.4% decrease in MAE (from 24.39 to 20.31)
- 2.8% increase in Pearson R
- 1.8% increase in spatial Pearson R

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
Kriging-based pseudo-label generation improves PM2.5 prediction by leveraging dense satellite data with sparse ground measurements. Ordinary kriging interpolates ground measurements to generate pseudo-labels for unlabeled satellite images, which are added to the training dataset, enabling the CNN-RF model to learn spatial patterns more effectively. Core assumption: The spatial interpolation via kriging accurately captures the underlying PM2.5 distribution across the study area.

### Mechanism 2
The CNN-RF model benefits from the augmented dataset by reducing prediction errors and improving spatial correlation. The CNN learns the residual between true PM2.5 values and RF predictions, while the RF provides a preliminary estimation based on meteorological attributes. The augmented dataset enhances both components' learning. Core assumption: The pseudo-labeled data are sufficiently accurate to improve the model's generalization without introducing significant noise.

### Mechanism 3
The selection of unlabeled satellite images ensures that each image has sufficient spatial information for accurate kriging interpolation. Images are filtered to ensure they are not within the area of interest (AOI) of any AQM station and have at least Ns sensor stations within a defined radius. Core assumption: The heuristic filtering criterion (Nsensor = 4, r = 0.2) is sufficient to ensure reliable pseudo-label generation.

## Architecture Onboarding

- **Component map**: Ground measurements -> Kriging interpolation -> Pseudo-labels -> Filtered satellite images -> CNN-RF model -> PM2.5 predictions
- **Critical path**: 1) Generate pseudo-labels using kriging, 2) Filter and pair unlabeled satellite images with pseudo-labels, 3) Augment training dataset, 4) Train CNN-RF model, 5) Evaluate performance
- **Design tradeoffs**: Kriging vs. other interpolation methods (accounts for spatial distance but computationally intensive); number of pseudo-labeled images (more can improve performance but may introduce noise); filtering criteria (stricter ensures better pseudo-labels but reduces usable images)
- **Failure signatures**: Poor pseudo-label quality due to sparse ground measurements or non-stationary PM2.5 distribution; overfitting to pseudo-labeled data; insufficient number of usable satellite images after filtering
- **First 3 experiments**: 1) Test kriging interpolation with different semivariogram models (spherical, exponential, Gaussian), 2) Vary the number of pseudo-labeled images (0, 1000, 5000, 10000) to assess impact on model performance, 3) Experiment with different filtering criteria (Nsensor, radius r) to optimize selection of unlabeled satellite images

## Limitations
- Effectiveness depends on spatial distribution of ground stations relative to satellite images, which is not validated across different geographic contexts
- Filtering criteria (Nsensor=4, r=0.2) appear arbitrary without sensitivity analysis to determine optimal values
- Claims about generalizability to other regions lack external validation beyond the Delhi case study

## Confidence

**High Confidence**: The methodology description is clear and the reported performance improvements (10.1% RMSE reduction, 11.4% MAE decrease) are internally consistent with the proposed approach.

**Medium Confidence**: The kriging-based pseudo-label generation is theoretically sound, but effectiveness depends on unvalidated assumptions about spatial stationarity and measurement density.

**Low Confidence**: Claims about generalizability to other regions and datasets are not supported by experiments beyond the Delhi case study.
