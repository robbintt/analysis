# HIP Network: Historical Information Passing Network for Extrapolation Reasoning on Temporal Knowledge Graph

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2402.12074
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.12074
- Reference count: 6
- Primary result: HIP Network achieves significant Hits@1 improvements on five TKG benchmark datasets

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces the Historical Information Passing (HIP) Network, a novel framework designed to address extrapolation reasoning on temporal knowledge graphs (TKGs). The key innovation lies in passing historical information from three distinct perspectives: temporal, structural, and repetitive. By modeling the evolution of events, interactions at the same time step, and known events respectively, HIP aims to accurately predict future events. The approach incorporates relation embedding updates and employs three scoring functions corresponding to each dimension of historical information.

## Method Summary
The HIP Network proposes a multi-dimensional information passing mechanism for temporal knowledge graph extrapolation reasoning. It updates entity and relation embeddings by aggregating information from temporal dynamics, structural interactions, and repetitive patterns. The framework employs three scoring functions aligned with these dimensions to evaluate potential future events. The method operates through iterative message passing that captures how historical events influence future predictions.

## Key Results
- HIP Network demonstrates significant improvements on Hits@1 metric across five benchmark datasets
- The model effectively captures temporal, structural, and repetitive patterns in TKGs
- Experimental results validate the framework's ability to accurately predict future events through historical information passing

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The HIP Network's effectiveness stems from its multi-perspective approach to historical information passing. By separately modeling temporal evolution, structural interactions, and repetitive patterns, the network can capture the complex dynamics of how past events influence future outcomes. The three scoring functions ensure that predictions are evaluated from all relevant historical dimensions, leading to more accurate extrapolation.
