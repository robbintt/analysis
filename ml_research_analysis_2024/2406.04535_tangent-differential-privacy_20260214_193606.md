# Tangent differential privacy

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2406.04535
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.04535
- Reference count: 20
- Key outcome: Introduces tangent differential privacy, a distribution-specific framework for protecting individual data points, with theoretical guarantees under entropic regularization for total variation and Wasserstein distances.

## Executive Summary
The paper introduces tangent differential privacy, a framework for protecting individual data points tailored to a specific data distribution, contrasting with traditional differential privacy which is uniform across all distributions. It allows for general distribution distances like total variation and Wasserstein. For risk minimization, the paper shows that entropic regularization ensures tangent differential privacy under general conditions. Specifically, Theorems 3 and 4 demonstrate that with bounded risk functions, tangent differential privacy is guaranteed for total variation distance, with sensitivity controlled by the product of entropic regularization strength and the risk bound. Theorems 5 and 6 extend these results to Wasserstein distance, using Sobolev norms. The work provides a more nuanced approach to differential privacy, adapting to the specific data distribution of interest.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
Tangent differential privacy works by adapting privacy guarantees to the specific data distribution of interest, rather than applying uniform privacy across all distributions. This is achieved through the use of general distribution distances, such as total variation and Wasserstein, which allow the framework to tailor privacy protections based on the characteristics of the data distribution. Entropic regularization plays a crucial role in ensuring tangent differential privacy, as it helps control the sensitivity of the risk function, which is a key factor in determining the privacy guarantees.
