# Combining Qualitative and Computational Approaches for Literary Analysis of Finnish Novels
## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2406.01021
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.01021
- Reference count: 0
- Primary result: Computational emotion detection successfully supports qualitative literary analysis of Finnish novels

## Executive Summary
This paper presents a hybrid methodology that combines computational emotion detection with traditional qualitative literary analysis to study Finnish novels from the early 20th century. The approach uses a carefully curated emotion lexicon adapted to historical Finnish literature, combined with word embeddings to map emotional spaces across seminal works. The method demonstrates that computational tools can effectively support large-scale literary analysis while maintaining alignment with established scholarly interpretations of authors' styles and thematic concerns.

The study analyzes four prominent Finnish authors (Juhani Aho, Minna Canth, Maria Jotuni, and F.E. Sillanpää) and provides emotion arcs for 975 Finnish novels, showing that computational lexicon analysis can reveal patterns in mood and tone that correspond with qualitative literary analysis. This hybrid approach offers a scalable method for comparing genres and national literary canons while preserving the depth of traditional close reading methods.

## Method Summary
The methodology employs a custom-curated emotion lexicon specifically adapted for early 20th-century Finnish literature, validated against existing emotion models. The approach uses word embeddings to create emotional spaces and generate emotion arcs across the corpus of Finnish novels. The computational analysis is integrated with qualitative literary studies through four case studies of prominent Finnish authors, allowing for validation of computational findings against established scholarly interpretations. The emotion detection relies on word frequency patterns to map emotional content, while the qualitative component provides contextual interpretation of literary devices and thematic elements.

## Key Results
- Computational lexicon analysis successfully supports qualitative literary analysis of mood and tone
- Emotion distributions align with scholarly interpretations of the authors' styles
- The approach enables large-scale comparison between genres and national canons while supporting traditional close-reading-based analyses

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method works by bridging computational pattern recognition with human interpretive frameworks. Word embeddings capture semantic relationships between emotionally charged terms, while the curated lexicon provides culturally and historically appropriate emotional mappings for Finnish literature. The computational approach identifies broad emotional patterns across large corpora, which qualitative analysis then interprets within specific literary contexts. This dual approach leverages the scalability of computational methods while maintaining the nuance and depth of traditional literary scholarship.

**Failure signatures:** Mismatched emotion distributions suggesting lexicon inadequacy, computational patterns contradicting established literary interpretations, inability to capture context-dependent emotional shifts

## Limitations
- Custom-curated emotion lexicon may not capture all culturally specific emotional expressions or semantic shifts over time
- Computational approach may miss nuanced literary devices like irony, metaphor, or context-dependent emotional shifts
- Validation relies on qualitative alignment with scholarly interpretations rather than systematic verification
