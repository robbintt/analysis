# Veni, Vidi, Vici: Solving the Myriad of Challenges before Knowledge Graph Learning

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2402.06098
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.06098
- **Reference count**: 40
- **Primary result**: Proposes a holistic framework to address four key deficiencies in Knowledge Graph learning systems

## Executive Summary
This paper identifies four critical deficiencies in current Knowledge Graph (KG) learning systems: lack of expert knowledge integration, instability to node degree extremity, lack of uncertainty and relevance consideration, and absence of explainability. Rather than addressing these issues in isolation, the authors propose the "Veni, Vidi, Vici" framework to conceptualize and solve these problems as a unified whole. The paper reviews existing literature and suggests future directions for integrated, holistic solutions that can advance both KG learning and broader machine learning domains.

## Method Summary
The paper conducts a comprehensive literature review to identify and characterize four key deficiencies in Knowledge Graph learning systems. The authors propose the "Veni, Vidi, Vici" framework as a conceptual approach to address these issues holistically. While no specific training procedures or concrete implementation details are provided, the framework serves as a roadmap for future research directions. The paper suggests that existing KG learning systems should be analyzed through the lens of these four deficiencies, with the goal of developing integrated solutions that consider all aspects simultaneously rather than in isolation.

## Key Results
- Identification of four key deficiencies in KG learning systems: lack of expert knowledge integration, instability to node degree extremity, lack of uncertainty and relevance consideration, and absence of explainability
- Proposal of the "Veni, Vidi, Vici" framework for conceptualizing holistic solutions to these deficiencies
- Review of existing literature and identification of future research directions for integrated, holistic approaches to KG learning

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework works by shifting from isolated problem-solving to a holistic approach that recognizes the interconnected nature of the four identified deficiencies. By addressing these challenges together rather than separately, the "Veni, Vidi, Vici" framework enables more effective co-empowerment of KG learning systems with human users. The mechanism relies on integrating expert knowledge throughout the learning process, stabilizing learning against node degree variations, incorporating uncertainty and relevance metrics, and providing explainable outputs that users can understand and trust.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Expert Knowledge Integration -> Uncertainty Quantification -> Relevance Filtering -> Explainability Layer -> KG Learning Core

**Critical Path**: The most critical path involves integrating expert knowledge early in the learning process, which then influences uncertainty quantification and relevance filtering, ultimately affecting the explainability layer and the core learning algorithm.

**Design Tradeoffs**: 
- Granularity vs. Performance: More detailed expert knowledge integration improves quality but may slow learning
- Complexity vs. Interpretability: More sophisticated uncertainty models may be harder to explain
- Precision vs. Coverage: Stricter relevance filtering may miss important patterns
- Model Capacity vs. Explainability: More complex models may sacrifice interpretability

**Failure Signatures**:
- Expert knowledge not properly weighted or integrated
- Uncertainty estimates consistently overconfident or underconfident
- Relevance filters removing critical information
- Explanations that don't align with model decisions
- Instability when node degree distributions change
