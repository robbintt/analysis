# Topology-Aware 3D Gaussian Splatting: Leveraging Persistent Homology for Optimized Structural Integrity

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2412.16619
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.16619
- Reference count: 40
- Introduces topology-aware Gaussian splatting with persistent homology constraints for improved structural integrity

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses structural integrity issues in 3D Gaussian Splatting for novel-view synthesis, specifically incomplete geometric coverage and insufficient topological constraints. The authors propose Topology-Aware 3D Gaussian Splatting (Topology-GS), which introduces Local Persistent Voronoi Interpolation (LPVI) to enhance point coverage in low-curvature areas while preserving topology, and PersLoss, a topology-focused regularization term based on persistent homology. LPVI adaptively switches between 3D and 2D Voronoi interpolation guided by topological differences, while PersLoss constrains distances between topological features of rendered and ground truth images. Experiments on three benchmarks show Topology-GS outperforms existing methods with PSNR improvements (e.g., from 28.84 to 29.50), SSIM increases (e.g., from 0.848 to 0.874), and LPIPS reductions (e.g., from 0.220 to 0.179), while maintaining efficient memory usage. This work pioneers the integration of topology with 3D-GS, demonstrating substantial improvements in both pixel-level and feature-level structural integrity.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method works by integrating persistent homology into the Gaussian splatting framework, allowing the system to maintain topological constraints during rendering. The LPVI mechanism ensures proper point distribution in low-curvature regions while preserving the underlying topological structure. PersLoss provides a direct optimization target that penalizes topological deviations between rendered and ground truth images, ensuring structural integrity is maintained throughout the rendering process.
