# Data Augmentation Techniques for Cross-Domain WiFi CSI-based Human Activity Recognition

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2401.00964
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.00964
- Reference count: 15
- Primary result: Random resized crop augmentation yields up to 22.5% accuracy improvement in cross-system WiFi CSI-based activity recognition

## Executive Summary
This paper investigates data augmentation techniques to improve cross-scenario and cross-system generalization in WiFi Channel State Information (CSI)-based human activity recognition. The authors collected a dataset using two different WiFi systems (PIFA and BQ antennas) across line-of-sight (LOS) and non-line-of-sight (NLOS) through-wall scenarios. Using EfficientNetV2 models, they conducted an ablation study examining the impact of various augmentations including random circular rotation, resized crop, amplitude scaling, and contrast adjustment on cross-domain performance.

The study reveals that specific augmentations significantly enhance cross-scenario generalization, with random resized crop achieving the most substantial improvements. Notably, the research demonstrates asymmetric generalization patterns between scenarios and systems, suggesting complex interactions between environmental conditions and hardware configurations. The findings highlight that carefully selected data augmentation can substantially improve model robustness to environmental and hardware variations in WiFi-based sensing applications, though the effectiveness depends on the target domain and specific augmentation techniques employed.

## Method Summary
The research employed two WiFi systems with different antenna configurations (PIFA and BQ) to collect CSI amplitude spectrograms in both LOS and NLOS through-wall scenarios. An EfficientNetV2-based architecture was used as the baseline model for activity recognition. The authors implemented and evaluated multiple data augmentation techniques including random circular rotation, random resized crop, amplitude scaling, and contrast adjustment. A systematic ablation study was conducted where models were trained with various combinations of augmentations and evaluated on cross-scenario and cross-system generalization tasks. The study measured performance improvements when models trained on one scenario/system configuration were tested on data from different scenarios/systems, providing insights into how augmentation affects robustness to domain shifts.

## Key Results
- Random resized crop augmentation improved cross-system generalization accuracy by up to 22.5%
- Specific augmentations showed significant improvements in cross-scenario generalization performance
- Asymmetric generalization patterns were observed between LOS and NLOS scenarios across different systems
- The effectiveness of augmentations varied considerably depending on the target domain and system configuration

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
Data augmentation techniques introduce controlled variations in the training data that mimic the diversity encountered in real-world deployment scenarios. For WiFi CSI-based activity recognition, these variations help models learn more robust feature representations that are invariant to environmental factors like wall obstructions, antenna configurations, and signal propagation differences. The augmentations effectively simulate domain shifts during training, forcing the model to develop more generalizable features rather than overfitting to specific hardware or environmental conditions. This approach is particularly effective because WiFi CSI signals contain both activity-specific patterns and environment-dependent characteristics, and augmentation helps separate these components.
