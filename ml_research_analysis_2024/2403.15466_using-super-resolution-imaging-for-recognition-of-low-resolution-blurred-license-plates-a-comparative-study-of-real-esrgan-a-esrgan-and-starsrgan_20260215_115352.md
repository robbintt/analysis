# Using Super-Resolution Imaging for Recognition of Low-Resolution Blurred License Plates: A Comparative Study of Real-ESRGAN, A-ESRGAN, and StarSRGAN

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2403.15466
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.15466
- Reference count: 40
- One-line primary result: Fine-tuning super-resolution models (Real-ESRGAN, A-ESRGAN, StarSRGAN) improves license plate recognition accuracy by enhancing low-resolution images before OCR processing.

## Executive Summary
This study addresses the challenge of recognizing blurry license plates from low-resolution surveillance cameras in Taiwan. The research fine-tunes three super-resolution models—Real-ESRGAN, A-ESRGAN, and StarSRGAN—to enhance the clarity of low-resolution license plate images. The enhanced images are then processed using Tesseract OCR for license plate recognition. The evaluation demonstrates that super-resolution techniques can significantly improve recognition accuracy, providing valuable insights for traffic management and security applications.

## Method Summary
The study fine-tunes pre-trained super-resolution models on a dataset of manually extracted license plate images that were downscaled to simulate low-resolution surveillance conditions. Three models were evaluated: Real-ESRGAN and A-ESRGAN with 4x magnification, and StarSRGAN with 7.5x magnification. The enhanced images were processed by Tesseract OCR, and recognition accuracy was measured using Accuracy and Precision metrics derived from confusion matrices comparing predictions to ground truth.

## Key Results
- Fine-tuned super-resolution models successfully enhanced low-resolution license plate images for improved OCR recognition
- The study provides a comparative analysis of Real-ESRGAN, A-ESRGAN, and StarSRGAN performance on license plate recognition tasks
- Evaluation metrics demonstrated measurable improvements in recognition accuracy through super-resolution enhancement
