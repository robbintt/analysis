# Integrating Heterogeneous Gene Expression Data through Knowledge Graphs for Improving Diabetes Prediction

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2404.14970
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.14970
- Reference count: 33
- Primary result: Knowledge graph integration improves diabetes prediction with F1-score of 0.683

## Executive Summary
This paper tackles the challenge of integrating heterogeneous gene expression data for diabetes prediction by constructing a knowledge graph (KG) that combines multiple datasets with domain-specific biological knowledge. The authors employ KG embedding methods to generate patient vector representations, which are then used as input to a decision tree classifier. The integration approach shows modest performance improvements over baseline methods that use individual datasets or simpler integration techniques, demonstrating the potential of KG-based methods for biomedical data integration while highlighting the ongoing challenges in diabetes prediction.

## Method Summary
The core methodology involves building a knowledge graph that integrates multiple gene expression datasets with domain knowledge about protein functions and interactions. KG embedding techniques (TransE, DistMult, RotatE) are applied to generate vector representations of patients from the integrated graph structure. These patient embeddings serve as features for a decision tree classifier to predict diabetes. The approach aims to leverage complementary information across heterogeneous datasets and incorporate biological relationships to improve predictive performance compared to using individual datasets or simple concatenation methods.

## Key Results
- Knowledge graph integration achieves F1-score of 0.683 for diabetes prediction
- Performance improves over baselines using individual datasets or simple concatenation
- Accuracy of 0.619, precision of 0.677, and recall of 0.739 demonstrate moderate predictive capability

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The integration of heterogeneous gene expression data through knowledge graphs enables the capture of complementary biological information across multiple datasets. By embedding both the gene expression data and domain knowledge about protein interactions and functions into a unified graph structure, the approach can learn patient representations that reflect complex biological relationships. KG embedding methods transform this structured information into vector space where similar patients are positioned close together based on their biological profiles and relationships, allowing the classifier to leverage these learned representations for improved prediction.

## Limitations
- Modest performance gains relative to the complexity of KG integration approach
- Single dataset evaluation (GEO GSE9006) limits generalizability of results
- Uncertainty about whether KG embedding methods are optimal compared to newer graph neural networks
