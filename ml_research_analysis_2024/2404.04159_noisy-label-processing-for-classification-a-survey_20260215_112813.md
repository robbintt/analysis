# Noisy Label Processing for Classification: A Survey

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2404.04159
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.04159
- Authors: Mengting Li; Chuang Zhu
- Reference count: 40
- One-line primary result: Survey categorizes noisy label processing methods and proposes novel synthetic noise generation algorithm

## Executive Summary
This paper provides a comprehensive survey of deep learning approaches for handling noisy labels in image classification tasks. The survey categorizes methods into four main approaches: noise transition matrix estimation, noise-robust regularization, sample selection, and semi-supervised learning-based methods. It also introduces three taxonomies of label noise patterns: instance-independent, instance-dependent, and human-annotated real-world noise. The authors propose a novel algorithm for generating synthetic noisy labels guided by real-world data patterns, specifically using the CIFAR-10N dataset as a benchmark. This algorithm combines feature distribution analysis and noise transition matrices to create more realistic synthetic noise. Experimental results show that state-of-the-art methods like LongReMix and DISC achieve high accuracy (93.44% and 93.86% respectively) on 20% noise ratio, but performance drops significantly with higher noise levels, highlighting the challenge of extreme noise scenarios.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The survey's effectiveness stems from its comprehensive categorization of existing methods and introduction of novel taxonomies for understanding label noise patterns. By organizing approaches into four main categories and introducing three distinct noise pattern taxonomies, the paper provides a structured framework for researchers to navigate the complex landscape of noisy label processing. The proposed synthetic noise generation algorithm leverages real-world data patterns to create more representative benchmark datasets, addressing a critical gap in the field. This systematic approach enables better evaluation of methods and identification of key challenges, such as the performance degradation in extreme noise scenarios, guiding future research directions.

## Architecture Onboarding

Component map: Data -> Noise Pattern Analysis -> Method Selection -> Model Training -> Evaluation

Design tradeoffs: Balance between model complexity and noise-handling capability; trade-off between sample selection aggressiveness and potential loss of useful information.

Failure signatures: Overfitting to noisy labels, underutilization of potentially clean samples, poor generalization to real-world noise patterns.

## Limitations
- Boundaries between method categories may not always be clear-cut in practice
- Taxonomy of label noise patterns may not capture all possible noise scenarios in real-world applications
- Effectiveness of proposed synthetic noise generation algorithm limited to CIFAR-10N dataset, requiring further validation on diverse datasets
