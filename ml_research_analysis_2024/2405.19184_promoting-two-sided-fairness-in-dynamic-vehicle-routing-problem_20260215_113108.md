# Promoting Two-sided Fairness in Dynamic Vehicle Routing Problem

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2405.19184
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.19184
- Reference count: 40
- Key outcome: 2FairGA achieves lower fairness variance while maintaining higher utility and efficiency compared to state-of-the-art baselines

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the Dynamic Vehicle Routing Problem (DVRP) by proposing a novel Two-sided Fairness-aware Genetic Algorithm (2FairGA) framework that simultaneously considers service provider fairness, customer fairness, and utility optimization. The core method integrates constrained K-means clustering for initial service provider placement and modifies genetic algorithm fitness functions to incorporate both fairness objectives alongside utility optimization. Experiments on real-world datasets (Melbourne parking violations and NYC taxi requests) demonstrate that 2FairGA outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, achieving lower fairness metrics (variance) while maintaining higher utility (capture rate/benefit) and efficiency (shorter total distance).

## Method Summary
The paper proposes 2FairGA, which combines constrained K-means clustering for initial service provider placement with a modified genetic algorithm. The clustering algorithm groups customer requests to determine optimal initial locations for service providers, while the genetic algorithm uses Leader-based Random Keys Encoding (LERK) and a multi-objective fitness function that balances service provider fairness (variance in accumulated utility), customer fairness (variance in capture rate/mean waiting time), and total utility. The algorithm iteratively evolves solutions through selection, crossover, mutation, and local optimization procedures.

## Key Results
- 2FairGA achieves lower fairness variance metrics compared to state-of-the-art baselines
- The method maintains higher utility (capture rate/benefit) while achieving better fairness
- Total routing distance is shorter with 2FairGA compared to competing approaches
- Ablation study confirms the importance of incorporating both service provider and customer fairness considerations

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
2FairGA works by explicitly optimizing for both service provider and customer fairness objectives simultaneously through its multi-objective fitness function. The constrained K-means clustering ensures initial service provider placement is geographically fair to customer distribution, while the genetic algorithm evolves solutions that balance the competing objectives of fairness and utility. The Leader-based Random Keys Encoding allows the algorithm to explore the solution space effectively while maintaining solution feasibility.
