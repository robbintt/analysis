# Diffusion priors for Bayesian 3D reconstruction from incomplete measurements

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2412.14897
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.14897
- Reference count: 40
- Combines diffusion models with Bayesian inference for 3D reconstruction from sparse measurements

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces a Bayesian approach for 3D reconstruction using diffusion model priors trained on 3D point cloud datasets. The method addresses the challenge of reconstructing 3D structures from incomplete measurements like 2D projections or partial substructures by combining diffusion model priors with likelihood functions that model the measurement process. The approach enables sampling from the posterior distribution of 3D structures given sparse observations.

The framework is demonstrated on ShapeNet datasets for household objects and synthetic cryo-EM data for biomolecular complexes. Results show significant improvements over maximum likelihood approaches, with 10-20% lower error in reconstruction metrics while maintaining meaningful structural fidelity. The method is particularly promising for cryo-EM applications where structural heterogeneity and limited data are common challenges.

## Method Summary
The authors train diffusion models on 3D point cloud datasets representing objects like household items and biomolecular complexes. These trained models serve as priors in a Bayesian framework for reconstructing 3D structures from incomplete data. The key innovation is combining diffusion models with reconstruction guidance through a likelihood function that incorporates the forward model of the measurement process. This allows sampling from the posterior distribution of 3D structures given sparse observations, effectively bridging the gap between the generative power of diffusion models and the specific measurement constraints of the reconstruction problem.

## Key Results
- ShapeNet experiments show 10-20% improvement in Chamfer and Earth Movers Distance compared to maximum likelihood approaches
- Cryo-EM reconstructions from 3-5 2D projections achieve RMSD values of 3-5 Å compared to ground truth structures
- The method successfully handles structural heterogeneity and limited data scenarios common in cryo-EM applications

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
Diffusion models learn the distribution of complete 3D structures during training, capturing the underlying manifold of valid shapes. When combined with a likelihood function that models how measurements are generated from 3D structures, the Bayesian framework can infer which structures are most probable given the incomplete observations. The diffusion prior regularizes the reconstruction by favoring structures that are plausible according to the learned distribution while the likelihood ensures consistency with the measurements. This combination is particularly effective for sparse measurements because the prior provides strong inductive bias about what complete structures should look like.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map:**
Diffusion Model Training -> Prior Learning -> Bayesian Framework -> Likelihood Integration -> Posterior Sampling -> Reconstruction Output

**Critical Path:**
Training data → Diffusion model → Prior distribution → Measurement likelihood → Posterior distribution → Sampled reconstructions

**Design Tradeoffs:**
- Model capacity vs. computational efficiency in diffusion model training
- Prior strength vs. measurement fidelity in the posterior distribution
- Sampling efficiency vs. reconstruction quality in posterior exploration

**Failure Signatures:**
- Posterior collapse to prior when measurements are too sparse
- Overfitting to measurement noise when likelihood is too strong
- Mode collapse in sampled reconstructions

## Limitations
- Performance on diverse real-world datasets beyond ShapeNet and synthetic cryo-EM data remains untested
- Computational cost of sampling may limit practical applicability for large-scale or real-time reconstruction
- Limited qualitative assessment of reconstruction fidelity and biological relevance for biomolecular complexes

## Confidence

**High confidence:**
- Mathematical framework combining diffusion priors with Bayesian inference is sound and well-established

**Medium confidence:**
- Performance improvements over maximum likelihood approaches are demonstrated but may not generalize to all object categories
- Cryo-EM reconstruction claims are promising but based on idealized synthetic data rather than experimental measurements
