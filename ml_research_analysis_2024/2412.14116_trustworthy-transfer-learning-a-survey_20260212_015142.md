# Trustworthy Transfer Learning: A Survey

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2412.14116
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.14116
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Comprehensive survey of trustworthy transfer learning addressing knowledge transferability quantification and trustworthiness enhancement across privacy, fairness, robustness, and transparency dimensions

## Executive Summary
This survey provides a systematic review of trustworthy transfer learning, addressing two fundamental questions: how to quantitatively measure and enhance knowledge transferability across domains, and how to ensure the trustworthiness of transferred knowledge. The authors examine recent theories and algorithms for understanding knowledge transferability under both IID and non-IID assumptions, while also analyzing the impact of trustworthiness dimensions including privacy, adversarial robustness, fairness, and transparency on transfer learning systems. The work highlights critical open questions and outlines future research directions for developing reliable and trustworthy transfer learning methodologies.

## Method Summary
The survey synthesizes recent advancements in transfer learning through a comprehensive literature review spanning 40 references. The authors organize their analysis around two main axes: knowledge transferability quantification and trustworthiness enhancement. They examine theoretical frameworks for understanding transfer learning under different data distribution assumptions, review algorithmic approaches for measuring and improving transferability, and analyze how various trustworthiness dimensions affect transfer learning performance. The methodology involves systematic categorization of existing work, identification of key research gaps, and synthesis of emerging trends in the field.

## Key Results
- Provides comprehensive analysis of knowledge transferability quantification under IID and non-IID assumptions
- Reviews trustworthiness enhancement techniques across privacy, adversarial robustness, fairness, and transparency dimensions
- Identifies critical open questions and future research directions for trustworthy transfer learning
- Highlights the interplay between transferability and trustworthiness in modern transfer learning systems

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The survey's effectiveness stems from its systematic organization of the rapidly evolving field of trustworthy transfer learning. By separating the analysis into knowledge transferability and trustworthiness dimensions, the authors create a clear framework for understanding how these two aspects interact. The coverage of both theoretical foundations and practical algorithms provides a balanced perspective that bridges the gap between abstract concepts and real-world applications. The emphasis on both IID and non-IID assumptions ensures relevance across diverse practical scenarios where data distributions may vary significantly.

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out
The survey highlights several critical open questions in trustworthy transfer learning, including the need for more rigorous theoretical analysis of non-IID assumptions and their impact on knowledge transferability, the development of comprehensive frameworks that effectively integrate all trustworthiness dimensions, and the challenge of validating trustworthiness enhancement techniques across diverse real-world scenarios. The authors also emphasize the importance of understanding the interplay between transferability and trustworthiness, particularly in the context of emerging foundation models and their implications for transfer learning reliability.

## Limitations
- Claims of comprehensive coverage require systematic verification across all transfer learning paradigms
- Treatment of non-IID assumptions may lack sufficient theoretical rigor for complex real-world scenarios
- Coverage of recent foundation model developments and their trustworthiness implications may be incomplete
- Limited empirical validation of proposed frameworks across diverse application domains
