# A Taxation Perspective for Fair Re-ranking

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID:** 2404.17826
- **Source URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.17826
- **Authors:** Chen Xu; Xiaopeng Ye; Wenjie Wang; Liang Pang; Jun Xu; Tat-Seng Chua
- **Reference count:** 40
- **Primary result:** Introduces Tax-rank, a taxation-based fair re-ranking method that outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in both fairness and accuracy

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces a novel taxation-based perspective for fair re-ranking in recommendation and advertising systems. The authors show that previous fair re-ranking methods can be reformulated as item-level tax policies, but these lack two key properties: continuity and controllability over accuracy loss. To overcome these limitations, they propose Tax-rank, a new method that levies taxes based on the difference in utility between two items. Tax-rank is optimized using the Sinkhorn algorithm in optimal transport. The authors theoretically demonstrate that Tax-rank exhibits superior continuity and controllability compared to existing methods. Experiments on two publicly available recommendation and advertising datasets show that Tax-rank outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in terms of both fair ranking performance and efficiency.

## Method Summary
Tax-rank addresses fair re-ranking by introducing a taxation-based perspective where items are taxed based on the difference in utility between two items. This approach overcomes limitations of previous methods that lack continuity and controllability over accuracy loss. The method is optimized using the Sinkhorn algorithm in optimal transport, which projects averaged exposures onto ranking results. The key innovation is the taxation mechanism that ensures fair distribution of ranking slots while maintaining ranking accuracy.

## Key Results
- Tax-rank demonstrates superior continuity and controllability compared to existing fair re-ranking methods
- Outperforms state-of-the-art baselines on both ranking accuracy (eCN/eCPM) and fairness (Gini Index) metrics
- Shows competitive performance and efficiency on two real-world datasets (Yelp and iPinYou)

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
Tax-rank works by reformulating fair re-ranking as a taxation problem where items are taxed based on utility differences. This creates a continuous and controllable mechanism for redistributing ranking slots fairly. The Sinkhorn algorithm optimizes this taxation scheme by finding the optimal transport plan that balances fairness and accuracy objectives.

## Foundational Learning
- **Optimal Transport (OT):** A mathematical framework for finding optimal ways to transform one probability distribution into another. Needed to optimize the taxation scheme and ensure fair redistribution of ranking slots.
- **Sinkhorn Algorithm:** An iterative method for solving optimal transport problems. Used to find the optimal tax rates that balance fairness and accuracy.
- **Gini Index:** A measure of inequality in a distribution. Used as the fairness metric to evaluate how evenly items are distributed across ranking positions.

## Limitations
- Requires careful tuning of the OT regularizer coefficient λot, which can significantly impact performance
- Implementation details of the Sinkhorn algorithm are not fully specified, affecting reproducibility
- Analysis is primarily theoretical with limited ablation studies on the impact of different taxation schemes
