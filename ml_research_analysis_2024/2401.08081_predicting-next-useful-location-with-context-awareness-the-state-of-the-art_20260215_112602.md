# Predicting Next Useful Location With Context-Awareness: The State-Of-The-Art

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2401.08081
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.08081
- Reference count: 40
- One-line primary result: Comprehensive survey of context-aware next useful location prediction methods, analyzing 30+ recent studies.

## Executive Summary
This survey provides a comprehensive overview of next useful location prediction with context-awareness, examining nearly 30 recent studies in the field. The paper categorizes and analyzes various prediction methods, challenges, datasets, metrics, and types of context used in human mobility prediction. It emphasizes the importance of leveraging diverse contextual information beyond spatiotemporal patterns to enhance prediction accuracy, as human mobility is influenced by multiple factors including user profiles, activities, social relationships, and environmental conditions.

## Method Summary
The survey systematically reviews and categorizes existing research on next useful location prediction, organizing the analysis around different perspectives including methodologies employed, challenges addressed, types of context utilized, datasets employed for training, and evaluation metrics. The authors examine various prediction approaches including Markov models, Bayesian networks, deep learning architectures, hybrid methods, and unsupervised learning techniques. The analysis focuses particularly on the usefulness of predicted locations and how different approaches incorporate context-awareness into their prediction frameworks.

## Key Results
- Context-awareness significantly improves next location prediction accuracy by incorporating semantic and temporal information beyond raw spatiotemporal data
- Deep learning models, particularly RNNs and CNNs, effectively capture complex spatiotemporal patterns and long-term dependencies in human mobility data
- Hybrid methods combining different approaches can effectively capture both regularity and conformity aspects of human mobility

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out

### Open Question 1
- Question: How can individual context be effectively incorporated into next location prediction models without compromising privacy?
- Basis in paper: [explicit] The paper highlights that individual context (e.g., user schedules, tasks, habits) is the most neglected type of context in human mobility prediction, with only one study incorporating it. It also mentions the challenges of data collection and privacy preservation when using individual context.
- Why unresolved: Individual context is difficult to collect and raises privacy concerns, as it involves accessing users' personal information. Existing methods primarily rely on spatiotemporal context, which is easier to obtain but may not provide the same level of accuracy.
- What evidence would resolve it: Development of privacy-preserving techniques for collecting and using individual context, such as federated learning or differential privacy, and empirical studies demonstrating the improved accuracy of next location prediction models when incorporating individual context.

### Open Question 2
- Question: How can the randomness and exploration aspects of human mobility be effectively modeled to improve next location prediction accuracy?
- Basis in paper: [explicit] The paper discusses the inherent randomness in human mobility and the importance of studying the exploration attitude alongside mining regularity patterns. It mentions that only a small percentage of places are visited multiple times, while the majority are visited once or are new places.
- Why unresolved: Existing methods primarily focus on capturing regularity patterns and may not adequately account for the randomness and exploration aspects of human mobility. Predicting a user's intention to explore before attempting to find their future location based on their movement pattern is suggested as a potential solution, but more research is needed.
- What evidence would resolve it: Development of novel prediction models that effectively combine movement pattern mining with POI recommendation techniques to capture both regularity and exploration aspects of human mobility, and empirical studies demonstrating improved accuracy in predicting next locations.

### Open Question 3
- Question: How can the cold start problem and data sparsity issues be addressed in next location prediction, especially when dealing with new users or locations?
- Basis in paper: [explicit] The paper discusses the cold start problem, which arises when there is no or limited historical data for a user, and the data sparsity problem, which concerns the lack of dense spatiotemporal data to illustrate users' movement patterns. It mentions that incorporating individual context can help recognize a user's personality and improve predictions for new users.
- Why unresolved: Existing methods struggle to make accurate predictions for new users or locations due to the lack of historical data. While incorporating individual context is suggested as a potential solution, more research is needed to develop effective techniques for addressing the cold start problem and data sparsity issues.
- What evidence would resolve it: Development of novel techniques, such as rule-based methods or transfer learning, that can effectively handle the cold start problem and data sparsity issues in next location prediction, and empirical studies demonstrating improved performance for new users or locations.

## Limitations

- The actual magnitude of improvement from context-awareness is not quantified or validated against a non-context-aware baseline
- The effectiveness of deep learning models for next location prediction is supported by weak evidence from corpus papers
- The survey does not discuss potential biases or privacy concerns associated with using contextual information for predictions
