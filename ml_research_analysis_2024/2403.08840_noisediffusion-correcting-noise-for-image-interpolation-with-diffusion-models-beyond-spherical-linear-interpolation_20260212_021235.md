# NoiseDiffusion: Correcting Noise for Image Interpolation with Diffusion Models beyond Spherical Linear Interpolation

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2403.08840
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.08840
- Authors: PengFei Zheng; Yonggang Zhang; Zhen Fang; Tongliang Liu; Defu Lian; Bo Han
- Reference count: 37
- One-line primary result: NoiseDiffusion achieves the best interpolation results, effectively reducing artifacts and preserving original image features compared to existing methods like spherical linear interpolation and direct noise introduction.

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of image interpolation using diffusion models, specifically focusing on improving the quality of interpolation results for natural images. The core method, NoiseDiffusion, corrects the invalidity of encoding noise by introducing subtle Gaussian noise and a constraint to suppress extreme noise values. It performs interpolation within the noisy image space and injects raw images to address information loss. The primary result is that NoiseDiffusion achieves the best interpolation results, effectively reducing artifacts and preserving original image features compared to existing methods like spherical linear interpolation and direct noise introduction.

## Method Summary
The NoiseDiffusion method addresses the problem of image interpolation using diffusion models by correcting the invalidity of encoding noise for natural images. The approach involves introducing subtle Gaussian noise and a constraint to suppress extreme noise values, performing interpolation within the noisy image space, and injecting raw images to counteract information loss. The method is evaluated on LSUN Cat-256 and LSUN Bedroom-256 datasets, demonstrating superior performance in reducing artifacts and preserving original image features compared to existing methods.

## Key Results
- NoiseDiffusion achieves the best interpolation results, effectively reducing artifacts and preserving original image features.
- The method outperforms existing approaches like spherical linear interpolation and direct noise introduction.
- Experimental investigations reveal that challenges in image interpolation stem from the invalidity of encoding noise, which may no longer obey the expected noise distribution.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- **Claim:** Introducing subtle Gaussian noise and constraining extreme noise values corrects the invalidity of encoding noise, enabling natural images to be interpolated without artifacts.
- **Mechanism:** The method recognizes that the latent variables of natural images often deviate from the expected normal distribution. By introducing subtle Gaussian noise and suppressing extreme values through clipping, it nudges these latent variables closer to the expected distribution, thereby reducing artifacts in the interpolated results.
- **Core assumption:** The latent variables of natural images do not follow the expected normal distribution and contain noise components outside the denoising threshold, leading to artifacts.
- **Evidence anchors:**
  - [abstract]: "Our experimental investigations reveal that these challenges stem from the invalidity of the encoding noise, which may no longer obey the expected noise distribution, e.g., a normal distribution."
  - [section 4.2]: "Based on Theorem 1, we can attribute the failure of spherical linear image interpolation to the mismatch between noise levels and denoising threshold."
- **Break condition:** If the introduced Gaussian noise is too large, it may lead to information loss, as stated in the paper: "However, at the same time, the image gradually loses some of its original features and introduces additional information."

### Mechanism 2
- **Claim:** Interpolating in the noisy image space and injecting raw images addresses the information loss caused by noise correction.
- **Mechanism:** Noise correction improves the validity of noise but often leads to a reduction in the signal-to-noise ratio, i.e., loss of original image information. To counteract this, the method interpolates in the noisy image space and injects raw images into these noisy counterparts, thereby preserving the original image features.
- **Core assumption:** Noise correction improves noise validity but reduces the signal-to-noise ratio, necessitating the injection of raw image information to preserve original features.
- **Evidence anchors:**
  - [abstract]: "In this context, promoting noise validity contributes to mitigating image artifacts, but the constraint and introduced exogenous noise typically lead to a reduction in signal-to-noise ratio, i.e., loss of original image information."
  - [section 4.4]: "Hence, NoiseDiffusion subsequently performs interpolation in the noisy image space and injects raw images into these noisy images to tackle the information loss issue."
- **Break condition:** If the raw image information injection is not properly balanced, it may lead to overly bright or dark images, as indicated by the paper's observation that "smaller values of µ and ν lead to darker images while increasing them results in overly bright pictures."

### Mechanism 3
- **Claim:** The combination of noise correction and raw image injection, along with boundary control, enables NoiseDiffusion to outperform existing methods in preserving original image features and reducing artifacts.
- **Mechanism:** NoiseDiffusion combines noise correction (subtle Gaussian noise and constraint) with raw image injection in the noisy image space. This combination, along with boundary control to suppress extreme noise values, allows it to interpolate natural images without causing artifacts or information loss, thus achieving the best interpolation results.
- **Core assumption:** The combination of noise correction, raw image injection, and boundary control is more effective than existing methods in preserving original image features and reducing artifacts.
- **Evidence anchors:**
  - [abstract]: "Consequently, NoiseDiffusion enables us to interpolate natural images without causing artifacts or information loss, thus achieving the best interpolation results."
  - [section 5.5]: "From the interpolation results, we observe that our method effectively reduces artifacts and maximally preserves information compared to directly applying spherical linear interpolation."
- **Break condition:** If the parameters (e.g., lubricating coefficient, boundary control) are not properly tuned, it may lead to either information loss or the introduction of additional artifacts, as suggested by the paper's discussion on the trade-offs between these parameters.

## Foundational Learning

- **Concept:** High-dimensional geometry and the properties of Gaussian distributions.
  - **Why needed here:** Understanding that in high dimensions, Gaussian noise behaves like a uniform distribution on a hypersphere is crucial for explaining why noise levels need to match the denoising threshold and how noise correction works.
  - **Quick check question:** Why does adding Gaussian noise matching the denoising level produce high-quality images, while noise levels above or below the threshold lead to artifacts or blurriness?

- **Concept:** Stochastic differential equations (SDEs) and their application in diffusion models.
  - **Why needed here:** The paper's method relies on encoding images into a latent space using SDEs and then decoding them through the reverse-time SDE. Understanding this process is essential for grasping how NoiseDiffusion works.
  - **Quick check question:** How do SDEs describe the noise injection and denoising process in diffusion models, and how are they used for image interpolation and editing?

- **Concept:** Signal-to-noise ratio and its impact on image quality.
  - **Why needed here:** The paper discusses how noise correction can lead to a reduction in the signal-to-noise ratio, causing information loss. Understanding this concept is crucial for grasping the need for raw image injection.
  - **Quick check question:** How does noise correction affect the signal-to-noise ratio, and why is it necessary to inject raw image information to counteract this effect?

## Architecture Onboarding

- **Component map:** Image encoding -> Noise correction (Gaussian noise + constraint) -> Interpolation in noisy space -> Raw image injection -> Decoding
- **Critical path:** The critical path for NoiseDiffusion involves encoding the input images into the latent space, applying noise correction and boundary control, interpolating in the noisy image space, injecting raw image information, and finally decoding the interpolated latent variables back into images.
- **Design tradeoffs:** The method involves a trade-off between noise validity and information preservation. Noise correction improves noise validity but can lead to information loss, necessitating the injection of raw image information. Additionally, boundary control can reduce artifacts but may also cause feature loss and darkening.
- **Failure signatures:** Potential failure modes include overly bright or dark images due to improper raw image information injection, introduction of additional artifacts if noise correction is too aggressive, and loss of original image features if boundary control is too restrictive.
- **First 3 experiments:**
  1. **Experiment 1:** Test the impact of the lubricating coefficient (γ) on image quality and feature preservation. Gradually increase γ from 0 to 1 and observe the changes in artifacts and image features.
  2. **Experiment 2:** Evaluate the effectiveness of boundary control in reducing artifacts. Compare the results of applying constraints before interpolation, after interpolation, and both before and after interpolation.
  3. **Experiment 3:** Assess the impact of raw image information injection on preserving original image features. Vary the parameters µ and ν to adjust the amount of original image information and observe the changes in image brightness and feature preservation.

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out
The paper acknowledges several limitations and potential areas for future work:
1. The effectiveness of NoiseDiffusion in other modalities beyond images, such as text, audio, or video, has not been validated.
2. Extending NoiseDiffusion to handle interpolation between in-distribution and out-of-distribution data, or between adversarial and natural images, is an exciting potential application that requires further investigation.
3. The impact of the boundary control parameter on the trade-off between artifact reduction and information preservation needs to be systematically analyzed.

## Limitations
- The method's effectiveness in other data modalities beyond images has not been validated.
- The impact of the boundary control parameter on the trade-off between artifact reduction and information preservation is not systematically analyzed.
- Direct quantitative comparisons with state-of-the-art interpolation methods are limited in the main results.

## Confidence
- **High** confidence: The core mechanism linking noise distribution mismatch to interpolation artifacts is well-supported by theoretical analysis and empirical observations.
- **Medium** confidence: The effectiveness of raw image injection for preserving features is supported by qualitative results but lacks quantitative metrics.
- **Low** confidence: The overall claim of achieving "best interpolation results" is based on limited benchmarking against competitive methods.

## Next Checks
1. Conduct a systematic ablation study varying the lubricating coefficient (γ) from 0 to 1 in increments of 0.1, measuring both artifact reduction and information preservation across multiple image pairs.
2. Implement a quantitative comparison framework using established metrics (FID, LPIPS, PSNR) to benchmark NoiseDiffusion against spherical linear interpolation and direct noise introduction on the LSUN datasets.
3. Test the method's robustness by evaluating interpolation performance across different noise levels and denoising thresholds, particularly focusing on images that significantly deviate from the expected normal distribution.
