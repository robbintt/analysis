# Graph Neural Network as Computationally Efficient Emulator of Ice-sheet and Sea-level System Model (ISSM)

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2407.01464
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.01464
- Authors: Younghyun Koo; Maryam Rahnemoonfar
- Reference count: 0
- Primary result: GCN emulator achieves 34x speedup over ISSM while maintaining correlation coefficients >0.998 for ice thickness and velocity predictions on Pine Island Glacier

## Executive Summary
This study presents a graph convolutional network (GCN) emulator designed to accelerate ice sheet modeling by replacing computationally intensive simulations of the Ice-sheet and Sea-level System Model (ISSM). The emulator is trained and validated on 20-year transient simulations of Pine Island Glacier, demonstrating exceptional accuracy with correlation coefficients exceeding 0.998 for ice thickness and velocity predictions. The GCN achieves a 34-fold speedup compared to CPU-based ISSM modeling while maintaining physical fidelity, enabling efficient prediction of future ice sheet changes under various melting rate scenarios.

## Method Summary
The researchers developed a GCN-based emulator to replace traditional ice sheet modeling by leveraging the spatial relationships inherent in glacier systems. The model was trained on 20-year transient ISSM simulations of Pine Island Glacier, using ice thickness and velocity data as primary outputs. The GCN architecture was specifically designed to capture the complex spatial dependencies in ice sheet dynamics, outperforming traditional convolutional neural networks. The emulator was implemented on GPU hardware to achieve significant computational acceleration, with performance evaluated against CPU-based ISSM simulations.

## Key Results
- GCN emulator achieves correlation coefficients >0.998 for ice thickness and velocity predictions
- 34-fold computational speedup compared to CPU-based ISSM modeling
- Successfully predicts future Pine Island Glacier changes under melting rates up to 30 m/year
- Melting rates exceeding 20 m/year lead to dramatic acceleration of mass loss and ice velocity

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The GCN emulator works by capturing the spatial relationships and dependencies in ice sheet dynamics through graph-based representations of glacier geometry. Unlike traditional convolutional neural networks, the GCN can naturally represent the irregular mesh structures used in ice sheet modeling and preserve the physical connectivity of ice flow systems. The graph structure allows the model to propagate information along flowlines and across the ice sheet surface, maintaining the essential physics of ice dynamics while dramatically reducing computational complexity.

## Limitations
- Validation limited to single glacier system (Pine Island Glacier) over 20-year period
- Performance under climate forcing scenarios beyond tested melting rates uncertain
- Correlation coefficients may not capture systematic physical biases
- GPU dependency may limit accessibility in resource-constrained settings

## Confidence
- GCN emulator accuracy on PIG (High): Strong correlation coefficients and direct comparison with ISSM outputs
- Computational speedup claims (High): Clear benchmarks provided with CPU-based ISSM
- Generalization to other ice sheet systems (Medium): Limited to PIG system; uncertainty about performance on different glacier geometries
- Long-term stability predictions (Medium): Validated for 20 years but extrapolation to longer timeframes uncertain
