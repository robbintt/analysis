## Executive Summary
This paper provides a comprehensive mathematical framework for representing an agent's uncertainty and choice preferences through coherent sets of desirable gamble sets. The work extends previous research by De Bock and de Cooman to the case of infinite sets of gambles, establishing fundamental results about the structure and properties of these representations. The paper demonstrates that coherent sets can be characterized using proper filters of coherent sets of desirable gambles, providing a powerful tool for modeling decision-making under uncertainty.

## Method Summary
The paper employs a rigorous axiomatic approach to define and analyze coherent sets of desirable gamble sets. The methodology builds on previous work in imprecise probability theory, extending it to handle infinite sets of gambles through careful mathematical construction. The author establishes the natural extension theorem as a central result, characterizing the minimal coherent extension of a given set of desirable gamble sets. The analysis includes proofs of equivalence between different axiomatizations under various conditions and examines the closure properties of collections of desirable gambles.

## Key Results
- Coherent sets of desirable gamble sets can be represented by proper filters of coherent sets of desirable gambles
- The natural extension theorem provides a complete characterization of minimal coherent extensions
- Equivalence between different axiomatizations holds when gamble sets are restricted to be finite
- All coherent sets can be represented using collections of desirable gambles with specific closure properties

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework works by establishing a systematic way to represent uncertainty through coherent sets of desirable gamble sets, which are then shown to have a natural correspondence with proper filters. This representation allows for the modeling of complex decision-making scenarios while maintaining mathematical rigor. The natural extension theorem provides a constructive method for extending partial information about preferences to a complete coherent representation, enabling practical applications in decision theory and uncertainty modeling.
