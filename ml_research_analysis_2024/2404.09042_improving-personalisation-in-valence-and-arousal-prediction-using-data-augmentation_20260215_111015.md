# Improving Personalisation in Valence and Arousal Prediction using Data Augmentation

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2404.09042
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.09042
- **Reference count**: 40
- **Primary result**: DWA achieved a maximum combined testing CCC of 0.78, compared to the baseline score of 0.76, and peak arousal and valence scores of 0.81 and 0.76, respectively

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of limited data for individual-specific emotion recognition by proposing a Distance Weighting Augmentation (DWA) method. DWA enhances personalization by augmenting an individual's dataset with similar samples from a global dataset, leveraging distance metrics to identify similar segments at the segment-level. The approach significantly improves the performance of features with low baseline performance without sacrificing the performance of high-performing features. This demonstrates effectiveness in improving personalization in real-world contexts, particularly for features that initially lag in performance.

## Method Summary
The paper proposes a segment-level distance weighting augmentation approach for personalized emotion recognition. The method augments an individual's dataset by selecting and weighting similar samples from a global dataset based on distance metrics. The augmentation process occurs at the segment level, allowing for fine-grained similarity matching between individual-specific and global data. The weighted augmented data is then used to train personalized emotion recognition models, with the goal of improving performance particularly for features that show lower baseline performance.

## Key Results
- DWA achieved a maximum combined testing CCC of 0.78, compared to the baseline score of 0.76
- Peak arousal and valence scores reached 0.81 and 0.76, respectively
- The method significantly improved performance for features with low baseline performance without sacrificing high-performing features

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The effectiveness of DWA stems from its ability to intelligently augment individual datasets with relevant samples from a larger global dataset. By using distance metrics at the segment level, the method identifies and weights similar emotional expressions, effectively expanding the training data for underrepresented or poorly performing features. This targeted augmentation allows the model to learn from a broader range of emotional patterns while maintaining the specificity required for personalization. The distance weighting ensures that more similar segments have greater influence on the learning process, preventing the dilution of individual-specific characteristics while still benefiting from the diversity of the global dataset.

## Architecture Onboarding

### Component Map
Individual Dataset -> Distance Metric -> Global Dataset -> Weighted Samples -> Augmented Dataset -> Personalized Model

### Critical Path
The critical path flows from individual dataset through distance metric computation to augmented dataset creation, which then trains the personalized model. The distance metric computation is the most computationally intensive step, requiring pairwise comparisons between individual segments and global dataset segments. The augmentation process must balance adding diversity with maintaining individual specificity, making the weighting scheme a crucial component of the pipeline.

### Design Tradeoffs
The method trades computational complexity for improved personalization. Computing distances between all individual segments and global segments is expensive but enables more precise similarity matching. The segment-level approach provides granularity but requires careful boundary definition. The weighting scheme balances the influence of similar versus dissimilar samples, with higher weights potentially leading to better performance but also risking overfitting to similar patterns.

### Failure Signatures
Poor performance may occur when distance metrics fail to capture meaningful emotional similarities, leading to irrelevant augmentations. If the global dataset is too dissimilar from the individual's expression patterns, augmentation may degrade rather than improve performance. Over-reliance on distance weighting may cause the model to become too specialized to similar patterns, reducing generalization. Performance degradation is likely when segment boundaries don't align with actual emotional transitions.

## Limitations
- Reliance on predefined segment boundaries from the RECOLA dataset limits generalization to continuous or user-defined segmentation scenarios
- Performance improvements are relatively modest (maximum CCC improvement of 0.02), raising questions about practical significance
- Evaluation focuses primarily on a single dataset (RECOLA), limiting generalizability to other contexts or emotion expression modalities

## Confidence

### High Confidence
- The core methodology of segment-level distance weighting augmentation is technically sound and well-implemented

### Medium Confidence
- The performance improvements are real but their practical significance and generalizability remain uncertain
- The comparison between DWA and global augmentation provides useful insights, though the optimal augmentation strategy depends heavily on dataset characteristics
