# Quantum Implicit Neural Compression

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2412.19828
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.19828
- Authors: Takuya Fujihashi; Toshiaki Koike-Akino
- Reference count: 4
- Key outcome: Hybrid quantum-classical INR framework (quINR) achieves up to 1.2 dB PSNR gain over JPEG2000 and COIN on LiDAR and Kodak images

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces quINR, a hybrid quantum-classical implicit neural representation framework for efficient multimedia signal compression. The approach leverages quantum neural networks' exponentially rich expressivity to capture high-frequency details that challenge small classical MLP architectures. By combining classical feature embedding with quantum circuits using folded-angle embedding and entangling layers, quINR demonstrates improved rate-distortion performance on LiDAR range images and Kodak color images compared to traditional codecs and classical INR methods.

## Method Summary
The quINR architecture combines classical feature embedding (linear + sinusoidal layers) with quantum circuits to create a hybrid implicit neural representation. Input coordinates are first processed through classical layers, then encoded into quantum states using folded-angle embedding with alternating RX and RZ gates. Multiple entangling layers iteratively process the quantum data, followed by probability measurements and QReLU activation to produce the final signal values. The framework is trained using MSE loss and evaluated on LiDAR range images from KITTI and Kodak color images.

## Key Results
- Achieves up to 1.2 dB PSNR gain over JPEG2000 and COIN in low to medium compression regimes
- Demonstrates superior performance on LiDAR range images compared to traditional codecs
- Shows potential for efficient compression of structured data with quantum expressivity advantages

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
Quantum neural networks capture high-frequency details more effectively than classical MLPs in small architectures through their exponentially rich expressivity, enabling better approximation of complex signal patterns.

### Mechanism 2
Folded-angle embedding enables compact yet expressive quantum encoding by alternating RX and RZ gates to pack more angle parameters into finite qubits while avoiding qubit-size restrictions.

### Mechanism 3
Hybrid quantum-classical architecture balances expressivity with practical implementation by using classical layers for stable coordinate-to-feature mapping while quantum layers handle complex pattern encoding.

## Architecture Onboarding

- Component map: Input -> Linear+Sinusoidal embedding -> Folded-angle embedding layer -> Multiple entangling layers -> Probability measurement -> QReLU activation -> Output
- Critical path: Coordinate input -> Classical embedding -> Quantum encoding -> Measurement -> Signal value output
- Design tradeoffs: Quantum circuit depth vs. parameter count vs. expressivity; classical preprocessing vs. direct quantum encoding
- Failure signatures: Poor PSNR despite training convergence suggests quantum circuit expressivity limitations; training instability may indicate embedding layer issues

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out

### Open Question 1
How can quantum NAS and distillation techniques be effectively integrated into the quINR architecture to improve its performance on color image compression? The authors suggest these techniques are needed but don't provide implementation details.

### Open Question 2
What are the limitations of the current folded-angle embedding technique in terms of expressivity and efficiency, and how can they be addressed? The paper introduces the technique but doesn't explore its trade-offs or potential improvements.

### Open Question 3
How does the quINR architecture scale with increasing image resolution and complexity, and what are the computational implications? The paper evaluates low to medium resolution images but doesn't discuss scalability to higher resolutions.

## Limitations

- Quantum advantage claims rely on theoretical expressivity arguments rather than direct comparisons with equivalently-sized classical architectures
- Results are primarily demonstrated on LiDAR range images with less conclusive results for natural color images
- Quantum circuit implementations use simulators rather than actual quantum hardware, leaving practical quantum advantage questions unanswered

## Confidence

- Medium: Claims about quantum expressivity advantage - supported by theoretical arguments but lacking direct ablation studies
- Medium: Folded-angle embedding benefits - novel architectural contribution with limited empirical validation
- Medium: Hybrid architecture effectiveness - hybrid approaches are well-motivated but specific benefits need more systematic evaluation
