# Informed Spectral Normalized Gaussian Processes for Trajectory Prediction

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2403.11966
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.11966
- Reference count: 4
- This paper proposes a novel regularization-based continual learning method for spectral normalized Gaussian processes (SNGPs) to enable the integration of informative priors in probabilistic deep learning models for trajectory prediction.

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces an informed Spectral Normalized Gaussian Process (SNGP) model for trajectory prediction in autonomous driving scenarios. The proposed method incorporates prior drivability knowledge through a regularization-based continual learning approach, enabling more data-efficient and robust predictions. The model builds upon established SNGP approaches while adding informative priors, requiring no rehearsal memory or parameter expansion. The authors demonstrate that their informed SNGP model outperforms non-informed and informed baselines across various performance metrics, particularly in low-data regimes.

## Method Summary
The informed SNGP model extends traditional SNGPs by incorporating informative priors through a regularization-based continual learning framework. This approach allows the model to leverage prior drivability knowledge when making trajectory predictions, improving data efficiency and robustness to location transfers. The method integrates these priors directly into the spectral normalization process of the Gaussian process, enabling the model to maintain uncertainty estimates while benefiting from the additional information. The authors apply this approach to autonomous driving trajectory prediction, demonstrating its effectiveness on two public datasets.

## Key Results
- The informed SNGP model outperforms non-informed and informed baselines across various performance metrics
- Significant improvements in data-efficiency, especially in low-data regimes
- Enhanced robustness to location-transfers between different driving environments
- The method requires no rehearsal memory or parameter expansion

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The informed SNGP model works by incorporating prior knowledge directly into the spectral normalization process of Gaussian processes. This regularization-based approach allows the model to leverage existing drivability knowledge while maintaining the probabilistic uncertainty estimates characteristic of Gaussian processes. By integrating informative priors into the normalization framework, the model can make more accurate predictions with less data and demonstrate improved generalization across different locations. The spectral normalization component ensures that the model remains well-behaved and maintains stability during training and inference.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map:** Prior Knowledge -> Regularization Layer -> Spectral Normalization -> SNGP Core -> Trajectory Output

**Critical Path:** The regularization layer is the critical component that enables incorporation of informative priors into the spectral normalization process, which then feeds into the SNGP core for trajectory prediction.

**Design Tradeoffs:** The main tradeoff is between incorporating informative priors (which may introduce bias) and maintaining the flexibility and uncertainty quantification of the base SNGP model. The regularization approach attempts to balance these competing objectives.

**Failure Signatures:** Potential failures may occur when prior knowledge is incorrect or when the regularization becomes too restrictive, limiting the model's ability to adapt to new scenarios.
