## Executive Summary

This research addresses the foundational "Symbol Grounding Problem," investigating whether a purely symbolic system can achieve meaningful semantic understanding without external reference. The core issue is that static, finite computational systems appear to rely on pre-established meanings, leading to infinite regression or circular definitions when trying to define their own symbols. The central technical mechanism is the "Grounding Act," defined as a meta-level operation categorized as either G-Expansion or G-Internal Connection, which bridges the gap between internal logic and external reality.

The study yields four primary formal theorems demonstrating specific logical limitations. The significance of this work lies in its formal refutation of the computationalist hypothesis that grounding can be achieved through internal derivation in static, finite systems.
