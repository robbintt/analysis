## üìù Executive Summary

> **The Challenge:** Vision-and-Language Navigation (VLN) agents suffer from a generalization bottleneck. Existing methods are typically dataset-specific and fail to transfer knowledge to new environments or instruction types without expensive retraining. Furthermore, while Large Language Models (LLMs) offer robust reasoning, their integration into navigation pipelines is hindered by hallucinations and a lack of low-level execution precision.

> **The Solution:** This paper introduces **FlexVLN**, a novel hierarchical framework that decouples high-level planning from low-level execution. It combines a supervised Instruction Follower (e.g., BEVBert) for precise movement with an LLM Planner (GPT-4o) for zero-shot generalization. To ensure reliability, a **Verification Mechanism** employs a Multi-modal LLM (MLLM) to validate guidance against environmental perception, mitigating hallucinations. A multi-model integration strategy triggers LLM assistance only when the Instruction Follower's predictions conflict with verified guidance.

> **The Impact:** FlexVLN establishes a new state-of-the-art for generalization in VLN. On the REVERIE dataset, it achieved an SR of 39.80%, outperforming MapGPT by +11.4%. On the SOON dataset, it improved upon the previous best (GBE) by +3.56% SR. Ablation studies confirm the critical importance of the verification mechanism and action space constraints. This work bridges the gap between dataset-specific supervised learning and the flexible reasoning of LLMs, providing a blueprint for reliable, scalable embodied agents.

## üß© Methodology

The proposed FlexVLN framework utilizes a hierarchical structure to balance reasoning with execution:

*   **Hierarchical Architecture:**
    *   Decouples the navigation process into two main components: an **Instruction Follower** and an **LLM Planner**.
*   **Hybrid Integration:**
    *   Combines the fundamental navigation capabilities of a supervised-learning-based Instruction Follower (e.g., BEVBert, GridMM) with the robust reasoning and generalization abilities of an LLM Planner.
*   **Optimization Mechanisms:**
    *   **Verification Mechanism:** Identifies and mitigates potential hallucinations generated by the LLM Planner.
    *   **Multi-model Integration:** Enhances execution accuracy by coordinating between the LLM and the Instruction Follower, specifically when predictions conflict.

## üõ†Ô∏è Technical Details

### System Architecture
*   **LLM Planner:** Utilizes **GPT-4o** or **GPT-4o-mini** to provide high-level guidance and enable zero-shot generalization.
*   **Instruction Follower:** Employs supervised agents like **BEVBert** or **GridMM** for low-level execution and movement control.

### Reliability & Perception
*   **Verification Mechanism:** Uses a Multi-modal LLM (MLLM) to validate LLM guidance against actual environmental perception to prevent hallucinations.
*   **Environmental Perception:** Utilizes **4 views** processed via **InternVL**.
*   **Multi-model Integration:** The system requests LLM assistance specifically when Instruction Follower predictions conflict with verified guidance.

### Data Representation
*   **Action Space:** Action phrases are constrained within a predefined space to ensure compatibility with the low-level controller.
*   **Navigation History:** Represented textually with the format: `{'directional phrase'} to {object}, facing toward {scene}`.

### Ablation Studies

*   **Verification Mechanism:** Removing the verification mechanism resulted in a performance drop of **-3.0% SR**.
*   **Action Space Constraints:** Removing constraints significantly reduced SR from **37% to 30%**.
*   **Perception Views:** Using 4-view InternVL (SR 37) outperformed the 8-view BLIP-2 baseline (SR 28).
*   **Model Integration:** Integrating BEVBert with FlexVLN boosted SR by **+7.0%** compared to direct transfer.
*   **LLM Variant:** GPT-4o demonstrated superior performance (SR 39, NE 6.71) compared to GPT-4o-mini (SR 37, NE 6.91).
