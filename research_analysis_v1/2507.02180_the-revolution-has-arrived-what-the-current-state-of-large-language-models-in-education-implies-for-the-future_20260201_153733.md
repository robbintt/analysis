> ### üìù Executive Summary
>
> This research addresses the critical lag between the rapid integration of Large Language Models (LLMs) into educational technology since 2022 and the absence of necessary pedagogical frameworks to support them. While LLMs have successfully established **Natural Language Processing (NLP)** as the default standard for Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), the current educational infrastructure is ill-equipped to handle this shift.
>
> The problem is not merely technological but structural; the swift adoption of these tools has disrupted the traditional operational dynamics between learners and educators without establishing the design principles needed to align system capabilities with actual learning outcomes. The paper‚Äôs primary innovation is the formulation of specific **"design imperatives"** required to evolve LLMs from general-purpose text generators into effective pedagogical agents.
>
> The analysis reveals specific successes, such as the widespread acceptance of LLMs as the standard for user interaction, alongside distinct failures in current system design. Key findings highlight that without the proposed design imperatives, existing LLMs fail to function as helpful educational aids; specifically, they currently disrupt the learner-educator relationship and cannot meet the reshaped expectations of users because system capabilities are not synchronized with pedagogical requirements.
