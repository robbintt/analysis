## Executive Summary

> The proliferation of autoregressive Speech Large Language Models (LLMs) is currently bottlenecked by the inefficiencies of existing audio codecs. Standard audio codecs operate at high frame rates, which necessitates the generation and processing of an excessive number of tokens. This sequential processing significantly hinders both training speed and inference latency, making real-time or low-latency Speech LLM applications computationally prohibitive.
>
> This research addresses this critical limitation by introducing **NanoCodec**, a neural audio codec optimized specifically for Speech LLM workflows. Informed by systematic ablation studies, the design targets a reduction in frame rates without compromising audio fidelity. Technically, NanoCodec is based on the Low Frame-rate Speech Codec (LFSC) architecture but reduces the parameter count by approximately 45%. It operates at an aggressive frame rate of **12.5 FPS** (compared to the typical 21.5 FPS) and utilizes a causal decoder to minimize first-token latency by removing the need for future token caching.
>
> NanoCodec achieved state-of-the-art performance, demonstrating that reduced frame rates do not necessitate a loss in audio quality. In comparisons against the Mimi codec at 1.1 kbps, NanoCodec achieved a Character Error Rate (CER) of 3.617 (vs. 7.221). In real-world inference testing using the Koel TTS model, NanoCodec improved the Real-Time Factor (RTF) by **2.33x** and reduced Time To First Audio (TTFA) by approximately **2.5x**. This research establishes a new benchmark for low-latency and efficient training within the domain of Speech LLMs.

## Key Findings

*   **Bottleneck Identification:** Existing audio codecs operating at high frame rates significantly hinder the speed of both training and inference in autoregressive Speech LLMs.
*   **Ablation Insights:** Studies confirmed that **frame rate**, **bitrate**, and **causality** are the critical factors influencing the reconstruction quality of audio codecs.
*   **Performance Efficiency:** The proposed NanoCodec achieves state-of-the-art audio reconstruction quality while operating at an extremely low frame rate of **12.5 FPS**.
*   **Broad Superiority:** NanoCodec outperforms related works across various bitrate ranges, validating its effectiveness for low-latency applications.

## Methodology

The authors employed a systematic empirical approach to investigate the specific impacts of various codec design choices. The process involved:

1.  **Ablation Studies:** Rigorous experiments were conducted to isolate the effects of frame rate, bitrate, and causality on reconstruction quality.
2.  **Architecture Optimization:** Insights from the ablation studies directly informed the design of the NanoCodec architecture.
3.  **Targeted Design:** The optimization process specifically targeted high-quality compression at minimal frame rates to facilitate efficient processing in Speech LLMs.

## Technical Specifications

### Architecture & Design
*   **Base Model:** Low Frame-rate Speech Codec (LFSC) with significant modifications.
*   **Efficiency:** Reduces parameters by approximately **45%** compared to the baseline LFSC.
*   **Latency Optimization:** Utilizes a **causal decoder** to minimize first-token latency and remove the need for future token caching.
*   **Configuration:**
    *   **Frame Rate:** 12.5 FPS (vs typical 21.5 FPS).
    *   **Sampling Rate:** 22.05 kHz.
    *   **Quantization:** Uses multiple codebooks (8 codebooks with 2 codes each in the 1.1 kbps configuration).

### Training Configuration

| Parameter | Setting |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hardware** | 48 A100 GPUs |
| **Batch Size** | Per-device 32 (Accumulated Total: 1,536) |
| **Data Scale** | ~301 million samples (1.1-second excerpts) |
| **Optimizer** | Adam ($\beta_1=0.8, \beta_2=0.99$) |
| **Learning Rate** | $2e^{-4}$ with exponential decay ($\gamma=0.998$) |

### Evaluation Protocol
*   **Metrics:** SQMOS (Perceptual Quality), PESQ (Wide-band Quality), Mel Dist (Spectral Accuracy), CER (Intelligibility via MMS), SECS (Speaker Similarity via ECAPA2).
*   **Datasets:** 44.1 kHz MLS test set (200 samples x 8 languages) and DAPS clean dataset.
*   **Standardization:** All metrics computed on audio downsampled to 16 kHz.

## Performance Results

### Ablation Study Results
The 12.5 FPS model showed comparable performance to the 21.5 FPS baseline, excelling in SQMOS while maintaining similar PESQ, SECS, and CER scores.

### Comparative Benchmarks

#### High Bitrate (1.78 kbps) vs. LFSC
*   **MLS Dataset:** SQMOS 4.441 (vs 4.432), SECS 0.862 (vs 0.841).
*   **DAPS Dataset:** SQMOS 4.697 (vs 4.677), CER 0.758 (vs 0.655).
*   **Note:** Achieved with ~45% fewer parameters and a causal decoder.

#### Mid Bitrate (1.1 kbps) vs. Mimi
*   **MLS Dataset:** CER 3.617 (vs 7.221), SECS 0.788 (vs 0.678).
*   **Quality:** SQMOS 4.426 (vs 4.333), PESQ 2.378 (vs 2.132).

#### Low Bitrate (0.8 kbps) vs. TS3 & WavTokenizer
*   **vs. TS3 Codec:** CER 6.519 (vs 8.159), SECS 0.706 (vs 0.672).
*   **vs. WavTokenizer:** CER 6.519 (vs 15.27), SECS 0.706 (vs 0.576).

#### Ultra Low Bitrate (0.6 kbps) vs. TAAE
*   **MLS Dataset:** SQMOS 4.407 (vs 4.370), SECS 0.701 (vs 0.323), CER 7.792 (vs 18.355).
*   **DAPS Dataset:** SECS 0.656 (vs 0.372).

### Speech LLM Inference (Koel TTS)
*   **Efficiency:** Real-Time Factor (RTF) improved by **2.33x**.
*   **Latency:** Time To First Audio (TTFA) reduced by approximately **2.5x** compared to LFSC.
*   **Context Trade-off:** Extending context audio from 5s to 10s improved speaker similarity (SECS) from 0.635 to 0.691.
