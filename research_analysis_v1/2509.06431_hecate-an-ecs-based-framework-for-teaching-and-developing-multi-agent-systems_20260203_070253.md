## ðŸ“‘ Executive Summary

Developing Multi-Agent Systems (MAS) traditionally requires specialized, agent-centric knowledge that creates a disconnect from standard distributed systems (DS) engineering practices. This gap acts as a barrier to entry, preventing developers proficient in DS from adopting MAS techniques and limiting the broader application of agent-based solutions.

This paper introduces **HECATE**, a framework built on the Entity-Component-System (ECS) architectural pattern and data-oriented design to implement MAS from a DS perspective. Technically, HECATE maps high-level agent concepts directly to ECS primitives: Agents are defined as Entities, Beliefs and Goals are represented as Components, and communication behaviors are implemented as Systems. The framework uses a dedicated "engine layer" to abstract the underlying environment and middleware, decoupling agent-to-agent communication from specific protocols.

The paper validates the framework through its adherence to three specific architectural requirements: strict ECS compliance (GR1), support for agent-oriented abstractions (GR2), and communication independence (GR3). The findings demonstrate that HECATE can successfully support diverse agent architectures, specifically those derived from the TAO framework, by flattening complex concepts like beliefs and goals into ECS components.

HECATE impacts the field by lowering the technical barrier to MAS development, enabling developers to apply familiar distributed systems patterns to agent-based problems. By serving as both a practical tool and an educational resource, the framework positions to increase the adoption of multi-agent methodologies in industry and academia.
