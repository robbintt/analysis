## üìù Executive Summary

Traditional explainability methods are increasingly insufficient for Agentic Large Language Models (LLMs) operating in high-risk domains such as law, healthcare, and public services. Current approaches typically focus on surface-level outputs or simple post-hoc attributions, failing to capture the complex reasoning pathways and systemic impacts inherent in agentic systems. This limitation poses a critical barrier to adoption in sensitive environments, where stakeholders require assurances that go beyond mere output plausibility to verify reasoning integrity and functional reliability.

The authors introduce the **TAXAL (Triadic Alignment for eXplainability in Agentic LLMs)** framework, which unifies explainability through the fusion of three dimensions: **Cognitive**, **Functional**, and **Causal**. Technically, the framework resolves the conflict between human comprehension and machine faithfulness by integrating "explanation-aware prompting" and stakeholder-specific design patterns.
*   The **Cognitive Dimension** ensures user understanding via natural language rationales.
*   The **Functional Dimension** guarantees truthfulness and compliance.
*   The **Causal Dimension** verifies faithfulness to the model‚Äôs internal computations.

By mapping specific metrics‚Äîsuch as Faithfulness to Causal and Plausibility to Cognitive‚Äîthe TAXAL model synthesizes disparate XAI techniques into a cohesive architecture that adapts to the needs of researchers, regulators, and end-users.

The authors demonstrate TAXAL‚Äôs utility through detailed case studies across law, education, healthcare, and public services. These qualitative validations reveal that the framework successfully identifies reasoning failures that standard methods miss; for instance, in the legal case study, TAXAL detected conflicts where plausible outputs masked unfaithful internal logic, while in the healthcare domain, it helped catch potential hallucinations by cross-referencing functional truthfulness against cognitive rationales.
