## Executive Summary

### **Problem**
This research addresses the critical vulnerability of multi-label image classifiers to adversarial patch attacks. While certifiable robustness has been extensively studied in single-label classification, the domain of multi-label classificationâ€”where an image must identify multiple objects simultaneouslyâ€”has lacked provable defense mechanisms. This gap is significant because many real-world computer vision applications, such as autonomous driving and medical imaging, rely on multi-label outputs, leaving them exposed to localized, physical-world attacks that existing defenses cannot guarantee protection against.

### **Innovation**
The authors introduce **PatchDEMUX**, a novel framework that achieves certifiable robustness by decomposing the multi-label classification task into a series of isolated binary classification problems. This architectural strategy allows the framework to generalize existing state-of-the-art single-label defenses, specifically PatchCleanser, to the multi-label domain. The system operates through an Input Stage, a Demultiplexing Stage that applies PatchCleanser's inference logic to independent binary classifiers, and an Aggregation Stage. Furthermore, PatchDEMUX optimizes robustness guarantees by implementing a specialized certification procedure for single-patch attacks, resulting in tighter bounds compared to general-case handling.

### **Results**
Evaluated on MS-COCO 2014 and PASCAL VOC 2007 datasets using a Vision Transformer (ViT) backbone, PatchDEMUX demonstrates that high robustness does not require significant sacrifices in clean accuracy. The model retains approximately 94% of the undefended Average Precision (AP) in non-adversarial settings. Under a standard configuration of a 2% patch size and 6x6 masks, the framework achieved a Certified AP of 44.902% and a certified precision of 56.038% at 50% recall on MS-COCO. Additionally, the approach shows resilience to architectural changes, with sensitivity analysis indicating that reducing the number of masks from 36 to 16 results in a maximum drop of only 2 points in AP.

### **Impact**
PatchDEMUX represents a foundational advancement in trustworthy machine learning by successfully extending provable robustness guarantees to the complex multi-label classification domain. The study validates the efficacy of problem decomposition as a strategy for securing multi-object classifiers and establishes a method for leveraging mature single-label certifiable techniques in broader contexts. This work bridges a major gap in computer vision security, paving the way for the deployment of multi-label systems in safety-critical environments where verifiable resistance to adversarial manipulation is essential.

## Methodology

PatchDEMUX functions through a comprehensive decomposition strategy:

1.  **Problem Transformation:** It redefines the multi-label classification task as a series of isolated binary classification problems.
2.  **Generalization:** This decomposition allows the framework to extend any existing certifiable defense designed for single-label classification to the multi-label domain.
3.  **Optimization:** For the specific scenario where an attacker is constrained to a single patch, PatchDEMUX implements a dedicated certification procedure to compute tighter robustness bounds.
4.  **Implementation:** The researchers utilized PatchCleanser as the backbone architecture to validate the methodology.

## Technical Details

**Architecture Strategy**
PatchDEMUX adapts single-label certifiable defenses (PatchCleanser) for multi-label classification by decomposing the problem into isolated binary classification problems. The architecture consists of three distinct stages:

*   **Input Stage:** Handles raw data ingestion.
*   **Demultiplexing Stage:** Applies SL-INFER (from PatchCleanser) to independent binary classifiers.
*   **Aggregation Stage:** Combines the binary outputs into the final multi-label prediction.

**Training**
*   Utilizes **defense fine-tuning** using Cutout variants.

**Algorithms & Complexity**
*   **Inference Algorithm:** Operates with a theoretical complexity of *O(c*f(n))* but a practical complexity of *O(f(n))*.
*   **Certification Algorithm:** Applies SL-CERT to generate a certification list and calculates robustness bounds (TP_lower, FP_upper, FN_upper).

## Results

**Experimental Setup**
*   **Datasets:** MS-COCO 2014 and PASCAL VOC 2007.
*   **Model backbone:** Vision Transformer (ViT).
*   **Standard Config:** 2% patch size and 6x6 masks.

**Performance Metrics**
*   **Clean Accuracy:** The defended model maintains ~94% of the undefended Average Precision (AP).
*   **MS-COCO Performance:**
    *   Certified AP: **44.902%**
    *   Certified Precision @ 50% Recall: **56.038%**
*   **Optimization:** Location-aware robustness improves certified AP by nearly 3 points.

**Sensitivity Analysis**
*   **Mask Reduction:** Reducing masks from 36 to 16 results in a maximum 2-point AP drop.
*   **Patch Size Impact:**
    *   Clean AP remains strong even with large patches (dropping from 85.731 to 69.952).
    *   Certified AP degrades significantly for patches >= 8% and drops to 0% at 32%.
