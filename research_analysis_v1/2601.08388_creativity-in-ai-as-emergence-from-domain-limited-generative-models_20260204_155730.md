## Executive Summary

> This research addresses the fundamental challenge of defining and understanding creativity within artificial intelligence, moving beyond traditional post-hoc evaluations of output quality. Current approaches often focus on assessing the final "creative" product without sufficiently explaining the underlying mechanisms that enable AI to generate novel content. This theoretical gap limits the field's ability to design systems that are genuinely creative rather than merely stochastic mimics. By shifting the focus from evaluative labels to the structural and contextual conditions that precipitate creative behaviors, the paper seeks to establish a rigorous technical basis for AI creativity.
>
> The core innovation is a "generative perspective" that reframes AI creativity as an emergent property arising from domain-limited generative models operating within bounded informational environments. Technically, the author introduces a four-part taxonomy to decompose creative behavior into interacting components: pattern-based generation, induced world models, contextual grounding, and arbitrarity. This framework utilizes structural analysis to examine how generative dynamics interact with domain-specific representations, specifically highlighting how multimodal systems leverage complex pattern recombination across data modalities (e.g., text-to-image, audio-visual) to drive the creative process.
>
> As a theoretical study, the results are conceptual rather than quantitative, foregoing traditional performance benchmarks in favor of structural validation. The research successfully demonstrates that creative output can be systematically attributed to the interaction of the four identified components within the proposed framework. The analysis validates that creativity manifests specifically through complex forms of pattern recombination in multimodal systems, confirming that the emergence of creative behaviors is dependent on the specific architecture of the bounded informational environment rather than arbitrary external labeling.
>
> This work significantly impacts the field by providing a technical framework for studying creativity as an emergent phenomenon, encouraging a shift from subjective evaluation to architectural analysis. The four-part taxonomy offers researchers a new tool for dissecting generative systems, facilitating the design of AI that exhibits more structured and intentional creative behaviors. By establishing that creativity is a product of specific system conditions, this paper paves the way for future research to optimize the generative conditions that lead to novel and contextually relevant outputs, ultimately moving the discipline beyond simple classification toward a deeper understanding of generative mechanics.
