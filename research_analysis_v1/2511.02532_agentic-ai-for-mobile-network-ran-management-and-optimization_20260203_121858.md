## Executive Summary

The rapid evolution of 5G and the impending emergence of 6G networks have created Radio Access Network (RAN) environments that are increasingly complex, dynamic, and difficult to manage using traditional automation. While "Agentic AI" offers a promising path toward autonomous network management, there is currently no established definition, standard framework, or operational design pattern for applying these advanced cognitive systems to telecommunications. This paper addresses the critical gap between general AI theory and practical RAN application, highlighting the necessity for systems capable of independent reasoning and decision-making to handle the scale and heterogeneity of next-generation networks.
