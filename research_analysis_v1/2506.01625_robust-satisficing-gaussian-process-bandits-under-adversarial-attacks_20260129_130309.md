## üìã Executive Summary

This research addresses the critical vulnerability of Gaussian Process (GP) bandits in adversarial environments, where input perturbations can lead to system failure in safety-critical applications such as autonomous navigation or financial portfolio management. Traditional optimization methods face a significant trade-off: worst-case robustness is often computationally intractable and overly conservative, while standard Bayesian optimization fails catastrophically under model mis-specification or malicious attacks.

The study introduces a paradigm shift by applying **"robust satisficing"** to GP bandits. Instead of maximizing outcomes, the objective is shifted to ensuring performance consistently meets a predefined threshold ($\tau$). The authors unify two distinct algorithmic formulations under this framework: **AdveRS-1** (Fragility Minimization) and **AdveRS-2** (Critical Radius Maximization). Both algorithms leverage GP Upper Confidence Bounds (UCB) to balance exploration with robust stability without relying on brittle worst-case estimates.

The rigorous theoretical analysis guarantees that Lenient Regret is bounded by $\tilde{O}(\sqrt{T})$, ensuring convergence over time. Empirically, the proposed methods demonstrate superior resilience compared to baselines like Robust GP-UCB, maintaining validity within the ambiguity set where standard methods fail. This work offers a principled, mathematically sound alternative to worst-case optimization, resolving the ambiguity set mis-specification problem and providing a reliable framework for hostile or unpredictable environments.

## üõ†Ô∏è Methodology

The authors utilize a **general robust satisficing framework** for Gaussian Process (GP) bandits. This approach prioritizes consistent performance targets over maximizing output.

The framework is designed to handle GP optimization problems involving unknown and varying adversarial perturbations. By shifting the focus from maximization to satisficing (meeting a specific threshold), the methodology allows for the development of algorithms that are inherently more stable and resilient to malicious input distortions.

## ‚öôÔ∏è Technical Details

The proposed approach utilizes a **Robust Satisficing (RS)** framework parameterized by $p$ to find solutions satisfying a threshold $\tau$ under adversarial perturbations, using Gaussian Process surrogate models.

| Component | Formulation | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Framework** | **Robust Satisficing (RS)** | Aims to find solutions that satisfy a threshold $\tau$ robustly against perturbations. |
| **Formulation 1** | **RS-1 (Fragility Minimization)** | Minimizes the rate of suboptimality per unit perturbation relative to the threshold. <br> *Use case:* Handling unknown adversarial budgets. |
| **Formulation 2** | **RS-2 (Critical Radius Maximization)** | Maximizes the distance a point can be perturbed while staying above the threshold. <br> *Use case:* Finding the most intrinsically stable points. |
| **Algorithm 1** | **AdveRS-1** | Implements *optimistic fragility* minimization based on RS-1. |
| **Algorithm 2** | **AdveRS-2** | Implements *estimated critical radius* maximization based on RS-2. |
| **Mechanism** | **GP Upper Confidence Bounds (UCB)** | Both algorithms rely on GP-UCB to estimate necessary metrics (fragility and radius) for decision making. |

**Key Metrics Defined:**
*   **Lenient Regret:** Measures the cumulative loss relative to the threshold $\tau$.
*   **Robust Satisficing Regret:** Measures the loss relative to the robust satisficing benchmark.
