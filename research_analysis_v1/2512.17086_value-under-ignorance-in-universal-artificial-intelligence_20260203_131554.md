## Executive Summary

The AIXI model, a foundational framework for Universal Artificial Intelligence, suffers from a critical theoretical constraint: its reliance on semimeasures where missing probability mass is interpreted as the agent "dying." This binary restriction limits the model to standard reward-based reinforcement learning, severely narrowing the class of admissible utility functions. Consequently, AIXI cannot effectively handle environments characterized by ambiguity or true ignorance, constraining the theoretical scope of general intelligence to scenarios with well-defined probabilistic risk.

The authors introduce a theoretical generalization of AIXI by reframing semimeasure loss through the lens of imprecise probability theory. Instead of treating probability gaps as terminal events, the model reinterprets this loss as "total ignorance." Technically, the authors utilize the Choquet integral to compute expected utilities under these non-additive belief distributions. This approach generalizes the standard recursive value function, allowing the agent to evaluate interaction histories with incomplete environmental hypotheses while maintaining mathematical consistency with previous formulations.

The study provides rigorous theoretical characterizations rather than empirical benchmarks. The authors prove that the Choquet integral of returns is mathematically equivalent to the standard recursive value function, ensuring backward compatibility. Crucially, they establish that the Choquet integral value function is $\Pi_2$-measurable, securing its limit-computability. Conversely, the authors demonstrate that general expected utilities derived under the traditional "death interpretation" cannot be characterized as Choquet integrals, mathematically distinguishing the two approaches and highlighting the structural limitations of the former.

This research significantly advances Universal Artificial Intelligence by decoupling the theoretical model from the restrictive "death" metaphor, thereby expanding AIXI's applicability to complex, ambiguous environments. By bridging UAI with imprecise probability theory and Choquet integration, the paper provides a robust new toolset for analyzing value functions under ignorance. This conceptual shift enables a more nuanced understanding of agency in incomplete information scenarios, broadening the theoretical bounds of AI to encompass a wider spectrum of utility-based interactions.
