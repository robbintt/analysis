## Executive Summary

This paper addresses the critical challenge of providing accurate, actionable, and contextually relevant agricultural decision support to smallholder farmers facing the complexities of climate change. Traditional AI systems often fail in this domain due to their static, single-agent nature, susceptibility to hallucinations, and inability to integrate real-time, localized data. This gap is particularly detrimental to vulnerable rural communities and non-English speakers who require reliable, multilingual guidance on strategies such as water conservation and soil management to ensure their livelihoods and food security.

To overcome these limitations, the authors introduce **AgroAskAI**, a novel multi-agent reasoning framework built on a modular, role-specialized architecture. Unlike static models, this system employs a chain-of-responsibility approach, utilizing specialized agents to coordinate autonomous tasks. The technical core integrates external APIs (OpenCage, OpenWeather) and a curated document repository with an OpenAI Large Language Model. Crucially, the architecture incorporates an internal governance structure featuring a "Reviewer Agent" to validate outputs and mitigate hallucinations, while supporting dynamic, multilingual interactions.

Notably, AgroAskAI delivered water conservation implementation steps in 13 instances where ChatGPT failed entirely and provided historical weather data in 16 runs compared to ChatGPT's 3. ## Technical Details

**System Architecture:**
AgroAskAI employs a **Multi-Agent System (MAS)** architecture consisting of role-specialized agents:

*   **Prompt Agent:** Handles initial query formulation.
*   **Parsing Agent:** Processes and interprets input data.
*   **Agent Manager:** Coordinates the workflow between agents.
*   **Weather Forecast Agent:** Retrieves real-time weather predictions.
*   **Weather History Agent:** Accesses historical meteorological data.
*   **Solution Agent:** Generates agricultural strategies and advice.
*   **Reviewer Agent:** An internal governance mechanism to validate outputs and mitigate hallucinations.

**Integrations & Data Sources:**
*   **External APIs:** OpenCage (Geocoding), OpenWeather (Weather Data).
*   **Knowledge Base:** A curated document repository.
*   **Core Engine:** OpenAI Large Language Model for generative capabilities and multilingual support.
