## 4. Technical Details

HIP-LLM is a hierarchical Bayesian framework using Imprecise Probabilities to quantify LLM reliability.

### Structural Hierarchy
The system organizes reliability across three levels:
1.  **System**
2.  **Domain**
3.  **Subdomain**

It uses **Operational Profiles (local and global weights)** for aggregation across these levels.

### Statistical Model
*   **Likelihood:** Binomial likelihood.
*   **Subdomain Parameters:** Uses a Beta-Binomial conjugate prior.
*   **Hyperpriors:** Beta and Gamma hyperpriors are applied for mean and precision at higher levels.

### Uncertainty Management
To handle epistemic uncertainty, the model utilizes an **'admissible set' of hyperparameters**. This generates **reliability envelopes (intervals)** rather than simple point estimates.

### Computational Approach
*   **Subdomain Level:** Uses closed-form conjugate analysis.
*   **Domain & System Levels:** Relies on calculating Cumulative Distribution Functions (CDFs) via **Monte Carlo sampling** of independent Betas to handle the complexity of weighted sums.
*   **Sequential Operations:** Defines reliability for consecutive operations via power transformations of the probability parameter.
