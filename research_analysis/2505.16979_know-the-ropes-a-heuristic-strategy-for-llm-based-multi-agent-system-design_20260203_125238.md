## Executive Summary

This research addresses the inherent unreliability and inefficiency plaguing current LLM-based Multi-Agent Systems (MAS). Existing systems frequently suffer from ill-posed task decompositions and fuzzy inter-agent contracts, resulting in high error rates and computationally expensive verification overhead. The authors argue that the "No-Free-Lunch" theorem renders universal prompting strategies ineffective for complex reasoning tasks, creating a critical need for a design methodology that moves beyond generic, zero-shot approaches toward structured, domain-specific solutions.

The core innovation is the **"Know-The-Ropes" (KtR) framework**, a heuristic strategy that translates domain priorsâ€”derived from classical algorithmsâ€”into hierarchical algorithmic blueprints. KtR employs "algorithm-aware decomposition," recursively splitting tasks into typed, controller-mediated sub-tasks that fit within a base LLM's zero-shot capabilities. Crucially, the framework minimizes verification overhead by relying on the structural integrity of the blueprint; by enforcing strict Typed I/O Contracts and deterministic hierarchical flows, the system isolates errors and reduces the need for extensive post-hoc checking.

Evaluations on combinatorial optimization problems demonstrate significant accuracy improvements over baselines. For the Knapsack Problem using GPT-4o-mini, a 3-agent KtR blueprint achieved **95% accuracy** on size-5 instances, a massive increase from the 3% zero-shot baseline. In the Task-Assignment Problem, a 6-agent architecture utilizing o3-mini secured **100% accuracy** for problem sizes up to 10 and maintained 84% accuracy on larger instances (sizes 13â€“15).

This work challenges the assumption that large monolithic LLMs are strictly necessary for complex tasks, proving that modest models can perform reliably when supported by rigorous structural design. By validating that algorithm-aware decomposition and targeted augmentation can compensate for smaller model capacity, KtR provides a blueprint for building cost-effective and verifiable AI systems.

## Methodology

The research introduces the **Know-The-Ropes (KtR)** framework, designed to convert domain priors into an algorithmic blueprint hierarchy.

*   **Task Decomposition:** KtR employs a recursive splitting strategy where tasks are broken down into typed, controller-mediated subtasks. This contrasts with ill-posed decompositions typical of conventional multi-agent systems.
*   **Execution Strategy:** The framework prioritizes a disciplined approach grounded in the No-Free-Lunch theorem. It attempts to solve subtasks zero-shot initially, applying the lightest viable augmentation (e.g., chain-of-thought, micro-tuning, self-check) only when necessary.
*   **Verification:** The approach aims to minimize verification overhead by relying on the structural integrity of the algorithmic blueprint rather than extensive post-hoc checking.
