Current Large Language Model (LLM) agents lack the psychological depth and consistency required to replicate human thinking in high-fidelity social simulations. These agents often fail to capture the deeper goals, emotional conflicts, and complex motivations that drive human interactions. This limitation presents a significant barrier to developing realistic agent-based models for social science research, as the agents cannot maintain the persona consistency necessary for authentic dialogue simulation.

To address this, the authors propose a Multi-Agent System (MAS) architecture grounded in Transactional Analysis (TA) theory. The efficacy of this design was evaluated through rigorous ablation tests comparing agents utilizing the retrieval mechanism against those relying solely on internal prompting. The experimental data demonstrated that agents equipped with the contextual retrieval mechanism achieved higher scores in these metrics compared to the baseline, providing measurable evidence that the enriched context leads to superior coherence and context-awareness in dialogue generation.

This research provides a blueprint for enhancing realism in social simulations by integrating psychologically grounded structures with advanced LLM capabilities. By validating the use of Transactional Analysis and contextual retrieval, the authors establish a viable new direction for creating agents capable of simulating complex human motivations.
