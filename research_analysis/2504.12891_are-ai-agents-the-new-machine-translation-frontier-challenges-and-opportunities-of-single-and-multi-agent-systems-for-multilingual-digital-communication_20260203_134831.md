## Executive Summary

This paper addresses the critical limitations of general-purpose Neural Machine Translation (NMT) in high-stakes professional domains, specifically legal translation. It identifies a performance gap where single-agent systems, while efficient for general or simpler tasks, fail to provide the domain-specific knowledge and contextual nuance required for complex scenarios. The research frames the central problem as determining whether the operational complexity of multi-agent systems can be justified by superior performance. By establishing a clear dichotomy between use cases, the study posits that single-agent systems are suitable for low-complexity translations, whereas multi-agent architectures are essential to overcome the rigidity of monolithic models in precision-sensitive environments.

The key innovation is a shift from monolithic NMT to agentic architectures defined by four core technical attributes: **Autonomy**, **Tool Use**, **Memory**, and **Workflow Customization**. The study implements a multi-agent workflow architecture validated through a pilot study utilizing a sequential pipeline of four specialized agents: Translation, Adequacy review, Fluency review, and Final editing. The study validates that multi-agent systems deliver translation quality superior to traditional MT and single-agent systems through a qualitative evaluation anchored by four specific Research Questions. While standard quantitative metrics like BLEU or COMET were not the focus, the results provide concrete evidence of significant improvements in domain-adaptability and contextual awareness. The pilot study confirmed that the structured four-agent workflow aligns more closely with human translation processes than monolithic models. Furthermore, the investigation yielded specific findings regarding the impact of model temperature and size on performance, demonstrating that the division of labor among specialized agents yields more accurate handling of nuanced legal text.

This work establishes a foundational blueprint for integrating AI agents into professional translation workflows, with immediate significance for the legal sector and other high-precision industries. ## Technical Details

The paper proposes a shift from monolithic Neural Machine Translation (NMT) to AI agent-based architectures.

### Core Attributes
*   **Autonomy:** Independent operation with specific instructions.
*   **Tool Use:** Dynamic integration of external resources like Translation Memory and RAG.
*   **Memory:** Feedback loops for iterative refinement.
*   **Workflow Customization:** Adaptable processes based on task needs.

### Architectural Workflows
1.  **Prompt Chaining:** A sequential pipeline of Preprocessing, Translation, and Post-Editing.
2.  **Routing:** Dynamic task allocation based on language, domain, or complexity.
3.  **Parallelization:** Concurrent execution of subtasks such as multilingual scaling or concurrent QA.
