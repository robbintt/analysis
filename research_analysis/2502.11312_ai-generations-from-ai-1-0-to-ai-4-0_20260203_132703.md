## Key Findings

*   **Four Distinct Generations:** AI evolution is categorized into four overlapping generations:
    *   **AI 1.0:** Information/Pattern Recognition
    *   **AI 2.0:** Agentic/Real-time Decision Making
    *   **AI 3.0:** Physical/Robotics & Autonomous Systems
    *   **AI 4.0:** Conscious/Self-directed AI
*   **Shifting Drivers:** Technological progress is driven by shifting bottlenecks among three core resources: algorithms, computing power, and data.
*   **Capability Evolution:** System capabilities evolve from static processing to adaptive planning, physical interaction, and finally, autonomous goal-setting.
*   **Persistent Synergies:** Generations do not replace one another; they build upon previous layers, creating a cumulative stack of intelligence.
*   **Ethical Escalation:** Ethical complexity increases significantly as AI systems transition from static tools to human-like autonomous agents.
