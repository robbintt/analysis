## üìë Executive Summary

This research addresses the challenge of controlling large-scale, Weakly-Coupled Markov Decision Processes (WCMDPs) with independent, heterogeneous arms under global budget constraints, overcoming the curse of dimensionality and limitations of previous homogeneity assumptions. The core innovation involves a **"projection-based Lyapunov method"** paired with an Index-based policy featuring reassignment, which projects high-dimensional dynamics onto a lower-dimensional representation to bound drift and correct budget violations dynamically.

The proposed method achieves an optimality gap of $O(1/\sqrt{N})$ in long-run average reward, demonstrating asymptotic optimality and polynomial computational complexity as the number of arms increases. As the first proof of asymptotic optimality for fully heterogeneous WCMDPs, this work provides a robust, scalable framework for managing diverse real-world systems such as power grids and communication networks.

## üîë Key Findings

*   **Efficient Optimality:** An efficiently computable policy was identified that achieves an **$O(1/\sqrt{N})$ optimality gap** in the long-run average reward per arm.
*   **Scalable Performance:** The performance of the policy improves as the system scales; the optimality gap vanishes as the number of arms ($N$) increases, effectively addressing the **curse of dimensionality**.
*   **Heterogeneity Support:** These results are achievable under mild assumptions, even when dealing with **fully heterogeneous settings** where every subproblem has distinct model parameters.
*   **Convergence:** The proposed method ensures that rewards and costs converge to an optimal region in the long run.

The research details a robust theoretical and practical framework for decision-making in complex environments.

*   **System Model:** The system is modeled as a Weakly-Coupled Markov Decision Process (WCMDP) consisting of $N$ arms in a fully heterogeneous setting. Each arm possesses distinct transition probabilities, rewards, and cost functions.
*   **State Representation:** Employs a matrix-based state representation utilizing one-hot vectors.
*   **Constraints:** Imposes global budget constraints for $K$ types that scale linearly with the number of arms ($\alpha_k N$), ensuring feasibility through a zero-cost action.
*   **Policy Mechanism:** Utilizes an **"ID policy with reassignment"** under the planning setting where model parameters are known a priori.
*   **Theoretical Core:** Relies on a projection-based Lyapunov method to construct specific Lyapunov functions used to mathematically certify the stability and performance of the policy.
